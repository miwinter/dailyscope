US Government and Allied Western Perspectives on Stricter Immigration Enforcement

This viewpoint emphasizes a stringent approach to immigration control focusing on national security and ideological alignment. The United States, under the Trump administration, implemented policies to tighten visa issuance, scrutinize social media for anti-American sentiments, revoke protections like TPS, and apply tougher naturalization criteria. These measures highlight a preference for limiting immigration flows to those deemed compliant and loyal, reflecting concerns around border security, migration management, and ideological conformity. Such policies provoke legal and humanitarian challenges but align with an exclusionary immigration paradigm prioritized by the US government and echoed by some European authorities.
