Shooting Near White House Targets US National Guard: Security and Immigration Concerns

US National Guard Shootings 2025-11-27 • 57 articles in total

In brief
  • A shooting near the White House injured two National Guard members and involved an Afghan suspect with an expired visa.
  • The incident increased security around the US capital and led to more National Guard deployment.
  • President Trump condemned the attack as terrorism linked to immigration failures and called for stricter policies.

A shooting incident near the White House critically injured or killed two US National Guard members, with the suspect identified as an Afghan national who entered the US in 2021. The attack has heightened security concerns around the US capital, prompting lockdowns and deployment of additional National Guard troops. President Donald Trump condemned the shooting as a terrorist act and a crime against humanity, linking it to immigration policies and calling for stricter measures. Investigations are ongoing to determine motives and prevent future incidents, while conflicting reports about the victims' conditions have emerged. The event has sparked international attention, emphasizing challenges in US domestic security and immigration enforcement.
