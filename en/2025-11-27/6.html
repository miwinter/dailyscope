Tensions and Cooperation at the G20 Summit with Africa in Focus

G20 Summit Africa Focus 2025-11-27 • 18 articles in total

In brief
  • The US excluded South Africa from the 2026 G20 summit, citing political and racial disputes that strained relations.
  • African leaders and international partners emphasized Africa's growing global role and called for cooperation on economic development.
  • Different countries advocated for inclusive and sustainable agendas while highlighting investments and partnerships with Africa.

The G20 Summit hosted by South Africa highlighted both diplomatic tensions and cooperative initiatives involving African nations. US President Donald Trump announced South Africa's exclusion from the 2026 Miami G20 summit, citing political and racial disputes, which strained US-South Africa relations. Meanwhile, African leaders and other countries emphasized the continent's growing role in global affairs, with calls for partnerships on critical minerals and economic modernization, notably involving China and the UK. Civil society movements and some G20 members advocated for inclusive and sustainable development agendas, contrasting with US opposition to certain summit declarations. Overall, the summit underscored Africa's rising international influence amid complex geopolitical dynamics.
