Military Coup in Guinea-Bissau Disrupts Electoral Process and Raises Regional Concerns

Guinea-Bissau Military Coup 2025-11-27 • 64 articles in total

In brief
  • On November 26, 2025, Guinea-Bissau's military staged a coup, arrested President Embaló, and suspended the electoral process amid disputed results.
  • The coup worsened Guinea-Bissau's political instability and drew condemnation from ECOWAS, the African Union, and the international community.
  • International bodies urge respect for constitutional order and peaceful dialogue to resolve the crisis and ensure regional stability.

On November 26, 2025, the military in Guinea-Bissau staged a coup d'état, detaining President Umaro Sissoco Embaló and suspending the ongoing electoral process amid disputed election results. Gunfire near the presidential palace and the closure of borders and media outlets marked the tense atmosphere as the military declared full control over the country. This takeover has intensified political instability in a nation with a history of coups, raising serious concerns about the future of democracy and governance in Guinea-Bissau. Regional bodies like ECOWAS and the African Union have condemned the coup and called for respect of constitutional order, while the international community watches closely for developments. The situation remains fluid with uncertainty about the military's plans and the country's political trajectory.
