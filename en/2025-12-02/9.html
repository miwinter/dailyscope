Deadly Landslide in Peru Causes Multiple Fatalities and Missing Persons

Peru Landslide Fatalities 2025-12-02 • 10 articles in total

In brief
  • A severe landslide in Peru sank two passenger boats, causing at least 12 deaths and around 30 people missing.
  • Rescue operations continue amid difficult conditions and disrupted local transport at the river port.
  • The disaster highlights the region's vulnerability to natural hazards and the need for improved safety and emergency preparedness.

A severe landslide in Peru has resulted in at least 12 to 13 confirmed deaths and around 30 people reported missing, primarily after two passenger boats were submerged in a river port area. Rescue and emergency operations are ongoing amid challenging weather and terrain conditions, with authorities striving to locate survivors and provide aid. The disaster has disrupted local transport and raised concerns about infrastructure resilience and disaster preparedness in the region. Heavy rains and flooding are cited as contributing factors, highlighting the vulnerability of Peru's coastal and riverside communities to natural hazards. The incident has drawn international attention to the urgent need for improved safety measures and emergency response capabilities.
