France restricts alcohol and outdoor activities amid extreme heat

France Heatwave Restrictions 2026-06-22 • 18 articles in total

In brief
  • France imposed alcohol bans and restricted outdoor events during a severe heatwave exceeding 40°C to protect public health.
  • Officials issued health advisories and mobilised services to shield vulnerable people and reduce strain on infrastructure.
  • These measures reflect a broader European pattern of escalating climate-driven extreme heat events affecting public policies and daily life.

Facing an intense heatwave and red alerts in many departments, French authorities have imposed temporary limits on alcohol consumption in public spaces and at large festivals and have restricted outdoor sports and events to reduce heat-related health risks. Officials issued public-health advisories, mobilised services, and warned vulnerable people to avoid travel and exertion as temperatures approached or exceeded 40°C. The measures led to cancellations or altered plans for major gatherings, including the Fête de la Musique, and increased strain on transport, energy and health infrastructure. Coverage places these actions both as immediate precautionary steps and as part of a broader European pattern of escalating climate-driven extreme-heat events.
