Venezuela's Interim Presidency Amid Political Turmoil and International Intervention

Venezuela Interim Presidency 2026-01-05 • 27 articles in total

In brief
  • Delcy Rodríguez was appointed interim president of Venezuela after Nicolás Maduro was captured, with support from the military and judiciary.
  • Opposition demands a democratic transition, release of political prisoners, and a leader elected through free elections.
  • International views are divided on U.S. involvement and emphasize the need for diplomatic dialogue and political stability.

Following the capture and removal of Nicolás Maduro by U.S. forces, Delcy Rodríguez has been appointed as the interim president of Venezuela, receiving support from the Venezuelan military and judiciary. This leadership change has sparked a complex political crisis marked by uncertainty, with opposition figures calling for democratic transition and the release of political prisoners. International reactions vary, with some emphasizing the U.S. role and others urging calm and respect for sovereignty. Despite the change in figurehead, Maduro's allies remain influential, and Venezuela faces significant challenges in achieving political stability and democracy. The situation remains tense, with ongoing power struggles and diplomatic efforts shaping the country's future.

Opposition and Democratic Transition Advocates

Represented by Venezuelan opposition figures and diaspora communities, this perspective stresses the need for a genuine democratic transition, including the release of political prisoners and respect for electoral outcomes. It views Rodríguez's interim presidency skeptically, urging perseverance until a democratically elected leader like Edmundo González assumes office. The opposition highlights ongoing human rights concerns and the importance of restoring democratic institutions.
