US Strategic Moves to Control Venezuelan Oil Amid Political Upheaval

US–Venezuela Oil Geopolitics 2026-01-05 • 26 articles in total

In brief
  • The US aims to control Venezuela's oil reserves through economic pressure and political interventions to replace Maduro's government.
  • US actions cause geopolitical tensions and disrupt global oil markets, affecting supply and prices.
  • Venezuela reduces oil output in response to US embargoes, complicating international energy dynamics.

The United States has intensified efforts to influence Venezuela's political landscape primarily through control and exploitation of its vast oil reserves, the largest in the world. Key US figures, including President Donald Trump and Senator Marco Rubio, emphasize economic pressure tactics such as oil blockades and military interventions aimed at ousting Nicolás Maduro and installing a government more aligned with US interests. These actions have led to significant geopolitical tensions, impacting global oil markets with fluctuating prices and uncertainty over supply stability. Venezuela's response includes reducing oil output in reaction to US embargoes, further complicating the energy dynamics. Overall, the US strategy reflects a broader geopolitical competition to prevent rival powers from accessing Venezuelan resources while securing American energy and political influence in the region.
