US Assertive Control and Threats in Venezuelan Political Crisis

Trump Venezuela Governance Threat 2026-01-05 • 20 articles in total

In brief
  • The US, led by Trump, claims it will govern Venezuela until a political transition is secure and threatens harsher actions against non-compliant leaders.
  • Venezuelan leaders and some international voices reject US intervention, viewing it as a violation of sovereignty and an aggressive power grab.
  • Views differ internationally, with some seeing US actions as necessary for stability and others condemning them for coercion and illegality.

Following the capture of Venezuelan President Nicolás Maduro by US forces, President Donald Trump declared that the United States would govern Venezuela until a safe political transition is achieved. Trump issued stern warnings to interim Venezuelan leaders, particularly Vice President Delcy Rodríguez, threatening harsher consequences if they do not comply with US demands. This assertive stance reflects a broader US strategy to control Venezuela's political and economic future, including its vital oil industry. The international community has expressed concern and criticism over the US's direct involvement and military actions, with some countries urging rejection of what they perceive as a power grab. The situation remains tense and fluid, with ongoing pressure on Venezuelan leadership and uncertainty about the country's sovereignty and governance.

Articles reflecting Venezuelan internal perspectives focus on the political tension following Maduro's capture, with the Supreme Court appointing Delcy Rodríguez as interim president who condemns foreign intervention and calls for national unity. This viewpoint underscores resistance to US involvement and highlights the internal struggle for legitimacy and sovereignty amid external pressure and threats from the US.

Critical International Perspectives on US Intervention

Articles from countries like Japan and others critical of US actions frame the US involvement as an aggressive power grab and violation of Venezuelan sovereignty. They call for rejection of US intervention and warn of broader international implications, emphasizing concerns about the legality and morality of foreign military actions and governance imposition in Venezuela.
