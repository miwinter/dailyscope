US Military Capture of Venezuelan President Maduro Sparks Global Political Turmoil

US–Venezuela Maduro Capture Operations 2026-01-05 • 139 articles in total

In brief
  • US forces captured Venezuelan President Nicolás Maduro in a coordinated military operation involving airstrikes and special raids, transferring him to custody in New York.
  • The operation sparked mixed international reactions, with some viewing it as a law enforcement act and others condemning it as a violation of sovereignty that intensified geopolitical tensions.
  • Maduro's capture caused political turmoil in Venezuela, raised legal questions, impacted global markets, and influenced US relations with major world powers.

In a highly coordinated and unprecedented military operation, US forces captured Venezuelan President Nicolás Maduro and transferred him to custody in New York, marking a significant escalation in US-Venezuela relations. The operation involved extensive intelligence gathering, airstrikes, and special forces raids, resulting in casualties including 32 Cuban nationals allied with Maduro. The US, led by President Donald Trump, declared intentions to govern Venezuela until a political transition occurs, provoking mixed international reactions ranging from support to condemnation. The capture has intensified geopolitical tensions, raised questions about sovereignty and legality, and triggered protests and political divisions within Venezuela and across Latin America. The event also reverberated in global markets and diplomatic circles, highlighting the complex interplay of regional stability, international law, and US foreign policy shifts.
