Natural Disasters Across Asia, Africa, and Beyond: Impact, Rescue Efforts, and Regional Disputes

2025-10-07 • 40 articles in total

The region spanning Nepal, India, and neighboring countries has been severely affected by heavy monsoon rains leading to devastating landslides, floods, and flash floods, resulting in a high death toll exceeding 70 and widespread displacement. Rescue and relief operations are ongoing, but damaged infrastructure and harsh weather conditions complicate efforts. High-altitude regions around Mount Everest faced extreme blizzards and storms, stranding hundreds of climbers and prompting international rescue missions. In East Africa and the Nile basin, flooding has exacerbated regional tensions, notably between Egypt, Sudan, and Ethiopia, due to disputes over water management linked to the Ethiopian mega-dam project. Other natural disasters include typhoons in China provoking mass evacuations, earthquakes in the Philippines and Afghanistan, and severe storms across Europe and the US causing fatalities and extensive damage.
