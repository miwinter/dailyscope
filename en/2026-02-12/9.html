EU Strategic Autonomy Amid Rising US Tensions and Global Challenges

EU Strategic Autonomy and US Threat Perception 2026-02-12 • 9 articles in total

In brief
  • European leaders call for strategic autonomy in defense, energy, and economy to reduce dependency on the United States.
  • The EU pursues military advancements and industrial cooperation to strengthen defense capabilities and compete globally.
  • Economic resilience against China and the US is essential for Europe's global competitiveness and sovereignty.

European leaders and experts increasingly emphasize the need for strategic autonomy in defense, energy, and economic competitiveness amid growing concerns about dependence on the United States. French President Macron and EU officials warn of profound transformations and urge reforms to strengthen the bloc's global position. Energy reliance on US liquefied natural gas and strained transatlantic relations, including trade tensions, fuel calls for diversification and self-reliance. Military advancements, such as Europe's hypersonic missile test and advocacy for indigenous combat aircraft, reflect ambitions to reduce dependency on US defense capabilities. Simultaneously, security concerns about Russia and economic competition from China and the US drive EU efforts to bolster resilience and autonomy.

French and Central European Calls for EU Reform and Defense Autonomy

French President Macron and Central European figures emphasize the urgent need for EU transformation and strategic autonomy to reduce reliance on the US. They highlight the risks of passivity and advocate for stronger defense capabilities and economic competitiveness to face geopolitical threats, including Russia. This perspective stresses proactive European leadership and self-reliance within NATO and globally.

German Industry and Security Perspectives on Reducing US Dependence

German voices, including political and industrial leaders, focus on practical steps toward European military autonomy, such as developing indigenous combat aircraft and addressing transatlantic tensions at security forums. They underline the importance of European defense innovation and cooperation to mitigate reliance on US military support. This viewpoint reflects a pragmatic approach to strengthening Europe's strategic capabilities.

Concerns Over Energy Dependence and Trade Tensions with the US

Articles from the Netherlands and Romania highlight the risks of Europe's growing dependence on US liquefied natural gas and the potential for trade conflicts, especially under volatile US political leadership. The European Parliament's readiness to suspend trade agreements signals deteriorating trust and a push for economic sovereignty. This cluster underscores economic and energy vulnerabilities linked to US relations.

EU Economic Resilience Against Global Competitors

The EU leadership focuses on enhancing economic resilience and competitiveness to counter challenges posed by China and the United States. Efforts include policy measures to protect European industries and maintain technological edge, reflecting a broader strategy of strategic autonomy beyond defense. This perspective frames economic strength as essential to Europe's global standing.

European Military Technological Advancements to Counter Russia

The successful test of a European hypersonic missile system demonstrates a commitment to advancing military technology to rival Russian capabilities. This development signals a shift toward greater European defense independence and strategic competition with Russia. It highlights the EU's intent to assert itself as a significant military power.
