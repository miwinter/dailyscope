US and Venezuela Move Toward Renewed Energy Cooperation

US–Venezuela Energy Deal 2026-02-12 • 12 articles in total

In brief
  • US and Venezuela are improving relations through energy cooperation and discuss lifting the US oil embargo.
  • US visits focus on increasing Venezuelan oil production and achieving energy market stability.
  • Venezuela seeks a long-term partnership with the US to support economic recovery and energy stability.

Recent developments indicate a significant thaw in US-Venezuela relations centered on energy cooperation. High-level visits, including that of US Energy Secretary Chris Wright, have led to discussions about lifting the longstanding US oil embargo and boosting Venezuelan oil production. Both countries express intentions to establish a long-term productive partnership despite ongoing political complexities. These engagements reflect a pragmatic approach to reviving Venezuela's oil industry and enhancing bilateral energy ties, with potential impacts on regional and global energy markets. The cooperation signals a notable shift from previous tensions toward mutual economic and energy interests.

US Perspective on Energy Engagement and Production Increase

US sources emphasize strategic efforts to deepen energy ties with Venezuela, focusing on increasing oil production and lifting embargoes. The US Energy Secretary's visits and statements highlight a pragmatic approach to re-engage Venezuela's oil sector as part of broader energy and geopolitical interests. The US frames these moves as historic and pivotal for energy dominance and market stability.

Venezuelan Viewpoint on Long-Term Partnership and Cooperation

Venezuelan officials, including Delcy Rodríguez, stress the establishment of a long-term, productive partnership with the US. They highlight ongoing dialogues and meetings that confirm cooperation despite past political differences. Venezuela presents this collaboration as a positive shift toward economic recovery and enhanced energy sector stability.
