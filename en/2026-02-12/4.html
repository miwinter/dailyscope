Cuba's Deepening Energy Crisis Disrupts Flights, Economy, and International Relations

Cuba Energy Crisis 2026-02-12 • 16 articles in total

In brief
  • Cuba faces a severe energy crisis causing power outages, flight suspensions, and economic instability worsened by U.S. sanctions and oil supply disruptions.
  • Several countries have suspended flights and are evacuating tourists, while Russia plans humanitarian oil shipments amid geopolitical tensions.
  • The crisis impacts daily life, education, and regional stability, prompting monitoring and humanitarian concern from nearby nations like Jamaica.

Cuba is facing a severe energy crisis marked by critical fuel shortages that have led to widespread power outages, suspension of flights, and disruptions in transportation and tourism. The crisis is exacerbated by U.S. sanctions and oil supply interruptions, severely impacting daily life and economic stability on the island. Several countries, including Russia and Canada, have suspended flights and are evacuating tourists due to the worsening situation. Jamaica is closely monitoring the impact on its students in Cuba, highlighting regional humanitarian concerns. Russia's planned oil shipments to Cuba as humanitarian aid underscore the geopolitical dimensions of the crisis amid strained international relations.
