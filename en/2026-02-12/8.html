Assassination Attempt on Colombian President Highlights Security Challenges

Colombia Assassination Attempt 2026-02-12 • 9 articles in total

In brief
  • Colombian President Gustavo Petro survived an assassination attempt linked to drug cartels and political violence.
  • Credible intelligence led to flight route changes that prevented the attack.
  • The incident highlights Colombia's deep security challenges and raises concerns about political stability.

Colombian President Gustavo Petro survived a targeted assassination attempt while traveling by helicopter, an event confirmed across multiple international reports. Authorities had credible intelligence warnings, prompting changes in his flight route and cancellation of planned landings to ensure his safety. The attempt is linked to ongoing political violence and drug cartel activities, reflecting deep-rooted security challenges in Colombia. This incident has intensified concerns about the stability of Petro's administration and the broader political environment. Investigations are ongoing to identify the perpetrators and prevent future threats.
