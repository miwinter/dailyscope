Regional bilateral deals: security, investments and diplomatic outreach

Region: Bilateral Deals 2026-05-11 • 125 articles in total

In brief
  • Major powers and regional players use diplomacy, defense cooperation, and economic deals to consolidate influence and advance security and trade interests.
  • Smaller states and regional blocs focus on practical cooperation through infrastructure projects, trade agreements, and development assistance.
  • Persistent tensions over borders and resources coexist with efforts to deepen connectivity, dispute resolution, and cross-border investment.

This selection of reports shows active bilateral diplomacy across regions, with states pursuing territorial claims, security pacts, trade and large-scale investments while attending high-level ceremonies and summits. Major powers and regional players are consolidating influence through defense cooperation, commemorative diplomacy and strategic economic deals — from Russia and China to Turkey and European partners. Smaller states and regional blocs likewise advanced practical cooperation: infrastructure projects, trade fairs, air and maritime agreements, and development assistance feature prominently. Persistent tensions and dispute-resolution efforts — over borders, maritime rights, water treaties and ceasefires — run alongside efforts to deepen connectivity and cross-border investment.
