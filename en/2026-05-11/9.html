Latvian defence minister resigns after drone incidents

Latvia Defense Minister Resigns 2026-05-11 • 11 articles in total

In brief
  • Latvia's defence minister resigned after drone incidents revealed airspace security gaps and caused political trust loss.
  • The drones are linked to the Ukraine conflict and prompted concerns over regional security and infrastructure attacks.
  • The resignation sparked debates on defence policy reforms and leadership changes to improve national security.

Latvia's defence minister Andris Spruds resigned after a series of drone incidents that exposed gaps in the country's airspace security and prompted demands for political accountability. Prime Minister Evika Silina said the minister had lost her and the public's trust, accelerating the leadership change and raising questions about defense preparedness. Several reports link the incidents to drones associated with the conflict in Ukraine, while others highlight an attack on critical infrastructure such as an oil storage site. The resignation has triggered domestic debate over defence policy and promises of a successor as officials consider reforms to strengthen border and aviation security.
