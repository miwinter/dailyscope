Mediation efforts and fragile truces

Russia–Ukraine Mediation 2026-05-11 • 21 articles in total

In brief
  • Russia offers limited ceasefires and negotiations conditional on Ukrainian concessions like Donbas withdrawal.
  • Western actors, including Germany, distrust Moscow's mediation proposals and doubt Russia's willingness to change conditions.
  • International efforts including US-linked truces and UN support face challenges due to ceasefire violations and lack of enforcement.

Recent reports show intensified diplomatic activity around Russia–Ukraine mediation: Moscow has announced short ceasefires, declared the war is nearing its end, and offered meetings while attaching conditions such as withdrawal from Donbas. Germany and other Western actors have been openly sceptical about some of Moscow’s proposals, notably the suggestion to appoint chancellor Gerhard Schröder as a mediator. U.S. president Donald Trump and U.S.-linked envoys have been reported as announcing or supporting short truces, but Kyiv and Moscow have quickly traded accusations of violations and several accords have not been implemented. The United Nations and other international actors have welcomed pauses and prisoner exchanges, while warning that sustained progress will require credible enforcement and political compromise.

Kremlin's conditional diplomacy

Russian official messaging emphasizes openness to negotiations and limited ceasefires while framing progress as contingent on Ukrainian concessions. Kremlin statements include offers to meet, claims the war is nearing its end, and explicit conditions such as withdrawal from Donbas.

German and Western scepticism

German and other Western sources express distrust of Moscow's proposed mediators and doubt that Russia has changed the conditions for ending the war. Berlin has publicly questioned the credibility of appointing ex-German figures like Schröder to mediate without substantive shifts in Moscow's position.

Trump-led truce and US envoys

Reports center on a U.S.-linked mediation track in which Donald Trump or U.S. envoys are credited with announcing short truces and proposals for humanitarian pauses. These initiatives have been immediately contested on the ground, with both sides blaming the other for violations.

Fragile ceasefires and non-implementation

Multiple accounts highlight that ceasefire talks and short agreements frequently fail to halt fighting or are not fully implemented on the ground. Observers report continued clashes, mutual accusations of breaches, and short-lived pauses such as reported May 9–11 agreements.

  • Ceasefire talks have not been implemented
  • Ukraine and Russia accuse each other of breaking the ceasefire
  • Russia and Ukraine have agreed to a ceasefire from May 9 to May 11

International organisations pressing for dialogue

The UN and multilateral actors have welcomed ceasefires and prisoner exchanges and are urging renewed dialogue and restraint. International reporting frames these moves as necessary but insufficient without enforceable guarantees and follow-through.
