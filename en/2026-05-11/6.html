Trump's May China Visit: High-Stakes Summit on Trade and Security

US-China Diplomacy 2026-05-11 • 38 articles in total

In brief
  • Trump will visit Beijing from May 13–15 for high-level talks covering trade, technology, Taiwan, and security issues.
  • Economic negotiations are underway in Seoul supporting broader U.S.-China diplomacy beyond political discussions.
  • The summit is influenced by regional tensions, including the Iran war and security concerns on the Korean Peninsula and Japan.

China has formally confirmed U.S. President Donald Trump will visit Beijing from May 13–15, signaling renewed high-level engagement between the two powers. Officials and analysts expect talks to span trade, tariffs, artificial intelligence, Taiwan, Iran and nuclear issues, while preparatory economic negotiations are being held in Seoul. The visit arrives amid regional anxieties — notably on the Korean Peninsula and Japan — and against the backdrop of the Iran war, which could give Beijing leverage in certain dossiers. Observers differ on whether the summit will stabilize tensions or simply reframe strategic competition, but most agree it is a consequential diplomatic moment with economic and security implications.
