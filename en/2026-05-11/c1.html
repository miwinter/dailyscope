Iran has issued stern warnings against the deployment of French and British naval forces near its shores, threatening decisive military responses. French President Macron has publicly denied plans to send warships, emphasizing diplomatic coordination and opposition to navigation restrictions, reflecting France's cautious approach to regional security and its desire to avoid direct confrontation.

France's renewed Africa policy and diplomatic challenges

France is attempting to revitalize its influence in Africa through the Africa Forward summit in Nairobi, signaling a shift from traditional Sahel-focused engagement to broader continental partnerships emphasizing economic transformation and sovereignty. President Macron has acknowledged the end of France's 'backyard' role in Africa, advocating for African nations' autonomy in development decisions. However, diplomatic strains persist, notably with Madagascar, which expelled a French diplomat over alleged destabilization, highlighting the complexities of France's evolving African diplomacy.
