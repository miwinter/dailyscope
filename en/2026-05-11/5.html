Hantavirus outbreak aboard MV Hondius prompts WHO-led evacuations

WHO Hantavirus Cruise Outbreak 2026-05-11 • 62 articles in total

In brief
  • A hantavirus outbreak on the MV Hondius led to an international evacuation and coordinated health screening centered in Tenerife.
  • The WHO assessed the outbreak risk as low, differentiated it from COVID-19, recommended 42 days of symptom surveillance, and coordinated the response.
  • Multiple countries deployed teams for testing, contact tracing, repatriation, and managing infections, while passengers reported confusion and distress during evacuations.

An outbreak of hantavirus aboard the passenger ship MV Hondius triggered an international evacuation and repatriation operation centered on Tenerife, where health teams screened and disembarked dozens of passengers. The World Health Organization has characterized the immediate public-health risk as low relative to COVID‑19, sent senior leadership to coordinate the response, and recommended 42 days of early symptom surveillance for evacuees. National health agencies, including U.S. authorities, have deployed response teams, emphasized that hantavirus transmission differs from COVID‑19, and are testing and monitoring evacuees; several people have shown symptoms or tested positive and a small number of deaths have been reported. Governments scrambled to track contacts and organize flights while passengers and influencers described confusion and distress, renewing broader concern about infectious-disease risks on cruise ships and port preparedness.
