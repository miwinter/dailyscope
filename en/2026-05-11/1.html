Talks Stall as Trump Rejects Tehran’s Reply, Mediators Keep Channels Open

US–Iran Diplomacy 2026-05-11 • 133 articles in total

In brief
  • President Trump publicly rejected Iran's peace proposal reply, emphasizing tough conditions and military warnings.
  • Iran demands sanctions relief and nuclear concessions as conditions for de-escalation, challenging U.S. acceptance.
  • Despite threats and skirmishes, regional mediators maintain active diplomacy amid rising risks to the fragile ceasefire and economic stability.

Across global coverage, President Trump has publicly dismissed Iran’s written reply to a U.S. peace proposal as “unacceptable,” reinforcing a hardline U.S. posture and raising doubts about an imminent deal. Tehran’s responses, reported to include demands for sanctions relief, reparations and concrete nuclear concessions, show Iranian negotiators seeking substantial concessions as a precondition for de‑escalation. Regional mediators and neutral states — notably Pakistan, Qatar and Oman — continue to transmit messages and keep diplomacy alive even as both sides trade threats and occasional skirmishes at sea. The exchanges have already affected markets and security calculations: oil prices ticked up and analysts warn that munitions levels, naval movements and recent clashes risk undoing the fragile ceasefire. Overall, the picture is one of active but fragile diplomacy, with mediation ongoing even as political leaders publicly harden their lines and the risk of renewed escalation persists.
