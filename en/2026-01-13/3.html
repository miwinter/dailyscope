Tensions Rise Over US Ambitions to Acquire Greenland

Denmark–US Greenland Dispute 2026-01-13 • 36 articles in total

In brief
  • The US under President Trump seeks to acquire Greenland to counter Russia and China and enhance strategic Arctic positioning.
  • Greenland and Denmark reject US acquisition attempts, emphasizing sovereignty and existing political arrangements.
  • European and NATO officials warn that US moves could destabilize alliances, while diplomatic efforts continue amid Arctic tensions.

The United States, under President Donald Trump and supported by some Republican lawmakers, has expressed strong interest in acquiring Greenland, citing strategic and geopolitical concerns, particularly to counter Russia and China. This ambition has manifested in public statements, proposed bills in Congress, and diplomatic pressure, escalating tensions with Denmark and Greenland. Greenland's government and Denmark firmly reject any US attempts to annex or control the island, emphasizing sovereignty and the importance of maintaining current political arrangements. European and NATO officials warn that aggressive US actions could destabilize alliances, while diplomatic visits and discussions continue amid heightened geopolitical competition in the Arctic. The situation remains a critical flashpoint reflecting broader international struggles for influence in the Arctic region.
