Global Divisions Over US Intervention in Venezuela

US Intervention in Venezuela 2026-01-13 • 9 articles in total

In brief
  • The US intervention in Venezuela divides international opinion along geopolitical lines, with China, Russia, and African experts condemning it as illegal.
  • Some Latin American countries support or justify the US actions to resolve Venezuela's crisis, while European and Latin American responses remain cautious or weak.
  • The situation reveals complex international diplomacy involving strategic, legal, and political challenges, with varied global perspectives and calls for balanced engagement.

The US intervention in Venezuela has elicited sharply divided international reactions, reflecting broader geopolitical alignments. Countries like China, Russia, and some African experts condemn the operation as a violation of international law and an infringement on Venezuelan sovereignty. Conversely, several Latin American nations, including Peru and Argentina, support the US actions or view them as necessary steps toward resolving the crisis. European and Latin American responses are characterized as weak or cautious, with concerns about the implications of US influence in the region. The situation underscores the complexity of international diplomacy surrounding Venezuela and the varied perspectives on US involvement.

Opposition from China, Russia, and African Experts

China, Russia, and African experts strongly oppose the US intervention, condemning it as a breach of international law and an attack on Venezuela's sovereignty. They call for the release of Nicolás Maduro and warn against the dangerous precedent set by such unilateral actions. This viewpoint highlights concerns about respect for international norms and the risks of escalating geopolitical tensions.
