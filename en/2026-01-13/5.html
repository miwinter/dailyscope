Escalating US Pressure and Cuban Resistance Amid Shifting Diplomatic Dynamics

US–Cuba Political and Sanctions Pressure 2026-01-13 • 25 articles in total

In brief
  • The US has increased pressure on Cuba through sanctions, oil supply threats, and support for opposition to force negotiations under its terms.
  • Cuba firmly rejects US demands, emphasizes sovereignty, and maintains resistance supported by allies like China and Russia.
  • The US allows Mexico to supply oil to Cuba, signaling a strategic, nuanced approach to regional stability.

The United States, under President Donald Trump, has intensified political and economic pressure on Cuba, including threats of sanctions and cutting off Venezuelan oil supplies. While the US has authorized Mexico to continue supplying oil to Cuba, signaling a nuanced approach, Trump and key US figures like Senator Marco Rubio have openly supported Cuban opposition efforts and urged Cuba to negotiate under US terms. Cuba firmly rejects these pressures, emphasizing its sovereignty and denying any ongoing negotiations, with President Miguel Díaz-Canel repeatedly affirming resistance to US demands. International allies such as China and Russia have voiced support for Cuba, condemning US sanctions and highlighting the geopolitical complexity of the situation. This dynamic reflects deepening tensions, with Cuba striving to withstand long-standing embargoes and new US strategies aimed at regime change or increased leverage.
