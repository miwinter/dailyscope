Escalating US-Venezuela Tensions: Military Actions and Political Claims

US–Venezuela Military and Political Tensions 2026-01-13 • 17 articles in total

In brief
  • The US has intensified military and political actions against Venezuela, including capturing Maduro, imposing naval blockades, and seizing vessels.
  • Venezuela and its allies condemn US actions as illegal and violations of sovereignty, asserting their legitimacy and criticizing interference.
  • International actors highlight regional security concerns and geopolitical impacts involving Russia, China, and neighboring countries.

The US-Venezuela relationship has sharply deteriorated with multiple military and political confrontations. The US has conducted operations including the capture of Venezuelan President Nicolás Maduro, naval blockades targeting Venezuelan oil tankers, and seizures of vessels linked to Venezuela and Russia, provoking strong condemnation from Venezuela, Cuba, Russia, and other international actors. Venezuelan leaders like Delcy Rodríguez assert their legitimacy and criticize US interference, while US figures including Donald Trump claim authority over Venezuela's political future. Reports of mysterious weapons used by US forces and regional security concerns further complicate the situation, highlighting a volatile geopolitical environment with broader implications involving Russia, China, and regional neighbors. International bodies have condemned US actions as violations of sovereignty and international law, intensifying diplomatic tensions.
