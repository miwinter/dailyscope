Escalating La Paz unrest: clashes, vandalism and a tightened security response

Bolivia La Paz Protests 2026-05-19 • 8 articles in total

In brief
  • Protests in La Paz demand President Rodrigo Paz's resignation and involve supporters of former president Evo Morales.
  • Demonstrations have turned violent with clashes, vandalism, looting, and disruption to daily life.
  • Authorities respond with arrests, injuries reported, accusations of armed groups, and detention orders for protest leaders.

Protests in La Paz have intensified amid Bolivia's political crisis, with demonstrators demanding President Rodrigo Paz's resignation and supporters of former president Evo Morales playing a prominent role. Demonstrations have repeatedly turned violent, featuring clashes with police, widespread vandalism and episodes of looting that disrupted daily life in the capital. Authorities report dozens of arrests and injuries, accuse armed groups of being involved, and prosecutors have ordered the detention of protest leaders as part of a broader security and legal response. Coverage frames the unrest as a mix of political mobilization and public-order challenges that could influence Bolivia's short-term political trajectory.
