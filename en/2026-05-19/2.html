Pause, Pressure and Fragile Talks

US–Iran Diplomacy 2026-05-19 • 92 articles in total

In brief
  • President Trump has paused planned strikes on Iran and is pursuing diplomacy backed by threats and deadlines.
  • Iran rejects some U.S. demands, submits counterproposals, and remains defiant, complicating negotiations.
  • Regional intermediaries and economic pressures support ongoing talks amid U.S. domestic political debates on Iran policy.

President Trump says he is in contact with Iran and has repeatedly paused or postponed planned strikes while framing negotiations as showing a real chance for a deal. At the same time he and other U.S. officials continue to warn Tehran — using deadlines and threats — underscoring that diplomacy is backed by the prospect of force. Tehran has submitted counterproposals and remains defiant in some statements, and U.S. officials say Iran’s offers so far do not meet Washington’s demands. Regional actors and intermediaries (Gulf states, Pakistan, Oman) are reported to be mediating exchanges, while discussions over sanctions relief and market signals add economic leverage as domestic U.S. politics pushes for both pressure and oversight.
