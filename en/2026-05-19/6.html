San Diego mosque shooting: casualties, suspects and unfolding probe

San Diego Mosque Attack Probe 2026-05-19 • 48 articles in total

In brief
  • The San Diego mosque shooting resulted in three to five deaths, including two attackers who may have committed suicide.
  • Investigators probe hate-crime motives as suspects allegedly stole firearms and had local ties, prompting increased mosque security.
  • Political leaders condemned the attack amid rising Islamophobia, highlighting community impact and national concerns over hate crimes.

Reporting on the San Diego mosque shooting shows evolving and sometimes conflicting early counts, with outlets alternately citing three or five fatalities and varying descriptions of where victims and suspects were found. Multiple sources identify teenage suspects, some of whom were later found dead and may have killed themselves, while investigators probe possible racial or hate-crime motives. Coverage highlights emerging suspect details — authorities say guns were taken from a relative’s home and at least one attacker has links to a nearby school — as law enforcement, including the FBI, gathers evidence. The incident prompted tightened security at mosques, political condemnation, and human-interest accounts of victims, while initial scene reports produced differing casualty updates as the investigation proceeded.
