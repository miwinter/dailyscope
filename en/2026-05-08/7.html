Court Blocks Trump's 10% Global Tariff as EU Ultimatum Looms

US Tariff Policy Ruling 2026-05-08 • 12 articles in total

In brief
  • A U.S. trade court ruled the administration's 10% global tariff unlawful, limiting its use.
  • The president is demanding a trade deal with the EU by July 4 under threat of higher tariffs or no tariffs.
  • Court rulings and presidential threats increase market uncertainty and complicate U.S.-EU trade negotiations.

A U.S. trade court has ruled the administration's 10% global tariff unlawful, either blocking it outright or narrowing its application. Coverage from multiple countries frames the decision as a judicial check on executive trade powers and a setback for the president's tariff agenda. At the same time, the president is issuing hardline demands and deadlines to the EU, threatening higher or tariff-free conditions unless a deal is reached by July 4. Observers warn the combination of court rulings and presidential threats will heighten uncertainty for markets, constrain U.S. negotiating leverage, and complicate transatlantic trade diplomacy.
