Escalating US sanctions and Cuban condemnation

US–Cuba Sanctions Diplomacy 2026-05-08 • 16 articles in total

In brief
  • The U.S. has increased sanctions targeting Cuban military-controlled enterprises to pressure Havana's economy and military networks.
  • The sanctions risk disrupting China-linked supply chains and metal markets while amid mixed U.S. rhetoric about military action causing regional unease.
  • Cuban authorities and UN experts condemn the sanctions as economic warfare harming civilians and violating international law.

The United States has intensified targeted sanctions on Cuban military-controlled enterprises, including measures aimed at a nickel operation, with officials presenting the steps as pressure on Havana's economic and military networks. Observers warn the sanctions could disrupt China-linked supply chains and global metal markets and may fit into broader scenarios Washington is studying to prompt political change. U.S. rhetoric is inconsistent: Donald Trump has made aggressive claims about seizing control of Cuba while other officials and foreign leaders publicly deny imminent military action, generating regional diplomatic unease. Cuban authorities and allied voices condemn the measures as economic warfare and violations of international law, concerns echoed by UN experts who say the blockade endangers access to medicine and other fundamental rights.

Cuban government: sanctions as siege and illegal coercion

Cuban officials and state-aligned outlets frame U.S. sanctions as a deliberate campaign of economic warfare intended to undermine Cuba's sovereignty and harm its population. They describe the measures as a siege or genocidal intent that contravenes the UN Charter and call for international solidarity against unilateral U.S. pressure.

Coverage emphasizes the policy objective of choking revenue sources for Havana while noting potential knock-on effects for trade and international supply chains.
