Escalating Venezuela–US Tensions: Military Pressure and Terrorist Designations

2025-11-24 • 38 articles in total

Tensions between Venezuela and the United States have sharply escalated, marked by increased US military presence in the Caribbean and plans for new operations targeting the Maduro government. The US has designated Venezuela's Cartel of the Suns as a terrorist organization and labeled President Nicolás Maduro himself a terrorist, intensifying sanctions and diplomatic pressure. Venezuela condemns these actions as aggressive and destabilizing, accusing the US of interventionism and resource-driven motives. Regional actors like Brazil and Spain advocate for dialogue and caution against military escalation, while reports of covert US operations and maritime confrontations with Russian vessels highlight the complex geopolitical stakes. The situation remains volatile, with international concerns over potential conflict and the future of Venezuela's political landscape.
