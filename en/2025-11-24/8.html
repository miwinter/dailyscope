Mass Kidnapping of Nigerian Schoolchildren Highlights Escalating Security Crisis

2025-11-24 • 15 articles in total

Over 300 students and a dozen teachers were abducted from a Catholic school in Nigeria, marking one of the largest mass kidnappings in the country's recent history. While about fifty students managed to escape captivity, hundreds remain held by armed groups, underscoring the persistent security challenges facing educational institutions. The Nigerian government has responded by closing dozens of schools in affected regions to protect students. Efforts by authorities to rescue the hostages and restore safety continue amid ongoing violence and instability. This crisis has drawn international attention to the urgent need for improved security measures in Nigeria's conflict-affected areas.
