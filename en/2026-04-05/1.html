Escalating US-Israel-Iran Conflict: Military Strikes, Rescue Missions, and Diplomatic Deadlocks

Iran War 2026-04-05 • 135 articles in total

In brief
  • The US and Israel conduct military strikes on Iranian facilities, escalating the conflict and causing casualties.
  • Iran retaliates with missile and drone attacks on Israel and Gulf states, asserting military strength and sovereignty.
  • International actors call for mediation amid fears of broader war, economic impacts, and potential war crimes.

The ongoing war between the United States, Israel, and Iran has intensified with multiple military strikes targeting Iranian nuclear and energy facilities, resulting in casualties and heightened regional tensions. Iran has responded with missile and drone attacks on Israel and Gulf states, asserting its military capabilities including shooting down US fighter jets, which has led to high-risk rescue operations for downed American pilots. Diplomatic efforts remain stalled, with ceasefire talks collapsing and international calls for de-escalation growing amid fears of broader conflict and economic repercussions such as rising oil and food prices. The conflict has drawn global attention, with various countries and international law experts condemning attacks and warning of potential war crimes, while some nations seek mediation. The situation remains volatile, with both sides issuing ultimatums and threats, and the risk of further escalation persists.

Iran's Defensive and Retaliatory Stance

Iran emphasizes its sovereignty and demands respect, listing conditions for ending the war while actively retaliating against US and Israeli strikes. Tehran highlights its military resilience, including shooting down US aircraft and conducting missile and drone attacks on Israel and Gulf countries. Iranian officials warn of severe consequences if its infrastructure is targeted and frame US-Israeli actions as violations of international law. Iran also projects internal control and mobilizes public support amid the conflict.
