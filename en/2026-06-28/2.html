Gulf Flashpoint: US–Iran Strikes and Rising Regional Spillover

Middle East Conflict Escalation 2026-06-28 • 137 articles in total

In brief
  • The US and Iran exchanged retaliatory strikes near the Strait of Hormuz, escalating tensions and threatening maritime security and global energy supplies.
  • Gulf states condemn Iranian attacks on their infrastructure and call for collective defense, while Lebanon's border with Israel shows risks of wider regional escalation.
  • Analysts warn fragile ceasefires and aggressive rhetoric increase the danger of miscalculation and broader conflict across multiple regional fronts.

Over the past days the United States and Iran have traded repeated strikes centered on the Strait of Hormuz, with Washington portraying its actions as retaliation for attacks on commercial vessels and Iran and its Revolutionary Guards claiming reprisals against U.S.-linked sites. Tanker and merchant-ship incidents have led to suspended evacuations and raised alarms about maritime security and global energy supplies. Gulf states and regional partners have condemned drone and missile strikes against their territories, raising air-defence alerts and pressing for collective security, while Lebanon’s border with Israel and Hezbollah’s rejection of recent agreements threaten another front of escalation. International actors and analysts warn that aggressive rhetoric, legislative moves in Washington and the collapse of fragile ceasefires increase the risk of miscalculation and a wider regional conflagration.
