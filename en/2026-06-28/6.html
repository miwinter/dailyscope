Vučić resigns amid protests and calls early elections

Serbia Leadership Transition 2026-06-28 • 24 articles in total

In brief
  • Serbian President Aleksandar Vučić will resign and call early elections following sustained anti-government protests.
  • Vučić plans to remain politically active, using resignation as a tactical move rather than a full exit.
  • Uncertainty surrounds election timing, raising concerns about political instability and Serbia's regional orientation.

Serbian President Aleksandar Vučić announced he will resign within weeks and has called for early presidential and parliamentary elections, a move widely reported across international outlets. Many accounts link the decision directly to prolonged anti-government and student-led protests that pressured the administration. Coverage diverges on consequences: some reports stress uncertainty about when elections will be held and the risk of political upheaval, while others emphasize Vučić’s statement that he will remain politically active and may try to retain influence. Observers also note the performative nature of recent rallies and consider potential implications for Serbia’s regional orientation and ties with Russia.
