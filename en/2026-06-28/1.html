Deadly twin quakes overwhelm Venezuela as rescue window narrows

Venezuela Earthquake Response 2026-06-28 • 178 articles in total

In brief
  • Powerful earthquakes in Venezuela caused a disputed death toll exceeding 1,400 and left tens of thousands missing amid ongoing aftershocks.
  • International and local efforts include rescue teams, field hospitals, humanitarian aid, and grassroots solidarity, amid frustration at slow government response.
  • UN agencies warn of catastrophic humanitarian and economic impacts, emphasizing urgent medical, child protection, and recovery needs.

A series of powerful earthquakes struck Venezuela, producing a rapidly rising and disputed death toll (reports range from dozens to more than 1,400) and leaving tens of thousands unaccounted for. International rescue teams, heavy machinery and field hospitals have been mobilized as the crucial window for finding survivors closes and aftershocks continue. UN bodies and UNICEF warn of catastrophic humanitarian and economic consequences, with millions affected and children especially vulnerable. Amid large-scale foreign assistance, scenes of local solidarity, anger at slow or politicized responses, and a stream of dramatic rescues and miracles have shaped public reaction to the disaster.
