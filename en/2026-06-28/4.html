Escalation, strikes across borders and political fractures

Russo-Ukrainian War 2026-06-28 • 55 articles in total

In brief
  • Ukraine intensifies long-range strikes on Russian military and energy targets to isolate Crimea, causing states of emergency and civilian disruptions.
  • Russia continues heavy missile attacks on Ukrainian cities, causing casualties and escalating humanitarian crises.
  • The conflict fuels information battles, Western divisions on support, Russian internal dissent, economic damage, and regional diplomatic tensions.

Reporting shows a clear intensification of Kyiv’s campaign of long-range strikes aimed at Russian military and energy targets and at isolating occupied Crimea, prompting Russian-declared states of emergency on the peninsula and widespread civilian disruption. At the same time Russia continues heavy missile and air attacks on Ukrainian cities, causing casualties and damage and deepening humanitarian needs. Cross-border drone incidents, disputed tallies of shootdowns and claims about strikes inside Russian regions fuel a noisy information battle while Western governments issue warnings, prepare defenses and remain divided on levels of support. The conflict’s strain is visible inside Russia through dissent, hardline calls to escalate and reported economic damage, and beyond the battlefield in prisoner exchanges, human trafficking allegations and diplomatic frictions with neighboring states.
