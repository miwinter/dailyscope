Guinea-Bissau Military Coup Sparks Regional and International Condemnation

Guinea-Bissau Military Coup 2025-11-30 • 50 articles in total

In brief
  • A military coup in Guinea-Bissau ousted President Embaló, leading to his exile and military rule over the government.
  • International organizations condemned the coup, suspended Guinea-Bissau's membership, and demanded a return to constitutional order.
  • Some analysts doubt the coup's legitimacy, suggesting a self-coup or staged event amid ongoing political unrest and protests.

A military coup in Guinea-Bissau led to the detention and exile of President Umaro Sissoco Embaló, triggering widespread condemnation from international and regional organizations including the United Nations, African Union, and ECOWAS. The coup disrupted the electoral process and resulted in the military declaring total control, appointing a new government while the ousted president fled first to Senegal and then to the Republic of Congo. Regional leaders and bodies have suspended Guinea-Bissau's membership and demanded the restoration of constitutional order, emphasizing the threat to democratic principles. Some commentators and former officials, notably from Nigeria, questioned the nature of the coup, suggesting it might be a self-coup or staged event, reflecting the complex political dynamics. The situation remains unstable with ongoing political violence, protests, and diplomatic efforts to resolve the crisis.

Their statements highlight concerns about regional stability and the importance of respecting electoral processes disrupted by the coup.
