Pope Leo XIV's Historic Turkey Visit Promotes Interfaith Dialogue and Regional Peace

Pope Peace Tour Turkey 2025-11-30 • 15 articles in total

In brief
  • Pope Leo XIV's Turkey visit fosters interfaith dialogue, respect for Islam, and strengthens Vatican-Turkey diplomatic relations.
  • He calls for Christian unity, peace, and family values to overcome conflicts and religious divisions.
  • The visit extends to Lebanon to promote hope and reconciliation amid regional crises.

Pope Leo XIV's visit to Turkey marks a significant milestone in fostering interfaith dialogue, religious tolerance, and diplomatic relations between the Vatican and Turkey. His historic visits to the Sultan Ahmed Mosque and the Blue Mosque, where he respectfully removed his shoes, symbolize a gesture of respect towards Islam and a commitment to mutual understanding. The Pope also emphasized Christian unity, peace, and the importance of family as a foundation for social cohesion amid regional tensions. His messages during Masses and public appearances called for an end to conflicts and the avoidance of religious divisions, highlighting the role of religion in promoting peace rather than conflict. The visit concludes with the Pope traveling to Lebanon to continue his mission of hope and reconciliation in a region facing ongoing crises.
