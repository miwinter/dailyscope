Escalating US Pressure and Cuban Defiance Amid Venezuela Crisis

US–Cuba Political and Sanctions Pressure 2026-01-12 • 46 articles in total

In brief
  • The US under Trump intensified pressure on Cuba by threatening to cut Venezuelan oil supplies and pushed for regime change through political and economic means.
  • Cuba firmly rejected US ultimatums, emphasizing sovereignty and readiness to defend against external threats.
  • Venezuela and regional allies condemned US actions and supported Cuba, while diplomatic talks between the US and Cuba continued amid tensions.

Following the capture of Venezuelan President Nicolás Maduro, US President Donald Trump intensified political and economic pressure on Cuba, threatening to cut off Venezuelan oil supplies and financial support to the island. Trump urged Cuba to reach an agreement with the US "before it's too late," even suggesting his ally Marco Rubio as a potential future Cuban leader, signaling a hardline stance aimed at regime change. Cuba, led by President Miguel Díaz-Canel, responded defiantly, emphasizing national sovereignty and readiness to defend against external threats, rejecting US ultimatums. Venezuela and other regional allies reaffirmed their support for Cuba, condemning US actions as violations of sovereignty and non-intervention principles. The situation highlights deepening geopolitical tensions in the Caribbean, with ongoing diplomatic efforts and sanctions shaping the complex US-Cuba-Venezuela relations.
