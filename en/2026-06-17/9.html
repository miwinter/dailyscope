Series of strong quakes around Palu and wider regional seismic activity

Palu Earthquake 2026-06-17 • 8 articles in total

In brief
  • Strong earthquakes struck Central Sulawesi near Palu, causing panic, destruction, and emergency response efforts.
  • Seismological agencies and international outlets reported preliminary magnitudes, locations, and ongoing aftershock monitoring.
  • The events highlight regional and global seismic risks, with recent quakes in Southeast Asia and a deadly earthquake in China.

A series of strong earthquakes struck Central Sulawesi near Palu and surrounding parts of the region, with reported magnitudes clustered around 6.2–7.0. Seismological agencies including the CENC and the USGS issued preliminary magnitudes and locations and warned of aftershocks while local authorities mobilized assessments. Photo-led coverage and local reports describe panic, destruction, and ongoing rescue and relief efforts as teams search for casualties and evaluate damage. Several international outlets placed the Palu events in the broader context of recent large quakes in the southern Philippines, and one U.S. report highlighted a separate, deadly earthquake in northwestern China, underscoring global seismic risk.
