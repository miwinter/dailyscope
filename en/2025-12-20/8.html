Putin's Firm Stance and Conditions in Annual Press Conference

In brief
  • Putin affirmed Russia's military actions in Ukraine and rejected blame for starting the war.
  • He insisted the West must respect Russia and holds the key to peace, while refusing to change current policies.
  • The press conference mixed geopolitical statements with personal revelations, projecting strength and conditional diplomacy.

In his annual press conference, President Vladimir Putin reaffirmed Russia's military actions in Ukraine, emphasizing that no new operations will occur if Russia is respected internationally. He portrayed Russia's position as firm, dictating peace terms with territorial demands and rejecting blame for starting the war. Putin expressed frustration with Western sanctions and insisted the West holds the key to resolving the conflict, while also mixing personal revelations and geopolitical assertions. Despite hopes for peace in 2026, he signaled no change in Russia's current policies or military course. The conference highlighted Russia's narrative of strength, sovereignty, and conditional diplomacy amid ongoing tensions.
