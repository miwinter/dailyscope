Laos cave rescue: survivors located, extraction continues

Laos Cave Rescue 2026-05-28 • 15 articles in total

In brief
  • Rescuers found five people alive in a flooded cave in Laos with two still missing amid dangerous conditions and complex underwater passages.
  • The rescue involves international cooperation, specialized cave divers, and urgent needs like additional oxygen for safe extraction.
  • Reports vary on survivor counts, reflecting evolving information during the high-pressure, technical rescue operation.

Rescuers located five people alive inside a flooded cave in central Laos after about a week, while most outlets report two others remain unaccounted for. Reports emphasize hazardous conditions—rising water, narrow submerged passages, and limited air—that have made the extraction slow and dangerous and prompted deployment of specialized cave divers and international assistance. Coverage highlights immediate needs such as additional oxygen and coordinated multi-agency efforts, while on-the-ground reporting conveys families' relief and the tense, technical nature of the underwater extraction. One account claims all seven were rescued, illustrating how information is evolving in a high-pressure rescue; overall the priority remains safely extracting survivors and locating the remaining missing people.
