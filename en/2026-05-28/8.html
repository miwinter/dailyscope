Bolivia on edge: military options, emergency powers and calls for dialogue

Bolivia Political Crisis 2026-05-28 • 9 articles in total

In brief
  • Bolivia faces severe protests causing shortages and prompting government moves to authorize military deployment and consider a state of emergency.
  • The president urges dialogue and proposes a multisectoral council to manage the crisis and de-escalate tensions.
  • Evo Morales politicizes the unrest as a regional rebellion against government submission to the United States, increasing political tensions.

Widespread blockades and protests in Bolivia have pushed the country toward a political breaking point, producing shortages of food, fuel and medicine and fuelling urgent warnings from the presidency. Lawmakers and the government have moved to authorize military deployments and are openly considering a state of emergency to regain control, prompting debates about legality and civil liberties. At the same time the president has repeatedly called for dialogue and proposed a multisectoral council as a path to de-escalation and coordination. Political figures such as Evo Morales frame the unrest in geopolitical terms, accusing the government of being submissive to the United States and portraying protests as a broader rebellion, heightening regional political tensions.
