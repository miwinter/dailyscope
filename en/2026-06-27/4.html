In brief
  • Ukraine is escalating long-range strikes and drone attacks to pressure Russian military and infrastructure.
  • Russia declares states of emergency in Crimea after Ukrainian strikes causing disruptions and anxiety.
  • Western allies increase military aid and sanctions against Russia while monitoring Kremlin tensions and limited prisoner exchanges.

Across these reports the war is intensifying: Ukraine has stepped up long-range strikes and large drone campaigns targeting Russian territory and military infrastructure while Kyiv pursues a 40-day pressure operation. Western governments and institutions are deepening military and financial backing for Ukraine—funding for drones and long-term military plans accompanies renewed sanctions and heightened NATO vigilance. At the same time, signs of Kremlin strain appear in reports of dissent, elite turnover and sharp rhetoric from Russian officials, even as limited prisoner exchanges and humanitarian gestures continue amid the fighting.
