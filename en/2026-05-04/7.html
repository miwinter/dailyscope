United Airlines hits pole and truck at Newark; investigations underway

US Aviation: Incident 2026-05-04 • 16 articles in total

In brief
  • A United Airlines Boeing 767 hit a lamppost and delivery truck near Newark, landed safely, but the truck driver was injured and hospitalized.
  • Investigations by the FAA, airline, and local authorities are ongoing, with emergency response causing brief disruptions.
  • Separate reports highlight other U.S. aviation incidents and broader airline operational challenges related to safety and reliability.

International reports describe a United Airlines Boeing 767 striking a lamppost/light pole and colliding with a delivery truck on the New Jersey Turnpike while on approach to Newark Liberty International Airport. The aircraft landed safely and passengers and crew avoided serious injury, although several outlets report the truck driver sustained injuries and was hospitalized. The FAA, the airline and local authorities have opened investigations, and video of the impact circulated while emergency crews and airport operations responded, causing brief disruptions. Other stories in the category highlight separate U.S. aviation incidents — a ground collision between United aircraft at San Francisco and an in-flight diversion after an alleged passenger attack — as well as wider airline disruptions, underscoring ongoing safety and operational concerns.
