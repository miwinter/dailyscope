Bilateral Deals: Energy, Defense and Strategic Partnerships

Region: Bilateral Deals 2026-05-04 • 106 articles in total

In brief
  • Governments worldwide are intensifying bilateral agreements focused on energy security, defense, investment, and infrastructure to strengthen supply chains and diplomatic influence.
  • Regional and global actors engage in pragmatic cooperation, including defense procurement, economic partnerships, and diplomatic normalization, despite ongoing tensions and geopolitical competition.
  • High-level diplomatic visits, trade liberalization, and multilateral support efforts complement these state-to-state deals, emphasizing innovation, security, and regional stability.

This set of reports shows an intensifying wave of bilateral agreements and high-level exchanges across regions, focused on energy security, defense procurement, infrastructure and investment. Governments are striking deals to shore up power supplies and critical minerals, deepen defense ties through arms sales and training, and pursue economic cooperation from aviation to tourism. Several countries are normalizing ties and re-establishing transport and diplomatic channels, while others register tensions reflected in expulsions, parliamentary breaks and strategic realignments. Overall, the coverage highlights pragmatic state-to-state deals and partnerships as tools to manage supply chains, bolster security and cement diplomatic influence.
