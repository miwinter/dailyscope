Tight Peru runoff: mixed early leads and technical ties

Peru Election Polls & Tally 2026-06-08 • 32 articles in total

In brief
  • Early tallies show Keiko Fujimori with a slight lead, while exit polls indicate a technical tie or slim advantage for Roberto Sánchez.
  • Electoral authorities reject widespread fraud, report isolated incidents, and expect final results after continued ballot counting.
  • The race is highly polarized with candidates reacting strongly, and the final outcome remains uncertain pending official tallies.

Early tallies and official counts in Peru's presidential runoff show conflicting signals: several outlets report Keiko Fujimori holding small leads in partial counts, while many exit polls and quick counts describe a technical tie or a slight edge for Roberto Sánchez. Electoral authorities and international observers have rejected broad fraud claims, reported only isolated incidents, and urged patience as scrutiny and counting continue. Candidates and their supporters have reacted strongly — Sánchez and allies celebrated projections, Fujimori acknowledged the tightness and pledged to respect results — underscoring the race's polarization. The outcome remains provisional and could shift as remaining ballots are tallied, with final official results and possible further scrutiny expected in the weeks ahead.
