Judge unseals alleged Epstein suicide note

Epstein Suicide Note Disclosure 2026-05-07 • 23 articles in total

In brief
  • A U.S. judge ordered the release of a handwritten note claimed to be Jeffrey Epstein’s suicide note after years of sealing.
  • The note’s authenticity is disputed and its release has revived scrutiny of Epstein’s death and possible legal impacts.
  • The disclosure intensifies public distrust and raises questions about institutional handling of Epstein’s case.

A U.S. judge recently ordered the release of a handwritten note said to be Jeffrey Epstein’s suicide note, a document that had been sealed for years and was reportedly found by his cellmate. The disclosure has reignited scrutiny of Epstein’s death in custody, with some reports quoting a line that suggests investigators ‘‘found nothing.’’ Many outlets emphasize the legal and procedural implications as the note is added to court dockets and could affect civil cases. At the same time, journalists and legal analysts underline persistent doubts about the note’s provenance and authenticity, and the release has renewed public and political debate over how the case was handled.
