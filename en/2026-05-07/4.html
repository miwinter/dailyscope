Mounting threats, broken truces and global concern over the Russia–Ukraine war

Russo-Ukrainian War 2026-05-07 • 76 articles in total

In brief
  • The conflict features reciprocal strikes, fragile ceasefires, and rising diplomatic tensions before Russia's Victory Day.
  • Moscow warns of large strikes and selective talks, while Kyiv accuses Russia of ceasefire violations and highlights civilian harm.
  • International actors respond with heightened alerts and calls for restraint amid broader political and security concerns.

Reporting across multiple countries shows a conflict marked by reciprocal strikes, fragile unilateral ceasefires and a barrage of diplomatic warnings ahead of Russia’s Victory Day. Moscow has repeatedly issued evacuation notices and threatened large-scale strikes on Kyiv, while Kyiv accuses Russia of violating ceasefires and documents civilian harm and attacks on first responders. International actors and neighbouring states are reacting with precautionary moves, calls for restraint and heightened alertness at embassies and borders. Separate investigative and analytical pieces highlight internal Russian measures, dissent and covert activities, underscoring the war’s broader political, humanitarian and security reverberations.
