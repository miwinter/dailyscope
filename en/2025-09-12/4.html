International Perspectives on Bolsonaro’s Historic Conviction for Coup Attempt in Brazil

2025-09-12 • 85 articles in total

The conviction and sentencing of former Brazilian President Jair Bolsonaro to 27 years and 3 months in prison for attempting a coup d'état marks a significant judicial and political moment in Brazil's recent history. Most international observers portray the ruling as an essential affirmation of democratic institutions and the rule of law, reinforcing accountability for anti-democratic acts following the 2022 elections. However, some sources, notably from the United States and aligned political voices, view the conviction as politically motivated or a 'witch hunt,' reflecting ongoing geopolitical tensions and differing interpretations of Brazil's internal affairs. The case has also intensified political polarization within Brazil, with Bolsonaro's supporters rejecting the verdict and continuing legal appeals both nationally and internationally planned by his defense team.
