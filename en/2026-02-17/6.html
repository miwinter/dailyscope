Tensions and Diverging Views at the Munich Security Conference

Munich Security Conference Tensions 2026-02-17 • 11 articles in total

In brief
  • The Munich Security Conference revealed geopolitical tensions and differing views on global security leadership, especially between the US and Europe.
  • European leaders stressed regional unity while facing internal disputes and concerns over the post-Cold War order involving Ukraine and Belarus.
  • Large protests against Iranian authorities in Munich highlighted civil society's role intersecting with international security issues.

The Munich Security Conference revealed significant geopolitical tensions and differing perspectives among international actors. US dominance in the Middle East and critiques of global governance, particularly by Senator Rubio, highlighted frustrations with current multilateral institutions. European leaders emphasized strengthening regional unity and security, while internal EU disputes and concerns over the post-Cold War order, especially regarding Ukraine and Belarus, underscored challenges to cooperation. Additionally, large protests against Iranian authorities in Munich reflected global civil society engagement. Overall, the conference showcased a complex interplay of security concerns, diplomatic disagreements, and calls for renewed leadership.
