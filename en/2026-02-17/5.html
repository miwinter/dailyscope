Expansion of Wagner's Sabotage Operations Across Europe

Russia Sabotage Operations 2026-02-17 • 12 articles in total

In brief
  • The Wagner Group conducts covert sabotage across Europe targeting NATO and EU countries as part of Russia's hybrid warfare strategy.
  • They recruit vulnerable Europeans and former mercenaries, using cryptocurrency payments and propaganda to destabilize European security.
  • Western nations warn of increased threats and urge vigilance against these coordinated efforts by Moscow.

The Wagner Group, a Russian private military company, is increasingly engaged in covert sabotage operations throughout Europe, targeting NATO and EU countries. Reports indicate a strategic shift from conventional conflict to hybrid warfare tactics, including recruitment of vulnerable Europeans and former mercenaries to conduct acts such as arson, espionage, and disruption. These activities are coordinated by the Kremlin to destabilize European security and cohesion, employing methods like propaganda and cryptocurrency payments for 'disposable' agents. Western intelligence and security experts across multiple countries warn of the growing threat and urge heightened vigilance. The expansion of Wagner's sabotage network underscores Moscow's broader hybrid warfare campaign beyond the Ukrainian battlefield.

These nations call for increased vigilance and preparedness against these hybrid threats orchestrated by Moscow.
