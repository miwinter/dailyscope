US Government Shutdown Paralyzes Aviation and Public Services Amid Political Deadlock

2025-11-08 • 37 articles in total

The ongoing US government shutdown, now the longest in history, has led to significant disruptions in air travel, including cancellations of over 1,000 flights and reductions in flight capacity by up to 20%. Major airports such as New York, Los Angeles, Chicago, and Atlanta are heavily affected, with shortages of air traffic controllers causing operational challenges nationwide. The shutdown has also exacerbated public hardships, including food insecurity, due to halted federal aid and services. Political negotiations remain deadlocked, with proposals from Democrats facing rejection by Republicans, prolonging the crisis and its economic consequences. Efforts to resolve the shutdown continue amid growing concerns over safety, economic damage, and public welfare.
