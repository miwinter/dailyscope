Typhoon Kalmaegi Causes Devastating Losses in Philippines and Hits Vietnam

2025-11-08 • 16 articles in total

Typhoon Kalmaegi wreaked havoc primarily in the Philippines, causing death toll estimates ranging from 5 to over 200, with Cebu being one of the hardest-hit provinces. The disaster led to widespread flooding, infrastructure damage, and forced many residents into emergency rescues, highlighting the region's vulnerability to extreme weather. The Philippines declared a state of emergency and continues extensive relief and recovery efforts. As the typhoon moved into Vietnam, it brought heavy rains and strong winds, causing additional casualties and prompting authorities there to brace for further impact. International reports vary in fatality figures, reflecting different updates and perspectives from the affected countries and external observers.

International estimates emphasizing high fatalities and severe Philippine impact

This group of articles, including sources from Mongolia, South Korea, Laos, Australia, Luxembourg, Indonesia, Malaysia, and Austria, emphasize the high death toll in the Philippines with figures ranging mostly over 100 and up to 204. They highlight the severe flooding, emergency declarations, and ongoing rescue operations in the Philippines, particularly in Cebu province, while also noting the typhoon's advance into Vietnam with preparatory actions underway. These reports give a broad picture of the humanitarian crisis and stress the prolonged recovery efforts needed in affected areas.
