Hurricane Melissa's Devastation and Multinational Response in the Caribbean

2025-11-08 • 17 articles in total

Hurricane Melissa caused widespread destruction across the Caribbean, severely impacting Jamaica, Cuba, and other islands. Jamaica received significant financial support, including a full $150 million payout from the World Bank catastrophe bond, alongside international aid from countries such as Japan, Canada, India, and the US. Cuba suffered extensive housing and agricultural damage, prompting a $74.2 million UN aid plan to facilitate recovery. Regional cooperation, including relief collections by Saint Lucia and collaborative scientific research linking climate change to Melissa's enhanced intensity, highlight a multifaceted response. The disaster underscores the Caribbean's vulnerability to increasingly intense hurricanes and the critical need for ongoing international assistance and resilience-building.
