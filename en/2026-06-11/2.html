Bilateral Deals: Security, Trade and Strategic Outreach Across Regions

Region: Bilateral Deals 2026-06-11 • 80 articles in total

In brief
  • Major powers deepen bilateral ties in defense, trade, energy, and infrastructure across Africa, Asia, and the Pacific to enhance strategic partnerships.
  • Smaller states pursue targeted bilateral agreements for development, governance, and regional integration amid geopolitical competition.
  • Bilateral tensions and cooperative mechanisms coexist, reflecting complex diplomatic, economic, and security interactions globally.

This set of articles shows a global wave of bilateral agreements and engagements spanning defense, trade, infrastructure, development and technical cooperation. Major powers are deepening strategic ties — Russia and China advancing economic, energy and security partnerships in Africa and the Pacific, while Japan and other Asian partners expand defence and maritime cooperation. Europe remains focused on defence procurement debates and transactional energy and industrial deals, even as the EU negotiates trade and development protocols with African partners. Smaller states pursue targeted bilateral accords on education, health, transport and law enforcement, reflecting a mix of commercial opportunity, regional integration and geopolitical competition.
