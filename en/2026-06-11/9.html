US immigration reform: restrictions and fees

US Immigration Reform 2026-06-11 • 5 articles in total

In brief
  • U.S. immigration enforcement has tightened with visa revocations and proposed limits on permanent residency affecting specific nationalities.
  • New visa-related fees raise costs for travelers and face legal challenges, including court rulings blocking some high fees.
  • Diplomatic issues arise from high-profile visa denials amid clashes over immigration policy and judicial pushback.

Recent U.S. immigration developments mix tighter enforcement with shifts in visa costs and legal challenges. The administration has moved to curb birth tourism and revoked hundreds of visas while Republican proposals would narrow pathways to permanent residency, potentially affecting thousands of Colombians. High-profile visa denials, such as the Somali referee, have provoked political and diplomatic fallout, and new expedited appointment fees are raising costs for travelers and business visitors. At the same time, courts are pushing back — a judge struck down a $100,000 H-1B fee as unlawful — underscoring clashes between executive actions, fee policy, and migrants' access.
