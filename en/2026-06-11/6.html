In multiple congressional appearances, Bill Gates testified that Jeffrey Epstein attempted to pressure or blackmail him by leveraging knowledge of his extramarital affairs, and that he cut ties after learning of Epstein's criminal conduct. Gates repeatedly denied any personal or improper relationship with Epstein and framed their meetings as mistakes linked to fundraising efforts that did not materialize. While many outlets emphasize his account of being coerced and his cooperation with investigators, other reports note Gates’ apology and insistence that he lacked knowledge of Epstein’s crimes. Lawmakers and observers remain divided, with some expressing skepticism and the hearings prompting renewed scrutiny of philanthropic governance and reputational risks for powerful donors.
