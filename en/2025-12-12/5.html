Russia Reaffirms Strong Support for Venezuela Amid US Pressure

Russia-Venezuela Alliance Call 2025-12-12 • 21 articles in total

In brief
  • Putin and Maduro have a strong strategic alliance supporting Venezuela's sovereignty against US sanctions and discussing military cooperation.
  • Russia presents itself as a key ally countering US influence in Latin America while the US downplays this alliance's impact.

Russian President Vladimir Putin has consistently reaffirmed Russia's support for Venezuelan President Nicolás Maduro amid escalating tensions and sanctions imposed by the United States. Multiple phone calls between the two leaders emphasize a strategic alliance focused on defending Venezuela's sovereignty and countering external pressures. This relationship includes discussions on expanding military and naval cooperation, signaling deepening bilateral ties. While Russia positions itself as a key ally to Venezuela, the United States downplays the significance of this alliance, maintaining its pressure campaign. The ongoing diplomatic exchanges highlight the broader geopolitical contest involving Russia, Venezuela, and the US in Latin America.

Russian and Venezuelan Perspective: Solidarity and Strategic Alliance

Articles from Russian and Venezuelan sources emphasize the close personal and political ties between Putin and Maduro, portraying their relationship as a brotherhood and a strategic partnership. They highlight Russia's unwavering support for Venezuela's sovereignty against US sanctions and interventions, including plans for military and naval cooperation. This viewpoint underscores Russia's role as a key ally in Latin America and its commitment to countering US influence in the region.

Western and US-aligned Perspective: Minimizing the Russia-Venezuela Alliance

Articles from Western-aligned sources, including the United States, tend to downplay the significance of the Russia-Venezuela alliance. They acknowledge the phone calls and reaffirmations of support but suggest that these do not substantially alter the geopolitical dynamics or pose a significant threat to US interests. This perspective reflects a cautious monitoring stance rather than alarm, emphasizing ongoing US efforts to maintain pressure on Maduro's government.
