Bulgaria's Government Resigns Amid Mass Protests and Political Instability

Bulgaria Cabinet Resigns 2025-12-12 • 16 articles in total

In brief
  • The Bulgarian government resigned due to mass protests demanding reforms and accountability over corruption and governance failures.
  • The resignation occurred just before a parliamentary vote and Bulgaria's euro adoption, causing concerns about political and economic stability.
  • The crisis highlights Bulgaria's domestic unrest and its significance for the EU's interest in member state stability.

The Bulgarian government, led by Prime Minister Rosen Zhelyazkov, resigned following widespread mass protests fueled by public dissatisfaction with corruption, economic policies, and governance. The resignation occurred shortly before a scheduled parliamentary confidence vote and just weeks before Bulgaria's planned adoption of the euro, raising concerns about political and economic stability. Protesters, including a significant youth movement, demanded reforms and accountability, marking one of the largest demonstrations since the 1990s. The political vacuum has sparked calls for early elections and attracted international attention, highlighting Bulgaria's ongoing challenges within the EU framework. The situation underscores deep-rooted frustrations with corruption and governance that have destabilized the country's political landscape.
