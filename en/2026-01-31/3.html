US Intensifies Economic Pressure on Cuba Through Sanctions and Tariffs

US–Cuba Sanctions and Tariffs 2026-01-31 • 32 articles in total

In brief
  • The US imposed strict economic sanctions and tariffs on Cuba to isolate its government and address security threats.
  • Cuba and its allies condemn these sanctions as aggressive actions that harm the Cuban people and economy.
  • Some countries warn the sanctions cause humanitarian crises and call for international opposition to US policies.

The United States under the Trump administration has escalated economic sanctions against Cuba, including declaring a national emergency and imposing tariffs on countries supplying oil to the island. These measures aim to isolate Cuba economically and pressure its government, which the US labels as a security threat. Cuba and its allies, including Venezuela, China, and regional groups like ALBA, strongly condemn these actions, describing them as aggressive, unjust, and harmful to the Cuban population. The sanctions have led to severe fuel shortages and humanitarian concerns, with countries like Mexico warning of a widespread crisis. The international response highlights a divide between US policy objectives and the opposition from Cuba's allies and some global actors.

US Hardline Policy and Security Justifications

Articles from US and allied perspectives emphasize the national security threat posed by Cuba, justifying the imposition of tariffs and sanctions as necessary measures. The US government frames these actions as responses to Cuba's destabilizing activities and hostile behavior, aiming to economically isolate the Cuban regime and cut off its energy supplies. This viewpoint supports a tough stance to pressure Cuba into changing its policies.

Cuba and Allies Condemn Sanctions as Aggressive and Harmful

Cuba and its allies, including Venezuela, China, ALBA, and the ANC, strongly denounce the US sanctions and tariffs as aggressive, unjust, and detrimental to the Cuban people. They characterize the measures as attempts to 'strangle' or 'asphyxiate' Cuba's economy, labeling them as criminal, fascist, or genocidal. These actors emphasize the humanitarian impact, fuel shortages, and economic hardships caused by the sanctions, calling for international solidarity and rejection of US policies.

Regional and International Concerns Over Humanitarian Impact

Some countries and organizations, such as Mexico and the ANC, express concern about the humanitarian consequences of the US sanctions on Cuba. They warn of fuel shortages and a potential widespread crisis affecting essential services and the wellbeing of the Cuban population. These perspectives highlight the broader regional implications and call for reconsideration of policies that exacerbate human suffering.
