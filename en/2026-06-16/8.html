Ukraine begins EU accession talks amid progress and caution

Ukraine-EU Accession Talks 2026-06-16 • 16 articles in total

In brief
  • The EU has officially started accession talks with Ukraine, focusing on justice and fundamental rights.
  • The talks mark a key step welcomed by Kyiv and EU capitals after Hungary lifted its veto.
  • The accession process will be long and gradual due to required reforms and ongoing war-related challenges.

The European Union has formally launched accession negotiations with Ukraine, opening the first negotiation cluster and initial chapters that focus on justice, fundamental rights and alignment with EU norms. Many EU capitals and Kyiv hailed the step as a major milestone after Hungary’s veto was lifted, and officials signalled the possibility of opening all negotiation areas in the coming months. Analysts and several member states warn the process will be long and demanding: Kyiv must carry out years of political, judicial and economic reforms while fighting ongoing Russian attacks. Domestic EU politics, differing member-state timelines and security considerations mean accession will be incremental rather than immediate.

Caution: long road, reforms and political obstacles

These pieces stress that accession talks do not guarantee quick membership, underscoring years of reforms, political hurdles and the disruptive effect of security pressures. They highlight internal EU divisions (notably Hungary’s earlier veto), the demands of rule-of-law and anti-corruption benchmarks, and the risks that Kyiv’s wartime reality poses for an accelerated timetable.
