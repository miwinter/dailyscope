B-52 bomber crashes in California; investigations under way

US Military Aircraft Crashes 2026-06-16 • 23 articles in total

In brief
  • A U.S. Air Force B-52 crashed after takeoff in California, causing heavy smoke and emergency response.
  • All eight crew members are reported killed or presumed dead, and military authorities launched a formal investigation.
  • The crash raises concerns about the bomber's maintenance, safety, and strategic impact while official details remain pending.

A U.S. Air Force B-52 Stratofortress crashed shortly after takeoff from a California base, producing heavy smoke and prompting an immediate emergency response. Numerous outlets report that all eight crew members were killed or are presumed dead, while military authorities have opened a formal investigation to determine the cause. Other reports remain cautious or offer few specifics pending official findings, and video of the crash site circulated widely. Coverage also highlights questions about safety, maintenance and the strategic implications of losing a long‑serving bomber.
