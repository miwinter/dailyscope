UK plans to ban social media for under-16s

UK Online Safety Regulation 2026-06-16 • 17 articles in total

In brief
  • The UK government plans to ban social media access for users under 16 to protect children's mental health and reduce online harms.
  • The policy includes age verification, platform curfews, and aligns with global trends towards stricter youth online regulations.
  • Civil-liberties groups warn the ban poses privacy risks and enforcement challenges, potentially causing unintended negative effects.

The UK government, led by Prime Minister Keir Starmer, has announced plans to ban social media access for users under 16 as part of a broader push to tighten online safety. The policy is presented as a child-protection measure aimed at reducing mental-health harms, bullying and addictive use, with proposals including age verification, platform safety controls and even curfews for under-18s. Coverage places the move in an international trend alongside Australia and potential actions in other countries, and some reports note a phased timeline for implementation. At the same time, civil-liberties groups, experts and campaigners warn about privacy risks, enforcement difficulties and unintended consequences, raising questions about legal, technical and social impacts.
