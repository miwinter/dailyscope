Leaders meet in Evian as diplomatic initiatives and regional tensions persist

World Leaders Dialogue 2026-06-16 • 80 articles in total

In brief
  • G7 summit in France emphasized unity amid divisions and focused on wars, economics, and technology challenges.
  • Ukraine sought face-to-face talks with Russia while regional diplomacy progressed on ceasefires and resource security.
  • Smaller states and international actors advanced bilateral ties, sector cooperation, multilateral reforms, and peacebuilding efforts.

World leaders convened at the G7 in France amid ceremonial pomp but limited consensus, with hosts urging unity while smaller partners and newcomers sought to shape outcomes. Ukraine pressed repeatedly for face-to-face talks with Russia on the sidelines, but Kyiv reported repeated refusals from Moscow even as Belarus signalled a softer tone. Regional diplomacy advanced on several fronts: Iran-related ceasefire and Hormuz developments drew praise from Gulf and regional capitals while Beijing and Moscow were credited with behind-the-scenes influence in shaping some deals. Beyond the summit, states large and small deepened bilateral ties, advanced sectoral cooperation on minerals, water and oceans, and debated wider governance issues from AI ethics to UN reform.
