Tariq Ramadan sentenced to 18 years in prison for rape in France

France: Ramadan Rape Verdict 2026-03-26 • 9 articles in total

  • Tariq Ramadan was sentenced in absentia to 18 years in prison by a French court for multiple rapes.
  • The case attracted international attention and raised issues on legal procedures, accountability, and justice for high-profile sexual assault cases.
  • The verdict sparked global discussions on cross-border legal challenges and societal debates about Islam, gender, and human rights in France.

Swiss Islamic scholar Tariq Ramadan was sentenced in absentia by a French court to 18 years in prison for multiple rapes committed between 2009 and 2016. The verdict, delivered in Paris, has attracted international attention and sparked debates on accountability, due process, and the handling of high-profile sexual assault cases in Europe. Ramadan's absence from the trial and sentencing has raised questions about cross-border legal procedures. The case also highlights broader societal discussions in France regarding Islam, gender, and justice. Various international media emphasize the legal and reputational implications of the ruling.
