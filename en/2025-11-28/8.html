US Tightens Green Card Reviews for Nationals from 19 Countries After Washington Attack

US Green Card Reexamination 2025-11-28 • 18 articles in total

In brief
  • The US government launched a review of green cards from 19 countries labeled as security risks after a White House shooting.
  • The review targets migrants from countries like Cuba, Venezuela, and Afghanistan to tighten immigration controls for national security.
  • This policy has drawn concern from Latin American countries and is seen as part of a broader US effort against immigration threats.

Following a shooting attack near the White House, the US government, under President Trump's directive, initiated a comprehensive review of green cards issued to nationals from 19 countries deemed security risks. This policy aims to enhance national security by scrutinizing immigrants from countries labeled as 'of concern,' including Afghanistan, Cuba, and Venezuela. The review reflects heightened immigration controls and has sparked international attention, with various countries noting the implications for their nationals. The move is part of a broader strategy to tighten immigration protocols amid rising domestic security concerns. While details on specific criteria remain limited, the policy underscores a cautious and security-focused approach to immigration.
