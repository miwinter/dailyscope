Military Coup in Guinea-Bissau Sparks Regional Condemnation and Political Uncertainty

Guinea-Bissau Military Coup 2025-11-28 • 70 articles in total

In brief
  • Guinea-Bissau's military seized power on November 26, 2025, arresting President Embalo and suspending elections, appointing General N’Tam as transitional leader.
  • Regional bodies condemn the coup, demand Embalo's release, restoration of democracy, and have suspended Guinea-Bissau from decision-making.
  • Embalo fled to Senegal with regional help, while international observers monitor the tense situation amid concerns over stability and security.

On November 26, 2025, Guinea-Bissau experienced a military coup that deposed President Umaro Sissoco Embalo shortly after disputed presidential and legislative elections. The military arrested Embalo, suspended the electoral process, and appointed General Horta N’Tam as transitional president for a one-year term. The coup has triggered widespread condemnation from regional bodies such as ECOWAS, the African Union, and the West African Elders Forum, all demanding the restoration of constitutional order and the release of the president. Embalo fled to Senegal with the assistance of regional actors, while the military tightened control by closing borders and banning demonstrations. The situation remains tense and uncertain, with international observers closely monitoring developments amid concerns over Guinea-Bissau's fragile democratic stability and regional security.

West African Regional Organizations Condemn Coup and Demand Restoration of Order

Regional bodies including ECOWAS, the African Union, and the West African Elders Forum uniformly condemn the military takeover as a violation of democratic norms. They call for the immediate release of President Embalo, restoration of constitutional governance, and urge political dialogue to resolve the crisis. These organizations emphasize the importance of stability and democratic principles in West Africa and have taken measures such as suspending Guinea-Bissau from decision-making bodies to pressure the military junta.

The international community calls for a quick return to democratic order and monitors the evolving situation closely.
