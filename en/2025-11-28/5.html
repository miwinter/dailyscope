Shooting of US National Guard Soldiers Near White House Sparks Security and Immigration Debate

US National Guard Shootings 2025-11-28 • 36 articles in total

In brief
  • Two US National Guard soldiers were critically injured near the White House; one, Sarah Beckstrom, died later.
  • The assailant, an Afghan national with CIA ties, was arrested, prompting debates on immigration and national security.
  • Authorities increased security and launched investigations amid political and social discussions on terrorism and refugee policies.

A shooting near the White House critically injured two US National Guard soldiers, one of whom, Sarah Beckstrom, later died. The assailant, an Afghan national with prior cooperation with the CIA, was apprehended, raising concerns about vetting and insider threats. The incident has intensified debates on immigration policies, with President Donald Trump blaming current immigration practices and calling the attack a terror act. Authorities, including the FBI, have launched investigations into the motive and security lapses, while security measures around key government sites have been heightened. The event has sparked widespread political and social discussions on national security and refugee policies in the United States.
