Labor Secretary exits amid cabinet turmoil

US Trump Cabinet Shakeup 2026-04-21 • 14 articles in total

In brief
  • Labor Secretary Lori Chavez-DeRemer resigned amid internal investigations and office scandals.
  • Her departure marks a broader leadership crisis with repeated cabinet exits destabilizing policy and governance.
  • The reshuffle suggests potential shifts in U.S. domestic labor policy and international relations before a possible second Trump term.

Lori Chavez-DeRemer, the U.S. Labor Secretary, has left the Trump administration, becoming at least the third high-level departure in recent weeks. Many outlets present the exit as part of broad, ongoing turnover that raises questions about leadership, succession and policy continuity within the White House. Several reports point to internal investigations, released messages or office scandals as proximate causes, intensifying scrutiny of the administration’s oversight. Observers note the reshuffle could affect domestic labor policy and signal broader shifts in priorities and international posture ahead of a possible second Trump term.

Countries covering this topic

Scandals and investigations focus

Outlets from Austria, Malaysia, Singapore and Portugal highlight internal investigations, released messages and an office scandal as the immediate reasons for Chavez-DeRemer’s departure. These reports frame the exit primarily as a consequence of alleged misconduct and legal or ethical scrutiny rather than as purely political turnover.
