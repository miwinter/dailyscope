African public diplomacy: trade, security and expanding partnerships

Africa Public Diplomacy 2026-04-21 • 55 articles in total

In brief
  • African states pursued economic outreach, security diplomacy, and image-building through forums, bilateral talks, and multilateral engagements.
  • Regional security and stability dominated diplomacy, focusing on mediation, troop deployments, and AU-led initiatives.
  • Domestic political tensions and governance issues shaped international perceptions and regional political discourse.

Across April 2026 coverage, African states and leaders pursued a mix of economic outreach, security diplomacy and image-building. Libya and several Sahel states hosted business forums, currency and trade discussions to attract investment and normalize ties with regional and European partners. Regional security and stability dominated engagements from Egypt and Saudi mediation on Sudan to Chad’s international deployments and AU diplomacy in the Horn. Simultaneously, states engaged bilateral partners — notably France, the United States, Austria and Italy — while domestic political tensions and governance critiques continued to shape international perceptions.
