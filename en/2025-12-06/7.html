Trump's National Security Strategy: Regional Focus and Transatlantic Tensions

US National Security Strategy Release 2025-12-06 • 16 articles in total

In brief
  • The US National Security Strategy shifts focus to regional dominance, especially in the Americas, opposing Chinese influence and seeking managed relations with Russia.
  • The strategy criticizes Europe as declining and supports nationalist movements while provoking European concern about security and transatlantic ties.
  • It frames cultural preservation and migration as security issues tied to nationalism and geopolitical strategy in Europe.

The newly released US National Security Strategy under President Donald Trump marks a significant shift from global engagement to a more regional focus, particularly emphasizing Latin America and the Western Hemisphere. It strongly criticizes Europe, describing it as facing 'civilizational erosion' or 'extinction,' and pledges support for nationalist movements while expressing skepticism about Europe's economic and military capabilities. The strategy aims to deter Chinese influence, especially regarding Taiwan and Latin America, and seeks stability with Russia through managed competition. These positions have sparked sharp criticism and concern among European nations about the future of transatlantic relations and regional security. Overall, the strategy reflects a nationalist, interventionist isolationism approach prioritizing American supremacy and regional dominance over traditional global alliances.
