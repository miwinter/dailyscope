US-Facilitated Peace Agreement Between DRC and Rwanda Amid Ongoing Conflict

US Brokers DRC–Rwanda Peace 2025-12-06 • 31 articles in total

In brief
  • The peace agreement between the DRC and Rwanda, brokered by US President Trump, aims to end conflict and promote regional stability.
  • African and international observers acknowledge the accord's significance but remain skeptical due to ongoing violence and implementation challenges.
  • The deal involves multiple regional and international actors and represents a crucial step towards dialogue despite renewed fighting.

A peace agreement between the Democratic Republic of Congo and Rwanda was signed in Washington, brokered and supported by U.S. President Donald Trump, aiming to end longstanding hostilities and foster regional stability. While the accord is hailed as a historic diplomatic achievement and a potential turning point for peace and economic cooperation, skepticism remains due to persistent violence and humanitarian crises in eastern Congo. African leaders and international partners have welcomed the deal, but civil society and observers question its effectiveness given ongoing clashes and implementation challenges. The involvement of regional actors like Togo and Qatar highlights a broader international effort to stabilize the Great Lakes region. Despite renewed fighting shortly after the signing, the agreement represents a significant step toward dialogue and cooperation between the two nations.

Articles from the United States emphasize the peace agreement as a major diplomatic achievement facilitated by President Donald Trump, highlighting the historic nature of the deal and its potential to stabilize the region. They acknowledge ongoing violence but frame the accord as a foundation for future peace and economic cooperation, underscoring US engagement in African geopolitics and resource access.
