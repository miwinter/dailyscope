The ongoing conflict in the Middle East has escalated into a complex multinational war involving the United States, Israel, Iran, and several regional actors. Israel and the US have intensified airstrikes and military operations targeting Iranian assets in Iran, Lebanon, and Syria, while Iran has retaliated with missile attacks on Israeli and US bases, expanding the conflict's geographic scope. The involvement of Kurdish forces, NATO interceptions, and military deployments by European countries highlight the widening international dimension of the war. Diplomatic tensions, including US criticism of allies and debates over the legality of the conflict, further complicate the situation. The conflict has resulted in significant casualties, infrastructure damage, and growing fears of a prolonged regional war with global implications.
