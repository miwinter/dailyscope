Hurricane Melissa's Devastating Path Across Caribbean Nations

2025-10-30 • 48 articles in total

Hurricane Melissa, the most powerful storm of the year, caused unprecedented destruction and loss of life across several Caribbean countries, primarily Jamaica, Cuba, and Haiti. Jamaica faced historic devastation with widespread damage to infrastructure, including hospitals, and power outages affecting up to half a million people, leading to a disaster zone declaration. The storm then moved to Cuba, where extensive evacuations were conducted and significant damage was reported, and to Haiti, where severe flooding claimed at least 25 lives and worsened humanitarian challenges. International and regional authorities are actively engaged in emergency relief, recovery, and infrastructure repair efforts. The hurricane underscores the Caribbean's vulnerability to increasingly intense tropical storms and the critical need for coordinated disaster preparedness and response.
