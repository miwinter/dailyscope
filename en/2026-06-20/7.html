Deadly fire forces mass evacuation at Dominican Republic beach hotel

Dominican Republic Hotel Fire 2026-06-20 • 9 articles in total

In brief
  • A large fire at a Dominican Republic luxury hotel caused one death and multiple injuries.
  • About 1,700 guests and staff were evacuated during a major emergency response.
  • Authorities are investigating the fire, raising safety concerns for regional tourist resorts.

A large fire erupted at a beachside luxury hotel in the Dominican Republic, prompting a major emergency response and the evacuation of roughly 1,690–1,700 guests and staff. Reports confirm one death — identified in several sources as a 46-year-old Italian woman — and multiple people were treated or hospitalized for smoke inhalation and other injuries. Dramatic footage captured flames and thick smoke as firefighters worked to contain the blaze, and local authorities have opened an investigation into the cause. The event has renewed scrutiny of safety standards and emergency preparedness at tourist resorts in the region.
