Bolivia reaches labor accord amid lingering roadblocks

Bolivia Labor Protests 2026-06-20 • 7 articles in total

In brief
  • Bolivia's government and major unions agreed to suspend protests and start technical talks to restore transport and address demands.
  • Despite the accord, roadblocks and local protests continue, delaying full normalization of commerce and daily life.
  • The agreement is fragile as key unions linked to ex-president Evo Morales have not joined, requiring more negotiations for lasting peace.

Bolivia's government and the Central Obrera reached an agreement after roughly six to seven weeks (around 50 days) of nationwide protests and road blockades, with unions announcing suspension of large-scale mobilizations and the government pledging to implement agreed points. The deal is presented as a step toward pacification, with technical working groups set to discuss protesters' demands and restore transport to La Paz, El Alto and other affected areas. Nevertheless, several reports note that roadblocks and local protests—especially by campesinos—continue to disrupt commerce and daily life, delaying full normalization. Observers caution the arrangement is fragile because negotiations with unions aligned to former president Evo Morales remain pending, making stabilization contingent on further talks.

Agreement incomplete — roadblocks continue

These reports underline that, despite a formal accord, road blockades and local disruptions persist across the country, signaling ongoing tensions between authorities and protest groups. They stress that practical obstacles to movement and commerce remain, so the situation is not yet fully resolved.
