Escalation: drone strikes on Moscow, pressure on Belarus and widening international responses

Russo-Ukrainian War 2026-06-20 • 58 articles in total

In brief
  • Ukraine intensifies drone and long-range strikes targeting Russian energy and military sites, including attacks on Moscow and Crimea.
  • Zelensky pressures Belarus to remove drone guidance equipment, threatening action and heightening regional tensions.
  • Russia faces domestic instability with contested information amid ongoing Western support for Kyiv and debates over the conflict's humanitarian impact.

Reporting across sources shows a marked escalation in long-range and drone strikes that Kyiv attributes to its campaign against Russian energy and military infrastructure, including repeated attacks on Moscow and facilities in occupied Crimea. President Zelensky has publicly pressured Belarus to remove drone‑guidance equipment, even issuing a one‑week ultimatum and threatening action, underscoring rising regional tensions. Russian domestic stability and information control are strained as state media, bloggers and official channels dispute facts, report chaos in Moscow and circulate counterclaims (including allegations against Ukraine). At the same time, Western backing for Kyiv continues through sanctions, funding and materiel commitments, while debates over propaganda, alleged weapons violations and the humanitarian cost of the conflict persist in international coverage.
