Centralization, Repression and Corruption in Russia

Russia Internal Politics 2026-04-11 • 13 articles in total

In brief
  • Russian authorities are centralizing power and repressing independent media, NGOs, and Western academic links to control information.
  • High-profile prosecutions in the defense sector reveal endemic corruption and signal elite conflict management.
  • Governance and security developments highlight a crisis of legitimacy and institutional decay in Russia's political system.

Recent reporting paints a Russia where power is increasingly centralized and the information space is tightly policed, with independent media, civil-society groups and Western academic links coming under legal and operational pressure. Parallel high-profile prosecutions in the defence sector — including multi-year jail terms for senior officials — highlight endemic corruption and efforts by authorities to signal accountability or manage elite conflict. Weekly digests and Moscow-focused roundups underscore a steady flow of governance and security developments, while analytical pieces argue these events reflect a broader crisis of legitimacy for the Kremlin’s governance model. Together the stories depict a state consolidating control internally while grappling with institutional decay and contested historical narratives.
