US-hosted Lebanon–Israel talks amid violence and tight Israeli limits

Lebanon-Israel Talks 2026-04-11 • 14 articles in total

In brief
  • The United States will host US-mediated Lebanon–Israel talks next Tuesday amid ongoing diplomatic preparations.
  • Israel agrees to direct negotiations but excludes some mediators and won't discuss a Hezbollah ceasefire, continuing military actions.
  • The talks face deep obstacles and violence threatens to undermine fragile diplomatic progress without new agreements reported.

The United States will host negotiations between Lebanon and Israel next week in Washington, with schedules confirmed by multiple governments and preparatory diplomatic contacts underway. Observers call the talks potentially historic but emphasize deep obstacles, including disagreement over concessions and a fragile truce. Several reports note Israel is willing to enter direct negotiations while simultaneously excluding some mediators and refusing to discuss a Hezbollah ceasefire, and Israeli forces continue operations against Hezbollah targets. Overall, diplomacy is advancing procedurally but under significant strain from ongoing violence and contested negotiation scope.
