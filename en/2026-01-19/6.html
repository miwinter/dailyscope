Chile Wildfires: Deadly Blazes Prompt State of Catastrophe and Mass Evacuations

Chile Wildfire Emergency 2026-01-19 • 29 articles in total

In brief
  • Chile faces a severe wildfire crisis with at least 19 deaths and tens of thousands evacuated in southern regions.
  • The government declared a state of catastrophe, mobilizing military and emergency resources while imposing curfews.
  • International aid and solidarity support firefighting efforts amid ongoing threats to communities and the environment.

Chile is facing a severe wildfire emergency with at least 19 fatalities and tens of thousands of people evacuated across multiple southern regions. The government has declared a state of catastrophe or emergency to mobilize resources, including military support, to combat the rapidly spreading fires exacerbated by extreme heat and strong winds. Curfews have been imposed in the hardest-hit areas to maintain order and facilitate firefighting efforts. International solidarity is evident, with countries like the US offering aid and neighbors expressing condolences. Despite massive firefighting efforts, the situation remains critical with ongoing threats to communities, infrastructure, and the environment.
