US-led Gaza Peace Council Sparks Mixed International Reactions

US–Gaza Ceasefire and Peace Efforts 2026-01-19 • 23 articles in total

In brief
  • The US initiated a multilateral Gaza Peace Council with invited countries to manage ceasefire and reconstruction efforts.
  • Israel opposes the council due to exclusion of its officials and concerns over sovereignty and legitimacy.
  • Human rights experts warn the council risks resembling external control and undermining Palestinian self-governance.

The United States, under President Donald Trump, has initiated a Gaza Peace Council and related governance boards aimed at overseeing ceasefire and reconstruction efforts in Gaza. Several countries, including Jordan, Kazakhstan, India, Pakistan, and Canada, have been invited or have shown interest in joining these bodies, reflecting a multilateral approach to stabilizing the region. However, Israel has strongly objected to the formation and composition of these councils, criticizing the exclusion of Israeli government officials and expressing concerns over sovereignty and legitimacy. Human rights experts and regional actors have also voiced apprehensions about the boards resembling external control or colonial oversight. The initiative highlights the complexities and tensions inherent in international peace efforts in Gaza, balancing diplomatic engagement with contested political realities.

US and Invited International Partners' Perspective

This group views the establishment of the Gaza Peace Council and related boards as a proactive multilateral effort led by the US to manage ceasefire, humanitarian aid, and governance in Gaza. Countries like Jordan, Kazakhstan, India, Pakistan, and Canada have been invited or are considering participation, emphasizing cooperation and shared responsibility in stabilizing the region. The US frames this initiative as a necessary step to bring administrative order and peace to a fragile situation.

Israeli Government and Opposition's Objections

Israel, including Prime Minister Netanyahu and opposition parties, strongly opposes the US-led Gaza Peace Council due to the exclusion of Israeli government officials and the inclusion of foreign actors like Turkey and Qatar. They argue this undermines Israeli sovereignty and complicates peace negotiations with Hamas. The objections reflect deep political divisions within Israel and concerns over the legitimacy and effectiveness of the US initiative without Israeli coordination.

Critical Views from Experts and Regional Observers

Human rights advocates and some regional commentators criticize the US-led boards as resembling colonial governance models that impose external control over Palestinian territories. They warn that such oversight may undermine Palestinian self-governance and complicate the peace process. This perspective highlights the tensions between international intervention and local autonomy in conflict resolution efforts.
