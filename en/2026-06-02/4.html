War grinds on: strikes, Ukrainian counterpressure, and wider regional strain

Russo-Ukrainian War 2026-06-02 • 80 articles in total

In brief
  • Russian strikes cause heavy civilian casualties and damage Ukrainian cities while Ukraine improves attacks on Russian logistics and oil facilities.
  • NATO and neighbors increase security measures against regional spillover and call for international action against Russia.
  • Russia faces economic strains, escalates conflict rhetoric, and faces allegations of war crimes like child deportations.

Russian long-range strikes and drone attacks continue to inflict heavy civilian casualties across Ukrainian cities, collapsing apartment blocks and prompting widespread rescue operations. Ukraine's leadership stresses growing strike capabilities against Russian logistics and oil infrastructure, and some officials express cautious optimism about ending the war before winter. NATO and regional states report spillover incidents (including a drone crash in Romania) and step up maritime and diplomatic actions to curb Russian activity, while Western nations also track rising Russian information operations and threats. At the same time Moscow faces mounting fiscal and logistical strain — fuel shortages, rising drone use and internal budget warnings — and rights groups allege Russian abuses such as child deportations, complicating any path to settlement.
