Magyar sworn in, marking end of the Orbán era

Hungary Leadership Transition 2026-05-10 • 34 articles in total

In brief
  • Péter Magyar became Hungary’s new prime minister, ending Viktor Orbán’s 16-year rule and promising anti-corruption reforms.
  • European institutions welcomed the transition, expecting closer EU ties and policy changes under Magyar.
  • The new government faces internal tensions, including institutional disputes and questions about domestic stability.

Péter Magyar has been sworn in as Hungary’s new prime minister, bringing a formal end to Viktor Orbán’s 16‑year rule after his party secured a parliamentary majority. Coverage emphasizes a promised program of sweeping change: anti‑corruption measures, investigations of oligarchs, and institutional reforms aimed at dismantling the previous system. European institutions and leaders welcomed the investiture, framing the transition as a possible reset for Hungary’s ties with the EU. Many outlets provided straightforward procedural accounts of the parliamentary vote and inauguration, while others flagged potential flashpoints, including confrontations with the presidency and questions about domestic stability. Observers across regions expect Magyar’s government to rapidly shape both Hungary’s internal governance and its foreign‑policy alignment with Europe and NATO.
