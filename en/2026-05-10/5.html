Hantavirus on MV Hondius: deaths, WHO-led evacuation and multinational response

WHO Hantavirus Cruise Outbreak 2026-05-10 • 47 articles in total

In brief
  • A hantavirus outbreak on the MV Hondius cruise ship caused multiple infections and at least three deaths, triggering international health responses led by WHO.
  • Spanish and Canary Islands authorities manage a tense evacuation and strict docking under public safety concerns and political debate.
  • Countries arrange repatriation, quarantine, and contact tracing while highlighting cruise ships' broader vulnerability to infectious diseases.

An outbreak of hantavirus linked to the cruise ship MV Hondius has resulted in multiple confirmed infections and at least three deaths, prompting intensive international public-health action. The World Health Organization is leading coordination and has reassured residents that overall public risk is low while organizing an unprecedented evacuation and medical screening operation. Spain and Canary Islands authorities face a tense balance between public-safety precautions, local reluctance, and national political decisions to receive and process the ship under strict controls. Several countries are arranging repatriation flights, quarantine or hospital isolation for their nationals, while epidemiological teams trace contacts, investigate patient-zero leads and test suspected cases; a few isolated passengers have tested negative. The incident has also renewed attention to infectious-disease risks on cruise ships more broadly, with separate norovirus outbreaks underscoring ongoing vulnerability in maritime travel.
