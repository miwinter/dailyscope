Denver runway fatality prompts security and safety probe

Denver Aviation Incident 2026-05-10 • 13 articles in total

In brief
  • A pedestrian who breached a perimeter fence was struck and killed by a Frontier Airlines plane during takeoff at Denver International Airport.
  • The collision caused an aircraft evacuation, reports of smoke, several minor passenger injuries, and triggered investigations into airport security.
  • A separate incident involved a wingsuit collision in Austria, resulting in two serious injuries and its own investigation.

A Frontier Airlines aircraft struck and killed a person who accessed the active runway at Denver International Airport during takeoff, several reports say the individual had breached a perimeter fence. The collision killed the pedestrian and led to an aircraft evacuation, reports of smoke, and a number of minor passenger injuries. Airport authorities, federal investigators and national officials have opened inquiries to determine how the person reached the tarmac and whether security or procedural failures contributed. Audio and video clips of the event circulated publicly, intensifying scrutiny of airport perimeter controls and emergency response. One article in the set is unrelated to Denver and instead reports a separate wingsuit collision in Austria that injured two people.
