US Defense Strategy Shift: Allies Urged to Assume Greater Security Roles Amid China Focus

US Defense Strategy Shift 2026-01-24 • 13 articles in total

In brief
  • The US defense strategy prioritizes homeland security and counters China by urging allies to take more defense responsibility, especially in the Indo-Pacific.
  • NATO and European allies face reduced US military support and must enhance their own defense amid strategic recalibration.
  • Regional allies like South Korea and Australia must increase security capabilities due to a more limited US military role in their regions.

The United States has unveiled a new defense strategy emphasizing homeland security and a strategic pivot towards countering China’s influence, particularly in the Indo-Pacific region. This approach marks a significant shift by urging allies, including European and Asian partners, to take primary responsibility for their own defense, reducing the US military support traditionally provided. While NATO and European alliances remain important, the US signals a recalibration of its commitments, focusing more on unilateral actions and regional self-reliance. Countries like South Korea and Australia are explicitly expected to enhance their security capabilities amid a more limited US role. This strategy reflects broader geopolitical realignments and a prioritization of US national interests over extensive global military engagement.
