Divergent International Views on US-led Gaza Reconstruction and Peace Initiatives

US Gaza Reconstruction and Peace Efforts 2026-01-24 • 31 articles in total

In brief
  • The US plans a 'New Gaza' reconstruction with economic development and created a controversial Peace Council with mixed international support.
  • Allied countries support the US initiatives aiming for Gaza's modernization and regional stability, while others criticize the council for undermining the UN and excluding key stakeholders.
  • Palestinians distrust US plans as control efforts, the UN calls for urgent humanitarian aid, and critics warn the Peace Council may fragment global peace efforts.

The United States has introduced ambitious plans for Gaza's reconstruction, including a 'New Gaza' vision featuring skyscrapers, airports, and economic development inspired by models like Dubai. Concurrently, President Donald Trump launched a 'Peace Council' or 'Board of Peace' aimed at overseeing peace and reconstruction efforts, which has attracted mixed international reactions. While some countries like Morocco and several Middle Eastern states have joined the council, others, including Canada and the European Union, express skepticism or outright rejection, fearing it undermines established institutions like the UN. Palestinians remain wary of US plans, perceiving them as attempts to exert control rather than genuine aid. The UN emphasizes urgent humanitarian needs in Gaza, highlighting the ongoing crisis amid these geopolitical maneuvers.

US and Supportive Allies Promote Ambitious Gaza Reconstruction and Peace Council

This group, including the United States and allied countries such as Morocco, Saudi Arabia, Turkey, and Egypt, supports the US-led initiatives for Gaza's redevelopment and the establishment of the Peace Council. They view these efforts as innovative steps toward peacebuilding, economic revitalization, and regional stability, endorsing the vision of transforming Gaza into a modernized area with improved infrastructure and governance. Their participation signals alignment with US diplomatic strategies and a willingness to engage in new multilateral frameworks.

Skeptical and Critical International Actors Question Legitimacy and Impact

Countries and entities such as Canada, the European Union, Germany, and Romania express reservations or outright criticism of the US initiatives. Concerns focus on the Peace Council's legitimacy, potential to undermine the United Nations, exclusion of key stakeholders, and the feasibility and sensitivity of the Gaza reconstruction plans. Canada's withdrawal and EU's doubts reflect apprehension about unilateral moves that may disrupt established international peace mechanisms. German and Romanian voices call for revisions and stronger European involvement to ensure balanced and effective conflict resolution.

Palestinian and UN Perspectives Highlight Distrust and Humanitarian Urgency

Palestinian groups remain skeptical of US proposals, fearing increased control rather than genuine assistance, complicating peace prospects. The United Nations underscores the critical humanitarian situation in Gaza, calling for urgent aid amid ongoing conflict and the formation of new peace initiatives. This viewpoint stresses the need for immediate relief and inclusive, legitimate peace processes that address the population's needs and respect international law, contrasting with the more politically driven US-led plans.

Controversy Over US Peace Council's Formation and Membership Dynamics

The formation of the Peace Council by Donald Trump at Davos has sparked controversy, with debates over its membership, exclusion of key leaders like Israel's Netanyahu, and its challenge to the UN's authority. Some countries rapidly joined, while others were excluded or withdrew, reflecting geopolitical tensions and strategic calculations. Critics warn that this parallel institution risks fragmenting international peace efforts and reviving historical patterns of unilateralism detrimental to global stability.
