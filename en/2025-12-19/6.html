EU-Mercosur Trade Agreement Postponed Amid Internal EU Disagreements

EU-Mercosur Agreement Delay 2025-12-19 • 18 articles in total

In brief
  • The EU-Mercosur trade agreement's signing is postponed to January due to unresolved EU concerns about agriculture and environment.
  • Key EU countries like Italy, France, and Poland oppose the deal and demand more negotiations to protect national interests.
  • Brazil and other Mercosur nations requested the delay to evaluate terms and secure better conditions.

The signing of the EU-Mercosur trade agreement, involving South American countries Argentina, Brazil, Paraguay, and Uruguay, has been postponed until January due to unresolved concerns primarily within the European Union. Key EU member states such as Italy, France, and Poland have expressed opposition, particularly regarding agricultural protections and environmental standards. Brazilian President Lula requested the delay to allow more time for evaluation and negotiation. The European Commission, led by Ursula von der Leyen, acknowledged the postponement as necessary to secure broader support among member states. This delay highlights the complex political and economic challenges in finalizing a trade deal that has been in negotiation for over two decades.

Countries covering this topic

European Union Member States Opposing Immediate Agreement

Several EU countries, notably Italy, France, and Poland, have voiced strong reservations about the Mercosur agreement, focusing on protecting their agricultural sectors and addressing environmental concerns. Political opposition within these countries, including interventions by leaders such as Italian Prime Minister Giorgia Meloni and French President Emmanuel Macron, has led to the postponement. These member states seek further negotiations to ensure the agreement aligns with their national interests and regulatory standards.

Mercosur Countries Requesting Delay for Further Consideration

Mercosur countries, particularly Brazil under President Lula, have requested postponement to allow more time to assess the agreement's implications and negotiate terms. This reflects their cautious approach to ensure the deal benefits their economies and addresses pending issues. The delay is seen as a strategic move to secure better conditions and address concerns raised by both sides before finalizing the agreement.
