Deadly New Year Fire at Swiss Ski Resort Claims Around 40 Lives

Swiss Ski Resort Deaths 2026-01-02 • 32 articles in total

In brief
  • A fire at a ski resort bar in Crans-Montana killed about 40 people and injured over 100 during New Year's Eve.
  • Authorities are investigating causes including fireworks and an explosion while emergency services and foreign embassies respond.
  • Identification of victims will take weeks due to injury severity, and the disaster has caused international shock and support efforts.

A catastrophic fire broke out during New Year's Eve celebrations at a ski resort bar in Crans-Montana, Switzerland, resulting in approximately 40 deaths and over 100 injuries. Emergency services responded swiftly amid chaotic scenes, with many victims suffering severe burns and some requiring airlift evacuation. Authorities are investigating multiple possible causes, including fireworks ignition and an explosion, but no definitive cause has been confirmed. The tragedy has deeply affected local communities and drawn international condolences, with foreign embassies providing support to their nationals. Rescue and recovery efforts continue as identification of victims is expected to take weeks due to the severity of injuries.
