Tensions Rise as China Conducts Military Drills Near Taiwan

China Drills Near Taiwan 2026-01-02 • 25 articles in total

In brief
  • China has conducted large-scale military drills near Taiwan to assert sovereignty and deter foreign interference.
  • Taiwan vows to defend its sovereignty while promoting peace and seeking international support.
  • The US condemns China's actions and urges dialogue to maintain peace in the Taiwan Strait.

China has conducted large-scale military exercises near Taiwan, including live-fire drills and naval blockades, signaling a strong assertion of its sovereignty claims. Taiwan's leadership has consistently vowed to defend its sovereignty and maintain peace, emphasizing restraint despite the heightened military pressure. The United States and other international actors have criticized China's actions, urging de-escalation and constructive dialogue to avoid conflict. China, however, rejects these criticisms and views the drills as necessary responses to perceived provocations and foreign interference. The situation remains tense with ongoing vigilance from Taiwan and global concern over regional stability in the Taiwan Strait.

Taiwan's Defensive Stance and Calls for Peace

Taiwanese authorities emphasize their commitment to defending sovereignty while avoiding escalation. Taiwan's president and military officials highlight vigilance and readiness in response to China's drills but stress the importance of peace and stability in the region. Taiwan calls for international support amid rising tensions.

United States Urges De-escalation and Criticizes China

The US government condemns China's military exercises as provocative and unnecessary, urging Beijing to halt pressure on Taiwan and engage in constructive dialogue. US officials emphasize the importance of peace and stability in the Taiwan Strait, warning against actions that could escalate conflict. Some US political figures downplay the severity of the situation, reflecting varied domestic perspectives.
