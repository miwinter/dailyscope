Tragic New Year's Eve Fire in Crans-Montana Kills Around 40

Crans-Montana Bar Fire 2026-01-02 • 32 articles in total

In brief
  • A fire at Le Constellation bar in Crans-Montana killed about 40 people and injured over 100 during New Year's Eve celebrations.
  • Investigations focus on the fire's rapid spread possibly triggered by a flare and flammable materials, while authorities manage victim identification and safety reviews.
  • The tragedy affected international victims, prompting global condolences and raising concerns about venue safety and emergency response challenges.

A devastating fire broke out during New Year's Eve celebrations at the Le Constellation bar in the Swiss ski resort of Crans-Montana, resulting in approximately 40 deaths and over 100 injuries, many severe. Emergency services responded promptly, but the rapid spread of the fire caused chaos and panic among patrons, with eyewitnesses describing desperate escape attempts. Investigations into the cause are ongoing, with some reports suggesting a flare ignited the blaze and plastic materials contributed to its rapid spread. The tragedy has deeply affected the local and international community, as victims include people from multiple countries, prompting condolences and aid from abroad. Authorities continue to manage victim identification, support efforts, and review safety measures for large gatherings.
