U.S. sanctions: enforcement, exemptions and evasion

US Sanctions Diplomacy 2026-04-23 • 8 articles in total

In brief
  • The U.S. uses sanctions and embargoes with tactical exceptions to manage geopolitical and market impacts.
  • Critics argue U.S. waiver decisions weaken pressure on adversaries and cause political backlash.
  • Sanctioned parties evade measures via ship-routing and cryptocurrency, challenging enforcement effectiveness.

U.S. sanctions diplomacy remains active and adaptive, combining continued embargoes and targeted measures with tactical exemptions to manage market and political effects. Washington has extended an arms embargo on Iran, authorized limited exceptions for Russian crude, and pursued actions against perceived security and data threats while also publicly pressuring foreign officials. Observers and allies have criticized certain U.S. choices, and reporting highlights how actors exploit ship-routing and cryptocurrency to evade restrictions. At the same time, the U.S. continues to designate individuals and companies tied to the Sudan conflict, using targeted listings to impose accountability across borders.
