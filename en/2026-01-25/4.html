Historic US Winter Storm Causes Widespread Disruptions and Emergency Declarations

US Winter Storm Warning 2026-01-25 • 37 articles in total

In brief
  • A historic and severe winter storm affected nearly half the US, causing extreme cold, heavy snow, and widespread disruptions.
  • The storm led to massive flight cancellations, power outages, emergency declarations, and mobilization of resources across many states.
  • It exposed vulnerabilities in US infrastructure and posed major challenges for public safety and disaster management.

A historic and extraordinarily severe winter storm has swept across the United States, impacting nearly half the country and threatening the safety of over 200 million people. The storm has caused massive disruptions including the cancellation of approximately 12,000 flights, widespread power outages, and hazardous travel conditions due to heavy snow, ice, and extreme cold temperatures reaching as low as -50°C. Emergency declarations have been issued in numerous states, with authorities mobilizing resources and urging residents to prepare for prolonged disruptions and dangerous conditions. The storm is described as one of the worst in decades, with a polar vortex intensifying the cold and complicating response efforts. This event highlights the vulnerability of US infrastructure and the challenges of managing natural disasters on such a large scale.
