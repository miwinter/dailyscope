Xi Jinping's Military Purge: Top PLA Leaders Investigated Amid Power Consolidation

China PLA Anti-Corruption Probe 2026-01-25 • 14 articles in total

In brief
  • China is conducting a large military purge targeting top generals to consolidate Xi Jinping's control and enforce anti-corruption within the PLA.
  • The purge reveals internal power struggles and mistrust amid regional tensions about Taiwan, affecting East Asian security.
  • International sources see the shakeup as a political move impacting military loyalty and signaling potential preparation for conflict.

China is undergoing its most extensive military purge in recent years, targeting top generals close to President Xi Jinping, including the second highest-ranking officer, Zhang Youxia. The investigations and removals are part of a broader anti-corruption campaign and efforts to consolidate Xi's control over the People's Liberation Army. These actions reveal intense internal power struggles and growing mistrust within the Chinese military leadership. The shakeup occurs amid heightened regional tensions, particularly concerning Taiwan, complicating East Asian security dynamics. The lack of detailed disclosures about the violations underscores the political sensitivity surrounding these disciplinary measures.

Chinese and Regional Perspectives on Anti-Corruption and Power Consolidation

Articles from Singapore, South Korea, Chile, Romania, Kazakhstan, and Azerbaijan emphasize the anti-corruption nature of the investigations and the political consolidation efforts by Xi Jinping. They highlight the purge as a strategic move to enforce loyalty and discipline within the PLA, reflecting internal scrutiny and ongoing efforts to root out corruption and dissent. These sources focus on the implications for military hierarchy and political control without extensive speculation on regional security consequences.

Western and Allied Views Highlight Power Struggles and Regional Security Implications

Sources from the USA, Australia, Portugal, Spain, and Japan frame the purge as a sign of intense internal power struggles within the PLA and Xi's mistrust of military elites. They underscore the significance of removing top generals amid preparations for potential military actions, such as a Taiwan invasion, and the broader impact on regional and global security. These articles stress the political and strategic ramifications of the military shakeup, portraying it as a destabilizing factor in East Asia.
