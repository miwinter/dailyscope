US Withdrawal from WHO: A Major Shift with Global Health Implications

US WHO Withdrawal 2026-01-25 • 25 articles in total

In brief
  • The US completed its withdrawal from the WHO due to concerns over its COVID-19 response and calls for reform.
  • International leaders warn the withdrawal weakens global health security and cooperation against pandemics.
  • Some US states continue WHO engagement, showing domestic divisions on global health collaboration.

The United States has officially completed its withdrawal from the World Health Organization, marking a significant change in global health governance and international cooperation. The US government justified the move by citing concerns over WHO's handling of the COVID-19 pandemic and calls for reform, while critics warn it undermines global pandemic response efforts. The WHO leadership and many international partners have publicly disputed the US reasons, emphasizing the importance of continued collaboration. Meanwhile, some US states like California have chosen to maintain ties with WHO networks, highlighting internal divergence. This withdrawal raises concerns about future global health coordination and funding, with potential long-term impacts on international solidarity against health crises.

International Concern and WHO's Rebuttal

Many international actors and the WHO leadership express regret and concern over the US withdrawal, disputing the reasons given by the US government. They emphasize that the decision weakens global health security and undermines international cooperation needed to tackle pandemics. The WHO stresses its commitment to global health despite the political tensions and warns of negative consequences for worldwide health efforts.

US Government's Justification and Policy Shift

The US government, particularly under the Trump administration, framed the withdrawal as a necessary response to perceived failures and lack of accountability within the WHO during the COVID-19 pandemic. This move is seen as part of a broader shift in US foreign policy and disengagement from multilateral institutions. The decision includes cessation of funding and reflects skepticism about the WHO's effectiveness and transparency.

Global Health Impact and Concerns from Other Countries

Several countries and international observers highlight the significant implications of the US exit for global health governance and pandemic preparedness. They express concerns about reduced funding, loss of US leadership, and potential setbacks in international health collaboration. The withdrawal is viewed as a setback for global solidarity and raises uncertainty about future cooperation.

US States Maintaining WHO Engagement

Despite the federal government's withdrawal, some US states, notably California, have chosen to maintain direct engagement with WHO initiatives such as the Global Outbreak Alert and Response Network. This reflects a divergence within the US on international health cooperation and a commitment at subnational levels to remain connected to global health monitoring and response systems.
