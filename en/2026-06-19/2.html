World leaders navigate war, diplomacy and multilateral ties

World Leaders Dialogue 2026-06-19 • 90 articles in total

In brief
  • Global leaders balance support for Ukraine with debates on diplomacy toward Russia amid differing Western strategies.
  • Asian and ASEAN countries deepen pragmatic ties with Russia and enhance regional cooperation through forums like BRICS and SCO.
  • Multiple regions witness fragile diplomacy and peace efforts, including Iran's rising influence, Venezuela talks, African partnerships, and institutional initiatives for multilateral cooperation.

Global leaders are balancing solidarity on Ukraine with debates over whether to reopen diplomatic channels to Russia, while G7 dynamics—shaped in part by the U.S. presence—dominate Western strategy. In Asia and ASEAN, governments push deeper ties with Russia and strengthen regional cooperation through forums such as BRICS and the SCO, reflecting a pragmatic focus on trade, energy and resilience. The Middle East remains fluid: Tehran’s maneuvers, fragile truces, and inspection arrangements are reshaping regional bargaining positions even as calls for broader peace persist. Parallel tracks of diplomacy include U.S.-facilitated talks on Venezuela, AU–EU engagement with African leaders, and a surge in institutional initiatives (research networks, UN centres, and regional dialogues) aimed at bolstering multilateral problem‑solving.
