Escalation over Moscow, drones reshaping the war

Russo-Ukrainian War 2026-06-19 • 70 articles in total

In brief
  • Ukraine conducts large-scale drone and strike attacks on Moscow, causing fires, disrupted flights, and environmental damage.
  • Moscow responds with threats and hardline measures, raising fears of escalation amid internal calls for stronger actions.
  • Western allies increase military support and sanctions on Russia while balancing economic concerns and addressing the broader humanitarian and strategic impacts.

Reports show a marked escalation as Ukraine has mounted large-scale drone and strike campaigns against targets in and around Moscow, including oil refineries and airports, producing fires, disrupted flights and reports of unusual 'oil rain'. Moscow has responded with stern rhetoric, threats of massive retaliation and hardline calls inside Russia for stronger measures, raising fears of further escalation. Western governments and Ukraine's partners are deepening military support, pledging funds and matériel while extending sanctions on Russia, even as some EU members voice economic concerns about the costs. The conflict's effects ripple beyond the battlefield: supply chains, energy markets and humanitarian conditions are strained, while investigations highlight recruitment networks and dual‑use supply leaks that sustain Russia's war effort.
