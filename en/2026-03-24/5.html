Deadly Crash of Colombian Military Hercules Aircraft Sparks Investigation

Colombia Military Aircraft Crash 2026-03-24 • 48 articles in total

In brief
  • A Colombian military C-130 crashed near Puerto Leguízamo with 80-125 soldiers onboard, causing at least 66 deaths and many injuries.
  • Rescue efforts and official investigations into technical and operational causes are ongoing amid national mourning.
  • The crash raises concerns about military aviation safety, operational protocols, and crisis response in Colombia.

A Colombian military C-130 Hercules transport aircraft crashed shortly after takeoff near Puerto Leguízamo in the Amazon region, carrying between 80 and 125 soldiers and crew. The crash resulted in a high death toll, with most reports confirming at least 66 fatalities and many injuries, while some sources mention survivors and ongoing rescue efforts. Authorities have launched investigations to determine the cause, with considerations including technical failure, operational challenges, and bureaucratic issues. The tragedy has raised serious concerns about military aviation safety, operational protocols, and crisis response in Colombia, highlighting the risks faced by armed forces in remote and challenging environments. Rescue operations continue amid national mourning and calls for improved safety measures.
