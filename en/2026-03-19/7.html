Global Health Diplomacy 2026-03-19 • 15 articles in total

In brief
  • Global health diplomacy involves regional cooperation, medical internationalism, and addressing challenges like violence and cyberattacks against health initiatives.
  • International health governance faces strains from political tensions, aid conditionality, and ethical reforms impacting global and national health policies.
  • Accelerating progress on health goals requires innovative policies to combat diseases, protect health workers, and meet Sustainable Development Goals worldwide.

The articles collectively highlight diverse global health diplomacy efforts, ranging from regional cooperation to combat cervical cancer in Saint Lucia under PAHO's guidance, to Cuba's extensive medical internationalism benefiting countries like Mexico and Jamaica. The urgency of meeting Sustainable Development Goals and addressing slowing progress in infant mortality is emphasized, alongside innovations and reforms in health policy and access worldwide.
