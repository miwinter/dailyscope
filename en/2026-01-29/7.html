Russia and Syria Negotiate Continued Russian Military Presence Amid Regional Instability

Russia–Syria Base Negotiations 2026-01-29 • 14 articles in total

In brief
  • Putin met Syrian leaders to discuss maintaining and expanding Russian military bases in Syria.
  • Russia aims to preserve its influence in the Middle East amid Syria's 2024 regime change and ongoing conflicts.
  • International and regional sources highlight Russia's strategic role and the geopolitical impact of its military presence in Syria.

Russian President Vladimir Putin has held multiple meetings with Syrian leaders, including Bashar al-Assad and Ahmed al-Shara, to discuss the status and future of Russian military bases in Syria. These talks emphasize Russia's strategic intent to maintain and possibly expand its military footprint in the country despite ongoing regional conflicts and international scrutiny. The discussions also reflect Russia's broader geopolitical goals of sustaining influence in the Middle East amid shifting alliances and the aftermath of Syria's regime change in 2024. Both sides highlight cooperation on security and territorial unity, with Russia positioning itself as a key power broker in the region. The negotiations underscore the complexity of Syria's conflict and Russia's determination to secure long-term military and political interests there.
