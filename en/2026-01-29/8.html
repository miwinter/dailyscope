Historic EU-India Free Trade Agreement Marks New Era in Global Trade

EU–India Free Trade Deal 2026-01-29 • 13 articles in total

In brief
  • The EU and India finalized a free trade agreement covering two billion people with reduced tariffs and enhanced cooperation.
  • The deal strengthens economic and strategic ties and counters US and Chinese dominance in global trade.
  • The US criticizes the pact for potentially undermining sanctions on Russia and complicating geopolitical relations.

After two decades of negotiations, the European Union and India have finalized a comprehensive free trade agreement that establishes a trade zone covering two billion people. The deal aims to significantly reduce tariffs on most goods, enhance economic cooperation, and deepen strategic ties between the two regions. It is viewed as a milestone in bilateral relations and a strategic move to counterbalance US and Chinese dominance in global trade. While the agreement is celebrated by both parties as a major victory, it has drawn criticism from the US, which expresses concerns over its implications for geopolitical and economic dynamics, particularly regarding Russia. The pact is expected to boost trade, investment, and cooperation, marking a significant shift in international economic relations.

US Criticism of EU-India Trade Deal

From the US viewpoint, represented in one article, the trade agreement is criticized for potentially undermining international efforts against Russia, especially given India's purchase of Russian oil. The US Treasury Secretary expresses concern that the EU's economic engagement with India may inadvertently finance conflict and weaken sanctions regimes. This perspective reflects growing friction between the US and EU over strategic economic policies and geopolitical alignments.

Trade Deal as a Counterbalance to US and China

Some articles highlight the agreement as a strategic move by the EU and India to counterbalance the influence of the United States and China in global trade. This viewpoint underscores the geopolitical significance of the pact beyond economic benefits, framing it as a response to shifting global power dynamics and a step toward greater competitiveness and autonomy in international commerce.
