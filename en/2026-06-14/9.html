EU Opens Accession Talks with Ukraine and Moldova

Ukraine-EU Accession Talks 2026-06-14 • 7 articles in total

In brief
  • EU envoys agreed to start the first phase of membership talks with Ukraine and Moldova beginning June 15.
  • Ukraine welcomes the accession talks and pledges to address all negotiation topics to advance EU integration.
  • Hungary lifted its veto after a bilateral agreement on minority rights, enabling the accession process to proceed.

EU envoys agreed to begin the first phase of membership talks with Ukraine and Moldova, with formal negotiations announced to start on June 15. European leaders framed the move as a major diplomatic milestone that advances integration while underscoring security and reform concerns. Kyiv welcomed the decision and said it is ready to open all topics in accession negotiations, portraying the step as meaningful progress toward EU membership. A prior Hungarian veto was lifted after a bilateral agreement on minority issues, removing a key obstacle and accelerating the process amid delicate intra-EU politics.
