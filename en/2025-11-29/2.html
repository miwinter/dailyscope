Military Coup in Guinea-Bissau Sparks Regional Condemnation and Political Uncertainty

Guinea-Bissau Military Coup 2025-11-29 • 49 articles in total

In brief
  • A military coup in Guinea-Bissau ousted President Embaló and led the military to suspend elections and impose curfews.
  • Regional bodies condemned the coup, suspended Guinea-Bissau's memberships, and demanded a return to constitutional order.
  • Some analysts question the coup's legitimacy, while certain countries remain silent or non-committal amid the crisis.

A military coup in Guinea-Bissau ousted President Umaro Sissoco Embaló, who fled to Senegal, while the military declared control over the country, suspending elections and imposing curfews. Regional bodies such as ECOWAS and the African Union strongly condemned the coup, suspending Guinea-Bissau from their organizations and demanding a return to constitutional order. The coup has intensified political instability and raised concerns about democratic governance and security in West Africa. International actors, including the UN and Brazil, have called for dialogue and peaceful resolution, while some local voices question the legitimacy and motives behind the takeover. The situation remains fluid with ongoing power shifts and contested legitimacy of the new military-led government.
