Frankfurt 787 landing‑gear collapse prompts safety probes

EU Aviation Safety Probe 2026-06-05 • 11 articles in total

In brief
  • A Lufthansa Boeing 787's landing gear collapsed at Frankfurt Airport, injuring several ground or airline staff while no passengers were aboard.
  • The incident disrupted flights and prompted investigations by Lufthansa and aviation authorities focusing on maintenance and ground procedures.
  • A separate UK helicopter crash killed three people and is under independent investigation unrelated to the Frankfurt event.

Several news outlets reported a landing‑gear collapse involving a Lufthansa Boeing 787 at Frankfurt Airport that injured multiple ground crew or airline staff while passengers were not on board. Emergency responders treated the injured on site and flights were disrupted, prompting Lufthansa and aviation authorities to open investigations. Coverage diverges: some accounts emphasize passenger panic and pre‑departure collapse, others focus on injuries and potential maintenance or regulatory follow‑up, while a few reports describe only minor disruption with no injuries. The event has drawn wider attention to 787 landing‑gear reliability and ground‑handling procedures among European regulators and carriers. Separately, one article covers an unrelated fatal UK helicopter crash that also triggered an official probe into circumstances.
