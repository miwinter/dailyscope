Trump's presidency: spectacle, fossil fuels, and institutional pushback

US: Trump Presidency 2026-06-05 • 52 articles in total

In brief
  • Trump uses national events and monuments for political self-branding amid polarized reactions from artists and critics.
  • His policies focus on coal support, immigration restrictions, and diplomatic shifts sparking debates and institutional pushback.
  • The administration's actions affect energy, culture, migration, diplomacy, legal scrutiny, and tech governance globally.

International coverage of Donald Trump’s presidency highlights a mix of performative politics and consequential policy moves. Reports focus on his use of national events and monuments as stages for self-branding while artists and critics decry the politicization of celebrations. Policy actions — notably a $700 million push for coal, immigration rule changes, and new stances in diplomacy — have sparked debates about economic priorities, climate trade‑offs, and impacts on migrants. At the same time, congressional maneuvers, legal developments, and media battles indicate growing institutional checks and public scrutiny on his agenda. Observers across regions also track the administration’s engagement with tech and AI, underscoring tensions between innovation, regulation, and political optics.
