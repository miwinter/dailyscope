Colombia's polarized presidential runoff

Colombia Elections Runoff 2026-06-05 • 7 articles in total

In brief
  • Colombia's presidential election heads to a runoff between Abelardo de la Espriella and Iván Cepeda amid record voter turnout benefiting the right-wing candidate.
  • Iván Cepeda dropped his Constituent Assembly proposal to broaden support, but analysts warn the polarized race may heighten political tensions.
  • The election day was orderly, yet analysts expect late campaign changes could influence the runoff outcome.

Colombia's presidential election has moved to a runoff between Abelardo de la Espriella and Iván Cepeda, highlighting a sharp national divide. Observers note historically high voter participation that benefited areas where the right‑wing candidate performed strongly. Cepeda has retreated from proposing a Constituent Assembly as he seeks to broaden support ahead of the second round, while analysts warn the contest is highly polarized and could intensify political tensions. Election day proceedings were reported as orderly, though commentators stress that late developments and campaign shifts could still shape the runoff outcome.
