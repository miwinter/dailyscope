Diplomacy in motion: great-power alignment, mediation efforts, and regional dialogue

World Leaders Dialogue 2026-05-23 • 72 articles in total

In brief
  • China and Russia strengthen their strategic partnership with coordinated meetings on security and economic issues.
  • Intense mediation efforts focus on Iran and the Strait of Hormuz amid stalled UN talks and firm Iranian positions.
  • Ukraine and Western partners debate negotiation formats while security consultations proceed amid calls for productive talks.

Leaders and regional actors pursued multiple parallel tracks of dialogue aimed at managing conflicts and deepening cooperation. China and Russia staged high-level meetings that publicly reinforced a strategic partnership and coordinated messaging on security and economic issues. Intense diplomacy focused on Iran and the Strait of Hormuz, with Qatar, Oman and Pakistan mediating while Tehran resisted key concessions on uranium and UN talks produced no consensus. The Ukraine crisis remained central to Western mediation debates as Kyiv called for designated mediators and new negotiating formats while U.S. officials conditioned engagement on productive results; at the same time NATO and regional groupings advanced security consultations. Across regions, religious and state leaders urged diplomatic solutions, and a range of subregional initiatives pursued pragmatic cooperation on fisheries, Arctic governance, and cross-border ties.
