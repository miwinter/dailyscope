China coal-mine explosions: deaths, trapped miners and safety scrutiny

China Mine Rescue 2026-05-23 • 14 articles in total

In brief
  • A gas explosion caused a deadly coal-mining accident in northern China with around eight miners dead and dozens trapped underground.
  • Rescue efforts include drilling and supplying air and water, while Chinese authorities have launched investigations and safety probes.
  • The incident highlights fluctuating casualty reports and renewed international concern over China's mine safety enforcement.

A series of reports describe a deadly coal-mining accident in northern China that has left miners dead and many more trapped underground, with casualty counts varying between sources. Several outlets report eight dead and dozens trapped—some specifying 38—while others cite an earlier toll of four dead with about 90 miners trapped, reflecting rapidly changing information. Accounts attribute the disaster to a gas explosion or lethal gas buildup (including high carbon monoxide levels) and describe urgent rescue efforts using drilling and supplies of air and water. Chinese authorities have launched investigations and pledged probes into safety lapses; state leadership was reported to have ordered inquiries. The event has reignited international concern over mine safety and enforcement of regulations in China’s coal sector.

Reports citing eight dead and dozens trapped

These sources present a version of events that converges on eight fatalities and dozens of miners still trapped (with some reports giving a precise figure of 38), emphasizing a gas explosion and high carbon-monoxide risk. They highlight government rescue operations, official investigations, and, in at least one account, direct orders from national leadership to probe the incident and improve safety oversight.
