In brief
  • The Middle East conflict involves repeated military strikes and escalations among Iran, Israel, the US, and regional actors with severe humanitarian and economic impacts.

  • International and local actors call for ceasefire and diplomacy to address civilian suffering amid complex proxy conflicts involving Hezbollah and Palestinian groups.

The Middle East conflict has escalated sharply with repeated military strikes, including airstrikes on Iranian infrastructure and missile attacks between Iran, Israel, and allied states. Regional actors such as Gulf states, Lebanon, and Yemen are increasingly involved, with diplomatic efforts ongoing but challenged by persistent hostilities and proxy engagements. The humanitarian impact is severe, with casualties reported and international calls for ceasefire and de-escalation growing louder. Economic repercussions are evident in volatile oil markets and regional economies, while geopolitical alignments, including Russia's engagement, shape the complex security landscape.
