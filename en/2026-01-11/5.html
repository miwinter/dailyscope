US Large-Scale Airstrikes Target ISIS in Syria Amid Retaliation and Ongoing Counterterrorism

US Syria Airstrikes 2026-01-11 • 44 articles in total

In brief
  • The US conducts large-scale airstrikes in Syria to degrade ISIS and retaliate against attacks on American forces.
  • US operations involve coalition partners like Jordan and aim to maintain regional stability and counterterrorism efforts.
  • Details on strikes are limited, but they highlight complex Middle East geopolitics and global US readiness to respond to threats.

The United States has conducted multiple large-scale airstrikes against Islamic State (ISIS) targets across Syria, often in retaliation for attacks that killed American personnel. These operations, frequently named or associated with 'Operation Hawkeye Strike,' aim to degrade ISIS capabilities, disrupt their infrastructure, and prevent further terrorist activities. The strikes are part of a sustained US-led coalition effort involving allied forces such as Jordan, reflecting ongoing commitment to counterterrorism in the region. While details on casualties and specific targets remain limited, the US military has publicly released some footage to demonstrate the scale and precision of these operations. This military engagement underscores the complex geopolitical dynamics in Syria and the broader Middle East, with the US emphasizing its readiness to respond globally to threats against its forces.
