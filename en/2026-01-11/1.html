US-Venezuela Diplomatic Tensions: Military Actions, Oil Control, and Diplomatic Thaw

US–Venezuela Diplomatic Tensions 2026-01-11 • 121 articles in total

In brief
  • The US has taken aggressive actions against Venezuela, including capturing President Maduro and controlling oil assets while urging American evacuations.
  • Venezuelan allies condemn US military and economic pressures as sovereignty violations causing humanitarian and political harm.
  • Both countries engage in diplomatic talks aiming to restore relations and ease tensions despite ongoing challenges.

The US-Venezuela relationship has been marked by intense diplomatic and military tensions, including the unprecedented capture of Venezuelan President Nicolás Maduro by US forces, which sparked global condemnation and regional unrest. The US has asserted control over Venezuelan oil assets, pushing for massive investments from American oil companies despite skepticism from the industry. Concurrently, the US government has issued multiple travel warnings urging its citizens to leave Venezuela due to security threats from armed militias. Amid these conflicts, both countries have initiated diplomatic talks aiming to restore relations, signaling a potential thaw after years of hostility. The situation remains complex, with international actors divided and ongoing debates about sovereignty, legality, and regional stability.
