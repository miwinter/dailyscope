Waves of deadly attacks expose Honduras' security crisis

Honduras Violence Attacks 2026-05-22 • 11 articles in total

In brief
  • Violent attacks in Honduras killed 15 to 25 people, mainly in Trujillo and Colón, involving plantation workers and police.
  • The violence is linked to organized crime, gang conflicts over plantations and drug routes, prompting military and police responses.
  • The incidents highlight ongoing security problems in border and agricultural regions, leading to investigations and increased patrols.

A series of coordinated and separate violent incidents across Honduras left between roughly 15 and 25 people dead, with the deadliest attacks reported in Trujillo and Colón. Victims include plantation workers and police officers, and some reports name individual victims, underscoring local human toll. Sources variously attribute the violence to organized crime, rival gangs fighting over palm-oil estates and drug-trafficking routes, or do not identify perpetrators while authorities deploy military and police responses. The events have prompted investigations, increased patrols and calls for accountability amid growing concern over security in border and agricultural regions.
