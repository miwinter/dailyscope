Cross-border prosecutions, sanctions and corporate liability

Cross-Border Legal Actions 2026-05-22 • 77 articles in total

In brief
  • Cross-border legal actions are increasing in areas like state prosecutions, corporate liability, and coordinated law enforcement.
  • The US targets Cuban officials with indictments and sanctions, causing diplomatic tensions with Havana and allies.
  • European courts prosecute cases from wartime atrocities to corporate crimes, while international cooperation expands in criminal, financial, and online safety enforcement.

These articles show a surge in cross-border legal actions including state prosecutions, corporate liability rulings and coordinated law‑enforcement operations. The United States has pursued high-profile charges and sanctions—most notably multiple indictments and related enforcement measures targeting Cuban figures—which has provoked diplomatic responses from Havana and allied actors. European courts and prosecutors have advanced accountability in cases ranging from espionage and wartime atrocities to the landmark convictions of Air France and Airbus over the 2009 crash, while international asset disputes and sanction enforcement underline growing financial litigation between states and companies. At the same time, coordinated extraditions, trafficking and organized‑crime busts, as well as continuing disclosures in the Epstein files and online‑safety enforcement, reflect expanding cross‑jurisdictional cooperation and legal contention across regions.
