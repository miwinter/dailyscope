Cuba–US tensions: deterrence, Pentagon readiness and covert talks

Cuba-US Diplomacy 2026-04-17 • 13 articles in total

In brief
  • Cuban leaders emphasize sovereignty and readiness to repel U.S. aggression while denying any desire for conflict.
  • U.S. military plans raise fears of rapid escalation, fueling Cuban defensive posturing.
  • Raúl Castro influences secret talks with the U.S., and U.S.-based Cuban exile groups shape policy discussions.

Across reports, Cuban leaders — led by President Miguel Díaz‑Canel — emphasize sovereignty and repeatedly state the island is prepared to repel any U.S. aggression while insisting they do not seek conflict. Parallel coverage highlights U.S. military preparedness, with mentions of Pentagon contingency planning that raise fears of rapid escalation. At the same time, statements from Raúl Castro’s family suggest he remains involved behind the scenes in negotiations with Washington, indicating parallel diplomatic channels. U.S.-based initiatives such as a new Florida International University center for Cuban exile studies show the diaspora and academic communities remain active actors in the broader policy environment.
