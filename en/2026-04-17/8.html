Malema sentenced and political fallout

South Africa: Malema Conviction 2026-04-17 • 7 articles in total

In brief
  • Julius Malema was sentenced to five years for illegal possession and firing of a firearm and plans to appeal.
  • The ruling has intensified political tensions and could affect opposition dynamics before elections.
  • Reactions are divided between viewing the verdict as judicial accountability and a political setback.

Julius Malema, leader of the Economic Freedom Fighters, has been sentenced to five years in prison after being found guilty of unlawfully possessing and firing a firearm. He and his lawyers say they will appeal, while hundreds of supporters rallied ahead of the sentencing. Coverage highlights both the legal ruling and its potential political consequences, including impacts on opposition dynamics ahead of upcoming elections and concerns about security. Commentators are divided: some see the verdict as judicial accountability for politically linked violence, others as a major setback with wide political ramifications.
