Alliance of China, Russia, and North Korea: Military Parade, Strategic Unity, and Global Tensions

2025-09-03 • 112 articles in total

In a significant demonstration of military and diplomatic solidarity, China, Russia, and North Korea convened in Beijing to participate in a massive military parade marking the 80th anniversary of the end of World War II. The leaders—Xi Jinping, Vladimir Putin, and Kim Jong Un—emphasized their deepening strategic cooperation and presented a united front challenging Western influence amid escalating geopolitical tensions. The event showcased advanced weaponry, symbolizing rising military capabilities and signaling intent to shift global power dynamics. Meanwhile, the United States, particularly through former President Donald Trump, condemned the alliance, accusing the three nations of conspiring against American interests, intensifying diplomatic and rhetorical conflicts.
