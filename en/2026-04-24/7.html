US invites Russia to Miami G20 amid mixed signals from Trump

US–Russia Putin Invite 2026-04-24 • 15 articles in total

In brief
  • The U.S. has officially invited Russia to the G20 summit in Miami despite current tensions.
  • President Trump publicly supports Putin's attendance but doubts he will come and at times denies inviting him.
  • The invitation is seen as a strategic move that could strain relations with Western allies and complicate summit discussions.

Multiple outlets report that the United States has extended an invitation for Russia to attend the G20 summit in Miami, with White House sources and diplomatic confirmations framing the move as engagement despite ongoing tensions. President Donald Trump has repeatedly said Putin’s attendance would be useful or beneficial, while also expressing doubts that the Russian leader will actually come and, at times, denying he personally issued an invite. Coverage highlights that Washington’s decision is being read as a strategic signal with potential consequences for alliances and the summit agenda. Some analysts warn the invitation could provoke friction with Western partners and underline the delicate diplomatic balancing act ahead of the meeting.
