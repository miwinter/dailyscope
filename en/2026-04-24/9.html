US soldier charged over insider bets on Maduro

US Insider Trading: Maduro 2026-04-24 • 9 articles in total

In brief
  • A U.S. service member faces charges for using classified information to place large bets on Venezuela's Maduro removal through online prediction markets.
  • The case reveals failures in protecting sensitive intelligence, challenges in regulating online betting, and raises military accountability issues.
  • It also highlights political effects tied to regime-change efforts and draws international attention to U.S. policy toward Venezuela.

U.S. authorities have charged a service member accused of using classified information to place large wagers—about $400,000—on the predicted removal or capture of Venezuelan President Nicolás Maduro via platforms such as Polymarket. Prosecutors allege the soldier exploited mission secrets and coordinated with others to profit, triggering insider-trading and related criminal charges and a federal investigation into misuse of sensitive material. Reporting highlights two intertwined concerns: weaknesses in handling classified intelligence and accountability within U.S. forces, and legal and regulatory gaps around online prediction markets that can be abused with privileged information. Observers also note broader political implications, as coverage connects the case to regime-change dynamics and potential effects on U.S. policy toward Venezuela, drawing international attention.
