US releases declassified UFO/UAP files

US UFO Disclosure 2026-05-09 • 28 articles in total

In brief
  • The US released decades of classified UFO files to promote transparency and support research without confirming extraterrestrial origins.
  • The disclosures focus on national security and aviation safety, with government agencies and experts urging caution about the materials' conclusiveness.
  • Media reactions vary from expert analysis to sensationalism, while some unrelated items were mistakenly grouped with the UFO materials.

The United States has released a large trove of previously classified files, photos and videos related to unidentified flying objects and unidentified aerial phenomena, spanning decades and including some Apollo-era materials. The disclosures — framed as declassification steps promoted under President Trump and carried out by the Pentagon, FBI and other agencies — are presented as moves toward government transparency and invite private and scholarly analysis. Officials and experts caution the released materials are largely inconclusive, stressing national security and aviation-safety dimensions rather than confirming extraterrestrial origins. Media coverage ranges from sober analysis and expert commentary to sensational reports and viral internet speculation, and a small number of unrelated items were included in some international roundups.
