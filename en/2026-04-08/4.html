The conflict between the United States, Israel, and Iran has intensified with multiple airstrikes targeting Iranian infrastructure, including bridges, railways, and energy facilities, leading to significant regional instability. The US and Israel maintain a hardline stance, with President Trump issuing threats of annihilation and pauses in bombing operations, while Iran asserts resilience, claims victory, and mobilizes domestically against attacks. International voices express concern over potential war crimes, the use of AI in warfare, and the risk of nuclear escalation, with calls for ceasefire and diplomatic negotiations from countries like China. Public sentiment within Iran shows cautious optimism following ceasefire announcements, though skepticism remains about enforcement and the durability of peace. The conflict's global implications include disruptions to energy markets, geopolitical shifts, and debates over the legality and morality of military strategies employed.
