Deadly Ecuador Prison Riots Highlight Escalating Gang Violence and Security Failures

2025-11-10 • 24 articles in total

A series of violent riots erupted across Ecuadorian prisons, particularly in Machala, resulting in at least 31 deaths due to shootings, hangings, and mass asphyxiation. These incidents expose the deepening crisis marked by gang rivalries using firearms and explosives, overcrowding, and poor prison management. Authorities face immense challenges in restoring order and investigating the causes amidst allegations of organized crime influence inside penitentiaries. The repeated outbreaks have attracted international attention, emphasizing systemic law enforcement and human rights concerns. Efforts to reform prison security and conditions remain a critical but daunting task for the Ecuadorian government.
