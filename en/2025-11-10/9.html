CELAC-EU Summit Highlights Cooperation Amid US Tensions and Regional Divisions

2025-11-10 • 13 articles in total

The IV CELAC-EU Summit held in Colombia focused on strengthening bi-regional ties in spite of geopolitical tensions, particularly with the United States. Latin American and European leaders jointly emphasized respect for international law, collaboration on green energy, and coordinated efforts against drug trafficking. The summit also raised contentious issues such as the US blockade on Cuba and regional criticisms of US actions in the Caribbean, reflecting divisions among CELAC members. Colombian President Gustavo Petro, while praising the summit's achievements, faced notable political isolation and abstentions, notably Venezuela’s refusal to sign the final declaration. Overall, the summit underscored both cooperative ambitions and ongoing fractures within Latin America regarding foreign policy and regional integration.
