Mass Kidnappings of Nigerian Schoolchildren Highlight Ongoing Security Crisis

Nigeria Schoolgirls Kidnapping 2025-11-26 • 19 articles in total

In brief
  • Nigeria faces a severe security crisis with multiple mass kidnappings of schoolchildren from Catholic schools.
  • Some kidnapped children have been rescued or escaped, but hundreds remain captive amid ongoing armed attacks.
  • International leaders and communities demand urgent protection and effective government action to end abductions.

Nigeria is facing a severe security crisis marked by multiple mass kidnappings of schoolchildren, with over 300 students abducted from Catholic schools in Niger and Kebbi states. While some kidnapped children, including 24 schoolgirls, have been successfully rescued or escaped, hundreds remain in captivity, prompting urgent appeals from international figures like the Pope and widespread concern. The Nigerian government and military have launched rescue operations, but persistent attacks by armed groups continue to threaten the safety of students and teachers. Families and communities are pleading for increased protection and effective security measures to prevent further abductions. This situation underscores the broader challenges Nigeria faces in combating violent kidnappings and ensuring the safety of its educational institutions.
