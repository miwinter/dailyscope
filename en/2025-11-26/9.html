In brief

The G20 Summit held in South Africa showcased a range of perspectives and initiatives emphasizing Africa's growing role in global affairs. African leaders highlighted economic reforms, climate leadership, and calls for strategic partnerships, such as Sierra Leone's proposal for a G20-Africa Compact on critical minerals. Despite the notable absence and opposition of the United States, the summit achieved a consensus on a declaration addressing shared global challenges, underscoring South Africa's leadership. External partners like the UAE and the UK announced significant investments in Africa's technology and economic sectors, reflecting international interest in the continent's development. Meanwhile, civil society groups organized alternative forums to advocate for social justice and environmental equity, illustrating diverse stakeholder engagement during the summit.
