France, under President Emmanuel Macron, is undertaking a significant expansion of its nuclear arsenal, marking a major shift in its defense policy to address rising global and regional security threats. This includes increasing warhead numbers, deploying nuclear-armed aircraft in Europe, and enhancing cooperation with European allies to strengthen collective deterrence. Concurrently, France is actively engaging in regional security, particularly in the Middle East, expressing readiness to defend Gulf countries against Iranian threats and participating in coordinated military actions with European partners. These developments reflect France's intent to assert strategic autonomy and reinforce its role as a key security actor amid evolving geopolitical tensions.
