Polarized Contest in Chile's 2025 Presidential Runoff

2025-11-17 • 31 articles in total

Chile's 2025 presidential election has culminated in a runoff between communist candidate Jeannette Jara and far-right contender José Antonio Kast, reflecting the nation's deep political polarization. The election campaign centers on sharply contrasting issues such as social inclusion, universal basic income, security, crime, and immigration policies. Observers note this runoff represents a critical juncture for Chile's political trajectory amid significant social and economic challenges. Voter turnout has been strong and the election closely watched internationally for its implications on Chile's democracy and governance. The outcome will decisively influence Chile's future domestic and regional policies.
