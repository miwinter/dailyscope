Cross-border strikes, civilian toll and mounting geopolitical responses

Russo-Ukrainian War 2026-06-04 • 88 articles in total

In brief
  • Ukrainian long-range and drone strikes, including attacks near Saint Petersburg, aim to pressure Russia and challenge Putin's image.
  • The war causes heavy civilian casualties and ongoing humanitarian crises across Ukraine and occupied regions.
  • The conflict intensifies a drone arms race and sparks political debates on international support and escalation risks.

Reports show a surge in long-range and drone strikes that have reached deep into Russian territory — notably multiple attacks around Saint Petersburg timed with a major economic forum — while Russia continues heavy missile and drone barrages across Ukraine. Kyiv and some Western officials portray Ukraine’s deeper strikes as leverage to negotiate and to put pressure on the Kremlin, even as allied capitals warn of escalation risks and debate further support. Analysts highlight the symbolic impact of strikes on high-profile events for Putin’s image and broader questions about Russia’s war economy, domestic politics and elite confidence. Parallel coverage documents advances in drone production, Ukrainian air-defence tests and the persistent humanitarian toll on civilians and infrastructure on both sides.
