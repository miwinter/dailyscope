New Delhi hotel fire kills at least 21

New Delhi Hotel Fire Response 2026-06-04 • 11 articles in total

In brief
  • A fire at a New Delhi hotel killed 21 people and injured many, with firefighters conducting rescue operations.
  • Investigations focus on fire cause, safety standards, and emergency response in budget accommodations.
  • Many victims were foreign nationals, prompting India's Ministry of External Affairs to coordinate with foreign embassies.

A deadly fire at a hotel in New Delhi has killed at least 21 people and left dozens injured, with firefighters and rescue teams conducting extensive search-and-rescue operations. Authorities and investigators are probing the cause while reviewing building safety, emergency response and compliance in urban and budget accommodations. Reports note a significant number of foreign nationals among the victims, prompting consular involvement and coordination by India's Ministry of External Affairs with foreign embassies. The incident has drawn international media attention and renewed scrutiny of fire-safety enforcement in the capital's hospitality sector.
