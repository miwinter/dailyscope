Tensions Rise as Slovakia and Hungary Leverage Energy Supplies Against Ukraine

Slovakia and Hungary Ukraine Ultimatums 2026-02-22 • 21 articles in total

In brief
  • Slovakia and Hungary demand Ukraine resume Russian oil transit, threatening to cut aid and block EU loans to secure their energy supplies.
  • Ukraine rejects these ultimatums as blackmail and blames Russia for the energy disruptions, urging neighbors to pressure Moscow instead.
  • The dispute reveals regional energy interdependence tensions and division within the EU over support for Ukraine amid the war.

Slovakia and Hungary have issued ultimatums to Ukraine demanding the resumption of Russian oil transit through pipelines critical to their energy supplies. Slovak Prime Minister Robert Fico threatened to halt emergency electricity aid to Ukraine if oil shipments are not restored promptly, while Hungarian Prime Minister Viktor Orbán declared he would block a major EU loan to Kyiv under similar conditions. Ukraine has strongly rejected these threats, accusing both countries of blackmail and urging them to direct ultimatums toward Russia instead. The dispute highlights the complex interdependence of regional energy security amid the ongoing war and exposes divisions within the EU regarding support for Ukraine. These developments have escalated diplomatic tensions and raised concerns about the stability of energy supplies in Central and Eastern Europe.

Slovak Ultimatum and Energy Leverage

Slovakia, led by Prime Minister Robert Fico, demands that Ukraine allow the transit of Russian oil through its territory, warning that failure to comply will result in Slovakia cutting off emergency electricity supplies to Ukraine. This stance reflects Slovakia's strategic use of its energy exports as leverage to secure oil deliveries essential for its own and regional energy security. Slovakia frames its position as a necessary response to disruptions caused by the ongoing conflict and emphasizes the urgency of restoring oil flows to maintain energy stability.

Hungarian Ultimatum and EU Loan Blockade

Hungary, under Prime Minister Viktor Orbán, has taken a firm position by threatening to block a significant European Union loan to Ukraine unless the transit of Russian oil through the Druzhba pipeline is resumed. Hungary's approach underscores its prioritization of energy supply security and economic interests, even at the risk of complicating EU support for Ukraine. This perspective highlights Hungary's unique position within the EU as a country still reliant on Russian oil imports and willing to use financial leverage to influence Ukraine's energy policies.

Ukraine's Rejection of Ultimatums and Call for Redirected Pressure

Ukraine firmly rejects the ultimatums and threats from Slovakia and Hungary, characterizing them as blackmail and coercion. Ukrainian officials insist that the disruptions in oil transit are consequences of Russian attacks and conflict, not deliberate actions by Kyiv. They call on Slovakia and Hungary to direct their demands and ultimatums toward Russia, the party responsible for the energy disruptions. This viewpoint emphasizes Ukraine's stance of seeking solidarity and support rather than succumbing to pressure from neighboring countries amid the ongoing war.
