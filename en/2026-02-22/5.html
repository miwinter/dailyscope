US Military Buildup Signals Imminent Iran Strike Amid Escalating Tensions

US Strike Prep: Iran 2026-02-22 • 25 articles in total

In brief
  • The US has significantly increased its military presence in the Middle East preparing for potential strikes on Iran.
  • Iran warns of retaliation and prepares counterproposals amid stalled nuclear negotiations and rising tensions.
  • US political concerns focus on risks of prolonged conflict with Iran before upcoming elections.

The United States has significantly increased its military presence in the Middle East, including naval and air forces, as preparations for a potential strike on Iran intensify. President Donald Trump is reportedly considering limited or broader military actions to compel Iran on its nuclear program, with decisions expected imminently. This buildup, the largest since the Iraq invasion, involves strategic positioning in locations such as Souda, the Azores, and Jordan, and includes deployment of major aircraft carriers. Diplomatic efforts appear to be faltering as Iran prepares counterproposals, while warnings from Iran and concerns from international observers highlight the risk of a wider regional conflict. The situation remains highly volatile, with political and security implications for the US, Iran, and the broader Middle East region.
