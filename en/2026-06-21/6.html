Diplomatic rift over Polish honours strains Poland–Ukraine ties

Poland-Ukraine Diplomacy 2026-06-21 • 22 articles in total

In brief
  • Poland revoked a high honour from Ukraine's President Zelensky over wartime historical disputes, prompting Ukraine to return Polish awards and officials to resign.
  • The dispute caused a diplomatic rift with symbolic acts from both sides, while leaders express regret but aim to maintain cooperation.
  • International observers warn the conflict may damage bilateral security collaboration and benefit rival nations like Russia.

Poland revoked or withdrew a high state honour previously awarded to Ukrainian President Volodymyr Zelensky amid a dispute over historical memory and a controversial reference to a wartime Ukrainian unit. In response Zelensky and several current and former Ukrainian officials returned Polish decorations and some Ukrainian officials resigned, turning symbolic awards into a broader diplomatic rupture. Polish officials, notably Karol Nawrocki, defended the revocation as a necessary reaction to a crossed ‘threshold of pain,’ while Ukrainian leaders called the move a strategic mistake but tried to signal continued cooperation. International coverage stresses the symbolic weight of the episode, warning it could damage bilateral coordination during the war and that the dispute may benefit external actors like Russia.

Polish official narrative

Polish sources focus on the decision to revoke Zelensky's Order of the White Eagle and present it as a justified response to remarks and actions seen as offending Polish historical memory. Coverage highlights Nawrocki's strong statements and documents Poland's formal steps, emphasizing the symbolic assertion of national dignity.

Ukrainian reaction and returns

Ukrainian and closely aligned outlets stress Kyiv's protest: Zelensky returned the medal by mail and several current and former Ukrainian officials renounced Polish awards or resigned in reaction. Reporting frames these acts as symbolic counters meant to register displeasure while some Ukrainian voices try to preserve practical cooperation despite the diplomatic rift.
