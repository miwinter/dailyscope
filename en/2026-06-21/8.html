Trial Ordered for Spain PM’s Wife Amid Travel Ban

Spain Sánchez Family Probe 2026-06-21 • 12 articles in total

In brief
  • Begoña Gómez, Spain's prime minister's wife, faces trial on corruption and influence-peddling charges with travel restrictions imposed.
  • The Socialist government claims the investigation is political persecution against the prime minister's circle.
  • The case raises concerns about corruption within Spain's political elite and tests political transparency.

Spain's prime minister's wife, Begoña Gómez, has been ordered by a Spanish judge to stand trial on multiple charges including corruption and influence-peddling, and her passport has been seized with a ban on leaving the country. Courts have imposed travel restrictions and periodic appearance requirements while the judicial process continues. The Socialist government has denounced the proceedings as political persecution, while other coverage highlights broader concerns about corruption within Spain’s political elite. Appeals and further hearings are expected, ensuring continued domestic and international attention on the case.
