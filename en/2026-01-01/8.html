Doumbouya's Consolidation of Power in Guinea's Controversial Presidential Election

Guinea Presidential Election 2026-01-01 • 13 articles in total

In brief
  • General Mamadi Doumbouya won Guinea's first presidential election since his 2021 military coup with over 86% of the vote.
  • The election marks Doumbouya's transition from military leader to elected president consolidating his control over Guinea.
  • The vote faced widespread criticism due to fraud allegations, opposition boycotts, and concerns about fairness and legitimacy.

General Mamadi Doumbouya, who led a military coup in Guinea in 2021, has been declared the winner of the country's first presidential election since the coup, securing a landslide victory with over 86% of the vote. The election marks a significant political transition as Doumbouya moves from military leader to elected president, consolidating his control over Guinea. However, the election has been widely criticized for allegations of fraud, opposition boycotts, and questions about its fairness and inclusiveness. International observers and civil society groups remain concerned about the implications for Guinea's political stability and democratic future. Despite these controversies, Doumbouya's victory is seen as a pivotal moment in Guinea's ongoing political trajectory.
