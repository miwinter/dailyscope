Recovery of Last Hostage's Remains and Gaza Operations Mark New Phase in Israel Conflict

Israel Hostage and Gaza Operations 2026-01-28 • 25 articles in total

In brief
  • The recovery of the last Israeli hostage enables Israel to advance military and political plans to disarm Hamas and demilitarize Gaza.
  • Reopening the Rafah crossing is crucial for humanitarian relief and peace efforts amid Israeli-Egyptian tensions and security concerns.
  • International actors view these developments as key to progressing peace talks despite ongoing violence and political challenges.

The recent recovery of the remains of Ran Gvili, the last Israeli hostage held in Gaza, has closed a painful chapter in the ongoing Israel-Hamas conflict and is seen as a critical step toward advancing peace negotiations and reopening humanitarian lifelines such as the Rafah border crossing. Israel continues its military operations in Gaza with a focus on disarming Hamas and demilitarizing the region, while internal political disagreements, such as opposition from Minister Ben-Gvir, complicate the implementation of ceasefire plans. The reopening of the Rafah crossing is anticipated to alleviate humanitarian pressures, with European and Palestinian officials preparing for its operation amid Israeli-Egyptian tensions. Despite these developments, violence persists, and the future of Gaza remains uncertain as Israel outlines multiple scenarios for its control and security strategy. International actors remain cautiously engaged, linking progress in hostage recovery and border reopening to broader peace efforts.
