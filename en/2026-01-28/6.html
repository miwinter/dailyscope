Spain's Historic Move to Regularize Half a Million Undocumented Immigrants

Spain Immigrant Regularization 2026-01-28 • 14 articles in total

In brief
  • Spain plans to regularize 500,000 undocumented immigrants, mainly from Latin America, to integrate them into the formal economy.
  • This policy marks a shift from restrictive immigration approaches, promoting social inclusion and addressing labor shortages.
  • The plan contrasts with deportation-focused strategies elsewhere and reflects a pragmatic migration policy amid European debates.

Spain has announced a landmark plan to regularize the status of approximately 500,000 undocumented immigrants, predominantly from Latin America. This initiative aims to integrate these individuals into the formal economy and society, addressing labor shortages and promoting social inclusion. The policy marks a significant shift from previous restrictive immigration approaches and contrasts with more deportation-focused strategies seen elsewhere, such as in the US. While supporters hail it as a historic achievement, some opposition voices warn of a potential pull effect that could increase migration. Overall, the measure reflects Spain's commitment to a more inclusive and pragmatic immigration policy amid broader European debates.
