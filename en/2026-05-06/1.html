Widespread bilateral push: trade, security and strategic partnerships expand regionally

Region: Bilateral Deals 2026-05-06 • 105 articles in total

In brief
  • Governments worldwide are increasing bilateral cooperation on energy, trade, security, and development through strategic partnerships and agreements.
  • Regional ties are strengthening via diplomatic visits, military collaboration, and economic integration, despite some contested maritime and resource disputes.
  • International actors are supporting capacity-building, infrastructure projects, and diplomatic normalization to boost stability and trade across multiple regions.

This collection of reports shows a surge in bilateral activity worldwide, with governments forging deals on energy, minerals, trade, infrastructure and security. Europe and Armenia moved visibly toward deeper political and economic ties, while Japan, Australia and regional partners pursued energy and critical-minerals cooperation to boost supply-chain resilience. China continued diversified outreach across Africa, Central Asia and the Middle East, and Pacific and African island states signed new development and investment pacts. Simultaneously, defence and security partnerships strengthened around Ukraine and in the Indo-Pacific, even as some maritime and resource agreements were contested or rescinded. Many items reflect diplomatic signalling—receipts of envoys, summit diplomacy and ceremonial visits—that underpin evolving practical cooperation across regions.
