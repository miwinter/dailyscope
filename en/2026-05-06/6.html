WHO coordinates Canary Islands reception as hantavirus spreads aboard cruise

WHO Hantavirus Cruise Outbreak 2026-05-06 • 24 articles in total

In brief
  • A hantavirus outbreak on the MV Hondius led WHO and Spain to coordinate routing the ship to the Canary Islands for care and containment.
  • Evacuations of seriously ill passengers via Cape Verde are ongoing while authorities investigate human-to-human transmission and conduct contact tracing.
  • At least three deaths and multiple infections are confirmed, with passengers experiencing confinement and uncertainty during the emergency response.

A hantavirus outbreak aboard the MV Hondius has prompted coordinated international public-health action, with the World Health Organization working with Spain to route and host the vessel in the Canary Islands. Several articles report evacuations and medical transfers carried out via Cape Verde as seriously ill passengers and crew are moved for treatment. WHO and other health authorities are investigating possible human-to-human transmission while describing the overall risk as low and conducting passenger tracing and surveillance. Reports describe at least three deaths and multiple confirmed or suspected infections, and authorities are tracing routes, contacts and onboard exposure. Accounts also highlight the human impact of confinement and uncertainty for passengers and crew during the emergency response.
