International Efforts and Challenges in Advancing Gaza Peace Talks

Gaza Peace Talks 2025-12-21 • 18 articles in total

In brief
  • Multiple countries cooperate on a multi-phase Gaza peace plan focused on stabilization and a lasting ceasefire.
  • The US insists on Hamas's disarmament and a long-term phased implementation, while Turkey stresses the ceasefire's fragility and adherence to terms.
  • Diplomatic efforts continue amid complex challenges, with humanitarian concerns and security guarantees central to talks.

Multiple international actors, including the United States, Turkey, Egypt, Qatar, and others, are actively engaged in advancing a multi-phase peace plan for Gaza aimed at stabilizing the region and establishing a lasting ceasefire. While progress has been reported in the initial phase, significant challenges remain, particularly regarding Hamas's disarmament and Israeli violations of ceasefire agreements. Turkey emphasizes the fragility of the ceasefire and the need for strict adherence to peace terms, whereas the US focuses on a long-term timeline for implementing the agreement and insists on Hamas's disarmament as a precondition for lasting peace. Multilateral meetings in Miami and other venues highlight coordinated diplomatic efforts, but the complexity of the conflict and differing priorities among stakeholders continue to slow progress. Humanitarian concerns and security guarantees remain central to ongoing discussions as parties seek to prevent further escalation and promote stability in Gaza.

US-Led Diplomatic and Security Focus

The United States prioritizes Hamas's disarmament and a phased, long-term implementation of the Gaza peace agreement, acknowledging the complexity and extended timeline required. US officials, including special envoys and senators, emphasize security solutions and coordinated international efforts involving Egypt, Qatar, and Turkey. The US also seeks to expand stabilization efforts by encouraging other countries, such as Pakistan, to participate in peacekeeping initiatives.

Turkey's Mediation and Ceasefire Adherence Emphasis

Turkey plays a key mediating role in talks with Hamas and other regional actors, stressing the importance of maintaining the ceasefire and condemning Israeli violations that threaten the peace process. Turkish officials highlight the fragility of the current truce and advocate for strict compliance with ceasefire terms to safeguard ongoing diplomatic efforts. Turkey's involvement reflects its strategic interest in Middle Eastern peace and humanitarian stability.
