Stalled negotiations as US weighs tougher pressure

US–Iran Diplomacy 2026-04-29 • 63 articles in total

In brief
  • U.S. officials cautiously review Iran's stalled proposals while President Trump distrusts Iran and prepares for longer blockades.
  • The U.S. escalates financial sanctions on Iran amid stalled talks, while mediators seek negotiation progress.
  • Iran blames the U.S. for failed talks and maintains resilience despite rising regional and international tensions.

Diplomatic talks between Washington and Tehran remain stalled as U.S. officials review a new Iranian proposal while signalling cautious engagement. President Trump has voiced scepticism about the terms, repeatedly criticizing the proposals, claiming Iran is unstable and directing aides to prepare for prolonged blockade options. The U.S. is simultaneously expanding sanctions and targeting Iran’s financial networks, increasing economic pressure even as mediators — notably Pakistan — work to revive negotiations. Tehran and its allies blame the United States for stalled talks and insist on their resilience, while regional and international partners warn of a frozen conflict and mounting risks to energy and security.
