Allied cooperation tested — royal diplomacy tries to steady transatlantic ties

US Allies Diplomacy 2026-04-29 • 100 articles in total

In brief
  • King Charles III’s U.S. visits seek to strengthen the US–UK bond and rally NATO and Ukraine support.
  • Disputes over Iran policy and Ukraine aid reveal fractures among U.S. allies and internal U.S. divisions.
  • U.S. pursues broad security cooperation globally while facing challenges from Chinese influence and allied coordination.

Allies remain central to U.S. diplomacy but fractures are evident across regions and issues. King Charles III’s high-profile visits and addresses in Washington aim to shore up the US–UK bond and rally support for NATO and Ukraine, using ceremonial diplomacy to bridge political differences. At the same time, sharp public disputes—particularly between U.S. leaders and German officials over Iran—and resignations among U.S. diplomats on Ukraine policy signal strains that could undermine cohesive action. Elsewhere, Washington pursues security cooperation in Latin America and the Indo-Pacific while mediating prisoner releases, even as concerns about Chinese influence and implementation shortfalls in pacts like AUKUS complicate allied coordination.
