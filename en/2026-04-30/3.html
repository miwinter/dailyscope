In brief

Reporting this period shows three interlinked escalation dynamics: repeated Israeli interceptions of international aid flotillas and blocked Gaza convoys have generated widespread international alarm over humanitarian access. At the same time US and Israeli planning, carrier movements and public discussions of strikes on Iran — and Iranian threats in response — have raised the prospect of a broader regional war. International institutions and markets are already registering the humanitarian and economic fallout, from UN warnings about mass impoverishment to sharp drops in aviation demand. Media disputes, symbolic incidents and domestic political moves are shaping narratives and complicating diplomatic efforts to de‑escalate.
