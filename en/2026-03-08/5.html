In brief

U.S. President Donald Trump has taken a firm and confrontational approach toward Iran, rejecting negotiations and demanding unconditional surrender. He has suggested direct military interventions, including deploying troops to control Iran's uranium reserves and influencing the selection of Iran's leadership. Trump also criticized allied countries like the UK for their involvement in the conflict and excluded Kurdish fighters from participation. Additionally, he predicted the imminent collapse of Cuba's regime, expressing confidence in a political renewal there. These statements reflect escalating geopolitical tensions and a hardline U.S. posture in the Middle East and Caribbean regions.
