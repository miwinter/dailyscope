Iran's Supreme Leader Succession: Consensus Amidst Internal Struggles and Regional Tensions

Iran Leadership Succession 2026-03-08 • 31 articles in total

In brief
  • Iran's Assembly of Experts is nearing consensus on Ali Khamenei's successor amid internal power struggles.
  • The leadership change raises regional security concerns with Israel threatening the new leader and US interest in the outcome.
  • The succession process is opaque and contested, risking instability and significant shifts in Iran's policies.

Following the death or imminent passing of Supreme Leader Ali Khamenei, Iran's Assembly of Experts is reportedly nearing or has reached a consensus on his successor, with some sources indicating the son of Khamenei as a leading candidate. However, the selection process is marked by internal power struggles among clerical factions and the Revolutionary Guard, reflecting deep political complexities. The transition occurs amid heightened regional instability, with Israel vowing to target the new leader and the US expressing interest in influencing the outcome. Analysts warn of potential chaos, shifts in regional power balances, and the possibility of intensified conflict depending on the successor's policies. The situation remains fluid, with international observers closely monitoring Iran's internal dynamics and the broader Middle East implications.
