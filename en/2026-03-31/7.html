China's Strategic Public Diplomacy and Regional Influence

China Public Diplomacy 2026-03-31 • 13 articles in total

In brief
  • China uses public diplomacy, cultural exchanges, and influencer engagement to boost its global image and soft power.
  • China pursues strategic diplomatic outreach to Taiwan's opposition and expands regional influence via North Korea flights and Central American ties.
  • These actions cause diplomatic friction, sovereignty concerns, and shifts in regional power dynamics.

China is actively employing public diplomacy tools such as influencer engagement, cultural exchanges, and infrastructure showcases to enhance its global image and soft power. Its diplomatic outreach includes inviting Taiwan's opposition leaders to visit Beijing, signaling attempts to influence cross-strait relations amid political sensitivities. Regionally, China is expanding its geopolitical footprint through resumed flights to North Korea, maritime activities near disputed territories, and growing economic ties in Central America, which challenge existing power dynamics. These efforts are met with mixed reactions, including diplomatic friction with Japan and concerns over sovereignty in partner countries. Overall, China's multifaceted approach reflects a strategic blend of soft power and geopolitical maneuvers to shape international perceptions and regional alignments.
