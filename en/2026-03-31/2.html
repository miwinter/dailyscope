US–Iran Diplomacy: Tense Negotiations Amid Threats and Mediation Efforts

US–Iran Diplomacy 2026-03-31 • 62 articles in total

In brief
  • The US maintains a hardline stance threatening Iran's critical infrastructure if no deal is reached, signaling a readiness for military escalation.
  • Diplomatic efforts continue with Pakistan mediating and some US officials cautiously optimistic despite Iran's denial of direct talks and rejection of US proposals.
  • Regional and international actors monitor the volatile situation, noting the complex geopolitical impact and calls for de-escalation.

The US–Iran diplomatic landscape is marked by a paradox of ongoing talks and severe threats. President Trump has issued multiple warnings to Iran, threatening destruction of critical infrastructure such as oil fields, Kharg Island, and energy resources if no deal is reached soon, reflecting a hardline stance. Simultaneously, there are reports of direct and indirect negotiations, with Pakistan playing a mediating role and some US officials expressing cautious optimism about progress. Iran, however, officially denies direct talks and dismisses US peace proposals as one-sided, maintaining a firm position against US demands. The situation remains volatile with regional and international actors closely monitoring potential escalations and diplomatic breakthroughs, including debates over the strategic Strait of Hormuz and the possibility of ending hostilities without reopening it.
