States Assert AI Sovereignty in Global Diplomacy

AI Sovereignty Diplomacy 2026-06-18 • 10 articles in total

In brief
  • Europe pushes for AI sovereignty by enforcing EU standards, reducing U.S. tech dependence, and attracting AI companies.
  • The U.S. combines tighter AI regulations with diplomatic engagement, avoiding binding international commitments.
  • Emerging economies build AI capacity to achieve regional technology leadership while ethical voices demand stricter AI harm prevention measures.

Governments and institutions worldwide are pushing for sovereign access, governance, and limits on AI, turning technological control into a diplomatic priority. Europe is especially active: regulators press member states to meet EU AI standards, French agencies are distancing themselves from U.S. vendors, and firms like Anthropic are shifting activity toward Europe. The United States is combining regulatory tightening—such as curbs on deepfakes—with high-level engagement with AI companies, signaling pragmatic diplomacy without binding commitments. Moral and consumer-protection voices, from the Pope to Japan’s regulators, call for disarmament of dangerous systems and stronger measures against AI-enabled fraud, while countries like Mexico pursue capacity-building to become regional AI hubs.

Countries covering this topic

European strategic autonomy and regulation

European actors emphasize reducing dependence on U.S. tech and building sovereign capabilities while enforcing EU AI standards. This view combines regulatory pressure, withdrawal from foreign platforms and efforts to attract AI companies to Europe as part of strategic digital autonomy.

U.S. regulation and industry engagement

U.S. policy mixes tighter domestic regulation—such as deepfake restrictions—with diplomatic outreach and talks with private AI firms, reflecting a balance between security concerns and maintaining industry engagement. Statements by leaders indicate willingness to negotiate but stop short of binding international commitments.

  • Trump hints at positive negotiations with 'Anthropic' CEO at G7 summit
  • United States restricts deepfake technology

Global ethics and anti-abuse measures

Religious and regulatory voices stress ethical constraints and prevention of harm, urging disarmament of dangerous AI capabilities and tougher action against AI-enabled scams. These perspectives call for coordinated international norms to protect dignity, privacy and consumers from misuse.

  • Japan calls for crackdown on AI scams
  • The Pope calls for disarming AI to prevent it from dominating humans

Capacity-building in emerging regions

Emerging economies prioritize developing domestic AI ecosystems to secure technological sovereignty through investment and infrastructure, aiming to become regional hubs. This approach focuses on capability growth rather than solely on regulation or decoupling.
