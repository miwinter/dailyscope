French Defense and Technology Policy

France is actively enhancing its defense capabilities and asserting strategic autonomy in technology. This includes a major EU loan to fund arms purchases, acquisition of European-developed drone interception systems, and a move away from reliance on U.S. tech firms like Palantir due to concerns over digital sovereignty. The country also hosted Eurosatory 2022, showcasing advanced military technologies, and took controversial steps such as closing Israeli stands at a defense expo, reflecting complex defense and geopolitical considerations.

Within France, political and social dynamics remain active and sometimes contentious. Regional and International Relations Beyond Europe

France is recalibrating its regional engagements, notably in Africa, as seen in Air France's permanent withdrawal from Mali, signaling shifts in its Sahel strategy. Cultural diplomacy is also active, with Guinea seeking restitution of historic artifacts from France. Security cooperation extends to new agreements with the UK on policing, and France is involved in global initiatives such as the G7's efforts to reduce dependence on critical raw materials, reflecting a broad approach to international partnerships and challenges.
