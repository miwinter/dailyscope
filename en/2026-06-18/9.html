Deaths of Foreign Nationals Abroad: Cameroonian Fatality in the US and Other Cross‑Border Incidents

Cameroon-US Death 2026-06-18 • 3 articles in total

In brief
  • A Cameroonian woman died in Maryland, a UK national died in a marine collision near Barbados, and a Honduran trucker died in Guatemala.
  • Local authorities are investigating all cases but offer limited details.
  • These deaths highlight risks faced by citizens abroad and the need for investigations and consular support.

A Cameroonian national, Njoya Kheo Laura, has been reported dead in Maryland, United States, according to Cameroonian media. Separate reports describe a UK national killed in a marine collision off Holetown, Barbados, and a Honduran trucker, Jimmy Martínez, who died in Guatemala, highlighting multiple cases of nationals dying abroad. All three accounts note that local authorities are investigating but provide limited public details about causes or circumstances. Together they underscore the cross‑border risks faced by citizens abroad and the need for thorough inquiries and consular support for affected families.
