Iran war squeezes routes and fuels global energy realignment

Global Energy Geopolitics 2026-04-26 • 33 articles in total

In brief
  • Iran conflict causes oil and jet-fuel price spikes and exposes vulnerabilities in key chokepoints like the Strait of Hormuz.
  • Western and regional actors respond with sanctions, alternative routes, maritime security, stockpiles, and emergency fuel measures.
  • Russia expands energy partnerships and nuclear plans while global efforts push for critical mineral supply diversification and stronger mining regulations.

The Iran-related conflict is driving oil and jet-fuel price spikes and exposing chokepoint vulnerabilities from the Strait of Hormuz to major ports, prompting market volatility and policy responses. Western actors are tightening sanctions and exploring alternative energy corridors and infrastructure to reduce exposure, while some governments consider emergency measures such as fuel rationing. Airlines and travel markets are already cutting capacity and warning of higher fares as jet-fuel shortages spread, and regional actors from Southeast Asia to Africa are bolstering maritime security and stockpiles to protect supply chains. Concurrently, Russia is advancing resource plays and partnerships, nuclear safety debates and new reactor plans are resurfacing, and efforts to diversify critical-minerals supplies and regulate new extraction technologies are gaining momentum.
