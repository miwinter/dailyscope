Evacuation and shooting at White House Correspondents' Dinner

US Presidential Security Incident 2026-04-26 • 115 articles in total

In brief
  • Gunfire at the White House Correspondents' Dinner led to the Secret Service evacuating President Trump and attendees, with no fatalities reported.
  • Authorities detained a suspect and an agent was wounded; FBI and other investigators are reviewing the case and security measures.
  • The incident prompted widespread visual coverage, historical comparisons, and calls for security reviews and potential charges.

Gunfire at the White House Correspondents' Dinner prompted a rapid Secret Service evacuation of President Donald Trump, the First Lady and other attendees; officials across outlets report the president was unharmed and praised protective agents. Authorities detained a suspect at the scene and initial accounts say an agent was wounded but survived, while investigators — including the FBI in some reports — are reviewing footage, weapons and motives. International coverage split between immediate reassurances and intense visual documentation, with many outlets publishing videos and photos of the moments of chaos and evacuation. The incident also revived historical comparisons to the Washington Hilton shooting of 1981 and prompted renewed scrutiny of venue security and checkpoint procedures as officials promise fuller reviews and potential charges.
