Shooting at White House Correspondents' Dinner: evacuation, suspect in custody

US Security: WHCD Shooting 2026-04-26 • 24 articles in total

In brief
  • A heavily armed suspect was detained after a shooting incident near the White House Correspondents' Dinner, prompting evacuation of President Trump and attendees.
  • President Trump and other VIPs were unharmed, with law enforcement responding rapidly amid reports of a serious security breach.
  • The incident caused panic among attendees and led to international condemnation and questions about security at political events.

A shooting incident at or near the White House Correspondents' Dinner prompted the evacuation of President Trump and other attendees and led to the detention of a suspect. Many reports describe the suspect as heavily armed — multiple guns, knives and a bulletproof vest — while officials and outlets largely reported no major civilian casualties though one security officer was said to be injured in an account. Coverage divides between immediate reassurance about the president's safety, live updates on the suspect’s identity and potential charges, and emotional eyewitness accounts from attendees and journalists. The episode has drawn international condemnation and renewed questions about security at high-profile political events in Washington.
