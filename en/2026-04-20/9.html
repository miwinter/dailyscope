China's diplomacy: soft power, trade and rising security tensions

China Asia Diplomacy 2026-04-20 • 13 articles in total

In brief
  • China uses economic statecraft, infrastructure, and cultural diplomacy to expand influence across Asia and the Pacific.
  • Chinese military actions and maritime disputes create regional tensions and international concern.
  • China's economic resilience supports its sustained regional expansion despite global geopolitical challenges.

Xi Jinping's China is projecting influence across Asia through a mix of economic statecraft, infrastructure and cultural diplomacy while also exercising hard-power measures that heighten regional tensions. State media and diplomatic events emphasize soft-power initiatives—cultural exchanges, commemorations and transport links—designed to deepen ties with neighbors from Laos and Vietnam to Tonga and Central Asia. Concurrently, Chinese military activity and maritime disputes fuel anxiety among nearby states such as Myanmar and the Philippines and prompt critical commentary from European outlets worried about growing Chinese sway. Beijing's actions are underpinned by notable economic resilience, allowing China to sustain and expand its regional footprint despite wider geopolitical shocks.
