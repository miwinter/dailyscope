International crackdown on transnational crime

Kinahan Arrests: International Crime 2026-04-18 • 11 articles in total

In brief
  • International law enforcement has intensified cross-border arrests and extraditions targeting organised crime, drug trafficking, and violent gangs.
  • Regional and Western authorities cooperate on prosecutions involving cartel leaders, political suspects, and transnational economic criminals.
  • Efforts face legal and diplomatic complexities including extradition challenges, evidence-sharing, and jurisdictional issues across countries.

A series of cross-border arrests and extraditions highlights intensified international cooperation against organised crime, from the high-profile detention of Daniel Kinahan in the UAE to US-led prosecutions of cartel and gang figures. Regional authorities across Latin America report arrests, deaths in capture attempts and extraditions that underline the transnational reach of drug trafficking and violent gangs. European and African outlets emphasise cooperation to pursue suspects abroad, whether for corruption, political offences or alleged extremist activity. Separately, law enforcement actions targeting scams and smuggling expose how transnational criminal methods exploit migration and porous borders. Together the reports show a multipolar enforcement push, with legal and diplomatic complexity around extradition, evidence-sharing and jurisdictional claims.
