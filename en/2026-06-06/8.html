Naval drone explodes near Constanța

Black Sea Drone Incident 2026-06-06 • 10 articles in total

In brief
  • A Ukrainian naval drone detonated near Romania's Constanța port, causing evacuations and security operations without injuries.
  • Romanian authorities investigated, increased port security, and used helicopters after detecting other drones and vessels.
  • Ukraine confirmed the drone, cited likely Russian electronic jamming for loss of control, and warned Romania before the explosion, amid rising Black Sea maritime risks.

A Ukrainian naval drone detonated near the Romanian port of Constanța, triggering local evacuations and security operations though authorities reported no injuries. Romanian officials opened an investigation, reviewed port security, and deployed helicopters to survey the area after detecting other drones and nearby vessels. Ukraine acknowledged the drone as its asset and said Russian electronic jamming likely caused the loss of control, and Kyiv reportedly warned Romania minutes before the explosion. The incident fits a pattern of drones losing navigation in the Black Sea, raising broader concerns about maritime safety and cross‑border escalation amid the war in Ukraine.
