ISS air leak and Russian-module crack trigger evacuations and strain partners

Space: ISS Crack 2026-06-06 • 11 articles in total

In brief
  • An air leak and crack in the Russian ISS segment led to crew sheltering in a SpaceX Dragon capsule and temporary suspension of operations.
  • Repairs and safety checks were conducted with no injuries, but the Zvezda module's long-term reliability is in question due to persistent cracks.
  • The incident revealed operational tensions between NASA and Roscosmos over repair responsibilities despite coordinated emergency procedures.

An air leak and an apparent crack in the Russian segment of the International Space Station prompted urgent safety measures and contingency planning. NASA ordered crew to shelter in a SpaceX Dragon capsule and prepared for possible evacuation while ground teams from mission control and Roscosmos conducted repairs and safety checks. There were no injuries reported and operations were temporarily suspended before being resumed after reassessment. Reports that the Zvezda transfer tunnel has persistent cracks and only limited mitigation by Roscosmos have raised concerns about the module's long-term reliability. The episode highlighted both routine international coordination under emergency protocols and emerging tensions between partners over repair plans.
