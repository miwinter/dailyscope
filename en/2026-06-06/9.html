Peru runoff: tight race and polarized campaign

Peru Runoff Polls 2026-06-06 • 8 articles in total

In brief
  • Peru's presidential runoff is extremely close and polarized, with last-minute voter shifts between Keiko Fujimori and Roberto Sánchez.
  • Campaigns focus on security, corruption, and the economy amid intense mobilization and a divided electorate.
  • The race highlights Fujimori's enduring political influence and Sánchez's confidence amid high political stakes, including congressional implications.

Peru’s presidential runoff between Keiko Fujimori and leftist Roberto Sánchez is shaping up as an extremely close and polarized contest, with late polls alternating between a Sanchez momentum and an essentially tied race. Polling firms and analysts report shifts in voter preferences and a shrinking share of blank votes, signaling last-minute movement that could decide the outcome. Campaign closings focused on security, corruption and the economy, underscoring a deeply divided electorate and intense mobilization in Lima and beyond. Some coverage highlights the Fujimori family’s enduring influence and identity politics, while other reports note Sánchez’s public confidence amid high political stakes including congressional manoeuvres.
