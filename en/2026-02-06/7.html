Cuba's Conditional Readiness for Dialogue with the US Amid Tensions

Cuba–US Dialogue 2026-02-06 • 15 articles in total

In brief
  • Cuba is willing to talk with the US without conditions and insists on preserving its sovereignty.
  • The US acknowledges ongoing diplomatic talks and urges restraint while maintaining key policy positions.
  • Both nations cautiously seek better relations despite historical tensions and external pressures.

Cuba has expressed a consistent willingness to engage in dialogue with the United States, emphasizing that such talks must occur without pressure, preconditions, or interference. Cuban leadership, including President Miguel Díaz-Canel, insists on maintaining sovereignty and rejects any negotiations aimed at regime change. The US, including the White House, acknowledges ongoing diplomatic discussions and calls for restraint from both sides. Despite longstanding tensions and external pressures, particularly under President Trump, both nations appear cautiously optimistic about improving bilateral relations. Cuba's approach balances openness to dialogue with firm adherence to its ideological principles and national dignity.

Cuban Government's Stance on Dialogue

The Cuban government, led by President Miguel Díaz-Canel, consistently expresses readiness to engage in talks with the US but firmly rejects any form of pressure, preconditions, or regime change demands. Cuba emphasizes sovereignty, respect, and equal footing in negotiations, reflecting a cautious but open approach to diplomacy while maintaining its revolutionary principles.

The US acknowledges Cuba's willingness to dialogue but remains firm on certain policy positions, reflecting a strategic approach to managing the historically complex bilateral relationship.
