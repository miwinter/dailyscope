Mixed Progress in US-Russia-Ukraine Ceasefire Talks in Abu Dhabi

US–Russia–Ukraine Ceasefire Talks 2026-02-06 • 12 articles in total

In brief
  • A trilateral ceasefire negotiation in Abu Dhabi resumed military contacts and exchanged prisoners but failed to resolve territorial issues.
  • All parties agreed to continue talks in coming weeks, with future meetings likely in the United States.
  • The talks show cautious progress toward peace amid ongoing conflict and diplomatic challenges.

The trilateral ceasefire talks held in Abu Dhabi between the United States, Russia, and Ukraine have produced a cautiously optimistic atmosphere, with some progress reported such as the resumption of military communications and a prisoner exchange. However, critical issues like territorial concessions remain unresolved, leading to a stalemate on key points. All parties have agreed to continue negotiations in the coming weeks, with future meetings likely to be hosted in the United States. The talks reflect ongoing international efforts to mediate the conflict despite persistent hostilities and challenges. Overall, the dialogue marks a tentative step toward de-escalation but underscores the complexity of achieving a comprehensive peace agreement.
