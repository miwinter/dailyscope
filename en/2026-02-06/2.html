Diplomatic Efforts Amid High Tensions in US-Iran Nuclear Talks

Iran–US Nuclear and Crisis Diplomacy 2026-02-06 • 65 articles in total

In brief
  • The US and Iran will resume nuclear talks in Oman amid deep mistrust and agenda disputes.
  • The US seeks broader negotiations including missiles and regional security, while Iran wants to focus only on nuclear issues.
  • Oman and international actors mediate talks amid military posturing and regional instability fears.

The United States and Iran have agreed to resume nuclear negotiations in Oman, marking a critical diplomatic effort to address longstanding tensions and prevent military escalation. Both sides express cautious optimism, though deep mistrust and significant disagreements over the agenda and scope of talks persist. The US emphasizes a broader framework including regional security and missile programs, while Iran insists on focusing strictly on nuclear issues. Military posturing and regional instability continue to shadow the talks, with the US deploying naval forces and Iran demonstrating missile capabilities. International actors, including Oman and France, play key mediation roles, underscoring the high stakes and fragile nature of these negotiations.

US Perspective: Pressure and Readiness for Broader Negotiations

The United States approaches the talks with a firm stance, combining diplomatic engagement with military readiness. US officials, including President Trump and Secretary of State Rubio, emphasize readiness to negotiate but also warn of military options if diplomacy fails. The US seeks to expand the negotiation agenda beyond nuclear issues to include Iran's missile program and regional activities. Military deployments near Iran and stern warnings reflect a strategy of pressure to compel Iran toward compliance.

Iranian Perspective: Focus on Fair, Nuclear-Centered Talks and Sovereignty

Iran stresses the importance of fair and mutually acceptable negotiations focused primarily on nuclear issues, resisting US attempts to broaden the agenda. Iranian officials highlight the need for respect and equitable dialogue, while also demonstrating military strength as a deterrent. The Iranian government faces internal pressures, including nationwide protests, influencing its cautious but firm negotiation stance. Oman is viewed as a neutral and trusted venue facilitating these delicate talks.

International and Regional Mediation and Concerns

Regional and international actors, including Oman, France, and Gulf states, play crucial roles in mediating and facilitating US-Iran talks amid fears of escalation. Oman serves as a neutral venue and mediator, while France undertakes diplomatic tours to de-escalate tensions. The broader Middle East watches anxiously as these negotiations could impact regional stability, with concerns about potential military conflict and political deadlock in neighboring countries. The international community remains cautiously optimistic but aware of the fragile situation.
