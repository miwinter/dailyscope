Chile's Presidential Runoff: A Shift to the Far Right

Chile Presidential Runoff 2025-12-16 • 33 articles in total

In brief
  • José Antonio Kast won Chile's presidential runoff with 58%, signaling a major rightward shift since Pinochet's era.
  • Kast's conservative campaign focuses on immigration control, law enforcement, and economic growth amid a fragmented Congress.
  • His presidency reflects Latin America's conservative gains and draws global attention for its impact on Chile's politics and international relations.

José Antonio Kast, a far-right candidate with conservative and ultracatholic views, won Chile's presidential runoff with about 58% of the vote, marking the country's most significant rightward political shift since the end of the Pinochet dictatorship. His campaign focused on immigration control, law and order, and conservative reforms, stirring memories of Chile's authoritarian past and raising concerns about future domestic and international policies. The election outcome reflects broader regional trends of conservative gains in Latin America and has drawn international attention and congratulations from various world leaders. Kast's transition period involves dialogue with outgoing president Gabriel Boric, with expectations of a moderate approach to governance despite his far-right platform. However, Kast faces challenges including a fragmented Congress and the need to address national security and economic issues amid a polarized political climate.
