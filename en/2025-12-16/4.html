In brief
  • A mass shooting at Bondi Beach targeted a Jewish holiday event, killing 15 to 16 people and injuring many.
  • The attack was carried out by a father and son inspired by Islamic State ideology with foreign military training.
  • The incident is condemned as antisemitic terrorism, prompting national mourning, security measures, gun law reforms, and ongoing investigations.

A mass shooting at Bondi Beach in Sydney, Australia, targeted a Jewish holiday event, resulting in 15 to 16 deaths and numerous injuries. The attack was carried out by a father and son duo, who were reportedly inspired by Islamic State ideology and had undergone military training abroad. The incident has been widely condemned as an antisemitic terrorist act, prompting national mourning, heightened security measures, and calls for gun law reforms. Eyewitness accounts highlight both the horror and acts of heroism during the attack, while international leaders and communities express solidarity with the victims. Investigations continue into the motives, background, and connections of the perpetrators as Australia grapples with the implications of rising extremism and domestic terrorism.
