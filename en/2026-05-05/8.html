Kremlin Tightens Security Amid Coup and Assassination Fears

Russia Elite Coup Risk 2026-05-05 • 9 articles in total

In brief
  • Security around Putin has tightened with bunkers, stricter staff vetting, defenses, and surveillance due to coup and assassination fears.
  • Internal rivalries, especially involving Defense Minister Shoigu, fuel speculation about elite dissent but no active coup plots are confirmed.
  • Analysts debate the credibility of elite dissent and see coup risks as possible scenarios, not imminent threats.

Multiple intelligence reports and foreign press accounts describe a marked tightening of security around Vladimir Putin, citing bunkers, stricter vetting of staff, perimeter defenses and heightened surveillance. Sources warn of dual threats: elite-led coup attempts and targeted assassinations, including concerns about drones, but none of the pieces present confirmed active plots. Lithuanian reporting highlights internal rivalry, singling out Defense Minister Sergei Shoigu as a focal point of speculation and intelligence interest. At the same time, analysts question how credible or cohesive elite dissent is, debating whether it could translate into an organized challenge to Putin’s rule. Overall, the coverage portrays a leadership on edge and preparing for contingencies rather than an imminent, externally verifiable overthrow.

Intelligence-led security warnings

Western and regional intelligence assessments emphasize concrete protective measures around Putin—bunkers, tighter controls on entourage, increased perimeter security and surveillance—and flag both assassination (including drone) and coup risks. These reports portray a Kremlin responding defensively to perceived instability but do not provide evidence of an active, successful plot against the president.
