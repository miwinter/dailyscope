Bilateral Deals: Transport, Security and Strategic Partnerships Multiply

Region: Bilateral Deals 2026-05-05 • 124 articles in total

In brief
  • Governments worldwide are advancing bilateral diplomacy through defense, economic pacts, transport links, and citizen facilitation schemes.
  • Major powers deepen economic and security ties in regions including Africa, Indo-Pacific, the Gulf, and Europe amid complex political, legal, and security dynamics.
  • Multiple countries engage in trade, technology, cultural, and strategic cooperation to enhance connectivity, resource security, and diplomatic relations.

This set of reports shows a burst of bilateral diplomacy across regions, with governments signing defense and economic pacts, reopening transport links, and launching facilitation schemes for citizens and pilgrims. China and other major powers are deepening economic ties in Africa through tariff moves, shipping links and trade promotion, while Indo-Pacific partners formalize defense and critical-minerals cooperation. The Gulf and OIC actors expand technical and logistical initiatives (including Mecca Route services) alongside security coordination, and the United States remains active in arms, financial and diplomatic gestures across multiple theatres. Latin American normalization steps and a cluster of high-profile extradition and legal requests highlight cross-border legal and political friction alongside rapprochement moves. Finally, Russia and partners continue security-oriented cooperation even as ceasefire announcements and Ukraine’s intensified outreach shape European and regional diplomacy.
