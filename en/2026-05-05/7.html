Deadly fireworks factory blast in China prompts probe

China Fireworks Explosion 2026-05-05 • 19 articles in total

In brief
  • A fireworks factory explosion in Hunan, China killed at least 21 people and injured dozens.
  • President Xi Jinping ordered a thorough investigation and some managers were detained.
  • Emergency teams conducted rescue operations amid safety concerns and evacuation reports.

A massive explosion at a fireworks factory in China’s Hunan province killed at least 21 people and injured dozens, with many reports citing about 61 injured. Large search-and-rescue operations were deployed and emergency teams worked to treat victims and secure the site. Chinese state media and several outlets reported that President Xi Jinping ordered a thorough investigation, with some accounts saying managers were detained. Coverage highlights longstanding safety concerns in the pyrotechnics and industrial sectors and signals possible accountability and tighter safety measures. A few reports note differing casualty figures and broader evacuations, underscoring uncertainty as investigations continue.
