Divergent International Views on Venezuela at the UN Security Council

UN Security Council Venezuela Debate 2026-01-06 • 12 articles in total

In brief
  • The United States and its allies reject Maduro's legitimacy and support intervention to restore Venezuelan democracy.
  • Russia and Latin American countries oppose foreign interference, emphasizing sovereignty and international law.
  • The UN urges respect for Venezuela's independence and diplomatic solutions, reflecting global divisions and deadlock.

The United Nations Security Council debates on Venezuela reveal a sharp divide among member states regarding the legitimacy of foreign intervention and the political status of Nicolás Maduro. The United States and its allies assert Maduro's illegitimacy and defend interventionist policies, while countries like Russia, Mexico, Brazil, and Colombia condemn such actions as violations of sovereignty and international law. The UN Secretary-General and other members emphasize respect for Venezuela's political independence and adherence to the UN Charter, urging diplomatic solutions. Despite urgent meetings and strong rhetoric, the Security Council remains deadlocked, reflecting broader geopolitical tensions and the complexity of the Venezuelan crisis. This impasse underscores the challenges in achieving multilateral consensus on Venezuela's future and regional stability.

Countries covering this topic

US and Allies Assert Maduro's Illegitimacy and Support Intervention

The United States and its allied countries maintain that Nicolás Maduro is not the legitimate president of Venezuela and justify their interventionist stance as necessary to restore democracy and stability. They emphasize the need to oppose Maduro's regime and support the Venezuelan opposition, framing their actions as aligned with international law and democratic principles. This viewpoint highlights a proactive approach to influence Venezuela's political future.

Latin American Countries Condemn US Intervention and Defend Sovereignty

Mexico, Brazil, and Colombia strongly criticize the United States for its aggressive actions in Venezuela, viewing them as violations of national sovereignty and threats to regional stability. They warn against undermining multilateralism and emphasize respect for international law and non-interference. This perspective reflects concerns about external powers destabilizing Latin America and advocates for diplomatic solutions respecting state independence.

  • Mexico, Brazil and Colombia Condemn US Attack on Venezuela at UN; Security Council Takes No Action

Russia and Allies Demand Respect for Maduro and International Law

Russia and allied countries call for the release of Nicolás Maduro and condemn US operations as breaches of international law. They stress the importance of respecting Venezuela's sovereignty and criticize unilateral actions that escalate tensions. This stance supports Maduro's regime and opposes foreign interference, framing the conflict as a violation of the UN Charter and international norms.

  • Russia at the Security Council: Release Maduro
  • The US and Venezuela at the United Nations: Broad Condemnation of US Actions

UN Officials and Other Members Urge Diplomacy and Legal Adherence

The UN Secretary-General and various Security Council members emphasize the need to respect Venezuela's political independence and adhere strictly to the UN Charter principles, including sovereignty and non-interference. They call for diplomatic engagement and caution against external political pressures that could worsen the crisis. This viewpoint seeks to balance concerns over human rights and political turmoil with respect for international law and multilateral dialogue.
