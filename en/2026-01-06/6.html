Political Turmoil and Leadership Shift in Venezuela

Venezuela Interim Presidency 2026-01-06 • 19 articles in total

In brief
  • Venezuela's political crisis escalated with U.S. forces capturing President Maduro and appointing Delcy Rodríguez as interim president, recognized by key national institutions.
  • The U.S. and international community debate the legitimacy and implications of Rodríguez's presidency amid concerns over sovereignty, intervention, and political stability.
  • Opposition groups dispute Rodríguez's legitimacy, highlighting ongoing protests, political polarization, and calls for genuine democratic progress.

Venezuela is undergoing a profound political crisis marked by the capture of President Nicolás Maduro by U.S. forces and the subsequent appointment of Delcy Rodríguez as interim president. This leadership change has been recognized by Venezuela's Supreme Court, military, and parliament, but remains contested by Maduro supporters and opposition figures claiming legitimacy. The United States plays a significant role, with debates over intervention motives and strategies for Venezuela's future governance. International reactions vary, with some emphasizing the need for transition and others warning of escalating tensions and instability. The situation remains fluid, with ongoing protests, security challenges, and diplomatic efforts shaping Venezuela's political landscape.
