US-Led Second Phase of Gaza Peace Plan Focuses on Governance, Disarmament, and Stabilization

US–Gaza Peace and Ceasefire Efforts 2026-01-15 • 50 articles in total

In brief
  • The US has launched the second phase of its Gaza peace plan focused on technocratic governance, Hamas disarmament, and regional stabilization.
  • Palestinian authorities and regional mediators support the plan while navigating complex political and humanitarian challenges.
  • The situation remains fragile with ongoing violence, incomplete ceasefires, and serious humanitarian concerns despite diplomatic efforts.

The United States has initiated the second phase of its Gaza peace plan, emphasizing the establishment of a technocratic Palestinian governing committee and the disarmament of Hamas. This phase aims to stabilize the region through political restructuring, ceasefire reinforcement, and humanitarian aid facilitation. Various international actors, including Palestinian authorities and mediators from Egypt and Qatar, have expressed support for these efforts, though challenges such as ongoing violence and political complexities persist. The plan also involves reconstruction initiatives and attempts to create a transitional authority to manage Gaza's administration. Despite cautious optimism, the situation remains fragile with continued hostilities and humanitarian concerns.
