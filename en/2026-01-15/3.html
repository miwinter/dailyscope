US Suspends Immigrant Visa Processing for 75 Countries Amid Security and Policy Concerns

US Immigrant Visa Freeze 2026-01-15 • 56 articles in total

In brief
  • The US indefinitely suspended immigrant visa processing for nationals of 75 countries to enhance security and enforce economic self-sufficiency.
  • The suspension affects permanent residency and family reunification visas but exempts tourist, student, and temporary work visas.
  • The policy disrupts international mobility and diplomatic relations, causing uncertainty among affected countries and immigrant communities.

The United States has indefinitely suspended immigrant visa processing for nationals from 75 countries, citing concerns over security, administrative procedures, and the economic self-sufficiency of immigrants. This suspension primarily affects immigrant visas related to permanent residency and family reunification, while tourist and temporary visas remain largely unaffected. The policy impacts a diverse group of countries across Africa, Asia, Latin America, the Middle East, and Eastern Europe, including nations such as Brazil, Russia, Iraq, and Jamaica. The move has generated significant diplomatic concerns and uncertainty among affected countries and immigrant communities. The rationale behind the freeze includes tightening immigration controls and addressing public assistance usage, though specific details and timelines remain unclear.

US Government Emphasizes Security and Administrative Reasons

US officials and spokespersons justify the visa suspension as a necessary measure to enhance security, improve immigration screening, and address concerns about immigrants' economic self-sufficiency. The policy is framed as part of a broader immigration overhaul aimed at tightening controls and ensuring that immigrants do not become public assistance burdens. However, details on the duration and specific criteria remain limited.
