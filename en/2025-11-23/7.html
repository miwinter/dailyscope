G20 Summit Declaration: Consensus Amid US Boycott and Divergent Views on Ukraine

2025-11-23 • 16 articles in total

The G20 summit in Johannesburg concluded with a joint declaration addressing global economic recovery, climate change, and international security, despite a notable boycott and opposition from the United States. Most member countries emphasized multilateral cooperation and reaffirmed commitments to peace, particularly urging resolution of the Ukraine conflict through diplomatic means without explicitly naming Russia. Argentina also diverged by refusing to sign the declaration, highlighting some fractures within the group. The summit underscored ongoing geopolitical tensions but demonstrated a collective effort by participating nations to advance global stability and cooperation. Calls from international leaders, including the UN Secretary-General, stressed the G20's critical role in leading on peace, climate action, and economic reform.
