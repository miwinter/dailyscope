Escalation, energy strain and political pressure in the Russia–Ukraine war

Russo-Ukrainian War 2026-06-29 • 72 articles in total

In brief
  • Ukraine conducts long-range missile and drone strikes on Russian military and energy infrastructure, causing shortages and fires.
  • Putin vows to continue military operations and strengthen defenses despite domestic strains and elite unease in Russia.
  • NATO and regional partners increase readiness and support Ukraine amid ongoing humanitarian crises and calls for accountability.

Across these reports, Ukraine has sustained and publicized a campaign of long-range and drone strikes against Russian military and energy infrastructure, prompting large fires and acknowledged fuel shortages inside Russia. Vladimir Putin frames the situation as hardship to be endured, rejects proposals to halt strikes, and vows to press on with front-line operations, while promising domestic measures to secure fuel supplies and strengthen air defenses. Coverage also highlights internal strains in Russia — mutiny rhetoric, elite unease and high-level criticism — that complicate Kremlin messaging. NATO and regional partners are raising readiness, discussing deterrence and pledging reconstruction aid for Ukraine, even as the humanitarian toll and allegations of abuses and civilian casualties continue to mount.
