Senegal government shake-up after Sonko's dismissal

Senegal Government Shakeup 2026-05-24 • 16 articles in total

In brief
  • President Bassirou Diomaye Faye dismissed Prime Minister Ousmane Sonko and dissolved the government, sparking uncertainty and unrest.
  • Sonko, his supporters, and PASTEF responded with defiance, internal directives, and reaffirmed political commitments.
  • Editorials view the dismissal as a turning point with major implications for Senegal’s governance and power balance.

President Bassirou Diomaye Faye dismissed Prime Minister Ousmane Sonko and dissolved the government, prompting immediate uncertainty about the country’s leadership and policy direction. International and regional outlets report unrest and protests, and analysts warn the move could reshape governance and parliamentary dynamics. Sonko and his supporters publicly reacted with defiance and calm rhetoric, while PASTEF issued internal directives and reiterated its political commitments. Editorial commentary frames the decree as a possible institutional turning point with implications for legitimacy and the balance of power.
