Bilateral Deals: trade, security, and diplomatic repositioning

Region: Bilateral Deals 2026-05-24 • 104 articles in total

In brief
  • Bilateral activity includes strategic partnerships, economic pacts, security cooperation, environmental agreements, and routine diplomatic credentialing.
  • China plays a central role in infrastructure, trade, political outreach, and security cooperation with various countries.
  • Diplomatic tensions arise alongside cooperation, shown by bans, expulsions, and contested sovereignty issues among states.

Recent bilateral activity spans strategic partnerships, economic pacts, security cooperation and diplomatic signalling. China features prominently as a partner in infrastructure, trade and political outreach, while a range of countries signed investment or business-agenda accords to spur growth and connectivity. Security and defence ties—active operations, joint exercises, and paused arms transfers—are reshaping regional calculations alongside counterterrorism and maritime discussions. Environmental and fisheries agreements and numerous diplomatic credentialings reflect routine but consequential steps to solidify ties and regional cooperation. Simultaneously, high-profile diplomatic frictions and gestures—entry bans, expulsions and rights-of-pass actions—underscore tensions that accompany these expanding bilateral networks.
