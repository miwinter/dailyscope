Dick Cheney's Death Marks End of Controversial Political Era

2025-11-05 • 35 articles in total

Dick Cheney, the influential former Vice President of the United States under George W. Bush, has died at the age of 84. He was widely recognized as a powerful and pivotal figure in American politics, particularly known for his advocacy of the 2003 Iraq invasion and his role in shaping the U.S. response to the 9/11 attacks and the subsequent War on Terror. His death has been noted internationally as the end of a significant and controversial era in U.S. foreign and domestic policy. Tributes and reflections underscore his lasting impact on U.S. conservatism, military strategy, and global politics, while controversies over his legacy remain prominent. Family announcements and official reactions have been reported worldwide.
