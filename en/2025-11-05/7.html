Longest US Government Shutdown Impacts Flights, Aid, and Governance

2025-11-05 • 23 articles in total

The United States government shutdown has reached 36 days, making it the longest in history and surpassing the 2019 record. This stalemate between Republicans and Democrats disrupts federal services, causing widespread impacts, especially in air travel where millions of passengers face delays and cancellations. Transportation officials warn of potential airspace closures and staffing shortages that could escalate into a collapse of aviation operations. The shutdown also threatens critical food assistance programs in US territories and mainland, endangering vulnerable populations. Political deadlock persists with no clear resolution in sight, raising economic, social, and humanitarian concerns both nationally and internationally.
