Renewed US-Venezuela Diplomatic Engagement Amid Political Transition Efforts

US–Venezuela Diplomatic Talks and Envoy Appointment 2026-02-01 • 24 articles in total

In brief
  • The US reestablished diplomatic relations with Venezuela by appointing Laura Dogu to reopen the mission in Caracas.
  • The Venezuelan opposition supports a democratic transition and international cooperation.
  • President Trump proposes uniting chavismo and the opposition to stabilize Venezuela without a clear timeline.

The United States has taken significant steps to restore diplomatic relations with Venezuela after years of estrangement, marked by the arrival of Laura Dogu as the new chargé d'affaires to reopen the US diplomatic mission in Caracas. This move signals a cautious thaw and a potential new chapter in bilateral ties, with both countries engaging in dialogue to address political and economic challenges. The Venezuelan opposition has expressed readiness to support a democratic transition, while US President Trump has proposed uniting chavismo and opposition factions to facilitate political stability. Despite these developments, timelines for Venezuela's democratic transition remain unspecified, reflecting ongoing uncertainties. Overall, the diplomatic efforts aim to reduce tensions, enhance cooperation, and contribute to regional stability amid Venezuela's complex political landscape.
