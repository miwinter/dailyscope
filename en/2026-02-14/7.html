Cuba's Deepening Energy Crisis Amid Sanctions and Infrastructure Failures

Cuba Energy Crisis 2026-02-14 • 14 articles in total

In brief
  • Cuba faces an energy crisis causing power outages, fuel shortages, and economic disruptions affecting daily life and key sectors.
  • The crisis worsens due to the U.S. embargo, halted Venezuelan oil support, and geopolitical tensions involving Russia and Cuban exiles.
  • International flights to Cuba are disrupted amid rescue efforts and sanctions debates, deepening Cuba's isolation and humanitarian challenges.

Cuba is experiencing a severe energy crisis marked by fuel shortages, refinery fires, and widespread power outages, severely impacting daily life, transportation, and key sectors like tourism and sports. The crisis is exacerbated by the U.S. embargo restricting oil supplies and recent disruptions in Venezuelan support, leading to international flight suspensions and economic instability. Various countries highlight different facets: Russia's involvement in rescue flights contrasts with its airlines suspending services, while Cuban exiles in the U.S. push for stricter sanctions alongside humanitarian aid. The situation has prompted institutional disruptions, travel warnings, and concerns over a growing humanitarian catastrophe. Overall, Cuba faces isolation and mounting challenges amid geopolitical tensions and internal infrastructural weaknesses.
