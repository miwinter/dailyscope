France Advances Naval Power with New Aircraft Carrier Project

France Aircraft Carrier Construction 2025-12-22 • 18 articles in total

In brief
  • France plans to build a larger, advanced nuclear-powered aircraft carrier by 2038 to replace the Charles de Gaulle.
  • The new carrier will boost France's naval modernization and reinforce its global military presence and strategic autonomy.
  • It symbolizes national power and aims to address evolving international security challenges, especially in Europe and the Middle East.

France has announced plans to build a new, larger, and more advanced nuclear-powered aircraft carrier to replace the aging Charles de Gaulle, with an expected service entry around 2038. This initiative is part of a broader effort to modernize French naval capabilities and strengthen its global military presence, reflecting France's commitment to maintaining strategic autonomy and influence, particularly in Europe and the Middle East. The new carrier will feature state-of-the-art technology, including the capacity to carry drones, and represents the largest warship construction in Europe. Announcements have been made in various international contexts, underscoring France's intent to project power and respond to evolving security challenges worldwide. The project is seen as a significant investment in defense infrastructure and a symbol of national power.
