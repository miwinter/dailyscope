Tight presidential race amid fragmented field and democratic concerns

Peru Presidential Vote 2026-04-12 • 8 articles in total

In brief
  • The Peru presidential race is very close between Ricardo Belmont and Keiko Fujimori amid over 30 candidates and voter fatigue.
  • The crowded ballot and polarized politics risk post-election instability and challenges in coalition-building.
  • Overseas voting by 1.2 million Peruvians adds complexity and influence to the election outcome.

Peru heads to the April 12 presidential vote in a surprisingly tight contest, with polls showing a near tie between Ricardo Belmont and Keiko Fujimori. The race unfolds against a crowded ballot of more than 30 candidates and widespread voter fatigue, raising questions about post-election governance and coalition-building. Commentators and analysts warn the outcome is pivotal for democratic stability, highlighting populist pressures and the risk of authoritarian tendencies from both right and left. Overseas voting by about 1.2 million Peruvians adds an influential and logistically complex element to the final result.
