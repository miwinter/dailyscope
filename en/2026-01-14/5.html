Escalating US-Venezuela Conflict: Military Actions, Political Claims, and Global Reactions

US–Venezuela Military and Political Tensions 2026-01-14 • 39 articles in total

In brief
  • The US captured Venezuelan President Maduro and increased control over Venezuela's oil, claiming to restore democracy amid international controversy.
  • Venezuela and its allies condemn US actions as illegal aggression violating sovereignty and call for resistance and Maduro's release.
  • Regional and global actors urge calm and dialogue, reflecting diverse public and geopolitical views amid a polarized and volatile crisis.

The US-Venezuela relationship has sharply deteriorated following a US-led military operation that resulted in the capture of Venezuelan President Nicolás Maduro, provoking widespread international condemnation and regional instability. The US has intensified economic and military pressure, including seizing Venezuelan-linked oil tankers and asserting control over Venezuela's oil resources, while Venezuela and its allies denounce these actions as violations of sovereignty and international law. The capture and subsequent trial of Maduro in the US, alongside the release of American prisoners by Venezuela, illustrate complex diplomatic maneuvers amid ongoing tensions. Regional actors and global powers like Russia, China, and Caribbean nations express divergent views, ranging from calls for calm and neutrality to outright condemnation of US aggression. The situation remains volatile, with armed resistance within Venezuela and polarized public opinions both domestically and internationally.
