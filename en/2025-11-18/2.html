UN Security Council Approves US-Backed Gaza Peace Plan Amidst Broad Support and Palestinian Opposition

2025-11-18 • 68 articles in total

The United Nations Security Council adopted a resolution endorsing former US President Donald Trump's 20-point peace plan for Gaza, including the deployment of an international stabilization force intended to maintain ceasefire and regional security. The resolution saw broad support from Western and many Arab states, but notable abstentions from Russia and China, reflecting geopolitical divisions. Palestinian factions, including Hamas, strongly opposed the resolution, criticizing it as inadequate and an imposition of external control infringing on Palestinian sovereignty. The plan also entails the creation of a Peace Council, reportedly to be chaired by Trump, aiming to oversee reconstruction and stabilization efforts in Gaza. Implementation details remain uncertain, and the resolution highlights ongoing diplomatic efforts amid continued tension and conflict in the region.

Countries covering this topic

Support from Western and many Arab states

Countries including the USA, New Zealand, Italy, France, Germany, United Kingdom, and various Arab nations supported the US-drafted resolution, viewing it as a significant diplomatic advancement toward stabilizing Gaza. The resolution was praised as a historic moment by former President Trump and others, emphasizing the goals of ceasefire maintenance, humanitarian aid, and the deployment of an international peacekeeping force. Abstentions by Russia and China did not prevent adoption, and supportive states called for rapid implementation, seeing the measure as an essential step to de-escalate conflict and assist reconstruction efforts.

Opposition and criticism from Palestinian factions and Hamas

Hamas and other Palestinian groups rejected the US-backed resolution, denouncing it as failing to meet Palestinian rights and accusing it of imposing foreign domination and disregarding core issues like occupation and self-determination. They opposed disarmament and the presence of an international force, seeing the plan as legitimizing ongoing aggression and sidelining Palestinian sovereignty. This stance signals significant challenges to the resolution's acceptance and foreshadows difficulties in implementing peace on the ground.

Geopolitical reservations by Russia, China, and others

Russia and China abstained from the vote and voiced criticisms regarding the US-backed resolution, questioning its content, effectiveness, and implications. Russia highlighted the lack of a UN supervisory role in ceasefire monitoring, expressing broader concerns about enforcement and scope. Their positions illustrate geopolitical divides within the Security Council and complicate unified international approaches to the Gaza conflict.

Neutral or cautious perspectives emphasizing process and challenges

Several countries and sources reported on the vote and the plan without explicitly endorsing or opposing it but stressed the resolution's significance amid ongoing tensions. These viewpoints underscore the fragile ceasefire, the uncertain implementation, and the complex diplomatic environment surrounding Gaza. They note the need for cooperation by all parties and recognize that while the resolution is a step forward, substantial obstacles remain to achieving lasting peace.
