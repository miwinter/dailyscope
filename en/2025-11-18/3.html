Venezuela–US Tensions: Military Pressure and Potential Dialogue

2025-11-18 • 52 articles in total

Tensions between the United States and Venezuela have escalated with the US increasing military presence near Venezuelan waters, including deployment of the USS Gerald R. Ford and thousands of troops, signaling readiness for possible intervention. Former President Donald Trump publicly did not rule out military action while simultaneously expressing willingness to engage in face-to-face talks with Venezuelan President Nicolás Maduro, reflecting a dual strategy of pressure and diplomacy. The US has intensified sanctions and plans to designate the Venezuelan 'Cartel of the Suns' linked to Maduro as a terrorist organization, aiming to undermine the regime and combat narcotics trafficking. Venezuela and its allies, such as China, condemn US actions as interventionist and reject coercive tactics, emphasizing sovereignty and denouncing military drills and sanctions. Regional actors like Colombia seek multilateral dialogue involving the US and China to ease tensions, while Maduro warns of dire consequences if the US pursues military strikes, maintaining a stance of resistance amid a fragile geopolitical environment.
