Train Collision Near Machu Picchu Causes Fatality and Multiple Injuries

Peru Train Crash 2025-12-31 • 14 articles in total

In brief
  • A train collision near Machu Picchu in Peru caused one death and multiple injuries.
  • Emergency services responded quickly despite the remote location, and investigations into safety are ongoing.
  • Train services are disrupted, raising serious safety concerns on this key tourist route.

A collision between two trains near the popular tourist destination Machu Picchu in Peru resulted in one death and between 15 to 40 injuries, according to various reports. Emergency services responded promptly despite the remote and difficult-to-access location, providing medical assistance and managing rescue operations. The accident has disrupted train services to the archaeological site and raised significant safety concerns about rail transport in the region. Authorities are actively investigating the cause of the collision, with attention focused on improving safety measures for this key tourist corridor. The incident has drawn international attention due to the involvement of tourists and the prominence of Machu Picchu as a global heritage site.
