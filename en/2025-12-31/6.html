UAE Troop Withdrawal from Yemen Amid Rising Saudi-UAE Tensions

Yemen: UAE Troop Pullout 2025-12-31 • 20 articles in total

In brief
  • The UAE is withdrawing its military forces from Yemen, shifting its role in the conflict.
  • The withdrawal follows tensions with Saudi Arabia after a Saudi airstrike on a weapons shipment linked to the UAE.
  • The UAE frames the pullout as voluntary for counterterrorism, while others see it as a response to intra-coalition disputes.

The United Arab Emirates has announced the withdrawal of its remaining military forces from Yemen, marking a significant shift in its involvement in the ongoing conflict. This decision follows escalating tensions with Saudi Arabia, particularly after a Saudi-led airstrike targeted a weapons shipment allegedly linked to the UAE and separatist groups. Saudi Arabia demanded the UAE's withdrawal within 24 hours, highlighting fractures within the coalition fighting in Yemen. The UAE frames its pullout as a voluntary move focused on counterterrorism, while analysts view it as a response to intra-coalition disputes and changing regional dynamics. This development is expected to alter the balance of power among Yemeni factions and impact future peace efforts.

UAE and Regional Allies' Perspective: Voluntary Withdrawal and Counterterrorism Focus

Articles from UAE and allied sources emphasize the withdrawal as a voluntary decision primarily aimed at ending counterterrorism operations in Yemen. They highlight the UAE's strategic recalibration and focus on reducing direct military involvement amid complex local and regional dynamics. The UAE stresses coordination with international partners and frames the move as a step toward de-escalation rather than a forced retreat.

Saudi Arabia and Coalition Viewpoint: Withdrawal as a Result of Tensions and Security Concerns

Sources from Saudi Arabia and coalition-aligned countries portray the UAE's withdrawal as a consequence of rising tensions following a Saudi airstrike on a weapons shipment linked to the UAE and separatist forces. Saudi Arabia demanded the UAE's forces leave Yemen within 24 hours, citing national security and coordination issues. This perspective underscores intra-coalition disputes and frames the UAE's pullout as a response to Saudi pressure and security imperatives.
