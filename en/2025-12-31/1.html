Escalating US Military Actions Against Drug Trafficking in Venezuela

US–Venezuela Military Anti-Drug Strikes 2025-12-31 • 56 articles in total

In brief
  • The US has escalated military strikes against drug trafficking sites in Venezuela, with CIA drone and ground operations confirmed by President Trump.
  • Colombian and regional leaders worry that US attacks risk escalating conflict and violating sovereignty, fearing potential invasions.
  • Venezuela's government denies drug trafficking allegations and condemns US actions as misinformation and aggression against its sovereignty.

The United States has intensified military operations targeting drug trafficking facilities and vessels in and around Venezuela, including drone strikes and ground attacks on ports and cocaine production sites. President Donald Trump publicly confirmed several of these strikes, highlighting CIA involvement and marking a shift from containment to direct military action. Colombian President Gustavo Petro and other regional leaders have expressed concerns about the implications of these strikes, fearing potential invasions and questioning US motives. Venezuela's government, led by Nicolás Maduro, denies allegations of drug trafficking and accuses the US of spreading misinformation. These developments have heightened tensions and complicated diplomatic relations in the region, reflecting a complex interplay of counter-narcotics efforts and geopolitical conflict.

Colombian Leadership's Concerns and Regional Implications

Colombian President Gustavo Petro and other regional leaders express apprehension about US military actions in Venezuela, particularly regarding strikes on cocaine production facilities. They highlight the risks of escalation, potential violations of sovereignty, and the possibility that these operations could pave the way for broader invasions. This perspective underscores regional tensions and the complex dynamics between Colombia, Venezuela, and the United States in addressing narcotrafficking and security challenges.

Venezuelan Government's Denial and Accusations Against the US

The Venezuelan government, led by President Nicolás Maduro, denies allegations of drug trafficking and condemns US military actions as misinformation and aggression. Maduro accuses the United States of spreading fake news and violating Venezuelan sovereignty through covert and overt military operations. This viewpoint emphasizes resistance to US intervention and frames the strikes as part of a broader campaign to destabilize Venezuela politically and economically.
