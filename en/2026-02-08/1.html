Complex US-Iran Nuclear Negotiations Amid Tensions and Economic Pressure

US–Iran Nuclear Negotiations 2026-02-08 • 87 articles in total

In brief
  • The US and Iran hold indirect nuclear talks marked by cautious optimism, with Iran refusing to halt uranium enrichment or discuss missiles.
  • The US combines diplomatic engagement and economic sanctions, with President Trump signaling both hope for a deal and potential harsh consequences.
  • Israel collaborates closely with the US, urging a strict approach on Iran's nuclear and missile activities amid regional security concerns.

The United States and Iran have engaged in multiple rounds of indirect nuclear negotiations in Oman, described by officials from both sides as a positive but cautious start. While Iran remains firm on continuing uranium enrichment and excludes missile programs from talks, the US maintains pressure through economic sanctions and threats, including tariffs on countries trading with Iran. US President Donald Trump has expressed both optimism about reaching a deal and warnings of severe consequences if negotiations fail, underscoring a dual approach of diplomacy and coercion. Israel, represented by Prime Minister Netanyahu, actively coordinates with the US, advocating a hardline stance and emphasizing missile restrictions. The expiration of the US-Russia New START treaty adds a broader context of nuclear tension, complicating global arms control efforts amid regional instability.

Iranian Perspective: Willingness to Negotiate Nuclear Issues but Firm on Red Lines

Iran expresses readiness to engage in nuclear talks with the United States, emphasizing a positive start to negotiations and willingness to continue dialogue. However, Iran firmly rejects suspending uranium enrichment and excludes missile programs from the negotiation agenda, viewing these as non-negotiable for national defense. Iranian officials stress the importance of avoiding threats and pressure, seeking a fair and equitable framework while maintaining sovereignty over key strategic programs.

US Perspective: Diplomatic Engagement Coupled with Economic and Military Pressure

The United States pursues a dual strategy combining diplomatic talks with Iran and economic sanctions, including tariffs on countries trading with Iran, to pressure Tehran into compliance. President Donald Trump has publicly described talks as 'very good' and expressed cautious optimism about a deal, while simultaneously warning of severe consequences and not ruling out military action. US envoys maintain a military presence in the region, exemplified by visits to aircraft carriers, signaling readiness to enforce US policy. Coordination with Israel is a key element of US strategy, reflecting concerns over Iran's nuclear and missile programs.

Israeli Perspective: Advocating Hardline Approach and Coordination with the US

Israel, led by Prime Minister Benjamin Netanyahu, actively engages with the United States to influence the approach toward Iran, emphasizing the need for a hardline stance on Iran's nuclear ambitions and missile programs. Netanyahu's meetings with US President Trump focus on ensuring missile restrictions and preventing any easing of sanctions that might empower Iran. Israel views Iran's nuclear program as an existential threat and seeks to coordinate closely with the US to shape regional security policies.
