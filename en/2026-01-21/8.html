UK-Mauritius Chagos Sovereignty Deal Sparks US Criticism and UK Defense

UK Chagos Sovereignty Dispute 2026-01-21 • 12 articles in total

In brief
  • The UK agreed to transfer sovereignty of the Chagos Islands to Mauritius while maintaining a military base lease with the US.
  • President Trump strongly opposed the deal, citing strategic concerns over the US military base on Diego Garcia.
  • The dispute has strained US-UK relations and involves ongoing UK parliamentary debate and Mauritius' sovereignty claims.

The United Kingdom's legislative agreement to transfer sovereignty of the Chagos Islands to Mauritius has ignited significant controversy, particularly from US President Donald Trump, who has repeatedly criticized the deal as "stupid" and a sign of British weakness. Trump's objections focus on the strategic importance of Diego Garcia, a key US military base on the islands, expressing concern over the implications for US defense interests. The UK government, however, has defended the agreement, emphasizing its benefits for regional stability and cooperation, and reaffirming the lease arrangement for the military base. This dispute has strained US-UK relations and complicated the geopolitical dynamics in the Indian Ocean region. Meanwhile, Mauritius continues to assert its sovereignty claims, with the UK Parliament actively debating the legislative text governing the islands' future.
