US Intensifies Seizures of Venezuelan-Linked Oil Tankers in Caribbean

US Tanker Seizures 2026-01-21 • 23 articles in total

In brief
  • The US has seized a seventh Venezuelan-linked oil tanker in the Caribbean to enforce sanctions and restrict Venezuela's oil exports.
  • These seizures are peaceful operations aimed at curbing illicit oil shipments and maintaining maritime security while escalating geopolitical tensions.
  • Russia objects to the seizures, citing diplomatic and humanitarian concerns over detained sailors and broader geopolitical conflicts.

The United States has escalated its enforcement of sanctions by seizing a seventh oil tanker linked to Venezuela in the Caribbean Sea, continuing a blockade initiated under the Trump administration. These seizures target vessels allegedly violating maritime quarantine and sanctions designed to restrict Venezuela's oil exports and pressure the Maduro government. The operations have been largely peaceful and controlled, with limited disclosure on cargo or vessel details. While the US emphasizes combating illicit trafficking and maintaining maritime security, tensions persist, notably with Russia, which has raised concerns over detained sailors aboard seized tankers. Overall, these actions underscore ongoing geopolitical struggles over energy resources and regional security in the Americas.

US and Allied Perspectives on Enforcement

Articles from countries including the US, Canada, France, Mexico, and others emphasize the legality and strategic necessity of the tanker seizures as part of enforcing sanctions against Venezuela. They highlight the operations as peaceful, controlled, and essential to curtailing illicit oil shipments that support the Maduro regime. The focus is on maintaining maritime security and applying economic pressure to achieve political objectives.

Russian Perspective on Detained Sailors

The Russian viewpoint, represented by statements from officials like Sergey Lavrov, centers on the diplomatic and humanitarian concerns regarding the detention of Russian sailors aboard seized tankers. Russia contests the US assurances about the sailors' release, framing the seizures as part of broader geopolitical tensions and strained relations with the West. This perspective highlights the human and diplomatic complexities intertwined with the maritime enforcement actions.
