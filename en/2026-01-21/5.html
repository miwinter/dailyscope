Global Power Politics: Rising Tensions and Calls for Respectful Multilateralism

World Order: Power Politics 2026-01-21 • 25 articles in total

In brief
  • Global leaders warn the rules-based world order is eroding under rising aggressive power politics and unilateral actions.
  • France, Canada, and China advocate respect, multilateralism, and resistance to coercion to maintain international stability.
  • Critiques of the US highlight imperial ambitions and prioritizing power over international law, urging middle powers to adapt to new geopolitical realities.

Recent international discourse, particularly at Davos 2026, reveals a growing concern over the erosion of the established rules-based world order amid rising aggressive power politics. Leaders from France and Canada emphasize the need for respect, diplomacy, and resistance to coercion, warning against a world governed by 'might makes right' and unilateralism. China positions itself as a defender of multilateral cooperation, cautioning against a descent into lawlessness, while critiques of US policies highlight tensions over imperial ambitions and the prioritization of power over international law. The shifting dynamics suggest a potential reconfiguration of global alliances and power structures, with middle powers urged to adapt to a new geopolitical reality marked by instability and strategic competition.

France's Advocacy for Respect and Sovereignty

French President Emmanuel Macron consistently advocates for a world order based on respect rather than dominance or bullying. France emphasizes sovereignty, autonomy, and multilateralism, rejecting unilateral pressures, particularly from the United States, which Macron accuses of revived imperial ambitions. This perspective underscores Europe's commitment to legal norms and cooperative international relations.

China's Vice President warns against a regression to a 'law of the jungle' in global affairs, emphasizing peaceful coexistence and multilateral cooperation. China positions itself as a defender of international rules and order amid escalating global tensions and power struggles. This stance promotes stability through adherence to established norms and collective governance.
