Divergent Perspectives on US Allied Diplomacy and Security Cooperation

US Allies Diplomacy 2026-03-23 • 43 articles in total

In brief
  • US diplomacy relies on varied allied cooperation with strong military and security ties in Romania, Taiwan, and Japan, and active Latin American anti-drug efforts.
  • Diplomatic strains arise with countries like South Africa, Brazil, and some European and Indo-Pacific allies over US policies, especially concerning Iran and Middle East strategy.
  • US political endorsements and complex negotiations with Ukraine and other partners reflect intertwined domestic politics and urgent geopolitical challenges.

US diplomacy heavily relies on allied cooperation across multiple regions, with significant military, security, and economic engagements evident in Europe, Latin America, and Asia. While some allies, such as Romania, Taiwan, and Japan, demonstrate close collaboration through military deployments, arms deliveries, and technology investments, others like South Africa and Brazil express diplomatic strains or criticism of US policies. The US also faces challenges in securing unified support from European and Indo-Pacific partners regarding Middle East strategies, particularly concerning Iran, revealing fractures within traditional alliances. In Latin America, the US pursues robust anti-drug trafficking partnerships, though investigations and political tensions, such as those involving Colombia's president, complicate relations. Overall, US diplomacy shows a complex mosaic of cooperation, contention, and strategic recalibration among its allies.
