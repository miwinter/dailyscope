Escalating Iran War: US-Israel Strikes, Iranian Retaliation, and Regional Tensions

Iran War 2026-03-23 • 135 articles in total

In brief
  • The US and Israel conduct airstrikes on Iranian nuclear and energy sites to pressure Iran and secure the region.
  • Iran retaliates with missile strikes on Israel and threatens to close the Strait of Hormuz, escalating regional tensions.
  • International actors warn of humanitarian damage, call for restraint, and note economic impacts amid the escalating conflict.

The conflict between the United States, Israel, and Iran has intensified with coordinated strikes on Iranian nuclear and energy infrastructure, including attacks on Tehran and Natanz. Iran has responded with missile strikes on Israeli cities and threats to close the strategic Strait of Hormuz, a critical global oil transit route, escalating fears of broader regional instability. The US, under President Trump, has issued ultimatums threatening to obliterate Iranian power plants if demands are unmet, while Iran warns of retaliatory attacks on energy and water infrastructure in the Gulf. Gulf states report repeated Iranian attacks, and international actors express concern over the humanitarian toll, including civilian casualties and damage to cultural heritage. The situation has triggered market volatility, international protests, and calls for restraint amid fears of a protracted and wider conflict.
