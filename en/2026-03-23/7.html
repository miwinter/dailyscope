Collision at LaGuardia Airport Causes Ground Stop and Emergency Response

LaGuardia Ground Stop 2026-03-23 • 8 articles in total

In brief
  • An Air Canada Express plane collided with a fire truck at LaGuardia Airport causing injuries and an FAA ground stop.
  • The incident disrupted airport operations and raised concerns about safety protocols at busy international airports.
  • Investigations are ongoing regarding the cause, emphasizing the vulnerability of airport ground operations and emergency response importance.

A collision occurred at New York's LaGuardia Airport involving an Air Canada Express plane and a ground vehicle, specifically a fire truck, prompting the FAA to issue a ground stop for all flights. Emergency responders quickly attended the scene, with several reports indicating injuries and ongoing investigations to determine the cause. The incident caused significant disruptions to airport operations and highlighted concerns about safety protocols at busy international airports. While some reports mention fatalities, others note that casualty details remain unclear or unconfirmed. This event has drawn attention to the vulnerability of airport ground operations and the importance of rapid emergency response.
