Cuba's Deepening Energy Crisis Amid US Sanctions

Cuba Energy Crisis 2026-02-11 • 17 articles in total

In brief
  • Cuba faces a severe energy crisis causing fuel shortages that disrupt aviation, healthcare, and daily life while damaging tourism and economic stability.
  • US sanctions and an oil blockade intensify the crisis, prompting government rationing and shifts to alternative energy amid geopolitical tensions.
  • The crisis worsens social disparities as Cubans adopt varied energy solutions, reflecting broader humanitarian and political challenges.

Cuba is facing a severe energy crisis exacerbated by tightened US sanctions and an oil blockade, leading to critical fuel shortages that impact aviation, healthcare, and daily life. International airlines, including Air Canada, have suspended flights due to the inability to refuel in Cuba, further isolating the island and damaging its vital tourism sector. The Cuban government has implemented rationing and emergency measures to protect essential services while the population increasingly turns to alternative energy sources like solar panels and charcoal. Geopolitical tensions between Cuba, the US, and allies such as Russia underscore the broader implications of the crisis. The situation raises concerns about Cuba's economic stability and humanitarian conditions as the energy shortages deepen.
