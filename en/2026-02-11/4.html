Tensions and cautious diplomacy mark US-Iran talks in Oman

US–Iran Diplomatic Talks in Oman 2026-02-11 • 22 articles in total

In brief
  • Iran shows willingness to negotiate nuclear matters but insists its ballistic missile program is non-negotiable.
  • The US adopts a hardline stance demanding broad concessions and warns of severe consequences if talks fail.
  • Israel urges the US to take a tougher approach toward Iran's missile capabilities to ensure regional security.

In 2023, indirect diplomatic talks between the US and Iran held in Oman have shown cautious progress amid persistent tensions. Iran signals willingness to dilute uranium enrichment if sanctions are lifted, yet firmly maintains its ballistic missile program as non-negotiable, complicating negotiations. The US, represented by figures including President Trump, issues stern warnings of tough actions if talks fail, reflecting a hardline stance. Israel, closely monitoring the situation, urges the US to adopt a tougher approach, particularly concerning Iran's missile capabilities. Oman plays a key mediating role, facilitating dialogue aimed at achieving a balanced agreement despite ongoing regional security concerns and internal Iranian repression.

Iranian perspective emphasizing cautious engagement and strategic red lines

Iran presents a dual approach: signaling openness to nuclear negotiations, such as potentially diluting uranium enrichment if sanctions are lifted, while firmly refusing to negotiate its ballistic missile program. Iranian officials engage in talks facilitated by Oman, emphasizing the importance of dialogue and regional cooperation. However, internal repression continues, highlighting the regime's complex domestic and international posture.

US and Trump administration's firm stance with conditional diplomacy

The US, particularly through statements by President Trump, maintains a tough negotiating position, warning of severe consequences if Iran does not reach an agreement. While acknowledging Iran's willingness to negotiate under conditions, the US stresses the need for comprehensive concessions, including on nuclear and missile issues. This perspective underscores a readiness to escalate pressure if diplomacy fails.

Israeli concerns driving calls for stronger US action against Iran

Israel, led by Prime Minister Netanyahu, actively seeks to influence US policy by urging a harder line on Iran, especially regarding its nuclear program and ballistic missile capabilities. Netanyahu's urgent trips to Washington and meetings with Trump highlight Israel's strategic priority to counter Iranian threats. This viewpoint reflects Israel's insistence on incorporating missile issues into any US-Iran agreement to safeguard regional security.
