Runway incidents: intrusion fatality, ground collisions and evacuations

ICAO Runway Incident 2026-05-13 • 7 articles in total

In brief
  • A pedestrian died after being struck during takeoff at Denver International Airport, raising security and investigation concerns.
  • Ground collisions involving United aircraft in San Francisco caused operational disruptions and triggered safety investigations.
  • A Turkish Airlines flight in Kathmandu was evacuated after smoke from landing gear, with no injuries and ongoing technical probes.

A series of runway and ground incidents reported internationally range from fatal runway intrusions to ground collisions and precautionary evacuations. Several reports focus on a pedestrian struck during takeoff at Denver International Airport, a death that has prompted questions about security, possible intent, and an ongoing investigation with some passengers sustaining minor injuries. Other coverage details ground handling and landing accidents — including a pushback collision between two United aircraft in San Francisco and an aircraft striking a lamppost and truck — that disrupted operations and triggered inquiries. Separately, a smoke sighting from landing gear on a Turkish Airlines flight in Kathmandu led to an orderly evacuation and inspection, with no injuries reported as authorities probe the cause.

Smoke and precautionary evacuation (technical/precautionary focus)

Coverage of the Kathmandu incident centers on a smoke observation from landing gear that prompted a precautionary evacuation of a Turkish Airlines aircraft and subsequent inspections by local authorities. Reports underscore that there were no injuries and that authorities and the airline are investigating the technical cause to confirm safety before the aircraft returns to service.
