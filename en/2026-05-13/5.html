Regional escalation across Gulf, Iran and Israel-Lebanon fronts

Middle East Conflict Escalation 2026-05-13 • 74 articles in total

In brief
  • Gulf states have conducted covert strikes on Iran while Tehran maintains missile capabilities and signals military readiness.
  • Israel's military moves coincide with heavy civilian tolls in Lebanon and Gaza, escalating regional humanitarian crises.
  • The US reports rising conflict costs near $29 billion and keeps escalation options open amid stalled diplomacy and regional tensions.

A rapid escalation across the Middle East is unfolding: Gulf states are reported to have carried out covert strikes on Iran while Tehran signals military readiness, nuclear hardening and retention of much of its missile capability. Israel’s military and political moves — including new legal measures and preparations to widen operations — coincide with heavy civilian tolls in Lebanon and repeated strikes in Gaza, stressing humanitarian crises. The United States is absorbing rising costs and signaling open policy options as Pentagon accounting places the conflict’s bill near $29 billion, even as diplomacy and mediation efforts by regional and external actors struggle to hold a fragile ceasefire. International reporting and regional statements (from the Arab League, Egypt, Turkey and others) reflect deep concern about wider spillover and the risk of broader confrontation in maritime and Hormuz corridors.
