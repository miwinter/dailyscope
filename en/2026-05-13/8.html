France hantavirus cluster and European containment efforts

France Hantavirus Outbreak 2026-05-13 • 9 articles in total

In brief
  • Eleven hantavirus cases linked to a cruise ship have prompted isolation and critical care in France.
  • French authorities and President Macron confirm the outbreak is under control with ongoing monitoring and containment.
  • Neighboring countries have quarantined exposed individuals and health officials advise vigilance despite low risk of wider outbreak.

A cluster of hantavirus cases linked to a cruise ship has prompted heightened public-health response in France and across Europe. French authorities report the number of identified cases rising to 11, including at least one patient in critical condition on life support and another requiring an artificial lung, while children and close contacts have been isolated. President Emmanuel Macron has publicly reassured citizens that the situation is under control and that monitoring and containment measures are in place. At the same time, neighbouring countries and hospitals have enacted quarantines and isolation after exposures, and international health officials advise vigilance even as they assess the risk of a wider outbreak.
