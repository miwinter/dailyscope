Escalation and Diplomatic Struggles in US-Iran Military Conflict

US–Iran Military Conflict and Diplomacy 2026-03-01 • 211 articles in total

In brief
  • The US and Israel launched military strikes against Iran to target leadership and nuclear capabilities, aiming to neutralize threats and promote regime change.
  • Iran condemned the strikes, retaliated with missile attacks, and emphasized defensive measures and ongoing diplomatic efforts despite the conflict.
  • Global reactions vary from calls for diplomacy and concerns over wider war and economic impacts to debates on the legality and morality of the strikes.

The US and Israel have launched extensive military strikes against Iran, targeting key military and leadership sites, including the reported killing of Supreme Leader Ali Khamenei and other senior officials. Iran has retaliated with missile attacks on US bases and allied countries in the Middle East, escalating regional tensions and causing widespread casualties, including civilians. The conflict has drawn varied international reactions, with some leaders condemning the attacks and urging diplomacy, while others support the US-Israeli actions. Diplomatic talks between the US and Iran continue cautiously amid the violence, reflecting a complex interplay of military confrontation and negotiation efforts. The situation remains volatile with fears of broader regional war and significant geopolitical and economic consequences, especially concerning global energy markets.
