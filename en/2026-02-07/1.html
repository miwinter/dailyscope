US-Iran Nuclear Talks Amid Tensions and Sanctions

US–Russia Nuclear Talks 2026-02-07 • 150 articles in total

In brief
  • The US and Iran hold indirect nuclear talks in Oman with cautious optimism but remain divided on uranium enrichment and discussion scope.
  • The US applies sanctions and military warnings to pressure Iran, while Iran defends its nuclear rights and continues dialogue despite tensions.
  • Oman mediates the talks amid international support aiming to prevent escalation and promote regional stability.

The United States and Iran have engaged in multiple rounds of indirect nuclear negotiations primarily held in Oman, aiming to address Iran's nuclear program and regional security concerns. Both sides describe the talks as a "good start" or held in a "positive atmosphere," yet significant disagreements remain, particularly over uranium enrichment and the scope of discussions. Despite diplomatic efforts, the US has concurrently imposed new sanctions and tariffs targeting Iran's oil exports and related entities, maintaining pressure amid ongoing tensions. Military threats and warnings from US leadership have underscored the fragile nature of the talks, while Iran insists on defending its nuclear rights and opposing excessive demands. The negotiations continue cautiously with international mediation and watchful global attention, reflecting a complex balance between diplomacy and confrontation.

Iranian Perspective on Negotiations

Iran emphasizes the nuclear talks as a positive and necessary step toward resolving disputes, describing the meetings as a "good start" and expressing cautious optimism. Iranian officials stress the importance of fair and equitable negotiations, firmly opposing excessive US demands such as halting uranium enrichment. Iran also highlights its readiness to defend its interests and insists that the nuclear issue remains central to discussions, while showing willingness to continue dialogue despite ongoing tensions and sanctions.

United States Perspective on Negotiations and Pressure

The United States approaches the talks with cautious optimism but maintains a firm stance through sanctions and military warnings. US officials describe the negotiations as "very good" or "positive," yet simultaneously impose new sanctions on Iran's oil sector and related entities to increase economic pressure. The US government has urged its citizens to leave Iran due to security concerns and has issued stern warnings about potential military action if negotiations fail. The US seeks a broader framework addressing nuclear, missile, and regional proxy issues, reflecting a hardline approach amid diplomatic engagement.

International and Regional Mediation and Reactions

Oman plays a key mediating role in facilitating indirect talks between the US and Iran, providing neutral ground for negotiations. International organizations and regional actors, including the UN Secretary-General and Gulf Cooperation Council, welcome the resumption of dialogue as a positive step toward de-escalation and regional stability. Other countries express cautious optimism while monitoring the talks amid fears of military conflict. The complex geopolitical environment involves multiple stakeholders advocating for diplomatic solutions to prevent escalation.
