Cuba's Energy Crisis Deepens Amid US Sanctions and Domestic Challenges

Cuba Energy Crisis 2026-02-07 • 9 articles in total

In brief
  • Cuba faces an energy crisis with power outages and fuel shortages due to US sanctions and disrupted oil imports.
  • The government enforces rationing and blackouts to manage scarce energy and limit economic impact.
  • The crisis results from external pressures and internal issues like mismanagement and political instability.

Cuba is facing a severe energy crisis marked by widespread power outages, fuel shortages, and rationing measures such as a four-day workweek and activity restrictions. The crisis is largely attributed to intensified US sanctions and threats to countries supplying oil to Cuba, which have disrupted fuel imports and exacerbated shortages. Cuban authorities have implemented emergency measures including rolling blackouts and rationing plans to manage the scarce energy resources and mitigate economic disruption. The situation has significantly impacted daily life and economic stability, with some analyses highlighting long-term mismanagement and geopolitical tensions as underlying factors. The crisis underscores Cuba's vulnerability to external pressures and the urgent need for alternative energy solutions or international support.

Cuban and Caribbean Perspectives on US Sanctions Impact

Articles from Caribbean sources emphasize the direct impact of US sanctions and oil supply disruptions on Cuba's energy crisis. They highlight how US policies have led to fuel shortages, power outages, and rationing measures that severely affect Cuban towns and daily life. The narrative focuses on the hardship faced by the population and the government's emergency responses to manage the crisis.

European Views on Political and Economic Dimensions

European articles provide a broader analysis linking Cuba's energy crisis to long-standing mismanagement and political instability, alongside the effects of US sanctions. They discuss the crisis as both a symptom of internal governance issues and external geopolitical pressures, noting the opportunity perceived by the US to influence Cuba's regime. This perspective situates the energy shortages within a complex political context.
