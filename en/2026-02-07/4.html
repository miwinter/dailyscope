Trump's Racist Obama Video Sparks Widespread Backlash and Political Polarization

Trump AI Hate Speech 2026-02-07 • 27 articles in total

In brief
  • Donald Trump shared a racist AI-generated video of the Obamas and refused to apologize despite widespread condemnation.
  • The White House deleted the video blaming a staff error amid bipartisan backlash from Democrats and Republicans.
  • The incident reflects deep racial tensions, political polarization, and challenges in social media conduct and misinformation.

President Donald Trump shared an AI-generated video depicting Barack and Michelle Obama as apes, which triggered widespread condemnation across political lines and the public. The video was subsequently deleted, with the White House attributing the post to a staff error, though Trump himself refused to apologize and claimed he did not see the offensive content. The incident intensified existing political and racial tensions in the United States, drawing criticism even from some Republicans. This controversy highlights ongoing challenges in political discourse, social media conduct, and race relations under Trump's influence. The episode also reflects broader debates about misinformation and the limits of acceptable political communication.
