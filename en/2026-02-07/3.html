Deadly Suicide Bombing Targets Shia Mosque in Islamabad, Pakistan

Pakistan Shia Mosque Bombings 2026-02-07 • 46 articles in total

In brief
  • A suicide bombing at a Shia mosque in Islamabad killed at least 31 people and injured over 130 during Friday prayers.
  • The Islamic State claimed responsibility, highlighting ongoing sectarian violence and militant threats in Pakistan.
  • International and regional responses condemned the attack and emphasized the need for improved security and counterterrorism cooperation.

A suicide bombing at a Shia mosque in Islamabad, Pakistan, resulted in at least 31 deaths and over 130 injuries, marking one of the deadliest attacks in the capital in recent years. The attack occurred during Friday prayers, targeting worshippers and exacerbating sectarian tensions in the region. The Islamic State has claimed responsibility for the bombing, highlighting ongoing militant threats and sectarian violence in Pakistan. Authorities are actively investigating the incident, while emergency services continue to assist the wounded. The attack has drawn widespread condemnation from international organizations and governments, emphasizing the urgent need for enhanced security measures and counterterrorism efforts.
