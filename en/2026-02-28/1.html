Escalation to Open War Between Pakistan and Afghanistan Amid Calls for Mediation

Pakistan–Afghanistan Border Conflict 2026-02-28 • 92 articles in total

In brief
  • Pakistan has declared open war on Taliban-controlled Afghanistan due to insurgent attacks and launched airstrikes on Afghan cities.
  • The Taliban defend their retaliatory strikes and show readiness for dialogue despite ongoing conflict and civilian casualties.
  • International actors demand ceasefires and promote diplomatic efforts to resolve the escalating violence and regional instability.

The conflict between Pakistan and Taliban-controlled Afghanistan has escalated dramatically into an open war, marked by Pakistani airstrikes on Kabul and other Afghan cities following border clashes and militant attacks. Pakistan accuses Afghanistan of harboring insurgents targeting its territory, while the Taliban have responded with retaliatory strikes and expressed openness to dialogue. The situation has drawn international concern, with regional powers like Iran, Saudi Arabia, and Qatar offering mediation efforts, and the United Nations calling for immediate ceasefires and peaceful resolution. The hostilities have resulted in significant casualties and heightened fears of regional destabilization, with both sides entrenched in military confrontations amid complex historical and political tensions. Despite the violence, diplomatic efforts continue to seek de-escalation and prevent further humanitarian crises.

Pakistan's Perspective: Open War and Military Response

Pakistan views the conflict as a necessary military response to Taliban aggression and cross-border attacks, declaring an 'open war' against the Taliban-led Afghan government. Pakistani officials emphasize their armed forces' readiness to decisively counter threats and have launched extensive airstrikes on Kabul and other Afghan cities. The government accuses Afghanistan of harboring militants responsible for attacks within Pakistan and stresses the need to protect national security and territorial integrity. This perspective highlights Pakistan's stance of firm military action to address longstanding border disputes and insurgent threats.

Afghan/Taliban Perspective: Retaliation and Openness to Talks

The Afghan Taliban acknowledge the conflict but emphasize their retaliatory actions against Pakistani strikes, accusing Pakistan of civilian casualties and aggression. Despite the escalation, Taliban leaders have expressed willingness to engage in dialogue to end violence, signaling a potential shift towards negotiation. This viewpoint reflects the Taliban's position of defending their territory while remaining open to diplomatic solutions amid ongoing hostilities and humanitarian concerns.

International and Regional Mediation Efforts and Calls for Peace

Various international actors, including the United Nations, Iran, Saudi Arabia, Qatar, and the European Union, have called for immediate ceasefires and peaceful dialogue between Pakistan and Afghanistan. These entities express concern over the escalating violence and its impact on regional stability and humanitarian conditions. Mediation efforts aim to de-escalate tensions and foster negotiations to prevent further conflict, highlighting the global community's interest in resolving the crisis through diplomacy rather than continued military confrontation.
