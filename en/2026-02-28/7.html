Progress and Challenges in Iran-US Nuclear Deal Negotiations

Iran Nuclear Deal Negotiations 2026-02-28 • 21 articles in total

In brief
  • Iran and the US are conducting cautious but serious nuclear talks with Oman's mediation.
  • Iran agreed not to stockpile enriched or weapons-grade uranium and urges the US to ease demands.
  • The US is dissatisfied with progress balancing diplomacy and military pressure, while international actors watch closely.

Recent rounds of nuclear negotiations between Iran and the United States, facilitated notably by Oman, have shown cautious progress with Iran agreeing not to stockpile enriched or weapons-grade uranium. Both sides have engaged in lengthy and serious talks, reflecting a mutual interest in diplomacy despite persistent tensions and unresolved differences. The US administration, including President Trump, has expressed dissatisfaction with the pace and terms of negotiations, balancing diplomatic efforts with military posturing in the region. Iran has urged the US to drop excessive demands, while accusations and mistrust continue to complicate the dialogue. Overall, optimism exists among mediators and some officials that a deal is within reach if given more time, but the situation remains delicate and uncertain.

Iran and Omani Mediation Perspective

Iran, supported by Omani mediators, emphasizes constructive progress in negotiations, highlighting agreements not to stockpile enriched or weapons-grade uranium as significant steps forward. Oman plays a key facilitation role, expressing optimism that a deal is achievable with additional time and unified efforts. Iran also calls on the US to moderate its demands, framing the talks as a positive diplomatic engagement despite ongoing challenges.

United States Diplomatic and Political Viewpoint

US officials acknowledge the seriousness and length of the talks but remain cautious and sometimes skeptical about the outcomes. President Trump has publicly expressed dissatisfaction with the negotiations' progress and approach, while still allowing more time for talks. The US balances diplomatic engagement with military readiness, as seen in regional deployments and ultimatums, reflecting a complex stance between negotiation and pressure.

International and Regional Observers' Analysis

International actors, including the IAEA and regional countries like Lebanon and Iraq, monitor the negotiations closely, noting both progress and persistent tensions. They highlight the critical nature of the talks for regional stability and nuclear non-proliferation. While some express cautious optimism, they also recognize the fragility of the process amid accusations and mistrust between Iran and the US.
