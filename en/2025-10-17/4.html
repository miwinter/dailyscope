Madagascar's Military Coup and Regional Response

2025-10-17 • 44 articles in total

Madagascar has undergone a significant political crisis marked by widespread protests, the impeachment and departure of President Andry Rajoelina, and a subsequent military coup led by Colonel Michael Randrianirina. The military takeover was accompanied by the suspension of key democratic institutions and promises of a transitional government lasting up to two years before elections. Regional bodies, particularly the African Union, have responded by suspending Madagascar from their organizations, condemning the unconstitutional change of power, and calling for a return to civilian rule. The United Nations has echoed these concerns, emphasizing the need for peace and constitutional order. Meanwhile, some local perspectives view the coup as a necessary intervention amid instability, with certain experts describing it as a "good coup," underscoring divided opinions within and outside Madagascar.
