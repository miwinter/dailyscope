US Suspends Immigrant Visa Processing for 75 Countries Amid Security and Policy Shifts

US Immigrant Visa Policy Actions 2026-01-16 • 44 articles in total

In brief
  • The Trump administration suspended immigrant visa processing for nationals of 75 countries, excluding tourist and student visas.
  • Affected countries seek clarifications and reassure citizens about unaffected visa categories amid widespread uncertainty.
  • The suspension is part of broader US immigration restrictions, impacting diplomatic relations and migration flows globally.

The United States government, under the Trump administration, has suspended immigrant visa processing for nationals of 75 countries, citing security and administrative reasons. This suspension affects permanent residency visa categories but excludes non-immigrant visas such as tourist and student visas, as clarified by several affected countries. The policy has caused widespread uncertainty and concern among applicants and governments worldwide, impacting diplomatic relations and migration flows. Some countries have issued official statements seeking clarifications or reassuring citizens about unaffected visa categories. The suspension is part of a broader tightening of US immigration policies, including large-scale visa revocations and increased border security funding.

Many countries included in the suspension list, such as Thailand, Liberia, Moldova, Saint Lucia, and Jamaica, have officially acknowledged the US decision and are actively seeking clarifications or providing guidance to their citizens. These nations emphasize the suspension's impact on immigrant visas while reassuring that tourist and student visas remain unaffected. Their perspective highlights the diplomatic efforts to manage the uncertainty and protect their nationals' interests amid the US policy changes.
