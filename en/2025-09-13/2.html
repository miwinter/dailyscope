International Perspectives on the Israeli-Palestinian Conflict and the Two-State Solution

2025-09-13 • 93 articles in total

The majority of United Nations member states have expressed strong support for a two-state solution to the Israeli-Palestinian conflict, often endorsing resolutions that propose a future Palestinian state excluding Hamas due to security concerns. Numerous countries and international organizations emphasize peaceful coexistence, diplomatic negotiations, and efforts to isolate militant groups like Hamas. While many nations and blocs advocate for Palestinian statehood and condemn violence, Israel and a few allies strongly oppose these resolutions, criticizing them as encouraging Hamas and undermining Israel’s security. Simultaneously, escalating military actions such as Israeli strikes on Hamas leaders in Qatar have heightened regional tensions, further complicating peace efforts and drawing varied international reactions based on geopolitical interests and alliances.

Pro-Two-State Solution and Palestinian Statehood Without Hamas

This viewpoint is held by a broad international coalition including many UN member states, European countries, and Muslim-majority nations, which support the establishment of an independent Palestinian state alongside Israel while explicitly excluding Hamas due to its militant activities. These countries emphasize diplomatic efforts, peaceful negotiation, and condemn violence, seeking to marginalize Hamas as a terrorist organization in order to create conditions favorable to a stable and recognized Palestinian governance. The large majority in the UN General Assembly voting for resolutions such as the 'New York Declaration' exemplifies this stance, highlighting a global consensus on the two-state solution as the preferred framework for resolving the conflict.

Israeli and Allied Rejection of UN Resolutions and Two-State Framework

This cluster represents Israel's firm rejection of UN-backed resolutions endorsing the two-state solution and Palestinian statehood, especially when such resolutions exclude or isolate Hamas. Israel views these measures as 'shameful,' 'disgraceful,' or encouraging terrorism and Hamas, which it considers a lethal threat to its security. Israeli leadership, including Prime Minister Netanyahu, explicitly refuses the establishment of a Palestinian state and pursues policies such as settlement expansion that are seen as undermining peace talks. This perspective highlights Israel's security concerns and skepticism regarding the effectiveness or fairness of international diplomatic efforts.

Support and Solidarity with Hamas and Condemnation of Israeli Military Actions

Countries and entities in this group, often from the Arab and Muslim world including Iran, Qatar, Turkey, Yemen, and organizations like the OIC, express condemnation of Israeli military attacks targeting Hamas officials, especially the strikes against Hamas leaders in Qatar. They view Israel’s actions as aggressive, terroristic, and destabilizing, calling on international bodies to respond or sanction Israel. Qatar’s role as a mediator and host to Hamas leadership complicates regional dynamics, while these countries often emphasize solidarity with Hamas as a legitimate Palestinian resistance movement. This stance reflects opposition to Israeli military tactics and advocacy for Palestinian factions including Hamas.

European Union and Some Western Countries: Balancing Diplomacy and Security Concerns

Many European countries and the European Parliament support the two-state solution, Palestinian recognition, and UN resolutions excluding Hamas, while trying to moderate language to maintain diplomatic balance and avoid escalations. They emphasize humanitarian concerns, peaceful statehood, and coexistence but sometimes restrain explicit condemnations or harsh measures against Israel. Some EU members contemplate or propose sanctions or suspension of agreements with Israel in response to the conflict. This pragmatic stance reflects a desire to foster peace and negotiation while managing internal political divisions and relations with both Israel and Palestine.
