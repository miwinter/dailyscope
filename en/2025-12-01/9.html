Sri Lanka Flood Crisis: Severe Impact and Ongoing Emergency Response

Sri Lanka Flood Crisis 2025-12-01 • 8 articles in total

In brief
  • Cyclone Ditwah caused severe floods in Sri Lanka with death toll estimates between 130 and over 330.
  • Thousands of homes were destroyed, displacing many people and prompting emergency declarations.
  • The government and international aid agencies are conducting rescue and relief efforts amid widespread devastation.

Sri Lanka is facing a devastating flood crisis caused by Cyclone Ditwah and related heavy rains, resulting in a death toll ranging from around 130 to over 330 according to various reports. Thousands of homes have been destroyed, displacing tens of thousands of people and prompting the government to declare states of emergency and disaster. Rescue and relief operations are ongoing amid widespread damage, blocked roads, and overwhelmed resources. The country has appealed for international aid to cope with the humanitarian crisis. Different sources report varying casualty figures but agree on the severity and scale of the disaster.
