Hurricane Melissa: Caribbean Faces Historic Destruction and Emergency Responses

2025-10-29 • 109 articles in total

Hurricane Melissa, a Category 5 storm, has devastated Jamaica with winds reaching up to 300 km/h, causing widespread destruction including damaged hospitals, flooded roads, power outages, and several fatalities. Authorities across Jamaica and neighboring countries including Cuba, Haiti, and the Dominican Republic have launched extensive emergency evacuations and declared disaster zones to mitigate further harm. The storm's slow movement and strengthening over warm waters have heightened its catastrophic impact, prompting international aid and humanitarian responses. Following the destruction in Jamaica, Melissa has downgraded somewhat but continues to threaten Cuba and the Bahamas with severe weather, while other natural disasters such as earthquakes and flooding in Southeast Asia underscore the global vulnerability to extreme events. The Caribbean region is coordinating efforts involving health organizations, government agencies, and regional utilities to address immediate needs and infrastructure recovery.
