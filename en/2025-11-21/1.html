Divergent Views on US-Backed Peace Plan for Ukraine Conflict

2025-11-21 • 125 articles in total

The United States, under the Trump administration, has proposed a detailed 28-point peace plan aiming to end the Russia-Ukraine war by requiring Ukraine to make significant territorial concessions, notably ceding Donetsk, Luhansk, and Crimea to Russia, alongside military reductions. While the US and Russia appear to have cooperated on this plan, it has sparked widespread controversy and skepticism, especially within Ukraine and Europe. Ukrainian President Zelensky has shown cautious engagement, expressing willingness to discuss but also strong opposition, viewing the plan as tantamount to capitulation. European nations and the EU emphasize the necessity of Ukraine and European involvement in any peace negotiations, often rejecting the plan as rewarding Russian aggression. The plan's reception reveals deep divisions among international actors regarding the balance between diplomatic resolution and preserving Ukraine's sovereignty.

United States and Russia: Advocates of the 28-Point Peace Plan

The US administration, particularly under Trump, in coordination with Russia, has developed a comprehensive 28-point peace plan that includes territorial concessions by Ukraine and military reductions, aiming to end the conflict through diplomatic means. They view the plan as mutually beneficial and a pragmatic approach to cease hostilities, emphasizing security guarantees and Western involvement to enforce compliance. This perspective prioritizes conflict resolution even at the cost of significant compromises by Ukraine.

Ukraine: Cautious Engagement and Strong Opposition

Ukraine, led by President Zelensky, has received the US peace plan and is engaging in discussions, including with Trump, but remains wary and often opposes the plan's terms, viewing them as a forced capitulation that undermines sovereignty and territorial integrity. Ukrainian officials and public opinion express concern over the plan's demands, especially territorial concessions and military reductions, while seeking a 'dignified peace' that respects national interests. This stance reflects the tension between diplomatic pragmatism and national resistance to perceived unfavorable compromises.

European Union and European Countries: Demand Inclusion and Reject Capitulation

European nations and the EU strongly insist that any peace plan must include Ukraine and European stakeholders to ensure legitimacy and respect for sovereignty. They express skepticism and opposition to the US-Russia peace plan, viewing it as rewarding Russian aggression and amounting to Ukrainian surrender. European leaders emphasize that peace should not come at the cost of capitulation and call for coordinated, inclusive diplomatic efforts to resolve the conflict.

Turkey, under President Erdoğan, is actively seeking to mediate the conflict by facilitating dialogue between Ukraine and Russia. The country aims to build momentum for peace negotiations and reduce hostilities, positioning itself as an influential international peace broker. This reflects Ankara's strategic interest in regional stability and diplomatic engagement.

Critical Voices and Analysts: Warning of Negative Consequences

Some analysts and commentators, including former intelligence officials, warn that the US peace plan could backfire by damaging US-Ukraine relations and undermining Ukraine's sovereignty. They criticize the plan as a 'Kremlin fantasy' or a disguised capitulation, cautioning against its acceptance. These perspectives highlight the risks of the plan exacerbating tensions rather than resolving them.
