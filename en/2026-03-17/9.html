Cuba's Worsening Nationwide Power Crisis

Cuba Power Crisis 2026-03-17 • 9 articles in total

In brief
  • Cuba faces severe nationwide blackouts due to energy shortages, infrastructure issues, and economic difficulties worsened by US embargoes.
  • The government is using emergency power systems and investigating the causes while the blackouts disrupt daily life and essential services.
  • International views differ, with some blaming US sanctions and others focusing on the crisis' humanitarian impact without assigning blame.

Cuba is experiencing repeated and severe nationwide blackouts due to longstanding energy shortages and infrastructural challenges. The government has activated emergency power systems while investigations into the causes of these outages continue. Economic difficulties, underfunding, and external factors such as US-imposed embargoes are cited as key contributors to the crisis. The blackouts have disrupted daily life, essential services, and sparked public unrest. International reactions vary, with some emphasizing geopolitical tensions and others focusing on humanitarian concerns.
