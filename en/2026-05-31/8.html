U.S. Reverses Overseas Green‑Card Departure Rule After Backlash

US Immigration Policy Reversal 2026-05-31 • 8 articles in total

In brief
  • U.S. authorities reversed a proposed rule requiring some green-card applicants to leave the country, now handling cases individually.
  • The reversal followed protests, political pressure, and criticism of hostile immigrant messaging.
  • The policy flip occurred amid broader immigration debates, including controversies over refugee admission increases.

U.S. authorities have reversed or softened a proposed rule that would have required some green-card applicants to leave the country to complete overseas processing. The Department of Homeland Security says decisions will be handled on a case-by-case basis rather than as a blanket requirement, and multiple outlets report the administration backtracked after protests, clashes and political pressure from lawmakers and immigrant advocates. Coverage differs by source: some emphasize street demonstrations and legal relief for affected migrants, others stress the need for clearer procedural guidance for overseas applications, while critics highlight hostile White House messaging toward immigrants. At the same time, separate immigration moves — such as a contested increase in the refugee admissions cap — have added diplomatic and public-relations tensions.
