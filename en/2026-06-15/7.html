Missouri skydiving plane crash kills 12

Missouri Parachute Plane Crash 2026-06-15 • 24 articles in total

In brief
  • A small aircraft carrying 11 parachutists and a pilot crashed near Butler, Missouri, killing all 12 aboard.
  • Authorities are investigating the cause, examining weather, maintenance, and flight operations while emphasizing safety concerns in skydiving.
  • The crash involves a New Zealand-made plane and is one of the deadliest U.S. skydiving incidents near a local airport and skydiving operation.

A small aircraft carrying parachutists crashed in western Missouri near Butler, killing 12 people aboard. Emergency responders secured the scene and local and state authorities opened an investigation to determine the cause, with officials examining weather, maintenance records and flight operations. Many reports emphasize the tragedy as a deadly recreational aviation accident and note that victims’ identities have not yet been released.
