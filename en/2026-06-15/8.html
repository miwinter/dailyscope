Geneva Protests Ahead of G7: Scale, Demands and Clashes

G7 Summit Protests 2026-06-15 • 22 articles in total

In brief
  • Thousands protested in Geneva before the G7 summit demanding climate justice, economic fairness, and greater accountability in global governance.
  • Swiss police used crowd-control tactics to manage large demonstrations that escalated into clashes and property damage, including attacks on a UN office.
  • The unrest included rioting, injuries, and arrests, highlighting security risks during the summit period.

Thousands gathered in Geneva ahead of the G7 summit to voice broad public dissent over global governance, economic policy and calls for climate justice and humanitarian aid. Organizers framed demonstrations as demands for greater accountability and fairer international policymaking, while many reports highlighted the sheer scale of the mobilizations. Swiss authorities deployed large numbers of police and used crowd-control tactics such as kettling, tear gas and water cannons to manage the demonstrations. In multiple locations initially peaceful marches escalated into clashes, rioting and notable property damage including attacks on a UN office and the burning of a Tesla, drawing international attention to tensions around the summit.
