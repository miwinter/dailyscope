Summit diplomacy under strain: G7, Iran deal momentum and high‑stakes backchannels

World Leaders Dialogue 2026-06-15 • 105 articles in total

In brief
  • The G7 summit featured limited consensus and prominent protests, with leaders like Macron promoting a rules-based order amid internal disagreements.
  • Trump's unpredictable talks with Putin and Zelensky influenced Ukraine and Middle East diplomacy, while mediation efforts cautiously advanced a US-Iran agreement.
  • Ukraine pushed EU accession and security support using G7 forums, as global diplomacy included routine state visits and calls for multilateral conflict resolution reforms.

World leaders gathered around the G7 meeting as both a forum for cooperation and a stage for sharp disagreements, with large protests in Geneva and limited consensus on key dossiers. Simultaneously, intense mediation efforts and regional envoys pushed progress toward a US‑Iran agreement, though officials repeatedly stressed that final texts, ratification and caveats remain unresolved. U.S. president Donald Trump emerged as a pivotal, unpredictable actor in reporting—receiving calls and holding talks with Vladimir Putin and Volodymyr Zelensky—that could reshape momentum on Ukraine and Middle East talks. Kyiv advanced formal EU accession steps and used G7 diplomacy to press its security case, while a steady stream of bilateral visits, condolences and development initiatives illustrated active statecraft across regions. The coverage reflects an unsettled diplomatic landscape where public dissent, informal backchannels and formal multilateral efforts interact to shape near‑term outcomes.
