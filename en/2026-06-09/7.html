Court Blocks $100,000 H‑1B Fee

US Visa Fee Ruling 2026-06-09 • 6 articles in total

In brief
  • U.S. federal judges ruled the Trump-era $100,000 H-1B visa fee unlawful and likened it to an improper tax.
  • The decision blocks the administration’s attempt to fund visa processing through high fees and affects future immigration financing.
  • Employers and skilled workers welcome the relief, anticipating impacts on hiring and immigration policy.

U.S. federal judges have struck down or blocked the Trump‑era proposal to impose an approximately $100,000 fee on H‑1B visa petitions, with rulings calling the charge unlawful and in some reports likening it to an unlawful tax. Coverage emphasizes immediate relief for employers and skilled foreign workers and frames the decision as a constraint on the administration’s attempt to fund visa processing through steep fees. Legal commentary highlights that the rulings undermine the government’s fee‑setting approach and could prompt revisions to how immigration programs are financed. Observers warn the outcome may ripple through hiring practices in the technology sector and shape future policy and litigation over immigration costs.
