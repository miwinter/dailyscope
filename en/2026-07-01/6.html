Supreme Court preserves birthright citizenship

US Birthright Citizenship 2026-07-01 • 32 articles in total

In brief
  • The U.S. Supreme Court blocked the executive order that sought to end birthright citizenship, upholding the 14th Amendment.
  • The ruling limited executive power on citizenship and sparked strong political reactions, especially from President Trump.
  • The decision affects future immigration laws and impacts immigrant families and communities nationwide.

The U.S. Supreme Court rejected efforts to restrict birthright citizenship, reaffirming longstanding interpretations of the 14th Amendment and preventing an executive order from removing automatic citizenship for most children born on U.S. soil. Several reports describe a definitive ruling that struck down or blocked the Trump administration’s decree, while others covered procedural pauses and pending appeals. The decision prompted strong political reactions, with President Trump criticizing the outcome and urging congressional action to change the law. Legal analysts say the ruling limits executive power on nationality questions and will shape future immigration policy debates. News outlets also emphasized what the ruling means for immigrant families and communities potentially affected by any attempted changes to birthright rules.
