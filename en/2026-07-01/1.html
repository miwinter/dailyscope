Widespread destruction, multinational rescue effort and growing anger over Venezuela quake response

Venezuela Earthquake Response 2026-07-01 • 116 articles in total

In brief
  • Two major earthquakes in Venezuela caused thousands of deaths and widespread destruction, complicating rescue due to aftershocks and damaged infrastructure.
  • International and regional responders, coordinated by the UN, deployed extensive rescue teams and supplies amid rising local frustration over slow, unclear aid.
  • Humanitarian groups warn of urgent health and food crises, emphasizing the need for sustained medical, shelter, and sanitation assistance.

Two powerful earthquakes devastated large parts of Venezuela, killing well over a thousand people and damaging or destroying tens of thousands of buildings, while aftershocks, rain and damaged infrastructure have complicated rescue and relief work. Dozens of countries and international organizations have deployed thousands of rescuers, supplies and logistical support — including large airlifts, naval shipments and on-the-ground engineering teams — coordinated in many cases by the UN. Amid dramatic survivor rescues, including children found alive days after collapses, public frustration is rising as many Venezuelans accuse authorities of slow, unclear or ineffective relief. Humanitarian agencies and the WHO warn of food shortages, disease risks and a narrowing window to find survivors, prompting calls for sustained international aid and shelter, medical and sanitation assistance.
