Widespread anti-immigrant protests expose tensions in South Africa

South Africa Immigration Protests 2026-07-01 • 17 articles in total

In brief
  • Mass anti-immigrant protests erupted in South Africa due to economic hardship, job competition, and divisive political rhetoric.
  • Demonstrations included peaceful marches and violent episodes, causing migrants to flee amid threats and intimidation.
  • The government deployed police and issued warnings, balancing security and migration policy amid rising xenophobia.

A wave of anti-immigrant protests swept South Africa, with thousands marching in multiple cities to demand the expulsion of undocumented foreigners. Analysts link the unrest to economic hardship, job competition fears and inflammatory political rhetoric, while civil-society actors warn of rising xenophobia. Demonstrations ranged from largely peaceful marches to episodes of violence, looting and activist-set deadlines that prompted migrants to flee. Many migrants described threats and intimidation, and officials reported tens of thousands departing as authorities scrambled to maintain order. The government deployed police and issued stern warnings, facing pressure to balance security, social cohesion and longer-term migration policy responses.
