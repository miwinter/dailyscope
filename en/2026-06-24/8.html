Fujimori Leads as Legitimacy Dispute Looms

Peru Election Watch 2026-06-24 • 9 articles in total

In brief
  • Keiko Fujimori leads a razor-thin presidential runoff with about 99.8% of votes counted but some ballots remain.
  • Opposition accuses fraud and refuses to accept Fujimori's win, risking a legitimacy crisis and contested transition.
  • The election reflects a broader regional shift to the right influencing politics and diplomacy in Latin America.

Peru's presidential runoff is extraordinarily close, with Keiko Fujimori holding a narrow lead at roughly 50.11% as vote counts near completion. Multiple reports indicate she may be proclaimed president-elect with about 99.8% of ballots tallied, though a margin of roughly 42,000 votes and tens of thousands of remaining or overseas ballots keep the outcome uncertain. Opposition figures, including Roberto Sánchez and other leftist leaders, have denounced the count, alleged fraud, and said they will not recognize a Fujimori government, raising immediate legitimacy and transition risks. Observers also frame the result as part of a broader regional shift to the right in Latin America, intensifying attention on political polarization and potential legal challenges.
