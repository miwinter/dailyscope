North Korea Announces Naval Nuclear Armament and Shipbuilding Push

North Korea Arms Build-Up 2026-06-24 • 10 articles in total

In brief
  • North Korea is equipping its navy and submarines with nuclear weapons and plans to build larger warships rapidly.
  • The country aims to expand nuclear forces to surpass global military capabilities as part of its modernization efforts.
  • Analysts warn these developments will increase regional tensions and complicate international diplomacy.

Across multiple reports, North Korean leader Kim Jong Un has announced that the country is equipping its navy—and submarines—with nuclear weapons while unveiling plans for larger warships and accelerated shipbuilding. State media described commissioning ceremonies and detailed ambitions for 5,000–10,000-ton vessels and orders to build multiple warships per year, including missile-launching cruisers. Some outlets relay Pyongyang’s rhetoric about expanding nuclear strength to ‘surpass the world,’ framing the moves as part of a broader military modernization. Analysts and international commentators warn the developments will heighten regional tensions, complicate diplomacy, and draw increased scrutiny from allies and the global community.
