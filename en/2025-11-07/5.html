Typhoon Kalmaegi Causes Widespread Death and Devastation in the Philippines and Vietnam

2025-11-07 • 25 articles in total

Typhoon Kalmaegi has inflicted severe casualties and extensive damage in the Philippines, with reported death tolls ranging from 26 to nearly 190, and many people missing. The hardest-hit areas include central regions and provinces such as Cebu, prompting declarations of states of emergency and extensive rescue operations. The storm has moved westward, reaching Vietnam where it continues to cause fatalities, flooding, and destruction, although with fewer deaths reported compared to the Philippines. The event highlights the growing vulnerability of Southeast Asian countries to extreme weather events exacerbated by climate change, pressing authorities to boost preparedness and response efforts. Recovery and relief operations are underway, but challenges remain due to the scale and severity of the disaster.
