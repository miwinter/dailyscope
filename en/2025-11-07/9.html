Fragile Humanitarian Ceasefire Amid Ongoing Sudan Conflict

2025-11-07 • 19 articles in total

Multiple reports detail a humanitarian ceasefire in Sudan largely agreed upon by the paramilitary Rapid Support Forces (RSF) under a US-led proposal, aiming to reduce violence and enable aid delivery. However, the Sudanese army has rejected this ceasefire and announced continued fighting, reflecting deep divisions among the warring factions. Sporadic explosions and ongoing clashes near Khartoum shortly after the ceasefire declaration highlight the unstable security situation and potential violations. International actors including the US, Egypt, UAE, Saudi Arabia, and regional groups continue pressing for peace, though skepticism remains due to previous failed accords. Humanitarian concerns grow urgent as displaced populations face worsening conditions amid uncertain prospects for sustained peace.

Support from Western and Allied Nations on US-Led Ceasefire

Countries like the United States, United Kingdom, Norway, Ireland, Portugal, Switzerland, the Netherlands, France, and India primarily emphasize the RSF's acceptance of the US-proposed humanitarian ceasefire as a critical step toward easing violence and facilitating aid. Their perspective highlights diplomatic efforts led by the US and allied actors seeking to stabilize Sudan through negotiation, viewing the ceasefire as a hopeful but fragile development in mitigating the conflict's human toll.

Concern Over Ceasefire Fragility and Ongoing Violence

Reports from countries including Turkey, Kuwait, Greece, and Sweden emphasize the fragile nature of the ceasefire, noting that the Sudanese army rejects the truce and fighting continues. They describe incidents of explosions close to Khartoum, stalled negotiations, and warnings from the UN about worsening humanitarian conditions. This viewpoint underscores skepticism about the ceasefire's durability and stresses the complex political and military challenges undermining peace efforts in Sudan.
