Patchwork of bilateral deals: security, investment and discreet diplomacy

Region: Bilateral Deals 2026-05-14 • 148 articles in total

In brief
  • Major powers and regional actors expand bilateral ties through security, trade, and development agreements across Africa, Asia, the Pacific, and the Arab world.
  • Diplomacy includes discreet negotiations, humanitarian aid as leverage, and pragmatic, issue-specific deals to promote economic recovery and stability.
  • States pursue strategic partnerships emphasizing long-term development, regional leadership, security cooperation, and geopolitical influence through high-level visits and investment commitments.

Recent reporting shows a surge in bilateral activity across regions, with major powers and regional actors deepening security, trade and development ties. China’s outreach spans Africa, the Arab world and small island states through investment, cooperation agreements and sectoral deals, while Russia sustains defence and strategic partnerships in Africa and beyond. High-profile discreet diplomacy is visible in contested reporting of an Israeli visit to the UAE, and the United States’ conditional humanitarian offers to Cuba underscore how aid can become a bilateral bargaining chip. Numerous smaller but consequential agreements — from trade roadmaps and export pacts to defence drills and consular arrangements — reflect pragmatic, often incremental diplomacy focused on reconstruction, connectivity and economic recovery.
