Beijing Summit: Partnership Rhetoric, Business Push and Security Cautions

US-China Diplomacy 2026-05-14 • 189 articles in total

In brief
  • President Trump's visit to China combined economic aims with security discussions, focusing on market access and issues like Taiwan and AI.
  • Chinese media highlighted diplomatic reset and partnership rhetoric from Xi Jinping, while U.S. media emphasized business leverage and negotiation efforts.
  • International analysts warned of limited breakthroughs, security risks, and the need to manage rivalry despite cooperative gestures.

President Trump’s state visit to China brought high‑level meetings, ceremonials and repeated calls from Xi Jinping for the United States and China to be “partners, not rivals.” The trip combined clear economic aims — a large U.S. business delegation and pressure for market access — with a security agenda that kept Iran, Taiwan, supply chains and AI squarely on the table. Chinese state and many host‑country outlets emphasized warm optics and a diplomatic reset, while U.S. and business reporting stressed leverage, commercial opportunity and efforts to open China. At the same time, analysts and allied governments warned breakthroughs might be limited and cautioned about strategic trade‑offs, secret deals, and the broader regional security implications.
