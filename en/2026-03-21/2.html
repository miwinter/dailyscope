Tensions and Cooperation in US Allies Diplomacy Amid Iran Conflict

US Allies Diplomacy 2026-03-21 • 92 articles in total

In brief
  • President Trump criticizes NATO allies for lack of support against Iran, calling them 'cowards' and a 'paper tiger'.
  • Key US allies like the UK, Japan, and Gulf states cooperate through base access, arms deals, and shared security efforts.
  • Some countries such as Sri Lanka and Italy remain neutral or cautious about US military actions in the Middle East.

The United States' diplomacy with its allies is marked by significant tensions and strategic cooperation, especially regarding the Iran conflict and regional security. President Trump has repeatedly criticized NATO allies, labeling them as "cowards" and a "paper tiger" for their perceived lack of support in military actions against Iran, highlighting strains in transatlantic relations. Meanwhile, key allies such as the UK, Japan, and Gulf states have shown varying degrees of cooperation, including granting base access for US operations and engaging in arms deals, reflecting complex alliance dynamics. Divergent priorities between the US and partners like Israel and Japan further complicate coordinated efforts, while some countries like Sri Lanka and Italy maintain cautious or neutral stances. Additionally, trade and security collaborations continue with the EU and other partners, underscoring the multifaceted nature of US diplomacy reliant on allied cooperation amid geopolitical challenges.
