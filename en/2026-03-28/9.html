Arrest of Nepal's Former PM Highlights Political Turmoil and Accountability Issues

Nepal Political Arrests 2026-03-28 • 9 articles in total

In brief
  • Nepal's former Prime Minister KP Sharma Oli was arrested over deaths during Generation Z protests, raising questions about political accountability.
  • The arrests occur amid Nepal's political transition and youth-led unrest, emphasizing ongoing instability and government change.
  • International reports highlight legal charges, political tensions, and the volatility of Nepal's evolving political landscape.

Nepal's former Prime Minister KP Sharma Oli has been arrested in connection with deaths during Generation Z protests, marking a significant moment in the country's ongoing political tensions. The arrests are tied to allegations of negligence and crackdown on protesters, raising questions about political accountability and the handling of public unrest. This development coincides with a period of government transition and political upheaval, including the appointment of a new prime minister from an unconventional background. International coverage reflects varied focuses, from legal proceedings and charges to broader implications for Nepal's political stability and public order. The case continues to unfold amid scrutiny and potential further arrests, underscoring the volatility in Nepal's political landscape.
