Venezuela's Oil Sector Reform and US Sanctions Easing

Venezuela Oil Overhaul 2026-01-30 • 16 articles in total

In brief
  • Venezuela reformed its hydrocarbons law to allow private and foreign investment in the oil sector amid economic challenges.
  • The US partially lifted sanctions on Venezuela's oil company but kept control over its revenues.
  • These changes aim to revive Venezuela's oil industry and shift its economic and international relations landscape.

Venezuela has enacted significant reforms to its hydrocarbons law, opening the oil sector to private and foreign investment for the first time in years. This legislative shift coincides with a partial easing of US sanctions, including lifting restrictions on the state oil company Pdvsa, though the US retains control over revenues. The reforms aim to revitalize Venezuela's struggling oil industry and attract international capital amid ongoing economic challenges. Perspectives vary internationally, with many viewing these changes as a strategic recalibration of Venezuela's energy policy and its relations with the US. The combined legal and diplomatic moves signal a potential new era for Venezuela's oil sector and broader economic recovery efforts.
