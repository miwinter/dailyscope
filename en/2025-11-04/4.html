Diplomatic Crisis: Peru Cuts Ties with Mexico over Asylum to Ex-PM Betssy Chávez

2025-11-04 • 30 articles in total

Peru has officially severed diplomatic relations with Mexico following Mexico's decision to grant asylum to former Peruvian Prime Minister Betssy Chávez, who is implicated in political controversies linked to ex-president Pedro Castillo. This diplomatic rupture highlights deep political tensions and accusations of interference in Peru's internal affairs. While Peru views Mexico's asylum grant as an unfriendly act undermining its justice system, international experts emphasize Peru's obligations under treaty law. The break has significant repercussions for bilateral relations and regional stability in Latin America, with both nations reevaluating their diplomatic strategies amid the crisis.

Peruvian Government Perspective on Diplomatic Severance

The Peruvian government perceives Mexico's granting of asylum to Betssy Chávez as an unfriendly gesture that interferes with Peru’s sovereignty and judicial processes. Peru has officially severed diplomatic ties, marking this as a substantial escalation in political tensions. The move reflects Peru’s stance that Mexico’s asylum decision undermines its national legal institutions and political stability. Peru is awaiting formal communication from Mexico for any future dialogue, underscoring a firm diplomatic break driven by internal political conflicts tied to the Castillo administration.

From Mexico's and some international perspectives, the asylum granted to Betssy Chávez is seen as legitimate under international law, with experts stressing Peru’s obligations under such treaties, including permitting her safe departure if requested by Mexico. This view underscores respect for international asylum protocols despite the diplomatic fallout.
