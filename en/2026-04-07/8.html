Bangladesh Responds to Deadly Measles Outbreak with Emergency Vaccinations

Bangladesh Measles Outbreak 2026-04-07 • 5 articles in total

In brief
  • Bangladesh faces a severe measles outbreak causing over 100 child deaths.
  • The government launched a nationwide emergency vaccination campaign to control the outbreak.
  • Health authorities urge community participation and emphasize the need for rapid, well-resourced response.

Bangladesh is facing a severe measles outbreak that has resulted in the deaths of over 100 children within a short period. In response, the government has launched a nationwide emergency vaccination campaign to contain the spread and protect vulnerable populations. Health authorities are mobilizing resources, including vaccination teams and disease surveillance, while urging communities and parents to participate actively. The outbreak highlights ongoing public health challenges in ensuring immunization coverage and preventing preventable childhood diseases. International and local agencies emphasize the urgency of a rapid and well-resourced response to mitigate further fatalities.
