Escalation of strikes, 40‑day operations and allied response

Russo-Ukrainian War 2026-06-26 • 63 articles in total

In brief
  • Ukraine has launched coordinated 40-day intelligence and drone strikes targeting Russian energy and military sites, escalating the conflict.
  • Western allies and NATO increase military aid, sanctions, and production while labeling Russia a long-term security threat.
  • Russia claims defensive successes against attacks, denies plans to involve Belarus, and tensions escalate with ongoing frontline casualties and diplomatic expulsions.

The coverage shows a marked escalation: Ukraine has sanctioned coordinated 40‑day SBU and intelligence operations and stepped up long‑range drone and strike campaigns reaching into Russian territory, targeting energy and military‑linked sites. Western allies and NATO are reinforcing support with weapons deals, production increases, loans and extended sanctions while stressing Russia as a long‑term security threat. Moscow responds by framing recent events as defensive successes — claiming to have shot down dozens of drones, repelling attacks and shifting air defenses toward the capital — while diplomatic tensions and tit‑for‑tat expulsions intensify. Frontline reports and regional pieces underline continuing casualties, infrastructure outages (notably in Crimea), and the risk of wider spillover through Belarus and maritime incidents.

Russian and pro‑Moscow reports emphasize defensive successes and damage mitigation, claiming to have intercepted or shot down large drone raids and to have struck Ukrainian command nodes. The Kremlin narrative stresses resilience, denies intentions to drag Belarus openly into the war, and foregrounds Moscow’s security responses to cross‑border attacks.

NATO and Western allies: deterrence, aid and sanctions

Western and NATO sources stress that Russia remains a long‑term threat and outline stepped‑up deterrence through arms deals, increased military production and large aid packages for Ukraine, while sustaining sanctions and maritime monitoring. The emphasis is on sustained political, financial and military support to prevent further Russian gains and to bolster European security.
