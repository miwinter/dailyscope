Durban migrant surge and rising xenophobic tensions

South Africa Migration Crisis 2026-06-26 • 5 articles in total

In brief
  • Durban's repatriation site is overwhelmed by migrant influx, needing urgent humanitarian and logistical support.
  • Xenophobic unrest and a foreign national's death have increased national tensions and prompted police investigations.
  • Public debate is divided between calls for stricter immigration controls and demands for inclusive reforms addressing economic issues.

A recent surge of migrants has overwhelmed Durban's repatriation site, stretching capacity and prompting calls for extra logistical and humanitarian support. The influx has intensified national tensions, with xenophobic unrest and a foreign national's death now under police investigation. Public debate is polarised: organised marches and calls for tougher border controls vie with civil-society and expert warnings that scapegoating migrants will not solve underlying economic problems. Authorities must balance urgent assistance and orderly processing with measures to maintain public order while pursuing longer-term regional border-management reforms.
