Escalating US-Iran Nuclear Talks Amid Military Buildup and Diplomatic Efforts

US–Iran Nuclear Talks 2026-02-21 • 75 articles in total

In brief
  • Iran prepares a draft nuclear deal for the US while reinforcing military defenses and warning of retaliation.
  • The US combines diplomatic talks with military threats, deploying forces in the Middle East to pressure Iran.
  • International actors warn of risks from escalating tensions and urge diplomatic solutions to prevent regional instability.

Tensions between the United States and Iran remain high as diplomatic negotiations over Iran's nuclear program continue alongside significant military preparations by both sides. Iran is preparing a draft nuclear agreement to present to the US within days, signaling a willingness to engage in dialogue, while the US, under President Trump, has repeatedly threatened limited military strikes if talks fail. The US has deployed substantial naval and air forces to the Middle East, including aircraft carriers, indicating readiness for potential conflict. Iran has responded by fortifying its military and nuclear sites and issuing stern warnings of decisive retaliation against any aggression. International actors express concern over the fragile situation, emphasizing the risks of escalation and the importance of diplomatic solutions.

Iranian Perspective: Diplomatic Engagement Coupled with Defensive Posture

Iran emphasizes its readiness to negotiate a nuclear agreement, preparing a draft deal to present to the United States, and clarifies that it has not agreed to cease uranium enrichment entirely. Simultaneously, Iran is strengthening its military defenses and issuing warnings of strong retaliation against any military aggression, reflecting a dual approach of diplomacy and deterrence. Iranian officials stress that the US has not demanded complete cessation of nuclear activities and seek to maintain dialogue despite rising tensions.

United States Perspective: Military Pressure and Conditional Diplomacy

The US administration, led by President Trump, is pursuing a strategy combining diplomatic negotiations with the threat and preparation of limited military strikes against Iran. The US has significantly increased its military presence in the Middle East, deploying aircraft carriers, bombers, and other forces to signal readiness for potential conflict. While negotiations continue, the US maintains pressure on Iran to curb its nuclear program, with officials considering targeted strikes and emphasizing prevention of nuclear weapon development.
