Trump's 10-15 Day Ultimatum to Iran Amid Rising Military Threats

Trump Iran Ultimatum 2026-02-21 • 20 articles in total

In brief
  • President Trump set a strict 10-15 day deadline for a nuclear deal with Iran, threatening possible limited military strikes.
  • Iran is preparing counterproposals and warns of strong responses to attacks while engaging in diplomacy.
  • Military buildup and naval tensions increase risk of conflict and raise global concerns about regional instability.

U.S. President Donald Trump set a firm 10 to 15-day deadline for Iran to reach a nuclear agreement, warning of severe consequences including possible limited military strikes if negotiations fail. This ultimatum has escalated tensions, with Iran preparing counterproposals and issuing warnings of decisive responses to any attacks. The U.S. military buildup in the Middle East and naval confrontations underscore the heightened risk of conflict. International observers remain concerned about the fragile diplomatic efforts and the potential for regional instability. The situation reflects a high-stakes brinkmanship between the two nations with global implications.
