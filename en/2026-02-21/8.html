US Military Continues Lethal Strikes on Suspected Narcotrafficking Vessels in the Pacific

US Military Narcoterror Strike 2026-02-21 • 16 articles in total

In brief
  • The US military has conducted multiple lethal strikes against narcotrafficking vessels in the eastern Pacific, causing at least 148 deaths across 43 strikes.
  • These operations are part of a Trump-era campaign targeting drug trafficking and terrorism, raising legal and ethical controversies internationally.
  • Regional countries report cocaine seizures and cooperative efforts that complement US military actions against maritime drug trafficking.

The United States military has conducted multiple lethal strikes targeting vessels suspected of narcotrafficking in the eastern Pacific Ocean, resulting in the deaths of three individuals in the latest operation. These strikes are part of an ongoing campaign under the Trump administration aimed at disrupting drug trafficking networks and narcoterrorism linked to designated terrorist organizations. While the US highlights these operations as successful efforts to combat organized crime and illicit drug flows, the legality and extrajudicial nature of the strikes have raised questions and controversies internationally. The campaign has involved numerous attacks, with at least 148 deaths reported across 43 strikes, reflecting an intensified US military presence beyond traditional conflict zones. Regional countries also report significant cocaine seizures, indicating a broader cooperative effort to address maritime drug trafficking challenges.
