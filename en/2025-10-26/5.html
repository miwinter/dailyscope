Hurricane Melissa Threatens Caribbean with Intensifying Wind and Flood Risks

2025-10-26 • 32 articles in total

Hurricane Melissa has rapidly intensified into a major storm, reaching Category 3 status as it moves through the northern Caribbean, with Jamaica, Haiti, the Dominican Republic, and surrounding islands under severe threat. Fatalities have been reported in Haiti and the Dominican Republic, while heavy rains and strong winds raise concerns of flooding and landslides, especially in vulnerable displaced persons camps. Authorities across the region, supported by international aid organizations and local governments, are closely monitoring the storm's path, issuing warnings, and preparing emergency responses. Evacuations, especially for foreign nationals, are underway and efforts to strengthen disaster response are highlighted. Meanwhile, the broader regional context includes other natural disaster challenges, such as flooding in South Sudan and geological events in Africa, underscoring the global impact of environmental crises.
