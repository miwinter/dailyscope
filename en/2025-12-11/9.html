In brief

  • Her public presence in Oslo symbolizes hope and resistance amid Venezuela's political crisis.

María Corina Machado, Venezuelan opposition leader and 2025 Nobel Peace Prize laureate, made her first public appearance in Oslo after nearly a year in hiding due to political threats from the Maduro regime. Her covert departure from Venezuela, reportedly supported by the US, and subsequent arrival in Norway mark a significant moment for the Venezuelan opposition on the international stage. Machado's presence in Oslo, including participation in Nobel-related events and public speeches, underscores ongoing calls for democratic change and resistance against authoritarianism in Venezuela. The international coverage emphasizes both the precarious situation of opposition figures in Venezuela and the global recognition of Machado's efforts for peace and freedom. Her reemergence is seen as a beacon of hope amid Venezuela's political crisis and a rallying point for continued opposition activism.
