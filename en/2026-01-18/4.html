US-led Gaza Peace Initiatives Amidst Regional Tensions

US–Gaza Ceasefire and Peace Efforts 2026-01-18 • 51 articles in total

In brief
  • The US, led by President Trump, formed a multilateral Peace Council including international figures and allied nations to stabilize Gaza using diplomatic and governance efforts.
  • Israel strongly opposes the US-led Peace Council and governance initiatives in Gaza, rejecting their legitimacy and citing security and sovereignty concerns.
  • Countries respond variably to US peace plans, with some participating and others showing skepticism amid complex regional tensions and humanitarian challenges.

The United States, under President Donald Trump, has spearheaded the formation of a Peace Council or Board of Peace aimed at stabilizing Gaza through diplomatic and governance efforts. This council includes prominent international figures such as Marco Rubio, Tony Blair, Jared Kushner, and invited leaders from countries including Turkey, Egypt, Argentina, and Canada. The US has also initiated a second phase of its Gaza peace plan, establishing Palestinian committees to oversee governance and reconstruction. However, Israel has strongly opposed these US-led initiatives, criticizing the lack of coordination and rejecting the legitimacy of the council and governance committees. The international community remains divided, with some countries accepting invitations to participate while others express skepticism, highlighting the complex and fragile nature of peace efforts in Gaza.

Israeli Government Opposition and Concerns

Israel strongly opposes the US-led Peace Council and related governance initiatives for Gaza, citing lack of coordination and conflict with Israeli policies. Israeli officials, including Prime Minister Netanyahu, have rejected the legitimacy of the council and Palestinian administrative committees proposed by the US. This opposition reflects Israel's concerns over sovereignty, security, and the political implications of external governance arrangements in Gaza. The tensions between Israel and the US on this matter complicate the peace process and highlight divergent priorities.

Mixed International Reactions and Regional Dynamics

Several countries and international actors show varied responses to the US peace initiatives, ranging from acceptance and participation to skepticism and cautious consideration. Countries like Argentina and Canada have accepted invitations, while Turkey and Egypt exhibit more cautious or conditional stances. Human rights advocates and analysts express concerns about the legitimacy and potential neo-colonial aspects of the Peace Council. The fragile ceasefire and ongoing humanitarian challenges in Gaza underscore the complexity of achieving lasting peace amid competing interests and regional tensions.
