Syrian Army Expands Control Over Kurdish-Held Territories Amid Ongoing Conflict

Syrian Army Advance 2026-01-18 • 25 articles in total

In brief
  • The Syrian army has reclaimed key territories in northern and eastern Syria from Kurdish forces, including strategic towns and resource-rich areas.
  • These advances challenge Kurdish autonomy and escalate conflict, drawing opposition from the United States and impacting regional stability.
  • Various sources acknowledge the military gains and political changes, reflecting the complex and volatile situation in Syria's ongoing civil war.

The Syrian army has made significant advances in northern and eastern Syria, reclaiming key territories previously held by Kurdish forces, including strategic towns, military airports, and vital oil and gas fields. These operations have shifted the balance of power in the region, challenging Kurdish autonomy and complicating international dynamics, especially with the United States opposing the offensive. Kurdish forces have responded with withdrawals, curfews, and continued clashes, while ceasefires and political gestures such as recognizing Kurdish language rights have been introduced amid the conflict. The advances underscore the Syrian government's determination to reassert control over resource-rich and strategic areas, intensifying the ongoing civil war and impacting regional stability. Despite international calls for restraint, the Syrian army continues to consolidate gains, signaling a new phase in the conflict's territorial and political landscape.
