Zelenskyy Appoints Kyrylo Budanov to Lead Presidential Office Amid War

Zelenskyy Appoints Budanov 2026-01-03 • 24 articles in total

In brief
  • Kyrylo Budanov was appointed as Zelenskyy's presidential office chief, replacing Andriy Yermak amid a corruption scandal.
  • The appointment aims to strengthen Ukraine's intelligence, security, and governance during the ongoing conflict with Russia.
  • Budanov's military and diplomatic expertise is seen as crucial for wartime leadership and peace negotiations.

Ukrainian President Volodymyr Zelenskyy has appointed Kyrylo Budanov, the head of military intelligence, as the new chief of his presidential office, replacing Andriy Yermak who resigned amid a corruption scandal. This leadership change is widely viewed as a strategic move to strengthen Ukraine's intelligence and security apparatus during the ongoing conflict with Russia. Budanov is recognized for his military expertise and diplomatic experience, which are seen as assets in navigating both military operations and peace negotiations. The appointment reflects Zelenskyy's focus on consolidating governance and enhancing wartime leadership amid escalating Russian attacks and internal political challenges. International coverage highlights the significance of this reshuffle as part of Ukraine's broader efforts to adapt and respond to the complex security environment.
