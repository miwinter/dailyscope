Cyclone Gezani Devastates Madagascar with Heavy Losses and Ongoing Relief Efforts

Cyclone Gezani Devastation 2026-02-13 • 14 articles in total

In brief
  • Cyclone Gezani caused severe destruction, killing 20 to 36 people and displacing hundreds of thousands in Madagascar.
  • The Malagasy government and international organizations are actively involved in emergency relief and recovery operations.
  • The disaster underscores Madagascar's vulnerability to storms and the urgent need for coordinated humanitarian aid and infrastructure rebuilding.

Tropical Cyclone Gezani has caused severe devastation in Madagascar, resulting in a death toll ranging from 20 to 36 and displacing hundreds of thousands of people. The cyclone brought extremely strong winds and heavy rainfall, leading to widespread destruction of homes, infrastructure, and flooding in major cities like Toamasina. Emergency response teams, government officials including the Malagasy president, and international organizations are actively engaged in relief and recovery operations. The disaster highlights Madagascar's vulnerability to tropical storms and the urgent need for coordinated humanitarian aid and rebuilding efforts. Fundraising campaigns and international support are being mobilized to assist the affected communities and restore critical infrastructure.
