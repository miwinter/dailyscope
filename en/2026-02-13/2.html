Bangladesh's Nationalist Party Triumphs Amid Political Transition and Electoral Controversies

Bangladesh Election Watch 2026-02-13 • 29 articles in total

In brief
  • The 2024 parliamentary elections in Bangladesh ended Sheikh Hasina's rule with the BNP winning a two-thirds majority under Tarique Rahman.
  • Most international observers recognize the election as historic and peaceful, while Islamist parties and some opposition allege fraud and unfairness.
  • India and Belgium criticize the elections for alleged violations, demanding free and fair polls amid political tensions.

The recent parliamentary elections in Bangladesh marked a historic turning point following the 2024 Gen Z-led uprising that ousted long-time Prime Minister Sheikh Hasina. The Bangladesh Nationalist Party (BNP), led by Tarique Rahman, claimed a sweeping victory, securing a two-thirds majority and signaling a major political shift. While many international observers and governments have recognized and congratulated the BNP's win, Islamist parties and some opposition figures have raised concerns about electoral integrity and fairness. The elections were conducted amid heightened security and political tensions, with calls for free, fair, and inclusive polls. This political transition is closely watched for its implications on Bangladesh's democratic stability and regional security.

International Recognition of BNP Victory and Political Shift

Countries such as Mexico, Norway, United Arab Emirates, United Kingdom, Brazil, Australia, Nepal, United States, France, Singapore, Pakistan, Saudi Arabia, and Kazakhstan emphasize the BNP's electoral success and the historic nature of the elections following the 2024 uprising. They highlight Tarique Rahman's leadership and the potential for significant changes in Bangladesh's governance and policy direction. These sources generally acknowledge the elections as a milestone in Bangladesh's political landscape and note the peaceful conduct and high voter turnout.

Skepticism and Criticism Regarding Electoral Integrity

Sources from India and Belgium present a critical perspective on the elections, focusing on allegations of electoral violations, manipulation, and lack of fairness. Indian Prime Minister Sheikh Hasina condemned the elections as a 'well-planned farce' and demanded free, fair, and inclusive polls, reflecting ongoing political tensions. Islamist parties and some opposition groups also question the credibility of the results, underscoring concerns about democratic stability and transparency in the electoral process.
