Divergent Views and Pressures Surround US-Backed Ukraine Peace Plan

2025-11-22 • 217 articles in total

The United States has proposed a 28-point peace plan aimed at ending the conflict between Ukraine and Russia, which includes significant territorial concessions to Russia and security guarantees for Ukraine. Russian President Vladimir Putin has expressed cautious openness to the plan, viewing it as a potential basis for peace, while Ukraine's President Volodymyr Zelensky has strongly criticized it, warning of a difficult choice between losing national dignity or risking the loss of key allies, particularly the US. European leaders have expressed concerns about the plan's terms and the exclusion of Ukraine and Europe from its drafting, preparing alternative proposals and emphasizing that no decisions should be made without Ukraine's involvement. The US has exerted considerable pressure on Ukraine to accept the plan by a set deadline, threatening to cut military and intelligence support, which has intensified diplomatic tensions and highlighted the complex geopolitical dynamics. Overall, the peace plan has sparked intense debate, reflecting contrasting national interests and the fragile balance of international diplomacy in resolving the Ukraine conflict.

United States: Advocating and Pressuring for Peace Plan Acceptance

The United States, led by former President Donald Trump, has actively promoted a 28-point peace plan that includes territorial concessions by Ukraine and security guarantees. The US insists on Ukraine's approval of the plan, setting firm deadlines and threatening to reduce military and intelligence aid to pressure Kyiv into acceptance. US officials emphasize the plan as a pathway to end hostilities and stabilize the region, despite criticism and controversy surrounding its terms.

European Union and Allies: Advocating Inclusion and Alternative Approaches

They express concerns about the US plan's terms, which they view as potentially capitulating to Russian demands, and are preparing alternative proposals. The EU stresses respect for Ukraine's sovereignty and territorial integrity and seeks a balanced, multilateral diplomatic approach to the conflict resolution.
