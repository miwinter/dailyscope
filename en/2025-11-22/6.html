Mass Kidnappings of Nigerian Schoolchildren Highlight Severe Security Crisis

2025-11-22 • 31 articles in total

Over 200 students and several teachers were abducted from Catholic schools in Nigeria in a series of violent attacks by armed groups, marking a disturbing escalation in the country's security challenges. These kidnappings, often attributed to jihadist groups like Boko Haram, have sparked national and international outrage, especially due to the targeting of Christian communities. Efforts to rescue the abducted children and staff are ongoing, with Nigerian authorities and international actors involved, though the situation remains precarious. The incidents have intensified calls for stronger security measures and possible foreign intervention to protect vulnerable populations and restore stability. The repeated attacks underscore the persistent threat to education and civilian safety in Nigeria's conflict-affected regions.
