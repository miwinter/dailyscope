Khamenei Blames Trump for Iran's Protest Casualties and Unrest

Khamenei Blasts Trump 2026-01-17 • 11 articles in total

In brief
  • Iran's Supreme Leader Ali Khamenei accuses US President Donald Trump of causing deaths and unrest in Iranian protests.
  • Khamenei claims Trump collaborates with foreign allies to destabilize Iran and fuel internal turmoil.
  • Western sources report Khamenei's accusations as part of ongoing geopolitical tensions between Iran and the US.

Iran's Supreme Leader Ali Khamenei has publicly condemned US President Donald Trump, labeling him a criminal responsible for causing casualties, damage, and suffering during recent protests in Iran. Khamenei accuses Trump of inciting unrest and collaborating with foreign allies like Israel to destabilize Iran. These statements reflect deep-seated animosity toward US policies under Trump's administration and highlight ongoing geopolitical tensions. The protests have resulted in thousands of deaths, which Khamenei attributes to foreign interference, particularly by the US and its allies. This rhetoric underscores the strained relations and heightened volatility between Iran and the West amid internal Iranian unrest.
