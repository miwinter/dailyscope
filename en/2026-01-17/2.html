US-led Initiatives for Gaza Ceasefire and Governance Amid Ongoing Challenges

US–Gaza Ceasefire and Governance Talks 2026-01-17 • 64 articles in total

In brief
  • The US launched a Gaza peace plan with a Peace Council led by Trump to oversee ceasefire, reconstruction, and governance.
  • A Palestinian committee manages Gaza's daily governance under US supervision amid skepticism and ongoing violence.
  • International groups criticize the US-led approach, citing humanitarian crises and doubts about its legitimacy and peace effectiveness.

The United States has launched the second phase of its Gaza peace plan, focusing on ceasefire enforcement, reconstruction, and governance. Central to this effort is the formation of a Peace Council chaired by President Donald Trump, including key figures such as Tony Blair, Marco Rubio, and Jared Kushner, tasked with overseeing Gaza's transitional administration and reconstruction. The US also supports a Palestinian technocrat committee to manage Gaza's day-to-day governance under international supervision. Despite these diplomatic and administrative efforts, skepticism and criticism persist among Palestinians and international observers regarding the effectiveness and legitimacy of these initiatives, especially as violence and Israeli military actions continue. The US aims to expand the Peace Council's authority, potentially positioning it as an alternative to traditional international bodies like the UN, reflecting a strategic shift in conflict resolution approaches.
