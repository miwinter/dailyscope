US FAA Issues Warnings on Military Activities Affecting Latin American Airspace

FAA Latin America Alerts 2026-01-17 • 22 articles in total

In brief
  • The FAA issued warnings about military activities and GPS risks over Mexico, Central America, and parts of South America affecting civil aviation.
  • The warnings reflect increased regional security concerns and urge caution among airlines operating in the affected airspace.
  • Latin American authorities emphasize the need for coordination and highlight impacts on regional aviation and geopolitical stability.

The US Federal Aviation Administration (FAA) has issued multiple warnings to airlines and pilots about ongoing and potential military activities over Mexico, Central America, and parts of South America, including Pacific airspace from Mexico to Ecuador. These alerts, generally lasting 60 days, emphasize risks such as possible military operations and GPS interference that could impact civil aviation safety. The warnings coincide with heightened regional security concerns, including speculation about US military actions related to drug cartels and recent geopolitical developments involving Venezuela. While specific details about the nature or actors of the military activities remain undisclosed, the advisories urge caution and vigilance among airlines operating in the affected airspaces. Regional aviation authorities have responded to these alerts, highlighting the potential implications for flight operations and international cooperation.
