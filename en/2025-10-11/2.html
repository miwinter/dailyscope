Intensification of US-China Trade Tensions over Tariffs and Rare Earths

2025-10-11 • 55 articles in total

Former US President Donald Trump announced an additional 100% tariff on Chinese imports, significantly escalating the ongoing trade war amid growing disputes over rare earth minerals and strategic resources. China's retaliatory measures, including imposing port fees on US vessels, highlight the deepening economic and geopolitical rift. While the United States pursues aggressive tariff hikes and export controls to pressure China, China cautiously engages in trade talks but enforces countermeasures to defend its interests. This escalation threatens global economic stability, supply chains, and international trade relations.

Articles from countries reflecting the US perspective predominantly emphasize Donald Trump's aggressive approach of imposing additional 100% tariffs on Chinese goods, citing China's hostile trade practices and restrictions on rare earth exports. The US views these measures as necessary to counter China's economic and strategic threats, protect domestic industries, and exert pressure on China to change its trade policies. Trump’s rhetoric includes cancelling meetings with Chinese leadership and warning of further retaliatory measures. This perspective stresses the US aim to regain trade balance and address geopolitical competition through tough economic actions.

China’s Strategic Response and Retaliation Measures

Articles originating from China and related sources present a view of cautious engagement in trade talks with the US while simultaneously implementing retaliation through port fees on US ships and tightening export controls on rare earth minerals. China highlights the opportunities within the trade dialogues but underscores the persistent challenges, signaling a willingness to negotiate but also to respond firmly. The country frames these developments as defense against US economic pressure and as efforts to protect its strategic industries and sovereignty.
