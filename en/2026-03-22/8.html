Solidifying South-South Cooperation through Trade, Regional Partnerships, and Political Solidarity

South-South Cooperation 2026-03-22 • 14 articles in total

In brief
  • Global South countries pursue economic cooperation, political solidarity, and regional stability through bilateral and multilateral initiatives.
  • Latin American nations emphasize ideological unity and resistance to external pressures, particularly from the U.S.
  • African and Eurasian regions focus on diplomatic solutions, infrastructure development, and educational exchanges to enhance regional integration despite financing challenges.

The articles collectively highlight diverse efforts among Global South nations to strengthen economic, political, and social ties. Several countries, including Brazil, Mozambique, and Haiti, emphasize bilateral cooperation in agriculture, public finance, and infrastructure development to foster regional growth. Venezuela, Cuba, and Mexico underscore political solidarity and mutual support amid external pressures, reinforcing ideological and humanitarian bonds. Meanwhile, African nations focus on regional stability and conflict de-escalation through diplomatic frameworks and multilateral engagement. These varied initiatives illustrate a shared commitment to unity and collaborative problem-solving within the Global South.

East African Infrastructure Integration Challenges

Kenya and Uganda's planned presidential meeting to discuss the Standard Gauge Railway extension highlights challenges in regional infrastructure development, particularly due to China's refusal to finance further expansion. This situation underscores the complexities of South-South cooperation when external financing conditions impact regional integration projects, prompting the need for alternative strategies and partnerships.
