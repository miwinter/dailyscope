The US–Iran diplomatic landscape under President Trump is marked by contradictory signals, including harsh ultimatums demanding Iran reopen the Strait of Hormuz within 48 hours, coupled with threats to destroy Iranian infrastructure if demands are unmet. Simultaneously, there are indications of potential de-escalation, with discussions about winding down military operations and exploring peace talks conditioned on strict terms. Iran maintains a hardline stance, rejecting ceasefires and threatening retaliation, while accusing the US of psychological warfare. Analysts highlight the complexity and volatility of the situation, with uncertainty about whether diplomacy or confrontation will prevail.
