US-Venezuela Oil Geopolitics: Control, Conflict, and Strategic Interests

US–Venezuela Oil Geopolitics 2026-01-04 • 22 articles in total

In brief
  • The United States aims to control Venezuela's oil resources to weaken Maduro and secure regional energy influence.
  • Venezuela and its allies resist US interventions and accuse them of violating sovereignty and international law.
  • International actors condemn US actions, highlighting the global geopolitical tensions and challenges in Venezuela.

The geopolitical struggle over Venezuela's vast oil reserves is central to the ongoing conflict between the United States and the Maduro regime. The US, particularly under President Donald Trump, has expressed clear intentions to control and exploit Venezuela's oil sector, viewing it as a critical asset for energy security and regional influence. Despite military actions and sanctions, significant logistical, political, and international challenges complicate US efforts to fully access these resources. Venezuela and its allies resist US pressure, while global actors like Russia condemn US interventions, highlighting the broader international tensions. The situation remains fluid, with US plans for investment and control facing both internal and external obstacles amid a contested political transition.
