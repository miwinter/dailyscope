US Military Capture of Venezuelan President Maduro Sparks Global Controversy and Regional Uncertainty

US–Venezuela Maduro Capture Operations 2026-01-04 • 180 articles in total

In brief
  • The US conducted a bold military operation to capture Venezuelan President Nicolás Maduro and transfer him to New York for narcoterrorism charges.
  • The operation caused deep international divisions, with some celebrating and many condemning it as a violation of sovereignty and international law.
  • The capture intensified geopolitical tensions, sparked fears of regional instability, and involved US intentions to govern Venezuela temporarily and control its oil resources.

In a bold and unprecedented military operation, the United States captured Venezuelan President Nicolás Maduro, removing him from Venezuela and transferring him to New York to face narcoterrorism charges. The operation involved extensive intelligence gathering, airstrikes, and special forces, marking a significant escalation in US-Venezuela relations and a direct intervention in Venezuelan affairs. International reactions are deeply divided: while some Venezuelan diaspora and opposition groups celebrate the removal of Maduro, many countries and organizations condemn the operation as a violation of international law and sovereignty. The capture has triggered fears of regional instability, with Venezuela's government vowing military retaliation and uncertainty looming over the country's political future. The US has declared intentions to govern Venezuela temporarily and exploit its vast oil reserves, intensifying geopolitical tensions involving global powers such as Russia, China, and regional actors.
