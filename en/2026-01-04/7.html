US Indicts Maduro for Narcoterrorism and Drug Trafficking

US Indicts Maduro Narcoterrorism 2026-01-04 • 17 articles in total

In brief
  • The US indicted Venezuelan President Nicolás Maduro, his family, and associates for narcoterrorism, drug trafficking, corruption, and weapons charges.
  • Maduro is accused of leading a narcotics cartel using Venezuela's state apparatus for illegal activities against the US for over two decades.
  • The indictments aim to hold Maduro legally accountable and increase international pressure to isolate his regime.

The United States has formally indicted Venezuelan President Nicolás Maduro, his wife, son, and several associates on charges including narcoterrorism, drug trafficking, corruption, and weapons possession. These charges allege Maduro's leadership of a narcotics cartel using cocaine trafficking as a weapon against the US, and accuse him of leveraging the Venezuelan state apparatus for illicit activities over two decades. The indictments follow Maduro's reported capture by US forces and represent a significant escalation in US legal and diplomatic pressure on his regime. Various international sources emphasize the US government's intent to hold Maduro accountable through judicial means, intensifying tensions between the US and Venezuela. This legal action is part of broader efforts to isolate Maduro internationally and disrupt his alleged criminal network.
