US-Ukraine Security Talks Amid Ongoing Conflict and Diplomatic Efforts

US–Ukraine Security Talks 2026-02-27 • 85 articles in total

In brief
  • The US and Ukraine conduct high-level talks preparing for trilateral peace negotiations with Russia amid ongoing conflict.
  • Russia refuses to set a deadline for war's end and escalates military attacks on Ukraine.
  • International responses vary, showing divided support and complex geopolitical impacts of the Ukraine war.

The US and Ukraine continue high-level security and diplomatic talks in Geneva, preparing for a trilateral meeting with Russia in early March, aiming to address the ongoing conflict and explore pathways to peace. Despite these efforts, Russia maintains a stance of no set deadline to end the war, accompanied by intensified military actions including large-scale drone and missile attacks on Ukraine. Ukraine emphasizes resilience and readiness for negotiations, while the US supports reconstruction and security cooperation. The international community remains divided, with some countries providing aid and others abstaining from resolutions, reflecting complex geopolitical dynamics. The talks underscore persistent tensions but also a shared interest in dialogue amid a protracted and devastating conflict.

Russian Perspective on War Duration and Military Actions

Russia maintains that there is no set timeline to end the war in Ukraine, signaling a prolonged conflict. Russian officials emphasize ongoing military operations, including drone and missile attacks, and express skepticism about international military support to Ukraine. This stance reflects Russia's strategic positioning and reluctance to rush peace negotiations, contributing to the conflict's complexity.
