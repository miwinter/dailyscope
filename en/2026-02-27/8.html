Uruguay and Argentina Lead Ratification of EU-Mercosur Trade Agreement

EU-Mercosur Trade Ratification 2026-02-27 • 12 articles in total

In brief
  • Uruguay and Argentina are the first Mercosur countries to ratify the EU trade agreement, aiming to boost trade and investment.
  • The pact reduces tariffs and fosters economic cooperation, promising export growth and market access expansion.
  • External trade tensions pressure the EU to promptly implement the agreement to strengthen global trade ties.

Uruguay and Argentina have become the first Mercosur countries to ratify the long-delayed trade agreement with the European Union, marking a significant milestone in strengthening economic ties between South America and Europe. The agreement aims to reduce tariffs, boost trade and investment flows, and promote economic cooperation across multiple sectors. Officials anticipate substantial export growth and expanded market access as a result of the pact. Despite some uncertainties within Europe, the ratifications by these two countries signal strong regional support and set a precedent for other Mercosur members. Additionally, external trade tensions, such as those caused by previous U.S. tariffs, have increased pressure on the EU to implement the agreement promptly.
