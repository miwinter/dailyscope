China's Military Drills Near Taiwan Escalate Regional Tensions

China Drills Near Taiwan 2025-12-29 • 25 articles in total

In brief
  • China has conducted large multi-branch live-fire military exercises around Taiwan to pressure it and warn foreign supporters amid rising regional tensions.
  • Taiwan condemns the drills as intimidation and reinforces its defense to protect its sovereignty and democracy.
  • International observers note these exercises escalate East Asian tensions and risk conflict, highlighting complex geopolitical rivalries involving the US, Japan, and others.

China has launched extensive military exercises around Taiwan, involving live-fire drills and multi-branch maneuvers, as a strong warning against Taiwan's moves toward independence and perceived foreign interference. These drills are part of Beijing's broader strategy to assert territorial claims and pressure Taiwan amid rising geopolitical tensions in East Asia. Taiwan has responded by deploying forces and condemning the exercises as acts of military intimidation, emphasizing its commitment to defend its democracy. The international community, including countries like the USA, Japan, and regional neighbors, closely monitors the situation due to concerns over regional security and potential escalation. The drills also serve as a message to external actors supporting Taiwan, highlighting the complex interplay of regional and global geopolitical rivalries.
