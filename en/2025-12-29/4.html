Myanmar's Military-Backed Elections Amid Conflict and Legitimacy Concerns

Myanmar Elections Under Military Coup 2025-12-29 • 30 articles in total

In brief
  • Myanmar's 2023 elections were held under military control amid civil war and political repression.
  • Voter turnout was low due to widespread skepticism and boycotts questioning election fairness.
  • International observers condemned the elections as a sham to consolidate military power.

Myanmar held its first general elections since the 2021 military coup amid ongoing civil war, political repression, and widespread skepticism about the vote's legitimacy. The military junta organized the elections primarily in areas under its control, with the pro-military Union Solidarity and Development Party favored to win. Voter turnout was notably low, especially among youth and opposition supporters, many of whom boycotted the polls citing unfair conditions. International observers and critics widely condemned the elections as a sham designed to consolidate military power rather than restore genuine democracy. Despite the junta's claims of free and fair elections, the political crisis and conflict continue to destabilize Myanmar's future.

Critical Viewpoint Emphasizing Election as a Sham and Low Turnout

These articles, mainly from Western and some Asian countries, express strong skepticism about the legitimacy of Myanmar's elections. They highlight the low voter turnout, especially among youth and opposition groups, and describe the elections as manipulated or sham processes orchestrated by the military junta. The ongoing civil war and repression are underscored as factors undermining any democratic progress, with calls for international concern and condemnation.
