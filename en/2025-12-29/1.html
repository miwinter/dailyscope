Trump and Zelensky's Ukraine Peace Talks: Progress Amidst Challenges

Trump Ukraine Peace Talks 2025-12-29 • 148 articles in total

In brief
  • Trump and Zelensky are negotiating a peace plan with progress on security but unresolved territorial disputes.
  • Russia warns and continues military actions while the international community supports Ukraine and coordinates diplomacy.
  • U.S. and Israeli leaders discuss Middle East security alongside Ukraine talks, reflecting broad diplomatic efforts.

U.S. President Donald Trump and Ukrainian President Volodymyr Zelensky have engaged in multiple meetings and discussions aimed at negotiating a peace plan to end the ongoing conflict between Ukraine and Russia. Both leaders express cautious optimism, highlighting significant progress on security guarantees and a 20-point peace plan nearing completion, though key territorial disputes, especially concerning Donbas and Zaporizhzhia, remain unresolved. Trump has also maintained communication with Russian President Vladimir Putin, emphasizing the seriousness of peace talks, while Zelensky seeks strong international support and security assurances. Despite these diplomatic efforts, Russia continues military actions and issues warnings, complicating the peace process. The international community, including European allies and Canada, remains actively involved, coordinating support and preparing for further multilateral discussions.
