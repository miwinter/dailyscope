US-Nigeria Joint Efforts Against ISIS in Northwest Nigeria

US Nigeria ISIS Strikes 2025-12-29 • 22 articles in total

In brief
  • The US conducts airstrikes with Nigeria to counter ISIS and extremist violence in northwest Nigeria.
  • These strikes are part of broader counterterrorism cooperation involving regional intelligence sharing, including with Ghana.
  • Both US and Nigerian sources view the operations as vital to disrupting terrorist networks and improving regional security.

The United States has conducted multiple airstrikes targeting Islamic State (ISIS) militants in northwest Nigeria, often in coordination with Nigerian forces. These strikes aim to disrupt terrorist networks, curb extremist influence, and improve regional security amid ongoing insurgency challenges. The operations have been framed as retaliation for violence against Christian communities and as part of broader counterterrorism cooperation in West Africa. While the strikes have been described as powerful and precise, questions remain about the long-term strategy and effectiveness of US involvement. Regional cooperation, including intelligence sharing with countries like Ghana, highlights the international dimension of the campaign against jihadist groups in Nigeria.

Countries covering this topic

US Perspective on Counterterrorism Strikes

US sources emphasize the strategic rationale behind the airstrikes, highlighting the need to combat ISIS expansion in Nigeria and protect vulnerable populations, including Christians targeted by extremists. The US frames these operations as part of a global counterterrorism effort and stresses ongoing military cooperation with Nigerian forces. Some US reports also discuss the potential for further strikes and the challenges of containing insurgency in the region.

Nigerian and Regional Viewpoints on US Strikes

Nigerian government and regional sources confirm and support the US-led airstrikes, viewing them as critical to weakening terrorist groups like ISIS and Lakurawa jihadists in northwest Nigeria. These perspectives highlight the importance of international military cooperation and intelligence sharing, including Ghana's role in surveillance operations. Nigerian authorities focus on assessing the impact of the strikes and emphasize the ongoing security challenges posed by insurgents in their territory.
