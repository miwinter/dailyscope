Kosovo Early Elections: Kurti's Party Secures Victory Amid Political Deadlock

Kosovo Early Elections 2025-12-29 • 18 articles in total

In brief
  • Kosovo's early elections ended a political deadlock with Kurti's Vetëvendosje party winning nearly half the vote, reaffirming his leadership.
  • Despite the victory, doubts remain about forming a stable government and overcoming ongoing political paralysis.
  • The election outcome is crucial for Kosovo's political stability, European integration, and regional relations, with calls for opposition cooperation.

Kosovo held early parliamentary elections to resolve a prolonged political deadlock after Prime Minister Albin Kurti's party failed to form a government earlier in the year. Across multiple international perspectives, Kurti's Vetëvendosje party is reported to have won decisively, securing close to or over 49% of the vote, reaffirming Kurti's political dominance. Despite the electoral victory, concerns remain about the potential continuation of political paralysis due to challenges in coalition building and governance. The elections are seen as a critical moment for Kosovo's political stability, European integration aspirations, and regional relations. Kurti has called for opposition cooperation to ensure effective governance moving forward.
