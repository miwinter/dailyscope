Bilateral Deals: Trade, Security and Strategic Alignments

Region: Bilateral Deals 2026-05-20 • 113 articles in total

In brief
  • Major powers deepen strategic partnerships in trade, security, and energy amid shifting global dynamics.
  • Regional and smaller states negotiate targeted bilateral agreements to enhance development, security, and infrastructure while balancing external offers.
  • Bilateral deals also address geopolitical aims through defense cooperation, trade dispute resolutions, and sensitive issues like migration and arms control.

This set of reports shows an active phase of bilateral diplomacy across trade, security, energy and development, from great-power summits to local partnership pacts. Major powers are consolidating strategic ties — notably deepening China–Russia cooperation — while the EU and US move to defuse tariff disputes and restore smoother transatlantic trade. Regional and small states are negotiating targeted agreements on infrastructure, maritime and police cooperation, and social protections, balancing offers from competing partners. Simultaneously, defence and security pacts, as well as controversial arms and migration issues, highlight how bilateral deals also serve geopolitical and domestic political aims.
