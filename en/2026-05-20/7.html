Global health diplomacy: assemblies, outbreaks, aid and access

Global Health Diplomacy 2026-05-20 • 13 articles in total

In brief
  • Countries engage multilaterally and bilaterally to address immunisation, TB, and infectious threats amid security challenges.
  • Donor states provide vaccines and screenings in low-income areas, while rapid outbreak responses include quarantine and experimental treatments.
  • Health systems face strain from crises, with calls for humanitarian aid and measures to protect medicine supply chains and health workers.

Countries are engaging multilaterally at the 79th WHO Assembly while simultaneously pursuing bilateral and regional health cooperation to address routine immunisation, TB, and emerging infectious threats. Donor countries are supplying vaccines and supporting screening projects in low-income settings, even as security threats hamper vaccination efforts in conflict zones. Several states and agencies are activating surveillance, quarantines, and experimental treatment exchanges in response to hantavirus and Ebola, highlighting cross-border risks and rapid operational coordination. Humanitarian appeals and warnings about medicine access underscore strain on health systems in crisis-affected countries and pressure on global supply chains.

Countries covering this topic

WHO assembly participation

Some states emphasize multilateral engagement and the importance of global governance by attending the World Health Assembly to shape policy and share best practices. Attendance is presented as a demonstration of commitment to international cooperation on health issues.

  • Oman participates in 79th WHO Assembly in Geneva
  • Turkmenistan delegation participates in the 79th session of the World Health Organization General Assembly

Donor support for immunisation and TB control

European donors are delivering vaccines and backing screening projects to strengthen routine immunisation and TB detection in Laos, framing aid as targeted public-health assistance. These actions reflect a development-oriented approach to prevent disease and reduce maternal and child morbidity.

  • Luxembourg steps up support for nationwide immunisation
  • French-backed Bokeo health project screens over 2,100 for TB

Outbreak response and cross-border surveillance

States are prioritizing rapid outbreak measures—quarantine, rodent tracing, enhanced surveillance and sharing experimental therapeutics—to manage hantavirus and Ebola risks that cross borders. This cluster highlights operational cooperation and preparedness as countries balance public health protection with evolving scientific evaluations of countermeasures.

  • Cruise ship evacuees due to hantavirus in the U.S. will remain in quarantine until May 31
  • Mexico activates epidemiological surveillance for Ebola and hantavirus
  • What we know about the latest Ebola outbreak after WHO declares global health emergency
  • Argentines hunting for source of hantavirus outbreak trap rats in southernmost city

Security threats to vaccination campaigns

Attacks on health teams illustrate how violence directly undermines immunisation delivery and jeopardizes public-health gains in conflict-affected areas. The perspective stresses the need for protection of health workers and continuity of vaccination despite security risks.

  • Attacks target polio teams in east Afghanistan, 5 killed

Humanitarian appeals amid strained health systems

UN agencies and international observers are calling for urgent support as health systems in crisis-affected settings face shortages, power outages and broader service collapse. The emphasis is on mobilizing humanitarian assistance to maintain essential healthcare for vulnerable populations.

  • UN says Cuba’s healthcare system under severe strain amid power outages and shortages
  • UNRWA calls for $94 million to support Palestinian refugees in Syria

Warnings on medicine access and supply chains

Regional authorities warn that disruptions and funding pressures could degrade access to essential medicines, urging cooperative measures to secure pharmaceutical supply chains. The view prioritizes policy responses to safeguard availability and affordability of treatments across borders.
