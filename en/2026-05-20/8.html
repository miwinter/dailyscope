Probe into Mango founder's death amid arrest and court moves

Spain Mango Founder Death Probe 2026-05-20 • 10 articles in total

In brief
  • Jonathan Andic is a suspect in the investigation into his father's 2024 death from a cliff with evidence including a changed will and a missing phone.
  • Spanish authorities have imposed bail, passport surrender, and judicial supervision on Jonathan Andic during the investigation.
  • The case has limited public details but may affect Mango's leadership and estate as legal scrutiny continues.

Spanish authorities have arrested and a Barcelona court has named Jonathan Andic, son of Mango founder Isak Andic, as a suspect in the investigation into his father's 2024 death from a cliff. Reports cite evidence being examined by prosecutors — including a changed will, a missing phone, and unusual visits — as investigators seek links between those elements and the circumstances of the fall. Judicial actions include a one-million-euro bail, surrender of a passport, placement under judicial supervision and at least one reported release on bail, reflecting active pretrial measures. International coverage emphasizes limited public details so far, the potential corporate and estate implications for Mango, and ongoing legal scrutiny as the case unfolds.
