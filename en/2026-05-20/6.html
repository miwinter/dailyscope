Trump's grip tested in GOP primaries

Trump GOP Primaries 2026-05-20 • 15 articles in total

In brief
  • Recent GOP primaries serve as a test of Donald Trump's control over the Republican Party and influence over nominations.
  • Trump’s public persona and tactics affect party loyalty and candidate fortunes, causing both support and internal division.
  • Polling shows declining Trump approval linked to economic concerns, suggesting limits to his broader political appeal.

International coverage treats recent GOP primaries as a direct test of Donald Trump's control over the Republican Party, with state-by-state results read as signals to voters, donors and potential candidates. High-profile losses for anti-Trump Republicans have been widely framed as evidence that Trump can shape nominations and enforce party discipline. At the same time, his public persona — from personal attacks and viral tweets to showpiece business projects like a $400 million ballroom — produces both loyalty and criticism that affect candidate fortunes. Polling that shows slumping approval and worries about living costs suggests limits to his appeal and potential challenges for candidate recruitment and party unity ahead of future contests.
