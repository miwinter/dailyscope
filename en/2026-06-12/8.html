Pentagon lockdown over suspected hazardous material later deemed a false alarm

Pentagon Lockdown False Alarm 2026-06-12 • 12 articles in total

In brief
  • A security alert at the Pentagon caused evacuations, HazMat teams deployment, air-quality checks, and disrupted operations for hours.
  • The alert was a false alarm with no injuries, lockdown was lifted, and officials are investigating the alert source.
  • Authorities are investigating a large unexplained marking on the National Mall amid broader security concerns.

A security alert at the Pentagon prompted multi-floor evacuations, building closures and HazMat and firefighting teams to respond amid concerns about a possible biohazard or hazardous substance. Several reports describe air-quality checks, decontamination protocols and precautionary masking by responders as operations were disrupted for hours. Multiple outlets later characterized the incident as a false alarm, with lockdown orders lifted, no injuries reported and officials opening investigations into the origin of the alert. Separately, authorities are also probing a large unexplained marking on the National Mall, prompting broader questions about security at Washington landmarks.
