US Military Intensifies Anti-Drug Strikes in Pacific and Venezuela

US Military Anti-Drug Strikes 2025-12-30 • 26 articles in total

In brief
  • The US conducted over 30 military strikes on drug trafficking vessels and facilities in the Pacific and near Venezuela causing multiple deaths.
  • President Trump confirmed these attacks, framing them as key blows against narcotics networks linked to Venezuela.
  • These operations highlight US efforts to disrupt drug flows, raise geopolitical tensions, and prompt concerns about transparency and legality.

The United States has escalated its military operations targeting drug trafficking in the Pacific Ocean and Venezuela, conducting over 30 strikes on suspected drug vessels and facilities. These operations have resulted in multiple fatalities, with many strikes focusing on boats and docks allegedly linked to narcotics smuggling networks, particularly those connected to Venezuela. President Donald Trump has publicly confirmed and emphasized these attacks, framing them as significant blows to drug trafficking routes and networks. While the US highlights these actions as part of a broader campaign to disrupt illicit drug flows and enhance regional security, details about casualties and evidence remain limited, raising questions about the operations' transparency and legality. The strikes reflect ongoing geopolitical tensions and the US commitment to combating narcotrafficking through military means in international waters and foreign territories.
