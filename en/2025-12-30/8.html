Zelensky Denies Russian Claims of Attack on Putin's Residence

Zelensky Denies Putin Residence Strike 2025-12-30 • 22 articles in total

In brief
  • Russia accuses Ukraine of a drone attack on President Putin's residence, framing it as a serious conflict escalation.
  • Ukraine denies involvement, calling the accusations false propaganda to hinder peace talks.
  • International figures express concern about the incident's impact on diplomatic efforts and conflict escalation.

Amid escalating tensions in the Russia-Ukraine conflict, Russia has accused Ukraine of launching a drone attack on one of President Vladimir Putin's residences. Ukrainian President Volodymyr Zelensky has consistently denied these allegations, labeling them as falsehoods and propaganda aimed at undermining peace negotiations. The accusations have drawn international attention, including reactions from figures like U.S. President Donald Trump, who criticized the timing and implications of the claims. Both sides continue to exchange conflicting narratives, reflecting the ongoing information warfare and mistrust that complicate diplomatic efforts. The incident underscores the fragile security situation and the challenges facing peace talks between the two nations.

Russian Perspective Accusing Ukraine

Russian sources and officials assert that Ukraine attempted or carried out a drone attack on President Putin's residence, presenting it as a significant escalation in the conflict. They emphasize the seriousness of the incident and suggest it justifies a tougher stance in peace negotiations. This viewpoint highlights Russia's narrative of being targeted and under threat, aiming to rally domestic and international support for its position.
