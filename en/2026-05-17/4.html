Bilateral momentum: security, infrastructure and strategic partnerships

Region: Bilateral Deals 2026-05-17 • 82 articles in total

In brief
  • Major powers expand targeted bilateral ties in defense, economy, energy, and health, balancing diplomacy with strategic cooperation.
  • Regional actors advance connectivity, trade facilitation, and infrastructure alongside crisis management and fragile ceasefires.
  • Smaller states focus on consular, cultural, and environmental deals to enhance people-to-people connections and address humanitarian responsibilities.

Across regions states are consolidating bilateral ties through defense, economic and technical agreements while mediators manage fragile ceasefires. Major powers advance targeted partnerships — Russia and China deepen economic and health-energy links, Europe and France balance diplomatic thaws with security cooperation for Ukraine, and India secures trade, energy and defence pacts. Regional actors pursue connectivity and infrastructure projects alongside trade facilitation to boost growth, while smaller states negotiate consular, cultural and environmental deals to strengthen people-to-people ties. Simultaneously, mediators and partners remain active in crisis management, extending ceasefires and arranging prisoner exchanges as part of broader diplomatic engagement.
