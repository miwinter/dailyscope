Trump‑Xi Summit: Reset Signals amid Persistent Tensions

US-China Diplomacy 2026-05-17 • 88 articles in total

In brief
  • The summit showcased cautious US-China engagement focused on partnership, trade dialogue, and risk management without resolving core rivalry.
  • Security issues, especially Taiwan and arms discussions, dominated amid pragmatic and limited crisis cooperation on Iran, the Strait of Hormuz, and global conflicts.
  • Media emphasize mutual suspicion during intense security measures and regional hopes for diversified partnerships and cautious adaptation to shifting dynamics.

President Trump’s visit to Beijing produced high‑level engagement that many governments framed as a cautious reset: leaders talked of partnership, strategic stability, and new economic dialogue mechanisms while avoiding dramatic breakthroughs. Washington accentuated trade optics and business diplomacy — with tech leaders in the entourage and claims of ‘fantastic’ deals — even as markets and analysts remained skeptical. Security flashpoints dominated coverage: repeated warnings about Taiwan, debates over arms and deterrence, and cooperative but tentative diplomacy on Iran and the Strait of Hormuz. The trip’s procession of ceremonial encounters, security precautions and mixed international commentary left the summit portrayed as important for risk management rather than a decisive resolution of core US‑China rivalry.
