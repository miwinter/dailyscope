Escalation, domestic strain, and diplomatic manoeuvres in the Russia–Ukraine war

Russo-Ukrainian War 2026-05-17 • 51 articles in total

In brief
  • The conflict involves large drone and missile attacks on Russian and Ukrainian targets with casualties and infrastructure damage.
  • Russia faces internal challenges including economic decline, unrest, and leadership insecurity while expanding presidential powers and controlling the narrative.
  • Western and Ukrainian parties debate policy responses, stress international support, and highlight risks of regional spillover and escalation.

Reporting across many countries shows an intensifying, multi‑front conflict: large drone strikes and missile raids have hit both Russian territory (including Moscow) and Ukrainian cities, while Kyiv and Moscow exchange accusations and occasional prisoner swaps. Kremlin statements and legislative moves are aimed at projecting control and expanding presidential powers even as analysts note economic contraction, domestic unrest and leadership insecurity in Russia. Western governments and commentators debate policy trade‑offs—troop posture, arms supplies and sabotage risks—while Ukraine urges continued international support and explores economic pressure on Russia. Regional incidents, unexploded ordnance and measures such as passport offers in Transnistria feed fears of spillover and recruitment for the war. Overall, the coverage blends battlefield developments, humanitarian consequences and geopolitical manoeuvres with growing concern about escalation and domestic political stability in Moscow.
