Escalating US-Iran Military Conflict and Regional Tensions

US–Iran Military Escalation 2026-03-12 • 121 articles in total

In brief
  • The US and allies conduct military strikes against Iran to protect maritime routes and curb regional aggression.
  • Iran and its allies launch retaliatory attacks on US and Israeli targets, asserting control over the Strait of Hormuz.
  • International actors call for restraint and diplomacy amid concerns over regional security and global economic impacts.

The US-Iran military conflict has intensified with multiple attacks on strategic maritime routes, military bases, and civilian sites, including the Strait of Hormuz, a critical global oil passage. Iran and its allies, such as Hezbollah, have launched coordinated strikes against Israel and US interests, while the US and Israel have conducted extensive air and naval operations targeting Iranian assets. The conflict has resulted in significant casualties, including US military personnel and civilians, and has caused substantial economic costs and disruptions to global energy markets. International actors, including the UN and G7, have called for restraint and security measures to protect shipping lanes, while diplomatic efforts continue amid accusations and counter-accusations. The situation remains volatile with no clear resolution in sight, raising concerns about broader regional instability and global economic impacts.
