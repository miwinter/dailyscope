Escalating Iran-US-Israel Conflict Threatens Regional and Global Stability

Iran–US–Israel Escalation 2026-03-12 • 17 articles in total

In brief
  • Iran, the US, and Israel are engaged in escalating military strikes with Iran prepared for a prolonged war of attrition.
  • The US and Israel aim to limit Iran's regional influence and military expansion through targeted operations.
  • The conflict risks severe regional instability and global economic impacts amid limited transparency and calls for diplomacy.

The conflict between Iran, the United States, and Israel has intensified with significant military strikes, including air raids targeting Iranian leadership and economic infrastructure. Iran has declared readiness for a prolonged war of attrition, warning of severe repercussions for the global economy and regional stability. The US and Israel aim to curb Iran's influence and reshape the Middle Eastern geopolitical landscape, while Iran emphasizes its determination to resist and retaliate, including targeting economic centers linked to its adversaries. The situation remains highly volatile, with concerns about expanding hostilities involving neighboring countries and the potential for an apocalyptic escalation. Information opacity and strategic restraint in certain areas, such as the island of Jark, further complicate the conflict's trajectory and international response.

Iranian Perspective: Defiance and Preparedness for Prolonged Conflict

Iranian sources emphasize their readiness to endure a long and destructive war, framing the conflict as a defensive struggle against US and Israeli aggression. They highlight the targeting of economic and financial centers as strategic retaliation and warn of the broader economic consequences globally. Iranian rhetoric underscores the impossibility of coexistence with Israel and stresses resilience despite significant casualties and leadership losses.

US and Israeli Perspective: Strategic Military Objectives and Regional Influence

From the US and Israeli viewpoint, the military actions aim to degrade Iran's capabilities and influence in the Middle East, with targeted strikes on leadership and military assets. The campaigns are portrayed as necessary to reshape regional power dynamics and prevent Iran's nuclear and military expansion. Despite ongoing operations, there is an acknowledgment of the complexity and potential for prolonged conflict, with some strategic restraint observed to avoid wider escalation.
