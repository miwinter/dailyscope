Escalation and Regional Impact of the Iran Conflict

Iran Conflict Spillover 2026-03-12 • 16 articles in total

In brief
  • The Iran conflict has expanded beyond its borders, involving regional and global actors and disrupting Gulf infrastructure and trade routes.
  • Iran and its proxies intensify military and strategic actions affecting Lebanon, Israel, the West Bank, and Gulf states, altering regional alliances and security.
  • Global countries like the US and Turkey highlight the conflict’s broad spillover and call for diplomacy to prevent wider regional war.

The Iran conflict has significantly escalated, extending beyond Iran's borders and involving multiple regional and global actors. Iran's expanded targeting of infrastructure and strategic sites in the Gulf, including the UAE and Oman, has heightened economic and security concerns, disrupting oil supplies and international trade routes. Israel faces increased challenges from Iranian-backed groups like Hezbollah, while its actions in the West Bank reflect exploitation of the broader conflict. Neighboring countries such as Pakistan experience direct spillover effects, and international players including the US and Ukraine are drawn into the conflict dynamics. Calls for diplomacy, notably from Turkey, emphasize the urgent need to prevent a wider regional war.
