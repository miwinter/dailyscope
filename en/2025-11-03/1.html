Global Natural Disasters: Earthquakes in Afghanistan, Hurricane Melissa in the Caribbean, and Landslides in Africa

2025-11-03 • 105 articles in total

Recent natural disasters have caused widespread devastation across multiple regions. A powerful 6.3 magnitude earthquake struck northern Afghanistan, resulting in at least 20 deaths, hundreds injured, and damage to cultural heritage sites like the Blue Mosque, highlighting the region's seismic vulnerability and inadequate infrastructure. Meanwhile, Hurricane Melissa inflicted severe destruction throughout the Caribbean, especially in Jamaica, Haiti, and Cuba, with over 60 fatalities and massive disruption to infrastructure, prompting international aid and regional cooperation. In Africa, heavy rains triggered deadly landslides in Kenya with over 20 deaths, hampering rescue operations amid challenging weather. Additionally, the Philippines faced proactive evacuations due to an approaching strong typhoon, emphasizing ongoing global risks from diverse natural disasters and the urgent need for coordinated relief and resilience efforts.

Hurricane Melissa's Devastation and Relief Efforts in the Caribbean

Multiple sources from Haiti, United States, Puerto Rico, Slovakia, Germany, Netherlands, Great Britain, Cape Verde, Dominican Republic, Sint Maarten, Jamaica, Bermuda, Bahrain, and others cover the severe impacts of Hurricane Melissa across the Caribbean, especially Jamaica, Haiti, and Cuba. Death tolls exceed 60 with widespread flooding, infrastructure damage, and displacement. There is significant international aid involvement including US government contributions, Pan American Health Organization and WHO medical support, and regional collaborations among EU members, Barbados, and neighboring islands. Financial aid such as the Caribbean Catastrophe Risk Insurance Facility payout supports Jamaica's recovery. The disaster raises calls for increased climate finance due to the vulnerability of small island states to extreme weather events. Coordination of relief operations and regional solidarity are emphasized in these reports.
