Siverek high-school shooting

Turkey School Shooting 2026-04-14 • 24 articles in total

In brief
  • A teenage former student carried out a shooting at a high school in Siverek, wounding 16–18 people and possibly dying by suicide.
  • Authorities evacuated students, launched investigations, detained suspects, and initiated manhunts amid initial confusion about the shooter's identity and motive.
  • The incident sparked chaos, prompted calls to improve school security, and raised concerns about safety in educational institutions.

A shooting at a high school in Siverek, Şanlıurfa, southeastern Turkey, left roughly 16–18 people — mainly students and staff — wounded. Many reports identify the attacker as a teenage former student (around 18–19) who, in several accounts, died by suicide at the scene, while other outlets initially reported uncertainty about the shooter’s identity. Authorities deployed special units, evacuated pupils and launched investigations into motive and circumstances; some sources mention detentions and ongoing manhunts in early reporting. The episode has renewed scrutiny of school security and prompted reviews of protective measures in the region.
