France is advancing legislation to facilitate the restitution of artworks looted during its colonial past, signaling a commitment to addressing historical injustices and improving relations with countries of origin. The National Assembly's unanimous adoption of bills to simplify restitution procedures reflects a broader policy shift towards post-colonial justice and cultural reconciliation. This legislative progress is part of France's ongoing efforts to reconcile with its colonial history and promote ethical stewardship of cultural heritage.
