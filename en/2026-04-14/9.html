Australia names first female army chief; Colombia approves hippo cull

Australia Army Appointment 2026-04-14 • 12 articles in total

In brief
  • Australia appointed Lieutenant General Susan Coyle as the first woman leader of the Australian Army, marking a historic gender equality milestone.
  • Colombia plans to cull about 80 hippos from Pablo Escobar's estate to protect ecosystems, sparking debates among scientists and animal welfare groups.

Australia has appointed Lieutenant General Susan Coyle as the first woman to lead the Australian Army, a development widely reported as a historic milestone. Coverage stresses the symbolic advance for gender equality within the Australian Defence Force and highlights Coyle's background and role in ongoing modernization. Commentators note potential impacts on recruitment, leadership culture and defense policy, while also pointing to domestic debates about barriers to advancement for women. Separately, several pieces cover Colombia's decision to cull or euthanize about 80 hippos descended from Pablo Escobar's estate, framing it as an ecological management measure that has provoked debate among scientists, authorities and animal welfare advocates.
