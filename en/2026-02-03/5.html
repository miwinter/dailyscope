Clintons Agree to Testify in Jeffrey Epstein Congressional Investigation

Clintons Epstein Testimony 2026-02-03 • 21 articles in total

In brief
  • Bill and Hillary Clinton have agreed to testify before U.S. Congress about Jeffrey Epstein's criminal network after months of resistance.
  • Their testimonies highlight political tensions and legal scrutiny surrounding Epstein's investigation and could reveal connections to high-profile figures.
  • This development has drawn global media attention and underscores international demands for transparency and accountability in Epstein's case.

Former U.S. President Bill Clinton and former Secretary of State Hillary Clinton have agreed to testify before the U.S. Congress as part of the ongoing investigation into Jeffrey Epstein's criminal activities and network. Their testimonies come after months of resistance and pressure from congressional committees, including threats of contempt charges. The Clintons' cooperation is seen as a significant development that may shed light on their connections to Epstein and broader implications involving high-profile figures. This move has attracted widespread international media attention and highlights the political and legal scrutiny surrounding Epstein's case. The hearings are expected to contribute to accountability and transparency in this high-profile investigation.
