Deadly Floods and Landslides Devastate Southeastern Brazil

Brazil Floods & Landslides 2026-02-25 • 17 articles in total

In brief
  • Severe rains in Minas Gerais, Brazil caused deadly floods, landslides, and displaced hundreds.
  • Rescue operations continue amid difficult weather and extensive infrastructure damage.
  • The disaster highlights Brazil's vulnerability and the need for coordinated disaster management.

Southeastern Brazil, particularly the state of Minas Gerais, has been severely impacted by torrential rains causing widespread flooding and deadly landslides. The disaster has resulted in at least 14 to over 30 fatalities, with dozens to hundreds reported missing, and has displaced hundreds of residents. Rescue and emergency operations are ongoing amid challenging weather conditions, with authorities closely monitoring the situation to prevent further casualties. The extensive damage to infrastructure and communities has prompted coordinated government responses and raised concerns about increasing extreme weather events linked to climate change. The crisis highlights Brazil's vulnerability to natural disasters and the urgent need for effective disaster management and support for affected populations.
