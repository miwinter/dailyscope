Iran's Military Drills in the Strait of Hormuz Escalate Regional Tensions

Iran Live-Fire Drills 2026-02-18 • 12 articles in total

In brief
  • Iran conducts joint live-fire exercises with Russia and China in the Strait of Hormuz to assert regional control and challenge U.S. pressure.
  • The drills cause temporary closures of the strait, threatening global oil shipments and raising security concerns.
  • Iran uses these military actions as both a show of defiance and a strategic tool amid nuclear negotiations.

Iran has conducted multiple live-fire military exercises in the strategic Strait of Hormuz, often in coordination with Russia and China, signaling a show of force amid heightened regional and international tensions. These drills have led to temporary closures of the strait, a critical chokepoint for global oil shipments, raising concerns among global markets and international observers about security and stability. Iranian leadership, including Supreme Leader Ali Khamenei, has issued strong warnings against U.S. pressure, emphasizing Iran's resilience and readiness to defend its interests. The exercises coincide with ongoing nuclear negotiations and diplomatic efforts, reflecting a complex interplay of military posturing and dialogue. Overall, Iran's actions underscore its intent to assert control over the vital waterway while responding to external pressures and regional disputes.
