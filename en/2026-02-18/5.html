US Military Intensifies Maritime Strikes Against Drug Trafficking Vessels

US Military Drug Strike 2026-02-18 • 26 articles in total

In brief
  • The US military has conducted over 40 strikes against drug trafficking boats, causing about 145 deaths to disrupt narcotics smuggling to the US.
  • Countries like the US, Canada, and France support the strikes as vital to regional security and combating drug cartels.
  • Denmark and Norway express concerns about lethal force use and call for transparency and caution in these maritime operations.

The United States military has conducted multiple strikes against suspected drug trafficking boats in the Pacific Ocean and Caribbean Sea, resulting in the deaths of 11 individuals in the latest operations. These actions are part of an ongoing campaign targeting narcotics smuggling networks, with over 40 attacks since September causing approximately 145 fatalities. The strikes aim to disrupt organized crime and narcotics flow to the US, emphasizing the US commitment to maritime security and counter-narcotics efforts. While details on the identities and exact locations remain limited, the operations have drawn international attention and sparked discussions on the use of lethal force in such interventions. Overall, these efforts highlight persistent US engagement in combating drug trafficking through maritime routes in international waters.

US and Allied Perspective on Anti-Drug Maritime Operations

Articles from countries such as the United States, Canada, France, Germany, and others emphasize the strikes as necessary actions against narcotics trafficking and organized crime. They highlight the operations as part of a broader campaign to disrupt drug smuggling routes threatening regional and international security. The focus is on the legitimacy and strategic importance of these military interventions to combat drug cartels and protect borders.
