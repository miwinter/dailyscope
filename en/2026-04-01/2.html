In brief
  • The US and Israel conduct military operations against Iran, with US leaders expecting a swift war conclusion in weeks.

  • Regional allies push for continued pressure on Iran while diplomatic efforts seek peace amid humanitarian and economic concerns.

The ongoing war involving the United States, Israel, and Iran has seen intensified military operations, including US airstrikes on Iranian infrastructure and Israeli strikes inside Iran. US leadership, particularly President Donald Trump, has repeatedly projected a swift end to the conflict within two to three weeks, signaling confidence in a rapid resolution. Iran has responded with missile and drone attacks on neighboring countries and threatened retaliatory strikes against US companies and tech firms, escalating regional tensions. Gulf allies urge continued pressure on Iran, while international actors like China and Pakistan propose diplomatic plans to end hostilities. The conflict has caused significant humanitarian concerns and instability in the Middle East, with global markets reacting cautiously to the evolving situation.
