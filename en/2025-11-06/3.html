Typhoon Kalmaegi Devastates the Philippines with High Death Toll and Widespread Damage

2025-11-06 • 44 articles in total

Typhoon Kalmaegi has caused catastrophic damage in the Philippines, with reported fatalities ranging from 26 to nearly 270, although most sources converge around 140 dead and over 100 missing. The hardest hit areas include Cebu province, which declared a state of emergency due to severe flooding and widespread destruction. Recovery and rescue operations are ongoing amid significant challenges, with entire towns flooded and infrastructure severely damaged. The typhoon is now moving towards Vietnam, prompting preparation and concern in neighboring countries. The disaster underscores the Philippines' vulnerability to increasingly frequent and intense tropical storms, compounded by climate change and existing recovery burdens such as previous earthquakes.
