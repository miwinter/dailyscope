Stalled diplomacy as Iran offers plans and the US expresses doubt

US–Iran Diplomacy 2026-05-03 • 99 articles in total

In brief
  • Iran has submitted a 14-point peace plan and set negotiation conditions while urging the U.S. to choose diplomacy over confrontation.
  • The U.S. reviews Iran's proposals but remains dissatisfied, retaining sanctions, a naval blockade, and military options.
  • International mediators report on developments as analysts warn continued conflict risks political damage and instability.

Iran has submitted a new 14-point peace plan and multiple proposals via mediators, outlining conditions for de-escalation and negotiations. The U.S. leadership says it is reviewing the material but repeatedly voices dissatisfaction and doubts the proposals are acceptable, while maintaining sanctions, a naval blockade and keeping military options on the table. Tehran insists the ball is in Washington’s court, defends its nuclear posture and warns the United States to choose diplomacy or confrontation as it sets red lines and readies defenses. International mediators and markets have reacted to the diplomacy (airspace, oil and naval movements), and analysts and polls warn that continued confrontation and hardline rhetoric risk political damage and further instability.

U.S. administration: cautious, skeptical and assertive

U.S. officials, led by President Trump, say they are reviewing Iran's proposals but are largely unsatisfied and doubt the plans will be acceptable. At the same time Washington has preserved pressure tools—sanctions, a naval blockade—and signalled that military options remain under consideration while reporting the formal end of hostilities to Congress in some communications.
