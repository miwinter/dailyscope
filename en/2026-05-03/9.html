Tanker Hijack off Yemen Raises Red Sea Security Concerns

Somali Piracy: Tanker Hijack 2026-05-03 • 7 articles in total

In brief
  • One or more oil tankers were hijacked off Yemen's coast amid unclear details and disputed characterizations as piracy or robbery.
  • Regional and international authorities are tracking the vessels and monitoring maritime security in the Red Sea and Gulf of Aden.
  • The incident raises concerns about broader risks to energy shipments and maritime trade stability in the region.

Reports describe one or more oil tankers hijacked off Yemen's coast, with some accounts saying Somali pirates seized or steered vessels toward Somalia while others characterize the incident differently. Sources diverge on terminology and motive — some label it piracy, one labels it a robbery under investigation by Yemeni authorities — and many operational details about the ships, crews and cargo remain unclear. Regional coast guards and international observers are tracking the vessels and monitoring routes in the Red Sea and Gulf of Aden. An account alleging two tankers were taken amplifies concerns about broader disruption to energy shipments and maritime trade in the area.
