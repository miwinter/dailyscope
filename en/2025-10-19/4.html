Pakistan-Afghanistan Ceasefire: Progress and Challenges Amid Fragile Peace

2025-10-19 • 35 articles in total

Afghanistan and Pakistan have agreed to multiple ceasefires mediated primarily by Qatar and Turkey, aiming to reduce decades of violence along their shared border. These agreements, often immediate and temporary, symbolize hopeful strides toward diplomatic dialogue and regional stability. However, the ceasefires remain fragile, with periodic escalations including airstrikes and attacks that threaten to undermine peace efforts. Both sides emphasize the importance of continuing negotiations and establishing robust mechanisms to consolidate and monitor these agreements. International observers view the ongoing talks in Doha as crucial to preventing further conflict and fostering long-term cooperation.

This viewpoint stresses the complex reality on the ground and the risks to sustained peace.
