Global Bilateral Deals and Regional Alignments

Region: Bilateral Deals 2026-06-30 • 107 articles in total

In brief
  • Major powers deepen strategic ties through military cooperation, debt relief, and infrastructure contracts to enhance influence and growth.
  • Regional and smaller states engage in security pacts, capacity-building, and bilateral agreements to protect sovereignty and boost development.
  • High-level visits and diplomatic efforts focus on crisis management, economic cooperation, and balanced partnerships across diverse regions.

This set of reports shows a broad wave of bilateral agreements and diplomatic engagement spanning defense, trade, infrastructure and development across regions. Major powers are deepening strategic ties—through military cooperation, debt relief and large infrastructure contracts—while regional actors pursue security pacts and technical assistance to protect sovereignty and boost growth. Several Gulf- and Hormuz-related accords and state visits underscore efforts to manage maritime security and frozen assets. Simultaneously, smaller states and development partners are signing targeted deals on ports, aviation, agriculture and capacity-building to attract investment and strengthen cross-border cooperation.
