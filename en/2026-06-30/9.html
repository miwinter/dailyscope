Pakistan-Afghanistan Cross-Border Strikes

Pakistan-Afghanistan Cross-Border Strike 2026-06-30 • 12 articles in total

In brief
  • Pakistan conducts cross-border strikes claiming to target militants and kill dozens.
  • Afghan officials, Taliban, and UN report higher civilian casualties and condemn the operations.
  • The incidents increase regional tensions and prompt calls for investigation and de-escalation.

Reports describe Pakistani air and ground operations across the Afghanistan border, with Islamabad framing the actions as counterterrorism strikes that killed dozens of militants. Afghan authorities, the Taliban and the UN cited substantially higher civilian tolls — including women and children — and reported dozens of deaths and many injuries. Casualty figures and their breakdowns remain contested and not independently verified, producing conflicting narratives between Pakistani security claims and Afghan/UN accusations of civilian harm. The incidents have heightened regional tensions and prompted calls for investigations, restraint and de-escalation to prevent further humanitarian and security fallout.
