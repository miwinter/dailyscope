Monaco explosion injures Ukrainian oligarch and family

Monaco Explosion 2026-06-30 • 16 articles in total

In brief
  • An explosion in a Monaco residential building injured three people, including Ukrainian oligarch Vadym Ermolaev and his family.
  • Authorities treat the blast as a deliberate attack and have launched intensive investigations involving special units and house-to-house searches.
  • The incident has drawn international attention with ongoing inquiries into motive, perpetrators, and possible political or security implications.

An explosion in a Monaco residential building injured three people, including a Ukrainian oligarch and members of his family. Several outlets report the device was a booby-trapped package or an explosive left in a bag, and authorities are treating the incident as a probable deliberate attack. Police and special units have launched searches and house-to-house operations while investigators say a suspect fled the scene; no arrests or claims of responsibility have been confirmed. The case has drawn broad international attention and prompted cross-border coordination as inquiries into motive and perpetrators continue.
