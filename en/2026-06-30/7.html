Deadly shooting in Stade: custody dispute, arrests and conflicting counts

Germany Mass Shooting 2026-06-30 • 23 articles in total

In brief
  • A mass shooting in Stade, Germany, killed five or six people linked to a youth or social welfare centre.
  • Authorities have detained several suspects, with at least one formally charged, and investigations are ongoing.
  • The attack is believed to stem from a custody dispute or family tragedy, prompting community and security concerns.

A mass shooting in Stade, northern Germany, left multiple people dead and has been reported with varying casualty counts (five or six) across outlets. Authorities have detained or charged several people in connection with the attack, with at least one suspect formally charged and others held for questioning. Multiple reports link the incident to a custody dispute or family tragedy and say many victims worked at or were associated with a youth or social welfare centre. Investigations are ongoing, with police appealing for information while communities and officials scramble to support victims’ families and review security at social services sites.
