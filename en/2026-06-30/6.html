Keiko Fujimori Clinches Presidency After Razor-Thin Runoff

Peru Fujimori Victory 2026-06-30 • 24 articles in total

In brief
  • Keiko Fujimori won Peru's presidential runoff by a narrow margin after a prolonged 22-day vote count and recount.
  • Her victory marks the return of Fujorismo, triggering political tension over past human-rights abuses and a likely shift to conservative policies.
  • The official result is finalized amid deep domestic divisions and international attention on the upcoming administration.

Peru's presidential runoff concluded with Keiko Fujimori declared the winner after official tallies were completed. The victory was razor-thin — around 49,641 votes (roughly 0.27 percentage points), with reports noting about 50.1% of the vote after a prolonged, 22-day count and recounts. The result has reopened deep domestic divisions and marked the return of Fujimorismo, prompting both celebration and protests as questions about past human-rights abuses re-emerge. Analysts and foreign outlets highlight a likely rightward shift in policy, including tougher crime measures and conservative economic priorities, while parts of the opposition at times refused to accept the outcome, leaving the transition politically tense. Authorities have nonetheless formalized the result and the incoming administration faces close domestic and international scrutiny as it prepares to govern.
