Divergent Reactions to US National Security Strategy Shift

US National Security Strategy Release 2025-12-08 • 23 articles in total

In brief
  • The US National Security Strategy shifts to assertive nationalism, emphasizing American supremacy and urging allies to increase their defense roles.
  • Russia welcomes the strategy for removing its threat designation and aligning with its security views, signaling possible US-Russia recalibration.
  • Europe fears the strategy weakens its security and transatlantic ties, raising concerns about instability and reduced US global leadership.

The newly released US National Security Strategy under the Trump administration marks a significant shift towards a more assertive and nationalist approach, emphasizing American supremacy and urging allies to take greater responsibility for their own defense. Russia has welcomed the strategy, noting the removal of its designation as a direct threat and expressing that the document largely aligns with its own security vision, signaling a potential recalibration in US-Russia relations. Conversely, European nations and experts have reacted with concern and apprehension, fearing the strategy undermines European security and autonomy, potentially leading to a weakening of transatlantic ties and increased geopolitical instability. The strategy also highlights a focus on hemispheric threats such as narcoterrorism, reflecting a broader reorientation of US defense priorities. Overall, the strategy has elicited mixed international responses, revealing deep divisions over the future direction of US foreign and security policy.

Russian Approval and Alignment

Russian officials and media have expressed strong approval of the new US National Security Strategy, highlighting the removal of Russia from the list of direct threats and emphasizing that the strategy aligns closely with Moscow's own security perspectives. This positive reception suggests a rare convergence of views amid ongoing geopolitical tensions and may indicate a strategic opening for limited cooperation or recalibration of bilateral relations. The Kremlin's stance reflects an interpretation of the US shift as beneficial or at least less confrontational towards Russia.

European Concern and Criticism

European countries and security experts have reacted with alarm and skepticism to the US strategy, perceiving it as a threat to European security and autonomy. The strategy's emphasis on American supremacy and calls for allies to defend themselves have raised fears of a diminished US commitment to European defense and a potential power vacuum. Analysts warn that this could lead to increased instability and challenge the future of transatlantic cooperation, with some viewing the approach as a retreat from global leadership and liberal internationalism.

US Domestic Perspective on Strategic Shift

Within the United States, officials such as Pete Hegseth have articulated the strategy as a pragmatic departure from idealistic foreign policy, focusing instead on national interests and hemispheric security threats like narcoterrorism. This viewpoint underscores a prioritization of practical defense measures and a call for allies to assume greater responsibility for their own security, reflecting a more unilateral and assertive US posture in global affairs.
