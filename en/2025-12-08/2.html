Benin Coup Attempt Foiled Amid Regional Security Concerns

Benin Coup Bid Foiled 2025-12-08 • 57 articles in total

In brief
  • On December 7, 2025, soldiers in Benin attempted a coup but the government quickly regained control and arrested suspects.
  • Regional bodies condemned the coup, threatened sanctions, and Nigeria offered military support to stabilize the situation.
  • The event highlights ongoing political instability in Benin and West Africa, prompting continued investigations and vigilance.

On December 7, 2025, Benin experienced an attempted military coup involving soldiers who briefly announced the overthrow of President Patrice Talon on state television. The government and loyalist forces swiftly regained control, arresting around a dozen soldiers implicated in the plot, while some leaders reportedly fled. Regional bodies like ECOWAS and the African Union condemned the coup attempt and threatened sanctions, with Nigeria confirming military support to stabilize the situation. The incident highlights ongoing political instability and security challenges in Benin and the wider West African region. Authorities continue investigations and maintain heightened vigilance to prevent further unrest.
