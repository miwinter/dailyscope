Trump's Peace Council: Ambitions, Global Reactions, and Geopolitical Divides

Trump Peace Council Proposals 2026-01-23 • 73 articles in total

In brief
  • Trump launched the Peace Council to address global conflicts, focusing on Gaza and the Middle East as a US-led alternative to the UN.
  • Several Middle Eastern and Asian countries joined the council, while European nations and US allies remain skeptical or declined to participate.
  • The initiative sparked diplomatic tensions over legitimacy, controversial policies, and geopolitical concerns, reflecting complex international reactions.

US President Donald Trump has launched a new international initiative called the 'Peace Council' or 'Board of Peace,' aiming to address global conflicts with a particular focus on Gaza and the Middle East. The council seeks to position itself as an alternative or complement to the United Nations, with Trump asserting significant control over its operations. While several countries from the Middle East, Asia, and some other regions have joined or expressed support, many European nations and traditional US allies remain skeptical or have declined participation, citing concerns over legitimacy, geopolitical implications, and potential rivalry with the UN. The initiative has sparked diplomatic tensions, including withdrawn invitations and debates over membership fees, and has been met with mixed international reactions ranging from cautious engagement to outright criticism. Overall, the Peace Council reflects Trump's continued influence in international diplomacy and his attempt to reshape global peace efforts amid complex geopolitical dynamics.

Support and Participation from Middle Eastern and Select Asian Countries

Countries such as Saudi Arabia, Turkey, Egypt, Jordan, Indonesia, Pakistan, Qatar, and the UAE have welcomed or joined Trump's Peace Council, viewing it as a constructive step toward regional peace and cooperation, especially concerning Gaza. Leaders from these nations emphasize diplomatic engagement and see the council as a platform to address ongoing conflicts and reconstruction efforts. Their participation signals a willingness to collaborate with the US-led initiative despite some geopolitical complexities.

European and Western Skepticism and Reluctance

Many European countries and traditional US allies have expressed skepticism or declined to join the Peace Council, citing concerns about its legitimacy, potential to undermine the United Nations, and geopolitical implications. Nations like the UK, France, Belgium, and the European Union have raised doubts about Russia's involvement and the council's alignment with international law. Some have withdrawn invitations or delayed decisions, reflecting caution and a preference for established multilateral frameworks.

Trump’s Vision and Controversies Surrounding the Peace Council

Donald Trump envisions the Peace Council as a powerful, US-led alternative to the United Nations, with ambitions to control global peacekeeping efforts and conflict resolution. The initiative includes controversial elements such as charging $1 billion for permanent seats and inviting contentious figures like Vladimir Putin. Trump's speeches and actions at Davos have been polarizing, drawing both support and criticism, and raising questions about the council's practical influence and geopolitical consequences.
