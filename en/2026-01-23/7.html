Diplomatic Engagements Between Trump and Zelensky on Ukraine Conflict

Trump–Zelenskiy Ukraine Meeting 2026-01-23 • 18 articles in total

In brief
  • Trump and Zelensky met at Davos to discuss security guarantees and efforts to resolve the Ukraine conflict.
  • They agreed on some security measures but unresolved issues like territorial sovereignty remain.
  • Trump expressed optimism about peace talks and emphasized concessions from all parties are needed.

U.S. President Donald Trump and Ukrainian President Volodymyr Zelensky held multiple meetings around the Davos forum, focusing on security guarantees and peace efforts amid the ongoing Ukraine conflict. Zelensky announced agreements on security guarantees with Trump, signaling potential diplomatic progress, though key issues like territorial sovereignty remain unresolved. Trump expressed optimism about both Zelensky and Putin's willingness to end the war and emphasized the need for concessions from all parties to achieve peace. The discussions also highlighted frustrations with European support and the preparation for upcoming trilateral talks involving Russia. Overall, these engagements underscore continued international efforts to mediate and resolve the Ukraine crisis.
