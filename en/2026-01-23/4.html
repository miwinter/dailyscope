US Withdrawal from WHO: A Major Shift in Global Health Cooperation

US WHO Withdrawal 2026-01-23 • 22 articles in total

In brief
  • The US officially withdrew from the WHO citing dissatisfaction with its COVID-19 response and alleged China bias.
  • The withdrawal raised global concerns about health cooperation and pandemic preparedness while highlighting US refusal to pay $260 million in dues.
  • The US later reversed its decision, ending the withdrawal and reconsidering unpaid debts, showing shifting policies on WHO involvement.

The United States has officially completed its withdrawal from the World Health Organization, a process initiated by an executive order from President Donald Trump. This move reflects deep dissatisfaction with the WHO's handling of the COVID-19 pandemic and allegations of bias, particularly towards China. The withdrawal has sparked widespread international concern about the future of global health cooperation and pandemic preparedness. Additionally, the US refusal to pay outstanding dues, totaling around $260 million, has intensified debates about its commitment to global health governance. The decision marks a significant realignment in US foreign health policy with uncertain implications for worldwide health initiatives.
