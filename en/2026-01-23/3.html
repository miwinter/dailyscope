France and US Enforce Sanctions by Seizing Russian and Venezuelan Oil Tankers

France and US Tanker Seizures 2026-01-23 • 26 articles in total

In brief
  • France seizes Russian oil tankers in the Mediterranean to enforce sanctions and uphold maritime security amid the Russia-Ukraine conflict.
  • The US intercepts Venezuelan oil tankers to pressure Venezuela and disrupt illicit oil trade, reflecting ongoing sanctions enforcement.
  • Both nations increase maritime vigilance to enforce international sanctions and control unauthorized oil shipments.

France has actively intercepted and seized multiple oil tankers linked to Russia's so-called 'ghost' or 'shadow' fleet in the Mediterranean Sea, demonstrating a firm commitment to enforcing international sanctions amid the Russia-Ukraine conflict. French President Emmanuel Macron and military sources emphasize that these operations aim to uphold international law and prevent illicit oil shipments that undermine sanctions. The United States has similarly seized several oil tankers connected to Venezuela, part of a broader strategy to pressure the Venezuelan government and curb unauthorized oil exports. Both countries' actions reflect heightened maritime security efforts and geopolitical tensions surrounding energy supply chains and sanction enforcement. These interdictions underscore the increasing vigilance of Western nations in monitoring and disrupting sanctioned oil transport routes.
