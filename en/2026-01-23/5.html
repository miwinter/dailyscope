Complex US-Venezuela Relations Marked by Conflict and Diplomatic Shifts

US–Venezuela Diplomatic Tensions and UAV Strikes 2026-01-23 • 20 articles in total

In brief
  • US drone strikes in Venezuela cause casualties and trigger sovereignty accusations by regional allies.
  • Secret negotiations between Venezuelan figures and the US suggest attempts at political transition amid controversy.
  • US diplomatic appointments and military tactics aim to influence Venezuela's political future amid international contestation.

The US-Venezuela relationship remains fraught with tension, highlighted by reports of US drone strikes causing significant Venezuelan casualties and accusations of sovereignty violations by regional allies like ALBA and Cuba. Simultaneously, behind-the-scenes negotiations involving Venezuelan figures such as Delcy Rodríguez and US officials suggest attempts at political transition and cooperation, though these are contested and controversial. The US has also signaled a potential diplomatic thaw by appointing Laura Dogu as head of its mission in Venezuela and engaging with Venezuelan political actors, while President Trump has publicly supported new Venezuelan leadership and revealed secret military tactics used in the region. These developments reflect a complex interplay of military actions, political maneuvering, and diplomatic efforts amid ongoing regional and international scrutiny.
