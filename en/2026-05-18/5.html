Threats, Preconditions and Fragile Talks Between Washington and Tehran

US–Iran Diplomacy 2026-05-18 • 59 articles in total

In brief
  • The U.S. applies intense pressure on Iran with military options and strict demands to force compliance.
  • Iran rejects U.S. terms, mistrusting intentions and insisting on its own conditions for talks.
  • Regional mediators pursue fragile diplomacy amid warnings of global economic and geopolitical fallout if talks fail.

Coverage shows a tense dual track: repeated, stark U.S. warnings and preparations for military options undercut fragile diplomacy, while Tehran publicly rejects U.S. terms and insists on its own preconditions and distrust of Washington. Multiple outlets report that the United States has set five conditions — including limits on enrichment and transfers of material — and that those technical demands have not bridged the gap. Regional mediators and limited confidence-building moves, such as pauses in operations and exchanged peace proposals, appear to slow escalation but have not produced a breakthrough. Analysts warn of economic and geopolitical ripple effects if talks fail and emphasize that both sides face domestic and international constraints that complicate a negotiated settlement.
