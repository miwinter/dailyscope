Global push of pragmatic bilateral deals: visits, sector pacts and strategic partnerships

Region: Bilateral Deals 2026-05-18 • 80 articles in total

In brief
  • States worldwide pursue diverse bilateral agreements on security, energy, trade, and infrastructure to boost capacity and influence.
  • Regional partnerships reveal geopolitical diversification with cooperation spanning traditional and emerging global powers.
  • Diplomatic efforts mix pragmatic economic, security, and cultural cooperation to strengthen connectivity and strategic autonomy.

Over the period, states across Africa, Asia, Europe and the Pacific pursued a wave of bilateral engagement through state visits, memoranda, and sector-specific agreements covering security, energy, trade, transport and development. Several initiatives emphasized defense and aviation cooperation, while others concentrated on energy projects, infrastructure and people-to-people ties such as tourism, education and health. A number of partnerships signal geopolitical diversification, with states deepening links with partners from Russia, China and Turkey alongside traditional Western and regional allies. Overall, the actions reflect pragmatic diplomacy aimed at strengthening capacity, connectivity and influence at national and regional levels.
