5.2-magnitude quake in southern China: casualties, evacuations and rescue operations

China Earthquake Response 2026-05-18 • 10 articles in total

In brief
  • A 5.2-magnitude earthquake hit southern China causing fatalities, building damage, and evacuations in the Guangxi region.
  • Emergency services have mobilized for search, rescue, and damage assessment amid warnings of possible aftershocks.
  • Casualty and damage figures remain provisional with reports varying on deaths, injuries, and missing persons.

A magnitude 5.2 earthquake struck southern China, centered in the Guangxi/southwest region, producing strong shaking across nearby towns. Reports on casualties vary: some outlets confirm two deaths while others report injuries, missing people or no immediate fatalities, and thousands of residents have been evacuated. Numerous buildings were damaged or collapsed in affected communities, and local authorities have mobilized search, rescue and relief teams. Officials are continuing damage assessments, warning of possible aftershocks and stressing that casualty and damage figures remain provisional.
