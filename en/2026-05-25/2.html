Escalation over Kyiv: hypersonic strikes, global condemnation, and regional security spillovers

Russo-Ukrainian War 2026-05-25 • 93 articles in total

In brief
  • Russian strikes cause heavy civilian casualties and infrastructure damage in Ukraine, using advanced missiles and drones.
  • Ukraine and allies condemn attacks, demand UN action, and expand sanctions while Russia claims military targets and retaliation.
  • NATO and neighbors report security risks and potential spillovers, while internal Russian unrest suggests Kremlin pressures.

A wave of intense Russian strikes — including reported use of hypersonic/Oreshnik and other long‑range missiles and mass drone barrages — has caused significant civilian casualties and infrastructure damage in Kyiv and beyond. Ukraine has repeatedly called for emergency UN Security Council action while President Zelensky and Kyiv officials accuse Moscow of deliberately targeting civilian sites. Moscow and affiliated sources assert the strikes hit military targets and openly confirm use of nuclear‑capable systems, framing some actions as retaliation. European and other international actors have strongly condemned the attacks, expanded sanctions and warned about risks of escalation, while neighbours and NATO partners report security incidents and rising concerns over spillover effects and the targeting of humanitarian and media infrastructure.
