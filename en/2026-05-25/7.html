Crackdown on Turkey's Opposition Escalates

Turkey Opposition Crackdown 2026-05-25 • 21 articles in total

In brief
  • Police used tear gas and rubber bullets to evict CHP opposition party members following court rulings invalidating party leadership.
  • The crackdown deepens Turkey's political crisis, threatens democracy, and worsens relations with the West amid opposition divisions.
  • The government reversed a controversial university closure after protests, marking a rare concession amid rising criticism.

A wave of police raids and forcible evictions swept the headquarters of Turkey's main opposition CHP, with numerous outlets reporting riot police using tear gas, rubber bullets and forced entry to clear supporters and officials. Several reports note that the operation followed court rulings that invalidated party leadership results and authorized evictions, while other pieces document leaders locking themselves inside or being physically removed. Analysts warn the measures deepen a political crisis, risk undermining democratic norms and could strain Turkey's relations with Western partners. The coverage also highlights internal divisions within the opposition, which may compound the party's ability to respond. In a related sign of pressure on civil institutions, the government quietly reversed a controversial university closure after protests, seen as a rare concession amid mounting criticism.
