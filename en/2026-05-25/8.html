Mass evacuations in California over chemical tank leak

California Chemical Evacuation 2026-05-25 • 15 articles in total

In brief
  • A damaged methyl methacrylate storage tank in southern California caused large-scale evacuations of 40,000 to 50,000 people and emergency response efforts.
  • Authorities issued evacuation and shelter-in-place orders while monitoring air quality and reported no casualties so far.
  • Hazmat teams are containing the leak, investigating the cause, and managing explosion and toxic vapor risks, with federal emergency aid requested.

A damaged storage tank holding methyl methacrylate in southern California prompted a major emergency response and large-scale evacuations. Reports vary on the number evacuated, citing roughly 40,000 to 50,000 people as authorities issued shelter-in-place and evacuation orders while monitoring air quality. Officials have sought federal emergency assistance, described the explosion and toxic-vapor risks, and reported a crack in the tank that may have relieved pressure as hazmat teams work to contain the leak. No casualties have been reported so far, but residents remain displaced as responders continue containment, investigations, and warnings about potential environmental and health impacts.
