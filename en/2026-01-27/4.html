Severe US Winter Storm Causes Widespread Disruption and Fatalities

US Winter Storm Warnings 2026-01-27 • 36 articles in total

In brief
  • A historic winter storm in the US has caused heavy snow, freezing rain, extreme cold, and affected over 200 million people.
  • The storm triggered widespread power outages, flight cancellations, hazardous travel, and at least 6 to over 30 fatalities.
  • Authorities declared emergencies across multiple states and are managing relief and restoration efforts amid infrastructure challenges.

A historic and severe winter storm has swept across the United States, affecting over 200 million people with heavy snow, freezing rain, and extreme cold temperatures. The storm has caused significant disruptions including widespread power outages affecting over one million homes, thousands of flight cancellations, and hazardous travel conditions. Fatalities reported range from at least 6 to over 30, with many deaths attributed to cold exposure and storm-related accidents. Authorities across multiple states have declared emergencies and are actively engaged in relief and restoration efforts. The event highlights the vulnerability of infrastructure and the challenges faced in managing large-scale natural disasters in the US.
