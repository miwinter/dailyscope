Escalation of US Tariffs on South Korean Imports Amid Trade Agreement Delays

US–South Korea Tariff Hike 2026-01-27 • 17 articles in total

In brief
  • The US increased tariffs on South Korean goods from 15% to 25% due to delayed ratification of a bilateral trade agreement.
  • The tariff hike targets multiple sectors and aims to pressure South Korea to meet trade commitments.
  • South Korean parties blame their government for delays and warn of harm to trade relations and economic stability.

The United States, under President Donald Trump, has increased tariffs on South Korean goods from 15% to 25%, citing delays in the ratification of a bilateral trade agreement by South Korea's legislature. This tariff hike targets various sectors including automobiles, pharmaceuticals, and lumber, reflecting a protectionist stance aimed at pressuring South Korea to comply with trade commitments. South Korean political parties blame their government for the delays, which they say have provoked the US response and risk harming bilateral trade relations. The move has sparked concerns about escalating trade tensions and potential negative impacts on global markets and economic stability. Overall, the situation underscores ongoing friction in US-South Korea trade relations despite their alliance and existing agreements.

US Perspective: Enforcement and Protectionism

From the US viewpoint, the tariff increase is a necessary enforcement measure against South Korea's failure to ratify the bilateral trade agreement promptly. The Trump administration frames the hike as a protectionist policy to defend American economic interests and address perceived unfair trade practices. This perspective emphasizes pressure on South Korea to comply with trade commitments and reduce trade imbalances.

South Korean sources focus on internal political dynamics, blaming the government and legislature for delays in ratifying the trade agreement, which they argue have triggered the US tariff hike.
