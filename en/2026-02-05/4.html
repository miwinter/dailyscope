Expiration of New START Treaty Sparks Global Nuclear Security Concerns

Russia Nuclear Treaty Expiry 2026-02-05 • 35 articles in total

In brief
  • The expiration of the New START treaty ends formal nuclear arms control between the US and Russia, raising global security concerns.
  • Russia rejects treaty limits and signals a shift to a world without nuclear constraints, increasing tensions and potential arms races.
  • Global leaders and experts urge renewed dialogue and multilateral efforts to restore arms control and maintain strategic stability amid emerging nuclear risks.

The expiration of the New START nuclear arms control treaty between the United States and Russia has raised widespread international alarm about the future of nuclear arms control and global security. Russia has declared it is no longer bound by the treaty, signaling a shift toward a world without formal nuclear limits, while the US and global leaders express concern over potential arms races and increased tensions. The United Nations and the Pope have called for renewed dialogue and treaty renewal to prevent escalation. Experts highlight the challenges posed by the absence of this key agreement, especially given China's growing nuclear arsenal and the involvement of new players. Overall, the lapse marks a critical setback in decades of nuclear disarmament efforts and underscores the urgent need for diplomatic engagement to maintain strategic stability.

Russian Perspective: Treaty Expiry as Freedom to Act

Russian sources emphasize that with the expiration of the New START treaty, Russia is no longer bound by its nuclear arms limitations and is prepared to take decisive measures to protect its national security. Moscow criticizes the United States for its stance and claims that proposals to extend the treaty were ignored. Russia signals readiness to operate in a world without nuclear arms control boundaries, reflecting a strategic shift and a more assertive posture.
