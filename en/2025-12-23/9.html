Assassination of Russian General Sarvarov Highlights Escalating Covert Conflict

Russian General Assassination 2025-12-23 • 19 articles in total

In brief
  • Lieutenant General Fanil Sarvarov was killed by a car bomb in Moscow, the third senior Russian general assassinated in sixteen months.
  • Russian sources blame Ukrainian intelligence for the attack, viewing it as part of covert warfare in the Russia-Ukraine conflict.
  • The assassination highlights internal Russian unrest and power struggles alongside growing security and political instability.

Lieutenant General Fanil Sarvarov, a high-ranking Russian military officer, was killed by a car bomb explosion in Moscow, marking the third assassination of a senior Russian general within sixteen months. Russian authorities widely suspect Ukrainian intelligence services of orchestrating the attack, reflecting the intense covert hostilities amid the ongoing Russia-Ukraine conflict. The assassination has heightened security concerns and political tensions within Russia, with some sources also pointing to possible internal betrayals or power struggles. This event underscores the volatile and dangerous environment surrounding Russian military leadership and the broader geopolitical instability in the region.
