Cambodia-Thailand Border Conflict: Escalation, Displacement, and Diplomatic Efforts

Cambodia–Thailand Ceasefire Talks 2025-12-23 • 28 articles in total

In brief
  • The Cambodia-Thailand border conflict involves frequent military clashes causing heavy civilian casualties and massive displacement, complicating peace efforts.
  • Cambodia demands ceasefire talks on neutral ground citing safety concerns, while Thailand insists on continuing military operations to counter attacks.
  • ASEAN and international actors mediate amid fragile ceasefires and ongoing violence, with the conflict causing a severe humanitarian crisis and regional instability.

The Cambodia-Thailand border conflict has escalated with frequent military clashes, including rocket and air strikes, causing significant civilian casualties and displacing over half a million people. Both countries accuse each other of aggression, complicating peace efforts despite ASEAN's mediation and international involvement. Cambodia insists on holding ceasefire talks on neutral ground, such as Malaysia or Kuala Lumpur, citing safety concerns, while Thailand has proposed border meetings. Although a ceasefire agreement was announced, hostilities have continued, reflecting the fragility of the peace process. Regional and international actors remain engaged to facilitate dialogue and reduce tensions amid ongoing humanitarian concerns.

Thailand's Perspective: Military Response and Condemnation of Cambodian Attacks

Thai sources emphasize their military actions targeting Cambodian positions in response to attacks, particularly BM-21 rocket strikes on Thai civilian areas causing injuries. Thailand condemns Cambodia for these attacks and highlights the ongoing security threats along the border. The Thai government maintains that military operations will continue until conditions for peace are met, reflecting a stance focused on defense and retaliation.

Cambodia's Perspective: Calls for Neutral Ground Talks and Accusations of Thai Aggression

Cambodia stresses the need for ceasefire talks to be held on neutral territory, such as Malaysia or Kuala Lumpur, to ensure fairness and safety amid ongoing violence. Cambodian authorities accuse Thailand of launching air strikes and continuing attacks despite announced talks, highlighting the fragility of peace efforts. The country also underscores the severe humanitarian impact, with massive displacement and civilian suffering due to the conflict.

Regional and International Mediation Efforts and Challenges

ASEAN and international actors, including the US and Malaysia, are actively involved in mediating the conflict, seeking to revive ceasefires and facilitate dialogue. Despite these efforts, ceasefire agreements have been fragile and sometimes perceived as rushed, with continued clashes undermining progress. The situation presents a significant diplomatic challenge for ASEAN, highlighting the complexity of resolving entrenched border disputes in Southeast Asia.

Humanitarian Impact and Ongoing Instability

The conflict has resulted in a large-scale humanitarian crisis, with nearly one million displaced people and damage to civilian infrastructure such as hospitals and schools. The ongoing violence exacerbates regional instability and raises concerns about broader escalation. Both countries face significant challenges in protecting civilians and restoring peace, underscoring the urgent need for effective conflict resolution.
