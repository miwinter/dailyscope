International Reactions to María Corina Machado's 2025 Nobel Peace Prize

2025-10-12 • 43 articles in total

María Corina Machado, a Venezuelan opposition leader recognized for her pro-democracy activism, was awarded the 2025 Nobel Peace Prize, drawing widespread global attention to Venezuela's political crisis. The award highlights her efforts to promote democratic rights and peaceful political transition amidst Maduro's authoritarian regime. Many countries and international institutions have welcomed the recognition as a beacon of hope for democracy and peace in Venezuela, while some governments, particularly those allied with Venezuela's current regime, have criticized or dismissed the prize. The award also sparked controversy due to Machado's dedication of the prize to former U.S. President Donald Trump and the geopolitical implications surrounding Venezuela's crisis.
