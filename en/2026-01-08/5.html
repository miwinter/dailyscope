US Withdrawals from International Organizations Mark Major Shift in Foreign Policy

US Treaty Withdrawals 2026-01-08 • 36 articles in total

In brief
  • The US under Trump withdrew from 66 international organizations, including UN bodies and key climate treaties, signaling a shift to unilateralism.
  • This withdrawal aims to protect US national interests and reduce commitments to international bodies seen as unnecessary.
  • The move raises global concerns about weakening cooperation on climate, health, trade, security, and international governance.

The United States, under President Donald Trump, has withdrawn from 66 international organizations, including 31 affiliated with the United Nations and key climate treaties such as the UN Framework Convention on Climate Change. This move reflects a significant shift toward unilateralism and a retreat from multilateral cooperation, justified by the US government as protecting national interests. The withdrawals span diverse sectors including climate, health, trade, and security, raising concerns globally about the weakening of international governance and cooperation. Reactions from the international community are mixed, with many expressing worry over the potential negative impacts on global diplomacy and environmental efforts. The decision underscores a broader US policy trend prioritizing sovereignty and skepticism toward global institutions.

US Government Justification and Policy Shift

Articles from or reflecting US official statements focus on the rationale that these organizations no longer serve American interests and that the withdrawals are part of a broader 'America First' policy. This viewpoint stresses national sovereignty, economic strength, and a desire to reduce commitments to what are seen as unnecessary or ideologically driven international bodies. The move is portrayed as a strategic realignment of US foreign policy away from multilateralism.
