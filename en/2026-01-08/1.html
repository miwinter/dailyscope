Escalating US-Venezuela Conflict: Military Operations, Sanctions, and Geopolitical Tensions

US–Venezuela Political and Sanctions Tensions 2026-01-08 • 130 articles in total

In brief
  • The US has conducted military operations and sanctions aiming to oust Maduro and limit allies' influence by controlling Venezuelan oil exports.
  • Venezuela and its allies condemn US actions as illegal and harmful violations of sovereignty causing civilian casualties.
  • International actors call for respect of international law and peaceful dialogue amid concerns over US unilateral interventions and regional instability.

The US-Venezuela relationship has sharply deteriorated with the US conducting military operations resulting in the capture of President Nicolás Maduro and significant casualties, including Venezuelan and Cuban nationals. The US has intensified sanctions and seized multiple oil tankers linked to Venezuela and Russia, aiming to control Venezuelan oil exports and isolate the Maduro regime from allies like Russia, China, Iran, and Cuba. Venezuela and its allies condemn these actions as violations of sovereignty and international law, while the US and some allies justify them as strategic moves to promote political transition and curb adversarial influence. International reactions are mixed, with the UN and various countries calling for respect of international law and peaceful dialogue, whereas others support or condemn the US interventions. The conflict has broad geopolitical implications, affecting regional stability, global energy markets, and great power rivalries involving the US, Russia, and China.

US and Allied Perspective: Strategic Pressure and Political Transition

This viewpoint emphasizes the US government's justification of military and economic actions against Venezuela as necessary to remove Nicolás Maduro, promote political transition, and curb the influence of adversarial nations like Russia, China, Iran, and Cuba. US officials outline phased plans for stability and recovery, assert control over Venezuelan oil exports, and defend tanker seizures as enforcement of sanctions. Supporters, including Israel and some Western allies, view these moves as strategic blows to hostile alliances and necessary for regional security.

Venezuelan and Allied Perspective: Sovereignty Violations and Humanitarian Impact

This perspective condemns US military operations and sanctions as illegal violations of Venezuela's sovereignty and international law, highlighting the humanitarian toll including deaths of civilians and soldiers. Venezuelan officials and allies like Cuba, Russia, and some African nations denounce the US actions as acts of aggression, kidnapping, and piracy. They call for respect of international norms, invite UN observers to assess damage, and emphasize resistance against foreign intervention.

International and Regional Reactions: Calls for Lawfulness and Diplomatic Resolution

Various international actors including the United Nations, European countries, African nations, and regional Caribbean leaders express concern over the US military intervention in Venezuela. They emphasize the importance of respecting international law, sovereignty, and the UN Charter, urging de-escalation and peaceful dialogue. Some criticize the US for unilateral actions that undermine global order, while others call for diplomatic engagement to resolve the crisis and prevent further instability in Latin America.
