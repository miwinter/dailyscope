Trade Policy Dynamics: US-China Truce and Divergent Global Reactions

2025-08-12 • 77 articles in total

The analyzed articles largely focus on the ongoing trade conflict between the United States and China, highlighting multiple 90-day extensions of a tariff truce aimed at stabilizing economic relations and postponing further escalation. Significant developments include unusual agreements where U.S. chipmakers Nvidia and AMD agree to share revenue from sales to China, reflecting complex negotiations balancing technological competition and economic interests. While the U.S. and China appear committed to dialogue and gradual easing of trade tensions, other countries express mixed reactions ranging from cooperation and pragmatic engagement to criticism and protective reactions against U.S. trade policies. Countries like India resist U.S. pressures with bans and trade autonomy strategies, whereas some like South Korea navigate tariffs while seeking investment opportunities. Overall, the global trade policy landscape remains tense yet cautiously optimistic for negotiated settlements amid varying national interests.

Third-Party Countries Embracing Trade Pragmatism Amid US-China Tensions

This group includes countries that engage pragmatically with trade tensions by fostering regional cooperation and managing impacts of global tariff policies. Nations like South Korea accept certain U.S. tariffs while securing large investments, reflecting a balancing act between compliance and economic growth. The common stance here is managing trade relations with an eye on stability and long-term strategic partnerships rather than confrontation.

Criticism and Resistance to US Trade Protectionism

Some countries strongly criticize or resist U.S. trade policies, perceiving them as protectionist and harmful to their economic interests. China publicly denounces U.S. protectionism affecting other countries like Brazil, while India bans American imports and pursues trade autonomy as resistance measures. Switzerland and Liechtenstein express discontent over high U.S. tariffs harming their industries. India also faces domestic calls to boycott American goods in retaliation. This perspective reflects concerns over sovereignty, economic harm, and nationalistic responses to perceived unequal trade pressures imposed by the U.S.
