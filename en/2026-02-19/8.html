Political Instability in Peru Continues with Election of José María Balcázar as Interim President

Peru Interim President Vote 2026-02-19 • 13 articles in total

In brief
  • Peru has had eight interim presidents in the last decade, showing chronic political instability and governance challenges.
  • José Jerí was removed by Congress over corruption scandals just before general elections, highlighting political crises.
  • José María Balcázar, a leftist, was elected as a transitional president to stabilize Peru until the new elected leader takes office.

Peru's political landscape remains highly unstable as the Congress removed interim president José Jerí amid corruption investigations and political controversies shortly before the general elections. Subsequently, leftist José María Balcázar was elected as the new interim president, marking the eighth leadership change in Peru over the past decade. Balcázar's appointment is seen as a transitional measure to stabilize the country until the newly elected president assumes office. The frequent changes in leadership highlight ongoing governance challenges and public distrust in Peru's political system. This turmoil underscores the difficulties Peru faces in achieving political continuity and stability.
