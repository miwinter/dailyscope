Hurricane Melissa Devastates Caribbean with Widespread Destruction and Rising Death Toll

2025-10-31 • 77 articles in total

Hurricane Melissa, one of the most powerful storms in recent history, caused significant destruction across several Caribbean nations including Jamaica, Haiti, Cuba, and the Bahamas. The storm left tens of thousands homeless, disrupted infrastructure, and led to a death toll approaching 50, with extensive flooding and damage reported throughout the region. Recovery and emergency response efforts are ongoing, involving local governments, regional neighbors, and international aid, highlighting challenges such as logistical difficulties and the effects of climate change on hurricane intensity. Various countries have expressed solidarity through humanitarian aid and support, while some have also focused on evacuations and preparations for the hurricane's northward path toward Bermuda. Scientists emphasize that climate change has increased the likelihood of such intense storms, underscoring the need for global climate action to mitigate future risks.
