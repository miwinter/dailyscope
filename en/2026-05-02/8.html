Caucasus: Armenia's Western Turn and Azerbaijan's Rift with Europe

Caucasus Diplomacy 2026-05-02 • 7 articles in total

In brief
  • Armenia is increasing European ties through summits and meetings while balancing Russian influence ahead of elections.
  • Azerbaijan opposes European institutions by suspending cooperation and plans to leave Euronest, labeling Brussels hostile.
  • Despite tensions, Azerbaijan and Armenia maintain bilateral contacts amid ongoing Russian influence and regional security concerns.

Diplomatic activity across the Caucasus is intensifying as Armenia deepens ties with Europe and regional partners while trying to balance Russian influence. Armenia is hosting European summits and scheduling high-level meetings, including with Ukraine's president, to project a move toward EU engagement ahead of key domestic elections. At the same time Azerbaijan has taken a confrontational stance with Brussels, suspending cooperation with the European Parliament and moving to leave the Euronest assembly, framing European institutions as hostile. Despite these broader rifts, bilateral contacts persist—Azerbaijani officials have visited Yerevan—while Moscow-focused reporting underscores continued Russian influence and fragile security dynamics in the region. The interplay of Western outreach, Azerbaijani pushback, and Russia's regional role complicates prospects for stable, long-term settlement.
