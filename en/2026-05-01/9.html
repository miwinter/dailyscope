Taiwan tensions shadow Trump–Xi preparations

US–China Diplomacy 2026-05-01 • 9 articles in total

In brief
  • Beijing warns Taiwan risks US-China ties and urges Washington to maintain stability.
  • US and Chinese officials engage in tense yet pragmatic talks ahead of the Trump–Xi summit.
  • Taiwan asserts diplomatic agency and domestic optics shape foreign-policy perceptions.

As Trump and Xi prepare to meet, Beijing has repeatedly warned that Taiwan is the greatest risk to US–China ties, using diplomatic exchanges — including a recent phone call between foreign ministers — to press Washington for restraint. At the same time, US and Chinese officials are maintaining frank channels: ministers have engaged in tense exchanges while trade teams hold candid talks aimed at smoothing economic disputes ahead of the summit. Taipei is actively asserting its diplomatic agency, with commentary from local actors and planned international travel by opposition leaders complicating cross-strait dynamics. Back home, symbolic domestic controversies show how optics and political perception can intersect with and shape foreign-policy debates. Together the reporting depicts a mix of warning, pragmatic negotiation, and regional maneuvering that will frame the leaders’ meeting.
