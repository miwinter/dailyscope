Africa public diplomacy: diplomacy, security and contested ties

Africa Public Diplomacy 2026-05-01 • 48 articles in total

In brief
  • African countries are actively engaging in diplomacy through state visits, trade talks, and multilateral forums to promote security, partnerships, and development.
  • Regional security issues dominate with coordinated efforts against militant threats and ongoing UN peacekeeping missions.
  • Bilateral tensions and diaspora safety concerns highlight challenges amid economic diplomacy and efforts to enhance influence and cooperation.

This set of reports shows active African diplomacy across state visits, credentialing and high-level summits as countries seek trade, investment and international partnerships while promoting national initiatives. Regional security in the Sahel and the aftermath of militant offensives dominate many outlets, prompting military losses, national tributes and coordinated security operations across borders. The UN extension of the South Sudan mission and multiple international engagements (AU‑EU, IGAD–Norway, Libya economic forums) underline ongoing multilateral involvement in African stability and development. Simultaneously, bilateral strains surfaced sharply in the Madagascar–France episodes, while diaspora safety, xenophobia and media representation remain recurrent diplomatic concerns. Economic diplomacy and soft‑power efforts — from festival programming to investment talks with business leaders — round out an emphasis on projecting influence and attracting partners.
