Historic $35 Billion Israel-Egypt Gas Deal Strengthens Regional Energy Ties

Israel–Egypt Gas Accord 2025-12-18 • 16 articles in total

In brief
  • Israel approved a $35 billion natural gas export deal with Egypt, the largest in Israel's history.
  • The agreement boosts energy cooperation, economic ties, and regional security between Israel and Egypt.
  • The deal reflects strategic partnership supported by the U.S. to promote Middle East stability and diplomacy.

Israeli Prime Minister Benjamin Netanyahu has approved and announced a historic natural gas export deal with Egypt valued at approximately $35 billion, marking the largest such agreement in Israel's history. The deal involves major energy companies like Chevron and is expected to enhance energy cooperation, boost economic ties, and contribute to regional energy security between the two countries. This agreement reflects a deepening strategic partnership amid complex geopolitical dynamics in the Middle East, with potential impacts on regional energy markets and diplomatic relations. The United States is also playing a role in encouraging this cooperation as part of broader efforts to stabilize the region and promote diplomatic engagement.
