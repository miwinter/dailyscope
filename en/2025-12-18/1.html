US-Venezuela Oil Tanker Blockade: Escalating Tensions and Divergent Views

Trump Orders Venezuela Tanker Blockade 2025-12-18 • 50 articles in total

In brief
  • The U.S. imposed a total blockade on Venezuelan oil tankers to pressure Maduro's government, designated as a foreign terrorist organization.
  • Venezuela and its allies condemn the blockade as aggression and maintain oil exports despite sanctions.
  • Analysts warn the blockade could harm Venezuela's economy and disrupt global oil markets and diplomatic relations.

U.S. President Donald Trump ordered a total blockade on sanctioned Venezuelan oil tankers entering or leaving the country, labeling the Maduro government as a foreign terrorist organization. This move aims to intensify economic pressure on Venezuela by restricting its vital oil exports, escalating longstanding political and economic tensions. Venezuela and its allies condemn the blockade as an act of aggression, colonialism, and a threat to regional stability, asserting that oil exports continue despite the sanctions. Analysts warn the blockade could severely impact Venezuela's economy but may also have unintended consequences for global oil markets and U.S. diplomatic relations. The situation remains volatile, with Venezuela seeking international support and deploying naval escorts to protect its oil shipments amid fears of further escalation.
