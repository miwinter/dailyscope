Turkey's Large-Scale Arrests Foil ISIS Holiday Attack Plans

Turkey ISIS Arrests 2025-12-26 • 21 articles in total

In brief
  • Turkish authorities arrested over 100 suspects linked to ISIS planning attacks on Christmas and New Year celebrations.
  • The operations targeted non-Muslim communities to prevent imminent terrorist threats during major holidays.
  • Media highlight Turkey's strong counterterrorism stance and ongoing investigations ensuring national security.

Turkish authorities have arrested over 100 individuals suspected of belonging to the Islamic State (ISIS) who were allegedly planning attacks targeting Christmas and New Year celebrations across the country. These arrests, numbering around 115 to 137 suspects, were part of coordinated operations primarily in Istanbul and other regions, aiming to prevent imminent terrorist threats during the holiday season. The suspects were reportedly planning attacks mainly against non-Muslim communities, reflecting ongoing concerns about ISIS's intent to destabilize Turkey. The crackdown highlights Turkey's persistent counterterrorism efforts and its commitment to national security amid regional extremist threats. Investigations and security measures continue to ensure public safety during this sensitive period.
