Zelensky Unveils US-Backed 20-Point Peace Plan Amid Ongoing Conflict

Ukraine Peace Plan Reveal 2025-12-26 • 20 articles in total

In brief
  • Ukrainian President Zelensky and the US jointly developed a 20-point peace plan aiming to end the war with Russia.
  • The plan proposes ceasefire, troop withdrawals, demilitarized zones, and territorial negotiations but faces uncertain acceptance from Russia.
  • International involvement highlights diplomatic efforts amid unresolved issues like territorial control and prisoner releases.

Ukrainian President Volodymyr Zelensky has revealed a comprehensive 20-point peace plan developed in cooperation with the United States, aiming to end the ongoing war with Russia. The plan includes proposals for ceasefire, troop withdrawals, demilitarized zones, security guarantees, and territorial negotiations, reflecting significant diplomatic efforts to resolve the conflict. Zelensky has expressed cautious optimism but awaits Russia's response, which remains uncertain as Moscow signals likely rejection. The initiative represents a notable shift toward negotiation and compromise, though key issues such as control over contested regions and prisoner releases remain contentious. International involvement, particularly from the US and Europe, underscores the complexity and global stakes of the peace process.
