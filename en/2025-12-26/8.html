Trump-Backed Nasry Asfura Wins Contested Honduran Presidency

Honduras Trump-Backed Election Victory 2025-12-26 • 17 articles in total

In brief
  • Nasry Asfura won the Honduran presidential election amid disputed vote counts and fraud allegations.
  • International organizations and countries recognize Asfura's win and hope for political stability and diplomatic ties.
  • Asfura's victory signals a political shift influenced by U.S. support and aims for national reconciliation.

Nasry Asfura, a conservative candidate endorsed by U.S. President Donald Trump, has been declared the winner of the Honduran presidential election after a prolonged and disputed vote count. His victory, marked by a narrow margin and allegations of fraud from opposition parties, has sparked political tension and calls for transparency within Honduras. The international community, including organizations like the OAS and various countries, has largely recognized and welcomed Asfura's win, emphasizing hopes for stability and continued diplomatic relations. Asfura has called for national reconciliation and peace amid the controversy, signaling his intent to unify the country. This election outcome is seen as a pivotal moment for Honduras' domestic politics and its future relations with the United States and the region.
