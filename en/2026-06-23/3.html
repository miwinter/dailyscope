Starmer resigns, Labour leadership contest begins

UK PM Resignation 2026-06-23 • 70 articles in total

In brief
  • Keir Starmer resigned as UK prime minister and Labour leader, prompting a leadership contest and caretaker period.
  • The Labour Party faces internal pressure with Andy Burnham emerging as a leading successor candidate.
  • The leadership selection process has a set timetable and raises questions about political stability since Brexit.

Keir Starmer announced his resignation as United Kingdom prime minister and leader of the Labour Party, triggering an immediate leadership contest and a caretaker period while a successor is chosen. Many outlets report internal pressure and loss of confidence within Labour, with Andy Burnham repeatedly mentioned as a leading contender and rapid moves to position successors. Several pieces explain the formal timetable and mechanics for selecting the next prime minister, noting the process could conclude within weeks or by September depending on party rules. Commentators and analysts place the resignation in the context of repeated leadership turnover since Brexit, debating whether this episode signals deeper political dysfunction or can be managed as an orderly transition.
