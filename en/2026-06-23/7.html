State of emergency eases blockades but tensions persist

Bolivia: State of Emergency 2026-06-23 • 9 articles in total

In brief
  • The government used emergency powers and police to clear roadblocks and reopen highways, leading to gradual economic normalization.
  • Evo Morales called the protest suspension a strategic pause to maintain political pressure and continue negotiations.
  • Violent clashes persist between miners and police, showing unresolved social conflicts despite overall de-escalation.

After President Rodrigo Paz declared a state of emergency, security forces moved to clear major roadblocks and reopen key highways, helping markets and transport begin to normalize. Authorities reported a steep drop in blockades after agreements with union leaders and continued monitoring of routes. Evo Morales and his supporters framed a suspension of protests as a tactical pause — a "truce, not surrender" — while pledging to continue political pressure and negotiations. Nonetheless, clashes such as confrontations between miners and police underline unresolved labor and social grievances, leaving the situation fragile despite signs of de-escalation.
