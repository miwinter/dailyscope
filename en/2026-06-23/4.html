Escalation of strikes, drone warfare and diplomatic pressure

Russo-Ukrainian War 2026-06-23 • 66 articles in total

In brief
  • The Russo-Ukrainian war is intensifying with drone attacks on Moscow and widespread strikes on Ukrainian cities causing significant civilian casualties.
  • Kyiv seeks diplomatic support at the UN while Western countries increase military aid, amid Russian claims that NATO prepares for confrontation.
  • Belarus faces regional tension over Kyiv's ultimatum on retransmitters, with domestic debate on potential consequences and calls for restraint.

The reporting shows an intensifying phase of the Russo‑Ukrainian war: cross‑border strikes and large drone attacks have hit Moscow and Russian infrastructure while Russia continues widespread strikes across Ukrainian cities and regions. Civilians on both sides have suffered significant casualties and damage to industrial and civilian sites, and analysts point to evolving Ukrainian drone tactics and gaps in Russian air defences. Diplomatically Kyiv has pressed its case at the UN and Western partners are increasing military and industrial support, even as Moscow and its allies depict NATO and the West as preparing for confrontation. Belarus has become a regional flashpoint after Kyiv’s ultimatum about retransmitters, and commentators warn that prospects for durable peace remain fragile.
