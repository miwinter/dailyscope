Escalating US-Iran Military Tensions in the Gulf

US–Iran Gulf Buildup 2026-01-26 • 11 articles in total

In brief
  • The US has deployed naval and air forces near Iran, including an aircraft carrier strike group, raising conflict fears.
  • Iran warns of 'all-out war' and harsh retaliation if attacked and its Supreme Leader is reportedly sheltering in a bunker.
  • The situation heightens regional tensions and risks escalation amid strategic military posturing by both sides.

The Gulf region is witnessing a significant military buildup as the United States deploys naval and air forces near Iran, including an aircraft carrier strike group, raising fears of a potential conflict. Iran has responded with stern warnings, declaring that any attack would be met with 'all-out war' and promising harsh retaliations. Reports of Iran's Supreme Leader taking refuge in an underground bunker underscore the heightened state of alert on both sides. The situation is further complicated by political rhetoric from US President Donald Trump, who has publicly announced the deployment of a US 'armada' to the Gulf, intensifying regional anxieties. Overall, the military posturing and strategic risks highlight the fragile security environment and the potential for escalation in the Gulf.
