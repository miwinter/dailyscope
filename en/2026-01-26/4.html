US-Canada Tariff Threats Amid China Trade Deal Tensions

US–Canada Tariff Threats Under Trump 2026-01-26 • 28 articles in total

In brief
  • The US threatened 100% tariffs on Canada if it signed a trade deal with China to counter China's influence and protect US interests.
  • Canada consistently denied plans for a free trade agreement with China to maintain stable trade with the US and preserve economic sovereignty.

During the Trump administration, the United States repeatedly threatened Canada with 100% tariffs if Canada pursued or finalized a trade agreement with China. These threats significantly escalated trade tensions between the US and Canada, placing Canada in a difficult position balancing its economic interests and geopolitical pressures. Canadian officials, including Prime Minister Mark Carney, consistently responded by clarifying that Canada had no intention of signing a free trade agreement with China, aiming to maintain stable trade relations and avoid antagonizing the US. The situation highlighted the complex interplay of North American and global trade policies, with Canada seeking to preserve its economic sovereignty amid US demands and China's growing influence. Overall, the tariff threats underscored the aggressive US trade stance and its impact on international alliances and negotiations.

US Perspective: Aggressive Tariff Threats to Deter Canada-China Trade

The United States, under President Trump, adopted a hardline approach by threatening to impose 100% tariffs on Canadian goods if Canada proceeded with a trade deal with China. This perspective reflects the US strategy to counter China's expanding economic influence and to maintain dominance in North American trade. The US framed these threats as necessary to protect its economic interests and to pressure Canada into aligning with US trade policies.

Canadian Perspective: Rejection of China Free Trade Agreement Amid US Pressure

Canadian officials, including Prime Minister Mark Carney, consistently emphasized that Canada had no plans to enter into a free trade agreement with China, directly responding to US tariff threats. This stance aims to preserve Canada's economic sovereignty while managing diplomatic relations with both the US and China. Canada sought to clarify its position to avoid escalating trade conflicts and to maintain stable bilateral trade relations with the US.
