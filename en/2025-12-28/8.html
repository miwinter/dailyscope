Deadly Mosque Bombings in Syria Highlight Sectarian Tensions and Regional Condemnation

Syria Mosque Bombings 2025-12-28 • 15 articles in total

In brief
  • Mosque bombings in Syria have killed at least eight people and worsened sectarian tensions.
  • International and regional actors condemned the attacks and called for peace and security.
  • Extremist groups claimed responsibility, and the attacks highlighted sectarian violence targeting the Alawite minority.

A series of bombings targeting mosques in Syria, particularly in Homs and Alawite areas, have resulted in at least eight deaths and numerous injuries, exacerbating the country's ongoing conflict and sectarian tensions. Various international and regional actors, including the UN, Muslim World League, and Middle Eastern countries like Yemen and Egypt, have condemned the attacks, emphasizing the need for peace and security. Some reports suggest the attacks targeted the Alawite minority, highlighting the sectarian dimension of the violence. The Syrian government has accused opposition groups such as the Syrian Democratic Forces of violating agreements, while extremist groups have claimed responsibility for some attacks. These incidents underscore the fragile security situation and the humanitarian challenges faced by civilians amid Syria's prolonged instability.
