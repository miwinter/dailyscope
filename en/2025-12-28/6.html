Myanmar Elections Amid Civil War and Military Control

Myanmar Elections Under Military Coup 2025-12-28 • 23 articles in total

In brief
  • Myanmar holds its first election in five years under military rule amid ongoing civil war and repression.
  • International observers condemn the election as illegitimate and manipulated to maintain military power.
  • Asian sources note the election as a political test with uncertain prospects for democracy amid instability.

Myanmar is conducting its first elections in five years under military junta rule amidst ongoing civil war and widespread conflict. The elections are heavily criticized internationally for lacking legitimacy, fairness, and inclusiveness, with many opposition parties barred or suppressed. The military-backed party is widely expected to win, reinforcing military dominance rather than restoring democracy. Despite the junta's portrayal of the elections as a step toward peace and stability, the political crisis and violence persist, drawing condemnation from the United Nations, Western countries, and pro-democracy groups. The electoral process remains overshadowed by repression, low voter turnout in conflict zones, and skepticism about its ability to resolve Myanmar's deep political divisions.

International Condemnation and Skepticism

This cluster reflects the viewpoint of Western countries, the United Nations, and international observers who condemn the elections as illegitimate and a sham. They emphasize the repression of opposition, lack of fairness, and the military's manipulation of the electoral process to maintain power. The elections are seen as undermining democratic principles and failing to address the ongoing civil war and humanitarian crisis.
