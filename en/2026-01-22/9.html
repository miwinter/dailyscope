Global Perspectives on South Korea's Martial Law Sentencing

South Korea Martial Law Verdict 2026-01-22 • 9 articles in total

In brief
  • Former South Korean Prime Minister Han Duck-soo was sentenced to 23 years for supporting martial law and related political unrest.
  • The verdict marks a significant step in South Korea's efforts to hold high-ranking officials accountable for past authoritarian actions.
  • International sources view the case as symbolizing South Korea's commitment to legal accountability, democracy, and addressing human rights abuses.

Former South Korean Prime Minister Han Duck-soo has been sentenced to 23 years in prison for his role in supporting the imposition of martial law and related political unrest. The verdict is widely seen as a significant step in South Korea's efforts to address past authoritarian actions and hold high-ranking officials accountable. Various international sources emphasize the legal and political ramifications, highlighting the connection to former President Yoon Suk-yeol and the broader coup allegations. The case has drawn attention globally as a symbol of South Korea's ongoing reckoning with its recent political crises and commitment to democratic governance. Perspectives differ slightly in focus, with some emphasizing human rights abuses, others the coup and rebellion aspects, and some the political consequences for South Korea's leadership.
