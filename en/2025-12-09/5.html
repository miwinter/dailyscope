Regional and International Responses to the Foiled Benin Military Coup Attempt

Benin Military Coup Attempt 2025-12-09 • 44 articles in total

In brief
  • A failed military coup in December 2025 threatened Benin's political stability but was swiftly quelled by the government and Nigerian military support.
  • Regional bodies ECOWAS and the African Union condemned the coup and pledged support to uphold constitutional order and regional peace.
  • Benin's government arrested mutineers, restored control ahead of elections, and emphasized a firm stance against political destabilization.

In early December 2025, Benin experienced a failed military coup attempt that threatened the country's political stability. The government, led by President Patrice Talon, swiftly regained control with the support of loyalist forces and significant military assistance from Nigeria, including airstrikes and troop deployments. Regional organizations such as ECOWAS and the African Union strongly condemned the coup attempt, emphasizing the importance of constitutional order and pledging support to Benin's legitimate government. The incident has raised concerns about political fragility in West Africa, prompting calls for enhanced security cooperation and democratic resilience. Arrests of involved soldiers and ongoing investigations reflect the government's determination to restore stability and prevent future unrest.

West African and Regional Organizations' Condemnation and Support

ECOWAS, the African Union, and the Pan-African Peace Consortium uniformly condemned the coup attempt, highlighting the threat it posed to regional stability and democratic governance. These organizations expressed readiness to take necessary actions, including deploying standby forces, to support Benin's legitimate government and maintain peace in West Africa. Their statements underscore a collective regional commitment to uphold constitutional order and deter similar destabilizing events.

Beninese Government and President's Perspective on Stability and Justice

President Patrice Talon and Benin's government emphasized that the coup attempt was foiled and the situation is under control. The president condemned the mutiny as a severe threat and reassured citizens of the government's resolve to punish those involved. The government highlighted the restoration of order through loyalist military forces and ongoing efforts to stabilize the country ahead of upcoming elections, reflecting a firm stance against political destabilization.

Nigeria's Military Intervention and Role in Quelling the Coup

Nigeria played a pivotal role in assisting Benin to suppress the coup attempt by deploying fighter jets and ground troops. Nigerian military operations targeted rebel forces, demonstrating Nigeria's active engagement in regional security and its influence within West Africa. This intervention was publicly acknowledged by Nigerian and Beninese authorities, highlighting increased military cooperation to preserve stability in neighboring countries.

Various reports detailed the unfolding of the coup attempt, including the announcement of power seizure by mutineers on national television, arrests of dozens of soldiers, release of kidnapped senior officers, and ongoing manhunts for coup leaders. These accounts reveal the complexity and volatility of the situation, with casualties reported and the government working to consolidate control amid political tensions and security challenges.
