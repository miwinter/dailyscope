US Senate Bipartisan Move to Limit Trump's War Powers on Venezuela

US Senate Limits Trump War Powers 2026-01-09 • 23 articles in total

In brief
  • The US Senate passed bipartisan resolutions to require congressional approval for military action in Venezuela, limiting President Trump's war powers.
  • The legislative efforts reveal Republican opposition to Trump and increased congressional oversight over foreign military interventions.
  • Trump publicly criticized senators restricting his authority, exposing political tensions between the executive and Congress.

The US Senate has taken significant bipartisan steps to restrict President Donald Trump's unilateral military authority concerning Venezuela. Multiple resolutions have been advanced or passed to ensure that any military action requires congressional approval, reflecting growing concerns about executive overreach. This legislative push includes notable Republican opposition to Trump's approach, signaling internal party divisions. Trump has publicly criticized senators who supported these measures, highlighting tensions between the executive branch and Congress. Overall, the Senate's actions underscore a broader effort to reassert congressional oversight over war powers and foreign military interventions.
