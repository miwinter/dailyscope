US Withdrawals from International Organizations Mark Major Shift in Global Engagement

US Treaty Withdrawals 2026-01-09 • 45 articles in total

In brief
  • The US under Trump withdrew from 66 international bodies to prioritize national interests and reduce financial burdens.
  • International critics warn the withdrawals undermine global cooperation and legal commitments, threatening climate and humanitarian efforts.
  • Experts fear US disengagement weakens global security alliances and multilateral institutions, risking international stability.

The United States, under President Donald Trump, formally withdrew from 66 international organizations, including 31 UN agencies and key climate treaties, signaling a significant shift toward unilateralism and prioritization of national interests over multilateral cooperation. This move has drawn widespread international criticism and concern, particularly regarding its impact on global climate initiatives, diplomatic relations, and the effectiveness of multilateral institutions. The US administration justified the withdrawals by citing inefficiency, mismanagement, and organizations no longer serving American interests. Meanwhile, the UN and other global actors have emphasized the legal obligations of the US to continue funding and the potential weakening of international governance. The decision has raised fears about the future of global cooperation, especially in addressing climate change and humanitarian issues affecting vulnerable populations.
