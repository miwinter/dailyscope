Knife attack in Belfast sparks riots, anti‑immigrant violence and policing response

UK-NI Belfast Attack 2026-06-10 • 26 articles in total

In brief
  • A brutal knife attack in Belfast led to a Sudanese suspect's arrest and charges including attempted murder.
  • The attack triggered citywide unrest with riots, arson, and protests linked to migrant tensions and sectarian divisions.
  • Authorities appealed for calm amid investigations, framing events within Northern Ireland's history of political violence.

A brutal knife attack in Belfast, described in some reports as an attempted beheading, left a victim critically injured and led to the arrest and charging of a Sudanese suspect. The assault sparked widespread unrest across the city: masked youths clashed with police, vehicles, homes and buses were set alight and large protests erupted. Political leaders and security forces repeatedly appealed for calm while investigations and criminal charges (including attempted murder and weapons offences) proceeded. Coverage frames the events as both a law‑and‑order crisis and a flashpoint for wider tensions over migration, sectarian divisions and the legacy of the Troubles.
