Shifting US-Venezuela Relations and Political Dynamics Post-Intervention

Venezuela US Intervention Fallout 2026-04-02 • 11 articles in total

In brief
  • Maduro is ousted but Venezuela's regime structures remain intact, showing limited political change.
  • The US lifted sanctions on interim president Delcy Rodríguez, signaling cautious optimism and policy shifts.
  • Military purges under US pressure aim to consolidate power amid ongoing instability and governance challenges.

Three months after the US operation in Venezuela, the political landscape remains complex with Maduro ousted but the regime's structures persisting. The US has notably shifted its approach by lifting sanctions on interim president Delcy Rodríguez, signaling a potential diplomatic thaw and recalibration of policy. US officials and Venezuelan leaders express cautious optimism about political recovery and upcoming elections, while ongoing military purges under Washington's pressure highlight efforts to consolidate power and stabilize governance. Regional and international observers debate the implications of these developments for Venezuela's future stability and US influence in Latin America. The evolving situation reflects a nuanced balance between pressure, engagement, and internal political restructuring.
