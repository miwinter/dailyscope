Escalating Iran War: Military Strikes, Regional Tensions, and Global Impact

Iran War 2026-03-25 • 61 articles in total

In brief
  • The US and Israel conduct airstrikes and deploy troops targeting Iran's strategic sites to deter aggression and maintain regional stability.
  • Iran retaliates with missile and drone attacks on Israel, Gulf neighbors, and US bases, emphasizing defense and resilience amid pressure.
  • International concerns focus on civilian harm, infrastructure damage, economic risks, and fragile diplomacy in the escalating, complex conflict.

The conflict between the United States, Israel, and Iran has intensified with multiple missile strikes, air raids, and troop deployments, notably around strategic sites such as the Strait of Hormuz and Iran's nuclear facilities. Iran has retaliated with missile and drone attacks targeting Israel and Gulf neighbors, while the US and Israel continue deep strikes inside Iran, raising fears of broader regional war. The war has caused significant civilian casualties and infrastructure damage, including threats to critical energy and water resources, exacerbating global economic and humanitarian concerns. International voices express alarm over the legality and consequences of the conflict, with some noting Iran's resilience and potential upper hand despite heavy pressure. Diplomatic efforts remain fragile amid ongoing military escalations and shifting alliances, underscoring the volatile and complex nature of this multifaceted war.
