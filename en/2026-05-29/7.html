Debate over a $250 Trump banknote

US Currency Denomination Debate 2026-05-29 • 11 articles in total

In brief
  • U.S. officials and the Treasury are reportedly pushing to create a $250 banknote featuring President Trump.
  • The proposal faces legal and constitutional challenges and public controversy for depicting a recent president.
  • There are practical concerns about design and security issues involved in issuing a new high-denomination note.

International reports highlight a growing debate in the United States about creating a $250 banknote that would feature President Donald Trump. Several outlets say U.S. officials and the Treasury are pressing the Bureau of Engraving and Printing to design and potentially issue the note. Other coverage frames the idea as a Trump-driven branding or fundraising effort and as media speculation about political symbolism. Critics and legal analysts warn of constitutional and regulatory obstacles to putting a recent president on currency. Journalists also note practical concerns about introducing a higher-denomination note, including design and security implications.
