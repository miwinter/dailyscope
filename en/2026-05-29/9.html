Europe's Early Heatwave: Records, Risks, and Warnings

Europe Heatwave Response 2026-05-29 • 7 articles in total

In brief
  • A severe early heatwave is breaking temperature records across Western and Southern Europe, exceeding 40°C in Portugal and France.
  • Authorities are issuing health warnings and red alerts, urging precautions for vulnerable people amid rising energy and transport challenges.
  • The heatwave has caused multiple open-water deaths in the UK, highlighting urgent public safety and emergency response needs.

An unusually early and intense heatwave is shattering temperature records across Western and Southern Europe, with readings above 40°C reported in Portugal and France and new highs in the UK. The extreme heat has prompted health warnings, local red alerts and concerns over rising energy demand and transport disruptions. Authorities are urging precautions for vulnerable groups while emergency services respond to heat-related incidents. The crisis has coincided with a spike in open-water fatalities in the UK, underscoring immediate public-safety challenges amid broader climate-related trends.
