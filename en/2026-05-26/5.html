Surge in bilateral deals and strategic ties across regions

Region: Bilateral Deals 2026-05-26 • 78 articles in total

In brief
  • Global states expand bilateral ties via infrastructure, trade, aid, defence, migration, and connectivity to address security, economic, and humanitarian goals.
  • The U.S. promotes Abraham Accords with cautious regional reactions, while China deepens long-term strategic partnerships through development and trade across continents.
  • Small states and regions diversify partners for development, resilience, and governance, supported by donors and diplomatic visits to sustain cooperation and normalize relations.

Recent reporting shows a surge of bilateral activity worldwide as states deepen strategic, economic and humanitarian ties. China and several partners continue to expand infrastructure, trade and aid links, while the Trump-era push to broaden the Abraham Accords remains an active U.S.-centred diplomatic initiative with mixed regional responses. Donors and development funds from Europe, the Gulf and Australia are financing resilience, transport and connectivity projects, and small states are diversifying partners in the Pacific, Caribbean and Africa. Simultaneously, defence cooperation, migration arrangements and transport corridors are being negotiated or operationalized, reflecting an emphasis on practical, bilateral solutions to security, mobility and economic challenges.
