UK sets new May temperature record amid wider European heatwave

UK May Heat Record 2026-05-26 • 8 articles in total

In brief
  • London and parts of Britain recorded record high May temperatures around 33.5–34.8°C linked to a European heat dome.
  • The heat dome causes lasting heat, stressing health services, energy, and outdoor event safety across several countries.
  • Extreme early-season heat has deadly impacts consistent with climate change, while a separate hantavirus case was reported on a cruise ship.

London and other parts of Britain recorded an exceptionally high May temperature this week, with reported highs around 33.5–34.8°C that mark provisional or historic May records. Forecasters link the spike to a broader European heat dome that has produced record May temperatures across several countries and prompted alerts and public-health advice. Authorities and experts warn the heat could persist for days, straining health services and energy systems and increasing risks for outdoor events. Separate reports document deadly consequences in parts of Europe, underlining concerns about the growing frequency and severity of early-season heat tied to climate change. One unrelated health story in the coverage concerns a hantavirus case aboard a cruise ship, focused on infection monitoring rather than the heat event.
