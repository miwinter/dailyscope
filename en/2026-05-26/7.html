Papal encyclical urges ethical guardrails and global AI governance

Catholic AI Ethics 2026-05-26 • 13 articles in total

In brief
  • Pope Leo XIV's encyclical warns of AI risks and calls for international regulation and ethical guidelines to prevent misuse and societal harm.
  • The pope urges disarming AI logics that cause domination, exclusion, and lethal harm, stressing protection of human dignity, workers, and critical thinking.
  • The encyclical prompts cooperation among policymakers, tech leaders, faith communities, and gains AI industry endorsements for responsible development.

Pope Leo XIV's new encyclical issues a broad warning about the risks of artificial intelligence, calling for international regulation and ethical guidelines to prevent misuse, bias, and societal harm. He urges that AI be "disarmed" of logics that produce domination, exclusion, and lethal outcomes, and stresses the need to protect human dignity, workers, and critical thinking. The Vatican presents the document as a major theological intervention meant to steer policymakers, tech leaders, and faith communities toward cooperative governance. Responses range from explanatory pieces on the role of encyclicals to endorsements from some AI industry figures, highlighting cross-sector engagement with the pope's message.
