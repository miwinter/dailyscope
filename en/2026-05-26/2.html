Escalation, diplomacy and defence in the Russia–Ukraine war

Russo-Ukrainian War 2026-05-26 • 97 articles in total

In brief
  • Russia claims its strikes on Kyiv target military sites and urges foreign diplomats to evacuate.
  • Ukraine counters with improved defenses, drone strikes on Russian logistics, and calls for stronger allied support.
  • European allies warn of regional spillover risks from redirected drones and urge collective vigilance.

The coverage shows an escalating campaign of Russian strikes, threats and diplomatic pressure—Moscow repeatedly warns of systematic strikes on Kyiv and urges foreign diplomats and citizens to leave the capital while claiming strikes target military sites. Ukraine highlights new defensive measures and offensive operations, reporting improved ways to intercept drones and missiles and continued strikes on Russian logistics and occupied positions. NATO members and Baltic states express growing alarm about hijacked or redirected drones and broader regional spillover risks, while analysts and third countries condemn Russian tactics and point to strains within the Kremlin. Across reports, the human cost is evident in civilian casualties, damaged cultural sites and displaced families, prompting calls for continued international support and vigilance.

Russian official threats and escalation

Moscow frames recent actions as retaliation and legitimate strikes against military targets, repeatedly warning of systematic attacks on Kyiv and urging foreigners and diplomats to evacuate. Russian officials also publicise legal and policy moves that expand military reach and justify cross‑border operations, seeking to shape diplomatic pressure and pre-empt international responses.

NATO, EU and Baltic security concerns

European allies—especially Baltic states—warn of regional spillover as drones and hijacked systems are redirected toward Estonia, Latvia, Lithuania and neighbouring states, prompting intelligence alerts and heightened readiness. NATO and EU actors flag the actions as destabilising, underline collective defence implications, and call for vigilance and stepped-up support to affected allies.
