Iran orders internet restoration after prolonged blackout

Iran Internet Restoration 2026-05-26 • 9 articles in total

In brief
  • Iran's president ordered the restoration of international internet after about three months of blackout.
  • The internet reopening is seen as a strategic move linked to diplomacy with the United States.
  • Internal disputes emerged as IRGC media questioned the president's authority on restoring internet access.

Iran's president has ordered the restoration of international internet access after a prolonged nationwide blackout lasting roughly three months (about 87 days), according to state media and multiple reports. Official accounts frame the previous shutdown as a security measure and present the reopening as a top-level decision to reestablish connectivity. Some reporting emphasizes the timing of the move alongside diplomatic engagements, notably talks with the United States, suggesting strategic motives behind the reversal. Other coverage highlights domestic strains—IRGC-affiliated outlets publicly questioned the president's authority—revealing internal divisions over information control. Observers warn that despite restored links, Tehran retains mechanisms to restrict or shape online access, so the scope and durability of openness remain uncertain.

This perspective reads the easing of connectivity as linked to external diplomacy, arguing the move was timed to influence or facilitate talks with the United States. It treats the reopening as a strategic calculation rather than a purely domestic policy change.
