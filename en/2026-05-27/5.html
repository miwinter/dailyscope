Escalation of strikes, warnings and international alarm over the Russia–Ukraine war

Russo-Ukrainian War 2026-05-27 • 58 articles in total

In brief
  • Russia intensifies attacks with missiles and drones while warning foreigners to leave Kyiv and limiting negotiation prospects.
  • Ukraine shows resilience with evolving strike capabilities but faces missile-defense shortages and risks regional spillover via Belarus.
  • Western and global actors condemn Russia, warn of hybrid and escalation threats, and emphasize sustained allied support and legal pressure.

Fighting around Ukraine has intensified with repeated Russian missile and drone strikes and Moscow issuing warnings for foreigners and diplomats to leave Kyiv, while Kyiv and its forces report mounting resilience and new strike capabilities. Western governments, the EU and the UN have jointly condemned Moscow’s threats, summoned Russian diplomats and issued security assessments raising concerns about broader regional fallout. Kyiv also warns of contingency targeting in neighbouring Belarus and reports gaps in missile-defence capacity that allies are urged to fill. Parallel trends show growing hybrid and information warfare, attacks on humanitarian infrastructure, and increasing attention to longer-range drone and loitering-munition technologies that complicate defence and escalation dynamics.
