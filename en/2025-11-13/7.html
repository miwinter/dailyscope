US-Venezuela Naval Standoff Escalates with Largest US Carrier Deployment

2025-11-13 • 14 articles in total

The United States has deployed the USS Gerald R. Ford, the world's largest and most advanced aircraft carrier, to the Caribbean near Venezuela, significantly escalating military presence and tensions in the region. The US justifies this deployment as part of efforts to combat drug trafficking and exert pressure on Venezuela's government, which it views as hostile. Venezuela perceives this move as aggressive and imperialistic, with its leaders denouncing it as a declaration of war and mobilizing militarily in response. The deployment has caused fear, uncertainty, and skepticism among Venezuelan citizens, while international observers monitor the situation closely over fears of potential escalation. This naval standoff highlights the deepening geopolitical friction between the two countries amid broader regional security concerns.
