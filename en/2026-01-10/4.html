Tensions Rise Over US Ambitions to Acquire Greenland

Denmark–US Greenland Dispute 2026-01-10 • 56 articles in total

In brief
  • The US under President Trump sought to acquire Greenland for strategic Arctic control, threatening force and offering financial incentives.
  • Greenlandic and Danish leaders rejected US attempts, stressing self-determination and the risks to NATO and regional stability.
  • European and NATO officials condemned the US moves, urging respect for international law and alliance cohesion.

The United States, under President Donald Trump, has expressed strong interest in acquiring Greenland from Denmark, citing strategic and security concerns related to Arctic competition with Russia and China. Trump repeatedly threatened to take control of Greenland by peaceful or forceful means, sparking diplomatic tensions with Denmark, Greenlandic leaders, and European allies. The US also considered financial incentives to Greenland residents to encourage separation from Denmark, which was met with resistance and calls for respect of Greenland's autonomy. European countries and NATO members voiced alarm over the potential impact on alliances and regional stability. Greenlandic political leaders firmly defended their right to self-determination, emphasizing adherence to international law amid growing geopolitical contestation in the Arctic.
