Sovereignty and Criticism of International Justice Systems

This viewpoint emerges primarily from African and some developing countries that challenge or withdraw from international judicial mechanisms such as the ICC, aiming to assert greater national sovereignty over legal matters and expressing dissatisfaction with perceived biases. These countries emphasize their prerogative to handle legal issues internally rather than rely on international institutions, highlighting tensions between domestic governance and supranational justice. This stance also appears in critiques related to transnational law enforcement actions perceived as infringements on sovereignty.
