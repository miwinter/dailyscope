Middle East Conflict and Diplomatic Efforts

France, under President Emmanuel Macron, is deeply concerned about the escalating conflict in the Middle East, particularly the US and Israeli strikes on Iran and Iran's retaliatory actions. Macron has called for urgent UN Security Council meetings and emphasized the need for de-escalation to prevent wider regional instability. France collaborates with European partners and regional leaders, including Saudi Arabia, to promote dialogue and stability, while also increasing vigilance against potential terrorist threats linked to the conflict.

========

Military Deployments and Naval Operations

France has significantly increased its military presence in the Mediterranean, Gulf, and Red Sea regions in response to the Middle East conflict involving Iran. This includes deploying an aircraft carrier, naval vessels, and authorizing US aircraft to use French bases, reflecting a strategic commitment to support allies and secure key maritime routes. The stalling of numerous French ships in the Gulf due to Iranian aggression highlights the operational challenges faced. France also coordinates with European partners to protect Cyprus and deter militant activities linked to Hezbollah.
