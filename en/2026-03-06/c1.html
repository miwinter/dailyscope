France is deeply involved in the escalating Middle East conflict, balancing military deployments, diplomatic efforts, and strategic alliances. It has deployed naval and air assets to the Mediterranean and Gulf regions, authorized US aircraft on its bases, and coordinated with European allies to bolster regional security, especially around Cyprus and Lebanon. President Macron emphasizes France's commitment to national defense, nuclear deterrence, and regional stability while avoiding direct military engagement against Iran. Concurrently, France is managing internal security concerns related to Iranian threats against exiles and pursuing diplomatic initiatives to prevent wider conflict expansion.
