Trump's Assertive Stance on Iran, Cuba, and Zelenski

Trump Geopolitical Pledges 2026-03-06 • 23 articles in total

In brief
  • Trump seeks to remove Iran's leadership without a ground invasion and wants U.S. influence over Iran's next leader.
  • He predicts Cuba's fall from U.S. pressure and sees it as a future focus after Iran and Venezuela.
  • Different sources analyze Trump's interventionist strategies, highlighting their risks and regional impacts.

President Donald Trump has articulated a firm and interventionist foreign policy stance, particularly regarding Iran and Cuba. He dismisses a ground invasion of Iran as ineffective but insists on the complete removal of its leadership, expressing a desire for U.S. involvement in selecting Iran's next leader. Trump predicts the fall of the Cuban regime, emphasizing ongoing U.S. pressure and contacts with Havana, and hints at Cuba as a future focus after Iran and Venezuela. Additionally, he criticizes Ukrainian President Zelenski, reflecting a broader critical view of current geopolitical actors. These positions underscore Trump's continued influence on U.S. foreign policy debates and his readiness to engage in regime change strategies.
