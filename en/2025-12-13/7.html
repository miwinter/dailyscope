Escape of María Corina Machado Highlights Venezuela's Political Crisis

Machado Venezuela Escape 2025-12-13 • 12 articles in total

In brief
  • María Corina Machado escaped Venezuela via a covert, multi-modal operation amid severe political repression under Nicolás Maduro.
  • The escape involved a US-led mission called 'Golden Dynamite' with disputed claims about Curaçao's involvement.
  • The event highlights Venezuela's human rights crisis and ongoing political tensions with Machado planning to return.

María Corina Machado, a prominent Venezuelan opposition leader, executed a perilous escape from Venezuela involving disguise, sea travel through rough waters, and international covert support. Reports indicate the operation, dubbed 'Golden Dynamite,' was led by a US war veteran and involved complex logistics including land, sea, and air routes, with alleged passage through Curaçao, which local authorities have denied involvement in. Machado's flight underscores the severe political repression under Nicolás Maduro's regime and the ongoing risks faced by dissidents. The escape has drawn international attention to Venezuela's human rights challenges and the difficulties in effecting political change. Despite her departure, tensions remain high with concerns about Maduro's hold on power and Machado's planned return.
