International Condemnation of Iran's Repeated Arrest of Nobel Laureate Narges Mohammadi

Iran Detains Narges Mohammadi 2025-12-13 • 31 articles in total

In brief
  • Narges Mohammadi, 2023 Nobel Peace Prize laureate, faces repeated violent arrests by Iran for her human rights activism.
  • Her detentions symbolize Iran's harsh crackdown on dissent, political prisoners, and women's rights advocates, sparking global condemnation.
  • International and regional sources highlight her arrest as a key example of Iran's political repression and the fight for democracy and civil liberties.

Narges Mohammadi, the 2023 Nobel Peace Prize laureate and prominent Iranian human rights activist, has been repeatedly arrested by Iranian authorities, most recently during public commemorations or funerals. Her detentions are widely viewed as part of Iran's ongoing crackdown on dissent, political prisoners, and activists advocating for women's rights and abolition of the death penalty. Reports describe her arrests as often violent, drawing significant international condemnation from governments, human rights organizations, and the Nobel Committee. The global community expresses deep concern over Iran's repression of civil liberties and calls for Mohammadi's immediate release and respect for human rights. These events highlight the broader tensions between the Iranian regime and advocates for democracy and justice within the country.
