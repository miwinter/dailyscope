US Navy Intensifies Anti-Drug Maritime Operations in Pacific and Caribbean

US Navy Anti-Drug Strikes 2025-12-17 • 15 articles in total

In brief
  • The US Navy has conducted aggressive air and naval strikes in the Pacific and Caribbean to disrupt drug trafficking and narcotics supply chains.
  • These operations, targeting vessels labeled as 'narco-terrorists', involve cooperation with Latin American and Caribbean partners but raise legal and transparency concerns.
  • US senators and international observers scrutinize the legality and oversight of these lethal strikes amid an escalated campaign to protect maritime security.

The US Navy has conducted multiple air and naval strikes against suspected drug trafficking vessels in the Pacific Ocean and Caribbean Sea, resulting in the deaths of eight individuals in recent operations. These actions are part of a broader, ongoing campaign to disrupt narcotics smuggling routes and drug trade networks, with US officials labeling the targets as 'narco-terrorists' or criminal enterprises. While the US emphasizes its commitment to combating drug trafficking, some operations have raised questions about transparency, legality, and the handling of such military actions, prompting scrutiny from US senators and international observers. Cooperation with regional partners in Latin America and the Caribbean is highlighted as a key component of these efforts. The strikes reflect an escalated and more aggressive US stance on maritime drug interdiction, aiming to curb the flow of illicit substances into the United States.
