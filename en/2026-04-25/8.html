Iran delegation in Islamabad as Pakistan mediates amid US-linked involvement

Iran–Pakistan Diplomacy 2026-04-25 • 13 articles in total

In brief
  • Iran's delegation led by Abbas Araghchi visits Islamabad for talks on regional security, trade, and ceasefire with Pakistan as host and mediator.
  • Iran refuses direct negotiations with the United States while U.S.-connected figures and White House confirm indirect engagement through Pakistan.
  • Analysts see potential regional impact from the talks but emphasize uncertain outcomes.

Multiple reports describe the arrival or imminent visit of Iran’s delegation to Islamabad, led by senior diplomat Abbas Araghchi, for high-level talks on regional security, trade and a fragile ceasefire. Coverage consistently frames Pakistan as the host and mediator, hosting meetings between Iranian and Pakistani officials including the foreign ministers and military leadership. Several pieces underline Iran’s cautious negotiating posture — including statements that it will not deal directly with the United States — even as U.S.-connected figures and White House confirmations point to indirect U.S. engagement. Analysts and commentators note the talks could influence wider regional alignments but stress outcomes remain uncertain.
