US defense debate: readiness, leadership and competing narratives

US Defense Policy Debate 2026-04-25 • 13 articles in total

In brief
  • U.S. defense faces material readiness challenges and rising costs threatening deterrence and alliances.
  • Military leadership turmoil raises concerns about command stability and civilian control.
  • Public debate is polarized over U.S. military role, draft policies, protests, and cultural views on power and strength.

Coverage of U.S. defense policy centers on three interlinked concerns: material readiness and rising costs, turmoil in military leadership and command responsiveness, and a polarized public narrative about the purpose of American force. Several reports warn of dwindling missile stocks, costly procurements and lagging naval modernization that could undermine deterrence and allied confidence. At the same time, sudden resignations, purges, and officials publicly awaiting presidential orders highlight questions about command stability and civilian control of the military. Domestic politics are visible in debates over draft registration, veterans’ protests against escalation, and competing narratives that either praise the military for producing peace through strength or criticize a growing cult of militarized performance. These threads together portray a defense debate driven by readiness gaps, leadership flux, and contested ideas about the role of U.S. power abroad.
