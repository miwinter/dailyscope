Diverse Global Reactions to Trump’s Gaza Peace Plan Amid Fragile Prospects

2025-10-01 • 128 articles in total

Former US President Donald Trump proposed a detailed 20-point peace plan for Gaza, aiming to end hostilities, establish ceasefire, release hostages, and create new governance structures excluding Hamas. While Israel, led by Prime Minister Netanyahu, has largely accepted the proposal (often conditionally), Hamas remains hesitant or unwilling, engaging in internal consultations influenced by regional mediators like Qatar, Egypt, and Turkey. European countries and certain Muslim states cautiously support the plan, viewing it as a potential breakthrough, whereas many Arab countries and Palestinians strongly reject it, perceiving the plan as biased and dismissive of Palestinian rights. International organizations such as the United Nations have expressed support and urged implementation, yet skepticism and tensions prevail worldwide over the plan’s fairness, feasibility, and the prospects for genuine peace.

Israeli and Pro-Israel Endorsement with Conditional Support

Israel, led by Prime Minister Benjamin Netanyahu, has officially accepted and endorsed Trump’s Gaza peace plan, viewing it as a strategic and diplomatic pathway to ceasefire and hostilities’ end. However, some Israeli officials, including right-wing factions, criticize it for concessions and question aspects such as immediate withdrawal or leniency towards Hamas. Israel emphasizes the necessity of Hamas's compliance and threatens continuation of military actions if Hamas rejects the plan. The Israeli stance often highlights security, the release of hostages, and firm opposition to Hamas’s role in Gaza governance, aligning closely with U.S. diplomacy.

Arab and Muslim Nations’ Mixed Stance: Cautious Support Coupled with Negotiation and Pressure on Hamas

Several Arab and Muslim countries, including Qatar, Egypt, Turkey, Jordan, and others, present a nuanced viewpoint showing conditional or cautious support for Trump’s peace plan. They often urge Hamas to consider or accept the proposal, mediating ongoing discussions and emphasizing peace while acknowledging the complexity and need for clarifications. Some nations simultaneously criticize parts of the plan or request negotiations regarding specific terms, especially regarding troop withdrawals and sovereignty issues. This group reflects a pragmatic approach balancing regional stability, diplomatic leverage, and support for Palestinian rights.

Palestinian and Arab Populations’ Rejection and Distrust of the Peace Plan

A significant section of the Palestinian populace, political factions, and allied Arab voices strongly oppose Trump’s Gaza peace plan. They view it as unjust, disregarding Palestinian rights, sovereignty, and humanitarian concerns, and accuse it of being biased towards Israel. Hamas remains doubtful or outright rejects the plan, albeit engaged in consultations under regional pressures. The broader Palestinian sentiment is one of mistrust toward the plan’s guarantees and skepticism over Israel’s compliance.

European Support and Optimism Towards the Peace Initiative

European leaders and institutions generally express cautious optimism and support for Trump’s Gaza peace proposal. They see the plan as a constructive diplomatic effort to achieve ceasefire and improve humanitarian conditions. European countries emphasize multilateral cooperation and highlight potential contributions to stabilize the region, endorsing international oversight mechanisms as part of the peace framework. However, some reservations about the plan’s long-term viability persist.

United Nations and International Organizations’ Endorsement for Diplomatic Implementation

The United Nations, particularly Secretary-General António Guterres, has publicly supported Trump’s peace plan, urging all parties to accept and implement it swiftly to prevent further hostilities and humanitarian crises. The UN’s role is framed as facilitative and supportive of diplomatic solutions, emphasizing the urgency and potential to stabilize Gaza. While backing the plan diplomatically, the UN also calls for cooperation from all involved stakeholders without explicitly siding against any party.

Skeptical and Critical Perspectives Emphasizing Political and Practical Challenges

Certain countries and commentators are skeptical about the plan’s feasibility, fairness, and actual chances of success. They point to unresolved political issues, the exclusion or sidelining of Hamas in negotiations, and the potential for escalation if deadlines are not met. Critics highlight the plan’s controversial elements, the ambiguity of international involvement, and possible contradictions in on-the-ground enforcement. This viewpoint often stresses the complexity of regional politics and the gap between diplomatic intent and practical outcomes.

Nations Prepared to Provide Peacekeeping or International Force Under the Plan

Some countries, including Pakistan and Indonesia, respectively, have expressed willingness to contribute troops or support an international stabilization force in Gaza as envisaged in Trump’s plan. These countries see participation as aligning with broader objectives of regional stability and improved international relations. Their involvement reflects support for the plan’s governance and security provisions aimed at post-conflict administration.
