Bilateral Deals: Trade Uncertainty, Security Pacts and Sectoral Partnerships

Region: Bilateral Deals 2026-07-02 • 113 articles in total

In brief
  • North American trade uncertainty grows as the US delays renewing the trade pact, causing concern in Mexico and Canada.
  • Major powers deepen security and military cooperation through defense pacts, crisis communication, and secret training amid geopolitical competition.
  • Countries advance pragmatic bilateral deals and symbolic diplomacy to boost regional connectivity, manage migration, and assert influence amid strategic contestation.

This collection of articles shows bilateral diplomacy focused on three broad currents: trade uncertainty in North America, expanding security and military cooperation among major powers and regional partners, and a steady stream of sectoral agreements on infrastructure, health, education and migration across regions. Washington’s refusal to extend the North American trade pact in its current form has reverberated through Mexico and Canada and raised industry concerns, while separate defense and crisis‑communication pacts (and reports of secret training) signal deepening security ties among states from the Gulf to Eurasia. At the same time, many countries pursued pragmatic bilateral deals—academic exchanges, regulatory cooperation, visa allocations, transport links and development financing—aimed at boosting connectivity and capacity. Regional ruptures and diplomatic gestures (e.g., Burkina Faso–France, island migration arrangements, and ceremonial exchanges) remind that bilateral diplomacy mixes cooperation, contestation and symbolism across differing national priorities.
