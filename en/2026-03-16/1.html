Escalation and Multipolar Perspectives in the Iran War

Iran War 2026-03-16 • 229 articles in total

In brief
  • Iran and its allies have launched missile and drone attacks on US, Israeli, and Gulf targets, signaling readiness for prolonged conflict and rejecting ceasefire talks.
  • The US and Israel have intensified airstrikes on Iranian military and oil infrastructure, promising continued operations and urging regional evacuations.
  • The conflict has caused civilian casualties, disrupted global oil markets, heightened regional security concerns, and drawn varied international calls for diplomacy and caution.

The Iran war has intensified with significant missile and drone strikes by Iran targeting US, Israeli, and Gulf states' military and infrastructure, including the first use of the 'Sajil' ballistic missile. The US and Israel have responded with airstrikes on Iranian military and oil facilities, notably Kharg Island, escalating regional tensions and causing global economic concerns, especially in oil markets. Iran warns of broader war escalation if other countries intervene, while the US and Israel plan continued military operations, with Israel projecting weeks more of conflict. International reactions vary: some countries emphasize diplomatic efforts and caution against escalation, while others highlight the humanitarian and economic toll. The conflict's complexity is deepened by Iran's alliances with Russia and China, proxy involvements, and the geopolitical stakes in the Gulf and beyond.

Iranian Perspective: Retaliation and Defiance

Iran emphasizes its right to retaliate against US and Israeli attacks, showcasing new missile capabilities like the 'Sajil' missile and threatening further strikes on regional targets including Gulf states and Israel. Iranian officials reject ceasefire talks, warn against foreign intervention, and assert readiness for prolonged conflict, framing their actions as self-defense against aggression and imperialism. Iran also highlights its military cooperation with Russia and China, reinforcing its strategic resilience.

The conflict also impacts foreign nationals and workers in the region, highlighting the broader human cost beyond military engagements.
