International Consensus on Putin's Responsibility for 2018 Novichok Poisoning

Putin Chemical Weapons Accountability 2025-12-05 • 10 articles in total

In brief
  • Multiple investigations conclude Putin authorized the 2018 Novichok poisoning of Sergei Skripal and his daughter.
  • The incident caused major diplomatic strains and led to UK sanctions against Russian entities and individuals.
  • Affected countries unanimously hold Putin and Russia morally and legally responsible and call for accountability.

Multiple international investigations and inquiries have concluded that Russian President Vladimir Putin authorized the 2018 Novichok nerve agent poisoning of former spy Sergei Skripal and his daughter in Salisbury, UK. These findings have led to widespread condemnation and have significantly strained diplomatic relations between Russia and Western countries, particularly the United Kingdom. In response, the UK has imposed sanctions targeting Russian individuals and entities, including the GRU intelligence agency, to hold Russia accountable and deter future chemical attacks. The incident remains a focal point of geopolitical tension, highlighting concerns over the use of chemical weapons and state-sponsored covert operations. Various countries emphasize the moral and legal responsibility of the Russian leadership in this act.

Countries covering this topic

Western and Allied Nations Holding Putin Accountable

Countries such as the UK, USA, Australia, Ireland, Portugal, Argentina, and Vietnam present a unified stance that Vladimir Putin personally authorized the Novichok poisoning attack. They emphasize the moral and legal responsibility of the Russian president and advocate for sanctions and diplomatic measures to hold Russia accountable. This viewpoint underscores the use of chemical weapons as a reckless and hostile act that threatens international security and norms.
