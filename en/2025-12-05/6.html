US-Facilitated Peace Agreement Between DRC and Rwanda Amid Ongoing Conflict

US Brokers DRC–Rwanda Peace 2025-12-05 • 35 articles in total

In brief
  • A peace agreement between the Democratic Republic of Congo and Rwanda was signed in Washington under U.S. President Donald Trump.
  • The deal aims to promote regional stability and economic cooperation but violence continues in eastern DRC.
  • Regional and international actors support the peace process amid challenges to lasting peace and security.

A peace agreement between the Democratic Republic of Congo (DRC) and Rwanda was signed in Washington under the auspices of U.S. President Donald Trump, marking a significant diplomatic effort to resolve longstanding tensions and promote regional stability. While the accord is hailed as historic and a potential turning point for peace and economic cooperation, fighting and violence continue in eastern DRC, casting doubt on the immediate effectiveness of the deal. The agreement also reflects broader geopolitical interests, including U.S. access to critical minerals and countering other global influences in the region. Regional actors such as the African Union and countries like Togo and Qatar have played supportive roles in facilitating the peace process. Despite cautious optimism from leaders, the fragile security situation underscores the challenges ahead for lasting peace.
