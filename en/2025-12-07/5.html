Divergent Reactions to US National Security Strategy: Alliance, Criticism, and Geopolitical Shifts

US National Security Strategy Release 2025-12-07 • 21 articles in total

In brief
  • European leaders affirm the US as a key ally despite the National Security Strategy's critical and nationalist tone.
  • Experts warn the strategy threatens transatlantic security and NATO cohesion by promoting skepticism and nationalist policies.
  • The strategy removes Russia as a direct threat and shifts US focus to Latin America while asserting global supremacy.

The newly released US National Security Strategy under President Trump has elicited a wide range of international responses. European leaders like Kaja Kallas reaffirm the US as a key ally despite the strategy's critical tone towards Europe and its nationalist undertones. Conversely, several European and regional experts express concern over the strategy's implications for transatlantic security and NATO cohesion, warning of increased risks. Russia welcomes the removal of its 'direct threat' label, signaling a potential thaw in US-Russia tensions. Meanwhile, the strategy's pivot towards Latin America and its emphasis on US supremacy reflect a broader shift in American foreign policy priorities, drawing mixed reactions globally.

European Leaders Affirm US as Key Ally Despite Criticism

European officials, particularly from the EU and Estonia, emphasize the enduring strength of the transatlantic alliance despite the US strategy's nationalist tone and critical remarks about Europe. They highlight the importance of continued cooperation to address shared security challenges and reassure that the US remains Europe's strongest ally. This perspective seeks to maintain unity and stability amid geopolitical uncertainties.

European and Regional Concerns Over US Strategy's Impact on Security

Several European commentators and experts express alarm at the strategy's potential to destabilize transatlantic relations and European security. They point to the strategy's nationalist approach, skepticism about Europe's capabilities, and warnings of 'civilizational extinction' as factors that threaten NATO cohesion and regional safety. This viewpoint underscores fears of a historic shift that could undermine collective defense and increase geopolitical risks.

Russia Welcomes Removal from 'Direct Threat' List, Signaling Diplomatic Shift

The Kremlin positively received the US decision to exclude Russia from the list of direct threats, interpreting it as a step toward easing tensions and opening dialogue. Russian officials view this as a diplomatic signal that may recalibrate bilateral relations despite ongoing challenges. This perspective highlights a cautious optimism about improved US-Russia interactions under the new strategy.

US Strategy Signals Shift Toward Interventionist Isolationism and Latin America Focus

The US strategy reflects a reorientation of American foreign policy, characterized by a blend of interventionist isolationism and a pivot away from Europe and the Middle East toward Latin America. This approach aims to address regional issues like immigration and drug trafficking while asserting US supremacy globally. The strategy's mixed reception internationally underscores debates about America's evolving global role and priorities.
