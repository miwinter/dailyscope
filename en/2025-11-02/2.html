Global Impact and Response to Major Natural Disasters

2025-11-02 • 72 articles in total

Recent natural disasters, including Hurricane Melissa in the Caribbean, severe floods in Vietnam and Pakistan, landslides in Kenya, and earthquakes in the Indian Ocean and Turkey, have caused extensive casualties and infrastructural damage across multiple regions. Hurricane Melissa severely affected Caribbean countries such as Jamaica, Haiti, Cuba, and the Dominican Republic, resulting in nearly 50 deaths and widespread destruction, prompting significant international and regional aid efforts. Flooding in Vietnam and Pakistan caused numerous fatalities and widespread humanitarian challenges, while Kenya faces critical conditions following deadly landslides. Additionally, avalanches in the Alps have resulted in multiple deaths among German mountaineers, highlighting risks in mountainous areas. Recovery, emergency response, and calls for enhanced disaster preparedness and climate finance are emphasized across these affected regions.
