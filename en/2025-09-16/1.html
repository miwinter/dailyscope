Middle East Tensions and Regional Realignments Following Israeli Strikes in Qatar

2025-09-16 • 120 articles in total

The recent Israeli airstrike on Qatar, targeting Hamas leaders, has sharply escalated tensions across the Middle East and prompted significant regional and international reactions. Numerous Arab and Islamic countries convened emergency summits, condemning Israel's actions, calling for united diplomatic and military responses, and discussing the formation of an Arab or Muslim military alliance similar to NATO. Meanwhile, Iran and some allied states have called for severing ties with Israel and increasing military confrontations, whereas other countries, including several Gulf states and global actors, advocate for political and diplomatic measures alongside regional security solidarity. The situation has further intensified existing divides, with some international players pushing for a two-state solution and others reinforcing opposition to Israeli policies, reflecting a dynamic and polarized geopolitical landscape in the Middle East.

Arab and Islamic Solidarity Against Israeli Aggression

Countries in the Arab League and the Organization of Islamic Cooperation (OIC), as well as other Islamic states, strongly condemn Israel’s strike on Qatar as a breach of sovereignty and international law. These nations emphasize unity and collective security responses, pressing for sanctions, diplomatic isolation of Israel, and the creation of an Arab military alliance akin to NATO to deter future aggression. They express solidarity with Qatar and Palestine, often characterizing Israeli actions as threats to regional peace and stability, and call for unified political and diplomatic efforts to counter Israel and uphold Arab and Muslim rights.

Iran’s Hardline Anti-Israel Position and Calls for Islamic Military Alliance

Iran adopts a confrontational stance against Israel, urging Muslim countries to sever all ties and actively opposing normalization with Israel. Tehran supports retaliatory military actions against Israeli targets and proposes forming an Islamic military alliance to counter Israel’s influence. Nevertheless, some regional actors, like Qatar, reportedly hesitate to fully embrace Iran’s military coalition, revealing divisions within the Muslim world regarding approach and strategy toward Israel.

Gulf States’ Strategic Solidarity with Qatar and Regional Security Coordination

The Gulf Cooperation Council (GCC) countries, including Qatar, Oman, Bahrain, and others, prioritize regional security cooperation in response to Israeli aggression. They stress collective defense mechanisms, joint military preparedness, and enhancement of unified commands to safeguard Gulf security. These states advocate for solid diplomatic responses, often calling on external powers like the United States to restrain Israel, while balancing between direct confrontation and political-diplomatic solutions to preserve stability in the Gulf region.

They seek diplomatic resolutions and express cautious optimism amid tense regional dynamics, despite awareness of ongoing violence and shifting alliances. These countries focus on legal frameworks and international legitimacy rather than militarized responses.

United States’ Diplomatic Ambivalence and Support for Israel

US political voices, including former President Donald Trump and Senator Marco Rubio, show a tendency to support Israel’s security position while occasionally expressing restrictions on Israeli strikes beyond immediate conflict zones, as seen in declarations ruling out further attacks on Qatar. The US seeks to manage tensions without fully alienating Gulf allies or exacerbating conflict, but remains entangled in regional rivalries. This stance includes a mixture of political backing for Israel and limited interventions aimed at reducing escalation, reflecting complex US regional policy considerations.

Israel’s Defensive Posture Amid Increasing Isolation and Military Escalations

Israel continues military operations against perceived threats such as Hezbollah and Hamas, exemplified by strikes in Lebanon and Qatar, signalling a policy of pre-emptive and retaliatory action. Its leadership acknowledges economic and diplomatic isolation pressures but stresses the need for self-reliance. Israeli authorities reject proposals for expanded military coalitions against them and defend ongoing settlement expansions and military presence in Palestinian territories, while facing growing international criticism and setbacks.
