Death of Libyan Army Chief Mohamed Al-Haddad in Plane Crash in Turkey

Libyan Army Chief Dies 2025-12-24 • 38 articles in total

In brief
  • Libya's Chief of Staff and other officials died in a plane crash after takeoff from Ankara following an electrical malfunction and emergency landing request.
  • The crash is a major blow to Libya's military leadership amid political instability and ongoing investigations in Turkey.
  • The incident drew international attention due to its impact on Libya's military operations and regional relations, especially with Turkey.

General Mohamed Al-Haddad, Libya's Chief of Staff, died in a plane crash shortly after takeoff from Ankara, Turkey, along with several other high-ranking military officials. The aircraft reportedly experienced an electrical malfunction and requested an emergency landing before losing contact and crashing. The incident has been confirmed by Libyan authorities and Turkish officials, with investigations ongoing to determine the exact cause. This sudden loss represents a significant blow to Libya's military leadership amid ongoing political instability and complex security challenges. The event has drawn international attention due to its potential impact on Libya's military operations and diplomatic relations, especially with Turkey.
