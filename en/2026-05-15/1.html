Trump–Xi Beijing Summit: Trade Gains, Taiwan Warnings, and Strategic Caution

US-China Diplomacy 2026-05-15 • 177 articles in total

In brief
  • Trump and Xi held talks focusing on trade progress and strategic issues with cautious optimism but limited concrete agreements.
  • Xi stressed Taiwan as the central sensitive issue and urged US to treat China as a partner to avoid conflict.
  • Both leaders signaled limited cooperation on regional security, while analysts noted ceremonial diplomacy amid risks of US-China rivalry escalation.

Donald Trump’s visit to Beijing produced a mix of public cordiality and substantive bargaining, with both leaders touting trade progress and prospective purchases even as details and confirmations remained thin. Xi Jinping repeatedly framed Taiwan as the pivotal and most sensitive issue, warning mishandling could lead to confrontation, while urging the United States to treat China as a partner rather than a rival. The two sides also signalled limited alignment on regional security matters—notably Iran and keeping the Strait of Hormuz open—though analysts judged summit language cautious and inconclusive. Coverage highlighted the ceremonial optics and business-heavy entourage accompanying Trump, as commentators flagged broader strategic risks, invoking the Thucydides Trap and urging careful management of rivalry to avoid miscalculation.
