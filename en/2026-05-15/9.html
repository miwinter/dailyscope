Latvia PM resigns amid drone incidents and coalition collapse

Latvia Prime Minister Resignation 2026-05-15 • 11 articles in total

In brief
  • Latvia's prime minister resigned amid a political crisis triggered by drone incidents and coalition collapse.
  • The resignation reflects disputes over drone origins, accountability demands, and heightened Baltic security concerns.
  • This event caused arrests, government reshuffles, and uncertainty about Latvia's future policies and alliances.

Latvia's prime minister Evika Siliņa has resigned amid a political crisis driven by a series of drone incidents and a consequent coalition collapse. Reporting diverges on the proximate cause: some sources stress controversy over stray Ukrainian drones and demands for accountability, while others frame the events as incursions linked to Russia that amplified Baltic security concerns. The resignation has triggered arrests, cabinet shake-ups, and a scramble over potential successors as parties reassess domestic and defense policies. Observers warn the leadership change could realign Latvia's stance within the EU and NATO and heighten short- and medium-term political instability while coalition partners negotiate a path forward.
