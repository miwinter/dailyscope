BRICS ministers meet amid Iran-driven agenda and visible rifts

BRICS Diplomacy 2026-05-15 • 11 articles in total

In brief
  • BRICS foreign ministers met to discuss global issues amid visible divisions over geopolitical alignments and unified action.
  • Iran pushed BRICS to condemn the US and Israel regarding Middle East conflicts and oil security concerns.
  • The group proposed a new technology-financing plan to boost economic cooperation despite internal disagreements.

BRICS foreign ministers met in New Delhi to discuss regional and global issues, with the summit presented as a platform for deeper cooperation and influence in global governance. The agenda was heavily shaped by Middle East developments, particularly Iran's war and oil concerns, as Tehran urged the bloc to condemn the United States and Israel and to resist perceived US pressure. Several reports and analysts highlighted visible divisions among members over geopolitical alignments and common positions, raising doubts about BRICS' ability to act in concert on trade, security and diplomacy. At the same time, proposals for a new technology-financing architecture and appeals for a more equitable international order signaled competing economic and developmental priorities within the group.
