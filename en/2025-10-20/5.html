Pakistan and Afghanistan agree on immediate ceasefire amid ongoing tensions

2025-10-20 • 35 articles in total

Following a week of intense clashes along their shared border, Pakistan and Afghanistan, with mediation from Qatar and Turkey, have agreed to implement an immediate ceasefire. The agreement aims to halt violence, reduce cross-border hostilities, and create a foundation for sustained peace and security in the region. Both countries have expressed commitment to maintain the ceasefire and continue diplomatic dialogues to address broader security concerns and border management. Despite hopes for stability, the ceasefire is fragile, with past breakdowns underscoring challenges ahead. International observers welcome this development as a hopeful step towards improved bilateral relations and regional stability.
