Policy shifts, sector impacts and human cost in US immigration

US Immigration Policy 2026-05-12 • 8 articles in total

In brief
  • U.S. immigration policy has toughened enforcement and restricted visas, causing a drop in international student enrollment and financial strain on universities.
  • USCIS resumed visa processing for certain skilled workers amid new compliance rules for F-1/M-1 students.
  • Enforcement practices have led to migrant deaths, family separations, and allegations of poor treatment in ICE detention, raising human-rights concerns.

Recent coverage of U.S. immigration policy highlights a mix of tougher enforcement proposals, administrative visa changes, and persistent humanitarian crises at the southern border. Reports describe hardline measures and visa clampdowns that have contributed to a sharp drop in international student enrollment, even as USCIS resumes processing for certain skilled professionals like foreign doctors. International students face technical compliance challenges to maintain F-1/M-1 status amid shifting rules and deadlines. Concurrently, multiple articles document migrant deaths, family separations after deportation, and allegations of poor treatment in ICE custody, underscoring the human-rights implications of enforcement practices.

Enforcement proposals and institutional fallout

Sources highlight a tougher U.S. enforcement stance under the current administration and its ripple effects: proposals to deport migrants without specific documentation and policy moves described as a visa clampdown. Coverage emphasizes both the political intent behind stricter measures and concrete institutional consequences, such as a significant drop in international student enrollment and financial strain on universities.
