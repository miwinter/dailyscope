Ceasefire on Life Support as US Rejects Iran’s Reply

US–Iran Diplomacy 2026-05-12 • 111 articles in total

In brief
  • The U.S. rejects Iran's ceasefire reply as unacceptable and keeps military options open amid sanctions and economic pressure.
  • Iran claims its response aims to end regional war, lift sanctions, and secure maritime guarantees.
  • Diplomatic efforts continue with mediators despite a fragile truce and ongoing security and economic tensions.

Reporting across sources shows a fragile ceasefire between the United States and Iran after Washington rejected Tehran’s written response to a U.S. proposal. President Trump and U.S. officials repeatedly described Iran’s reply as unacceptable or “garbage,” warning the truce may collapse and keeping military options on the table. Tehran and its officials maintain the response was reasonable and pressed for an end to the wider regional war, sanctions relief and maritime/security guarantees. Meanwhile the U.S. is tightening economic pressure with new sanctions and banks urged to flag suspect finance, while mediators such as Pakistan seek to keep diplomacy alive amid market jitters over oil supply.
