Suspended Trial and Renewed Arrest Warrant Deepen Bolivia Political Crisis

Bolivia Morales Trial 2026-05-12 • 9 articles in total

In brief
  • Bolivia suspended the criminal case against Evo Morales after he missed court, leading to renewed arrest warrants and him being declared in rebellion.
  • Morales's supporters threaten mass protests if he is detained, raising fears of political unrest and polarization before elections.
  • Morales and his legal team refuse to attend court, demanding an impartial process and claiming political persecution.

Bolivia's criminal case against former president Evo Morales has been suspended after he failed to appear in court, prompting judges to renew an arrest warrant and formally declare him 'in rebellion'. Prosecutors and courts have escalated legal measures—including reissued warrants and trial postponements—raising stakes in an already polarized political environment ahead of upcoming elections. Morales's supporters have threatened mass protests if he is detained, fueling fears of unrest and further political polarization. Morales and his legal team insist he will not attend and demand an impartial process, framing the proceedings as politically motivated.
