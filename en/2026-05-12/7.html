Trump attack probe: pleas, footage and public doubt

Trump Attack Investigation 2026-05-12 • 10 articles in total

In brief

  • Newly released security footage shows the suspect firing at a U.S. Secret Service agent, impacting the investigation.
  • About 25% of Americans believe the attack was staged, reflecting political polarization and distrust in media narratives.

Reports from multiple outlets show the accused in the alleged attempt on President Donald Trump has repeatedly pleaded not guilty in initial federal court appearances. Coverage emphasizes procedural developments—charges filed, court appearances, and questions about the number and nature of counts—as the legal process moves forward. Separately, newly released security footage reportedly captures Cole Allen firing at a U.S. Secret Service agent, raising fresh scrutiny of protective measures and investigative evidence. Public reaction is sharply divided: a poll reports about one in four Americans believes the incident was staged, highlighting deep political polarization and distrust in narratives around the attack. Investigations and court proceedings remain active and are likely to produce further evidence and public debate.
