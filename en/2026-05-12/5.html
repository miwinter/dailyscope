Hantavirus outbreak on MV Hondius triggers international evacuations

WHO Hantavirus Cruise Outbreak 2026-05-12 • 51 articles in total

In brief
  • A hantavirus outbreak linked to the MV Hondius cruise caused multiple infections and deaths, prompting large-scale evacuations and quarantines.
  • Authorities and international agencies coordinated complex evacuations, medical care, contact tracing, and ongoing epidemiological investigations.
  • Media coverage varies by focus: logistics and port operations, medical impacts, public health measures, outbreak origins, and cruise industry repercussions.

A hantavirus outbreak linked to the expedition cruise MV Hondius has caused multiple confirmed infections and several deaths, prompting large-scale evacuations and medical repatriations from the Canary Islands. Authorities coordinated complex air and maritime operations to move passengers to the Netherlands and other home countries while screening, isolating and quarantining contacts. National public health agencies and the WHO have been monitoring the situation, advising vigilance but continuing to stress that risk to the general public remains low. Investigations into the outbreak’s origin—including reporting that a Dutch ornithologist may be the initial case—are ongoing as epidemiological tracing and decontamination efforts continue.
