Regional Diplomacy in Motion: Defense, Investment and Connectivity Deals

Region: Bilateral Deals 2026-05-12 • 71 articles in total

In brief
  • Global bilateral diplomacy increases with focus on defense, investment, infrastructure, and diversified partnerships beyond traditional trade.
  • Europe integrates migration control and political dialogue while deepening defense support to Ukraine amid broader regional cooperation.
  • China, Russia, Africa, and regional states expand economic, security, and development ties through investment, sectoral projects, and multilateral frameworks.

Recent reporting shows an uptick in bilateral deals and high-level diplomacy across regions, driven by defense cooperation, large-scale investment packages, and infrastructure or connectivity projects. European actors continue to pair security and migration-management talks with financing for defense and regional outreach, while China’s state and commercial partners secured multi-billion-dollar accords in Central Asia and new Pacific engagements. African states pursued energy, transport and trade agreements alongside renewed ties with European partners, and Pacific and Caribbean nations forged development, health and aviation links with external partners. Technology, fintech and maritime security partnerships also featured prominently, reflecting diversified bilateral agendas beyond traditional trade and aid.
