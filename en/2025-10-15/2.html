Military Coup and Political Upheaval in Madagascar Following Presidential Impeachment and Protests

2025-10-15 • 76 articles in total

Madagascar is currently experiencing a significant political crisis marked by a military coup after the impeachment of President Andry Rajoelina, who fled the country amid escalating protests and political unrest. The military, including elite units like CAPSAT, has taken control, suspending key democratic institutions and promising elections within two years, though the situation remains highly uncertain. Widespread public demonstrations, led notably by Generation Z activists protesting economic hardships such as power and water shortages, have fueled the instability. International reactions vary, with some reports highlighting concerns about democratic governance, while others note foreign involvement including the evacuation of the president by French military forces. The formation of military councils and a shift away from constitutional norms represent a major upheaval with significant implications for Madagascar's future governance and regional stability.
