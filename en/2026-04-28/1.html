Shooting at White House Correspondents' Dinner: evacuation, charges and security scrutiny

US Presidential Security Incident 2026-04-28 • 92 articles in total

In brief
  • A gunman shot near the White House Correspondents’ Dinner, causing evacuation and arrest with federal charges filed.
  • The incident raised security concerns, political reactions, conspiracy theories, and international condemnation.
  • Media covered suspect details, court proceedings, motives, protective failures, and political framing by Trump and others.

A gunman opened fire near the White House Correspondents’ Dinner attended by President Donald Trump, prompting an immediate evacuation and the suspect’s arrest. Federal prosecutors have charged Cole Tomas Allen and numerous outlets report court appearances and potential life sentences as the criminal case advances. The episode triggered wide reviews of security protocols, debate over possible lapses by protective services, and investigative coverage of the attacker’s path, manifesto and motives. Political reactions ranged from Trump defending the Secret Service and using the incident to press policy aims, to the White House and others blaming political rhetoric; the event also gave rise to conspiracy theories and broad international condemnation.
