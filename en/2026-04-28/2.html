Stalemate in US–Iran diplomacy as Washington rejects Tehran’s offers

US–Iran Diplomacy 2026-04-28 • 77 articles in total

In brief
  • U.S. and Iran diplomacy is intense but stalled, centered on nuclear issues and trust deficits despite mediation efforts.
  • Iran offers conditional confidence-building steps while blaming U.S. pressure for talks breakdown.
  • European leaders view the talks as a U.S. setback and the Strait of Hormuz remains a key geopolitical and market concern.

Recent reporting shows intense but stalled diplomacy between the United States and Iran: President Trump and his advisers have met repeatedly and expressed clear displeasure with Tehran’s latest proposals, insisting nuclear issues remain central. Iran has both blamed the United States for breakdowns and publicly set conditions for any restart of talks while offering confidence-building steps such as reopening the Strait of Hormuz under conditions. Pakistan and other mediators are actively trying to salvage face-to-face negotiations, but trust deficits and cancelled delegations have undercut momentum. European leaders and some allies have framed the talks as a diplomatic setback for Washington, and market signals — especially around Hormuz control — reflect the elevated geopolitical risk.

US: sceptical, hardline stance

U.S. leaders, notably President Trump and senior advisers, are skeptical of Iran’s recent proposals and insist that nuclear constraints be addressed first. Washington is maintaining pressure — including naval measures and threats of sanctions — and US officials (including Rubio) defend firm leverage while drawing clear red lines.
