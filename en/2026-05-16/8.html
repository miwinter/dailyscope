Cuba's deepening electricity crisis

Cuba Electricity Crisis 2026-05-16 • 6 articles in total

In brief
  • Cuba faces a worsening electricity crisis with long outages, infrastructure failures, and a recent major blackout partly resolved.
  • The crisis is driven by severe fuel shortages, especially dwindling Russian oil aid, disrupting transport, industry, and daily life.
  • Authorities work on restoring power, assessing damage, seeking alternatives, and managing protests linked to the power disruptions.

Cuba is experiencing a deepening electricity crisis marked by prolonged daily outages, large-scale blackouts and infrastructure failures. Residents in Havana reported outages lasting up to 22 hours while a recent nationwide blackout has been at least temporarily resolved, underscoring chronic reliability problems. The situation is compounded by dwindling donated Russian fuel and severe nationwide shortages that have left transport and industry constrained. Authorities are assessing grid damage and seeking alternative supplies and reforms even as protests and political tensions are linked to partial collapses and service disruptions.
