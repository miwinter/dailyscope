New Ebola outbreak in DRC prompts regional mobilization

DRC Ebola Response 2026-05-16 • 20 articles in total

In brief
  • A new Ebola outbreak in northeastern DRC's Ituri province has caused around 65 reported deaths with several hundred cases.
  • Cross-border transmission to Uganda has occurred, prompting increased surveillance and regional cooperation.
  • Response faces logistical challenges in remote areas, with some sources reporting inconsistent death tolls.

A new Ebola outbreak has been confirmed in northeastern Democratic Republic of the Congo, centered in Ituri province, with official reports largely citing about 65 deaths and several hundred suspected or confirmed cases. National authorities, Africa CDC and other international partners are mobilizing rapid response teams, surveillance and vaccination campaigns to contain transmission. Some reports emphasize the operational challenge of responding in remote health zones such as Mongwalu and Rwampara, where access and resources are limited. Several neighbouring countries, notably Uganda, have reported linked cases or a cross-border death, prompting intensified border surveillance and contact tracing. Reporting discrepancies exist, including at least one source claiming a much higher death toll, highlighting uncertainty in early outbreak figures.
