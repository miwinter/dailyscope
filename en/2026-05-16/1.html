Trump–Xi summit: Deals, stability pledges and Taiwan tensions

US-China Diplomacy 2026-05-16 • 135 articles in total

In brief
  • The summit marked a diplomatic reset with U.S. and China pledging dialogue on trade, strategic stability, and global issues.
  • Taiwan was the most sensitive topic, with warnings against independence and risks of conflict from both sides.
  • Observers saw the visit as largely theatrical, with few concrete policy breakthroughs despite symbolic gestures and grand rhetoric.

Donald Trump’s visit to Beijing signaled a renewed phase of high‑level U.S.–China diplomacy emphasizing dialogue on trade, strategic stability and wider global issues. Both leaders presented a cooperative narrative — proposing a joint trade council, pledging to implement existing agreements and outlining a framework for managed strategic stability and future exchanges. Trump touted commercial wins, including large aircraft and energy purchases and traveled with major business figures, but many outlets noted Beijing’s silence and little independent confirmation of headline deals. Taiwan remained the most sensitive flashpoint: Trump warned Taipei against declaring independence while leaving arms decisions as potential leverage and Xi warned mishandling could risk conflict. Observers broadly judged the summit more theatrical than transformational, with concrete policy shifts largely unresolved even as both sides sought to limit escalation and influence topics from AI to Iran.
