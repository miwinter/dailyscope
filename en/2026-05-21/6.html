International reactions to Trump’s contested power and policies

US: Trump Presidency 2026-05-21 • 13 articles in total

In brief
  • U.S. Senate and bipartisan efforts are pushing back against Trump’s presidential powers and agenda.
  • Legal deals may shield Trump and his family from tax probes while some Republicans distance themselves strategically.
  • International reports highlight U.S. aggressive migration policies, diplomatic threats, and global economic impacts of Trumponomics.

International coverage portrays a presidency still defined by sharp institutional and political friction. U.S. Senate moves and bipartisan efforts to curb presidential war powers, along with procedural setbacks, point to congressional pushback against Trump’s agenda. At the same time, legal developments and deals that could shield Trump and his family from tax probes, plus signs of Republicans distancing themselves, raise acute questions about accountability and party strategy. Overseas reports also highlight aggressive executive moves on migration, diplomatic threats tied to visas and protocol, and analyses of Trumponomics’ global economic effects, underscoring wide international concern about U.S. policy direction.
