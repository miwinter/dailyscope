Bilateral momentum across trade, security and development

Region: Bilateral Deals 2026-05-21 • 82 articles in total

In brief
  • Governments and institutions worldwide pursued diverse bilateral deals focusing on trade, development, security, and connectivity despite diplomatic frictions.
  • Countries adopted pragmatic, multi-directional diplomacy involving high-level visits, economic partnerships, and regional cooperation amid political and strategic tensions.
  • Bilateral agreements addressed issues like tariff accords, infrastructure, migration, climate vulnerability, and security to advance regional integration and capacity building.

Across regions governments and institutions advanced a diverse set of bilateral deals and commitments this period, from trade and tariff accords between the EU and the United States to zero‑duty and investment initiatives linking China with African partners. Smaller states and subnational actors also pursued targeted cooperation: infrastructure and connectivity projects in Central and Southeast Asia, Pacific island development and security pacts, and bilateral agreements on energy, transport and agriculture. Diplomatic friction and protocol disputes appeared alongside closer ties, with expulsions and municipal ruptures contrasting with high‑level state visits and memoranda of understanding. Taken together, the items reflect a global pattern of pragmatic, often transactional diplomacy focused on trade, development, and security while remaining vulnerable to political tensions and strategic competition.
