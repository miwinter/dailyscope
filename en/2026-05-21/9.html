Protests force cabinet reshuffle as Bolivia seeks dialogue

Bolivia Protests Dialogue 2026-05-21 • 4 articles in total

In brief
  • Bolivia faces protests demanding dialogue and political change, prompting President Paz's inclusive cabinet reshuffle.
  • Economic hardship deepens political instability, limiting the reshuffle's ability to restore order.
  • Protesters and organizers urge peaceful demonstrations and renewed dialogue to resolve disruptions.

Bolivia has seen weeks of protests that included large marches and road blockades, with demonstrators pressing for dialogue and in some cases calling for the president's resignation. President Rodrigo Paz pledged to listen and announced a cabinet reshuffle presented as an effort to make the government more inclusive and to calm unrest. Reporting also highlights mounting economic pressures that analysts say deepen political fragility and could limit the reshuffle's stabilizing effect. Local organizers stressed peaceful demonstrations and urged a return to dialogue to resolve disruptions to daily life. Observers warn that without substantive concessions and improvements in economic conditions, volatility may continue despite the personnel changes.
