Deadly High-Speed Train Collision in Spain Sparks National Mourning and Investigation

Spain Train Accident and Crash 2026-01-20 • 50 articles in total

In brief
  • A high-speed train collision near Córdoba, Spain killed 39 to 41 people and injured over 150, one of the worst recent rail disasters there.
  • Authorities are investigating technical faults or track failures, excluding sabotage, with the government pledging transparency and declaring three days of mourning.
  • The accident raised national mourning, international condolences, and concerns about Spain's railway safety and infrastructure maintenance.

A catastrophic collision between two high-speed trains in southern Spain has resulted in at least 39 to 41 deaths and over 150 injuries, marking one of the deadliest rail disasters in recent Spanish history. The accident occurred near Córdoba and Andalusia, with emergency services responding swiftly to rescue and recovery efforts. Authorities have launched thorough investigations focusing on possible track failure or technical faults, while ruling out sabotage. The Spanish government declared three days of national mourning, with Prime Minister Pedro Sánchez pledging full transparency and accountability. The tragedy has drawn international condolences and raised urgent concerns about rail safety and infrastructure maintenance in Spain.
