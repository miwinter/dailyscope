Tensions over Greenland: US Ambitions and European Resistance

US–Greenland Tariff and Strategic Tensions 2026-01-20 • 115 articles in total

In brief
  • The US under Trump aggressively sought to acquire Greenland to counter Russian and Chinese influence using military threats and tariffs.
  • Europe, led by Denmark and the EU, opposed US moves to protect Greenland's sovereignty and proposed NATO military cooperation.
  • Trump's Greenland plans faced domestic and international criticism for risking alliances and Arctic stability.

The United States, under President Donald Trump, pursued aggressive strategies to acquire Greenland, citing its strategic importance in countering Russian and Chinese influence in the Arctic. Trump's approach included threats of military force, imposition of escalating tariffs on European countries opposing US plans, and linking his ambitions to personal grievances such as not receiving the Nobel Peace Prize. European nations, led by Denmark and the EU, strongly opposed these moves, emphasizing Greenland's sovereignty and proposing increased NATO presence and military cooperation to safeguard the region. The dispute triggered diplomatic crises, protests in Greenland and Europe, and raised concerns about the stability of transatlantic relations and NATO unity. The situation reflects broader geopolitical competition in the Arctic involving the US, Europe, Russia, and China, with economic and security dimensions intertwined.

US Perspective: Strategic Control and Economic Pressure

The United States, particularly under President Trump, viewed Greenland as a critical asset for national and global security, aiming to counter perceived Russian and Chinese threats. Trump's administration employed a mix of direct acquisition attempts, military posturing, and economic coercion through tariffs targeting European allies to assert influence over Greenland. Personal motivations, such as Trump's resentment over the Nobel Peace Prize, also colored the US stance, leading to controversial statements and diplomatic tensions. The US emphasized the necessity of controlling Greenland to ensure global security and was willing to escalate trade and military measures to achieve this goal.

European and Greenlandic Response: Sovereignty and Collective Security

European countries, led by Denmark and supported by the EU, strongly rejected US attempts to acquire Greenland, emphasizing respect for Greenland's sovereignty and self-governance. They coordinated diplomatic and economic countermeasures, including emergency summits, proposed NATO missions, and military deployments to reinforce security in the Arctic. European leaders warned against US tariff threats and sought to maintain alliance cohesion despite tensions. Greenlandic populations actively protested US ambitions, asserting their right to self-determination and opposing foreign pressure. The European stance reflects a commitment to uphold international law and regional stability amid growing geopolitical competition.

Internal US Political Opposition and International Criticism

Within the United States, significant opposition emerged against Trump's Greenland plans, including from members of Congress and officials who criticized the proposals as reckless and damaging to US alliances. Internationally, NATO members and global observers expressed concern that US actions could fracture alliances and destabilize Arctic security. Critics warned that military intervention or economic coercion could have severe diplomatic repercussions. This viewpoint highlights the complexity and controversy of US policy on Greenland, reflecting divisions domestically and abroad over the approach to Arctic geopolitics.
