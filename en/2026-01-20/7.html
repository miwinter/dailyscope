Bulgaria's President Radev Resigns Amid Political Turmoil and Speculation

Bulgaria Presidential Resignation 2026-01-20 • 16 articles in total

In brief
  • Bulgarian President Rumen Radev resigned before early elections amid political instability and protests.
  • His resignation could reshape Bulgaria's political landscape and influence its EU role and foreign policy, especially on Ukraine.
  • Observers highlight the historic nature and uncertainty of governance and regional implications following his departure.

Bulgarian President Rumen Radev has announced his resignation ahead of early parliamentary elections, marking an unprecedented political event in the country's recent history. His departure comes amid ongoing political instability, public protests, and speculation about his plans to form a new political party. The resignation has raised questions about Bulgaria's future governance, domestic political shifts, and its role within the European Union and regional affairs. Observers note that this move could significantly reshape Bulgaria's political landscape and influence its foreign policy, especially regarding the Ukraine conflict. The situation remains fluid as the country prepares for upcoming elections and potential changes in leadership.
