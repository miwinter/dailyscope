Global Reactions to Trump's Proposed $1 Billion 'Board of Peace'

Trump Board of Peace Proposal 2026-01-20 • 44 articles in total

In brief
  • Trump proposed a $1 billion-fee international peace group, the Board of Peace, seen as rivaling the United Nations.
  • Some countries accept or consider joining despite high fees, seeing it as a new peace effort opportunity.
  • Many allies reject the council, fearing it undermines the UN and questions its legitimacy.

US President Donald Trump has proposed a new international peace organization, the 'Board of Peace,' requiring a $1 billion fee for permanent membership. The initiative aims to address conflicts such as the Gaza situation but is widely viewed as a rival to the United Nations, raising concerns about undermining established multilateral institutions. Invitations have been extended to numerous world leaders, including Putin, Morocco's King, and Kazakhstan's president, with mixed responses ranging from acceptance to cautious skepticism or outright refusal. Many allied countries express reservations about the high financial barrier and the council's legitimacy, while some see it as a novel but controversial approach to global diplomacy. The proposal continues to spark debate over its potential impact on international peace efforts and geopolitical alignments.

Countries covering this topic

Supportive Engagement from Some Nations

Certain countries and leaders, such as Morocco, Kazakhstan, and some invited Asian nations, have shown willingness to participate or have accepted invitations to join Trump's Board of Peace. They view the initiative as an opportunity to play a role in resolving conflicts like Gaza and to engage in new diplomatic frameworks. This group tends to emphasize the potential benefits of additional peace efforts despite the high membership fee and political controversies.

Cautious or Skeptical Allies and Western Nations

Many allied countries, including Canada, France, Singapore, and some European states, have expressed skepticism or reluctance toward the Board of Peace, particularly criticizing the $1 billion fee and the initiative's challenge to the UN's authority. These nations are concerned about the legitimacy, inclusivity, and potential undermining of existing international institutions. Their responses range from evaluating the invitation carefully to outright refusal to participate or pay the fee.

Critical Views on Financial and Political Implications

Several articles emphasize the controversial $1 billion fee for permanent membership, viewing it as exorbitant and a potential deterrent that cools enthusiasm among invited nations. Critics argue that the financial demands and the council's structure may create exclusivity and undermine the UN's role. This perspective questions the legitimacy and effectiveness of Trump's peace council and highlights concerns about its political motivations and impact on global diplomacy.

Concerns Over Undermining the United Nations

A significant viewpoint among international leaders and experts is that Trump's Board of Peace could undermine the authority and effectiveness of the United Nations. This concern is shared by countries like France and others who have declined participation, fearing the creation of a parallel institution that disrupts established multilateral peace efforts. The debate centers on the potential geopolitical repercussions and the future of global governance.
