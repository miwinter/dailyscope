Divergent International Responses to US-led Gaza Peace Initiatives

US–Gaza Ceasefire and Peace Efforts 2026-01-20 • 34 articles in total

In brief
  • The US proposed a Gaza Peace Council led by figures like Tony Blair to stabilize Gaza and invited global powers for broader involvement.
  • France, Canada, and Israel oppose the US initiative due to legitimacy and security concerns, revealing divisions among allies.
  • Regional entities like Egypt and the Palestinian Authority pursue parallel governance and humanitarian efforts in Gaza.

The United States, under President Donald Trump, has proposed a Gaza Peace Council and a Board of Peace aimed at stabilizing the region and overseeing governance and reconstruction efforts. This initiative includes invitations to global leaders such as Russian President Vladimir Putin, and figures like Tony Blair and Marco Rubio, signaling an attempt to broaden international involvement. However, key allies like France and Canada have declined participation or financial support, reflecting skepticism about the council's framework. Israel has expressed strong objections to the council and related Gaza executive bodies, citing concerns over legitimacy and security, while regional actors like Egypt and the Palestinian Authority are engaged in parallel governance and humanitarian efforts. The initiative has also raised concerns about sidelining the United Nations and the potential for increased geopolitical tensions in the peace process.

US and Allies Promote Multilateral Peace Council

The United States has spearheaded the creation of a Gaza Peace Council and a Board of Peace, appointing prominent figures such as Tony Blair and Marco Rubio to lead diplomatic and stabilization efforts. The US has extended invitations to major international players including Russia, Jordan, and Pakistan, aiming to create a broad coalition to manage Gaza's governance and reconstruction. This perspective emphasizes a new multilateral approach to peace, seeking to reshape traditional diplomatic frameworks and involve diverse stakeholders.

French and Canadian Skepticism and Refusal to Join

France and Canada have expressed reservations and outright refusal to participate in the US-led Gaza Peace Council, citing concerns about the initiative's legitimacy and objectives. France's President Macron and government officials have publicly declined the invitation, signaling a cautious approach and differing priorities in addressing the Gaza conflict. This stance reflects a critical view of the US plan and highlights divisions among Western allies regarding the best path to peace.

Israeli Opposition and Security Concerns

Israel has voiced strong opposition to the US-led Gaza Peace Council and related governance bodies, questioning their legitimacy and expressing security concerns. Israeli officials have called for the closure of US-led coordination centers and criticized the inclusion of certain countries in the council. This viewpoint underscores Israel's desire to maintain control over Gaza-related decisions and skepticism toward international mechanisms perceived as undermining its interests.

Regional and Palestinian Governance Efforts

Regional actors such as Egypt and Palestinian authorities are actively engaged in managing Gaza's humanitarian and administrative challenges, with initiatives like the National Committee for the Management of Gaza operating from Cairo. Discussions between Egyptian intelligence and Gaza leadership focus on aid and reconstruction, while Palestinian leaders call for political unity and restoration of governance responsibilities. This perspective highlights local and regional efforts parallel to international peace initiatives.
