Devastating twin quakes in Venezuela trigger state of emergency and widespread rescue efforts

Venezuela Earthquake Response 2026-06-25 • 116 articles in total

In brief
  • Two strong earthquakes hit northern Venezuela, causing building collapses, infrastructure damage, tsunami alerts, and a national emergency declaration.
  • Government and emergency teams launched rescue operations, suspended activities, and closed the main airport amid ongoing aftershocks and infrastructure challenges.
  • International agencies warn of high casualties and damage while political debate continues over Venezuela's preparedness and response transparency.

Two powerful earthquakes — an initial shock around magnitude 7.1–7.2 followed by a stronger 7.5 aftershock — struck northern Venezuela, toppling buildings, disrupting infrastructure and prompting tsunami alerts for parts of the Caribbean. The government declared a national state of emergency, suspended classes, closed Maiquetía airport and mobilized health and rescue services as emergency teams and volunteers began search-and-rescue operations amid continuing aftershocks. Early estimates and USGS-derived projections raised fears of very high casualties, with international agencies and some governments warning of thousands to potentially much larger death tolls while numbers remain provisional. International offers of assistance and messages of solidarity arrived even as political figures and analysts questioned the country’s preparedness and the speed and transparency of official information.
