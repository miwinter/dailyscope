Europe heatwave strains health and energy systems

Europe Heatwave Watch 2026-06-25 • 25 articles in total

In brief
  • A persistent heat dome caused record temperatures and power outages across Europe, prompting red alerts and emergency measures.
  • Health and meteorological agencies warn of severe risks to vulnerable populations and strained services due to extreme heat.
  • Scientists link the heatwave to climate change and call for improved preparedness and infrastructure resilience.

A persistent heat dome has driven record temperatures across much of Europe, forcing authorities to issue red alerts and exceptional safety measures. The extreme heat has stressed electricity grids, triggering major outages in France and prompting warnings that UK power networks could be threatened as demand soars. Health agencies, including the WHO, and meteorologists warn of growing risks to vulnerable populations and strained health services, while cities and schools adopt emergency measures such as early closures and cooling centres. Reports also highlight broader consequences: drownings, disrupted daily life, and the low prevalence of air conditioning that increases exposure. Scientists link the pattern of consecutive records to climate change and call for better preparedness and infrastructure resilience.
