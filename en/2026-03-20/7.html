Egypt Strengthens Gulf Security Ties Amid Regional Tensions

Gulf Security Diplomacy 2026-03-20 • 8 articles in total

In brief
  • Egypt ties its national security to Gulf stability by strengthening relations and condemning attacks on Gulf energy infrastructure.
  • International actors call for de-escalation and protection of critical infrastructure to maintain Gulf stability and global energy security.
  • The US arms sales to the UAE and multilateral efforts like the OIC meetings reflect ongoing military support and regional diplomatic cooperation.

Recent developments highlight Egypt's strategic approach to Gulf security by deepening diplomatic and security ties with the United Arab Emirates and Qatar, framing Gulf stability as essential to its own national security. Egypt's leadership has publicly condemned attacks on Gulf energy infrastructure and emphasized solidarity with Gulf states. Meanwhile, international actors including the EU, France, Spain, Bahrain, and India call for de-escalation and protection of critical infrastructure, underscoring global concern over Gulf tensions. The US has approved significant arms sales to the UAE, reflecting ongoing military support and complex regional security dynamics. Multilateral organizations like the Organization of Islamic Cooperation continue to promote cooperation among Arab and Islamic states to address these challenges collectively.

Countries covering this topic

Egypt's Integration of Gulf Security into National Policy

Egypt positions Gulf security as inseparable from its own national security, demonstrated by President Sisi's visits to the UAE and Qatar and public condemnations of attacks on Gulf energy sites. This perspective underscores Egypt's commitment to regional stability through strengthened bilateral relations and solidarity with Gulf partners.

  • President Abdel Fattah Sisi: The security of Gulf states is an inseparable part of Egypt's national security
  • Egypt’s Sisi visits UAE, Qatar, says Gulf security is extension of national security
  • Egypt condemns sinful attacks on Gulf energy sites following missile strikes in Qatar and Riyadh

International Calls for De-escalation and Protection of Gulf Stability

Countries such as France, Spain, Bahrain, India, and the European Union emphasize the need for restraint, adherence to international law, and protection of critical infrastructure like energy and water sites. Their statements reflect a shared interest in maintaining Gulf stability and open navigation routes vital for global energy security.

  • France, Spain, Bahrain, India condemn attacks on Qatar
  • EU asks for reopening of the Strait of Hormuz and no more strikes on energy, water sites

US Military Support and Regional Security Dynamics

The United States' approval of a substantial weapons package for the UAE highlights ongoing military engagement and the strategic importance of arms sales in shaping Gulf security. This perspective points to the complex interplay of defense relationships influencing regional power balances.

  • Wall Street Journal: The US Administration Approved Weapons Worth About $7 Billion for the UAE

Multilateral Cooperation Among Arab and Islamic States

The Organization of Islamic Cooperation's commendation of consultative meetings among Arab and Islamic foreign ministers in Riyadh illustrates efforts to foster collective dialogue and cooperation on regional security issues, aiming to address tensions through diplomatic channels.

  • Organization of Islamic Cooperation commends the holding of the consultative ministerial meeting of foreign ministers of Arab and Islamic states in Riyadh

Regional Security Challenges and Political Commentary

Reports of attacks across Saudi Arabia, Qatar, and the UAE, coupled with commentary from leaders like Netanyahu and Trump, highlight the heightened security challenges in the Gulf. This viewpoint situates recent violence within broader geopolitical tensions affecting energy markets and regional stability.
