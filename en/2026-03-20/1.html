Escalating Iran War: Multi-Front Conflict and Global Energy Impact

Iran War 2026-03-20 • 171 articles in total

In brief
  • The conflict between the US, Israel, and Iran escalates with strikes targeting Iranian energy and military sites and Iranian retaliations against Israel and Gulf states.
  • The US conducts heavy military operations with significant budget requests amid political dissent and low public support for invasion, while Israel claims to have weakened Iran's nuclear and missile capabilities.
  • Global calls for diplomacy arise due to humanitarian damage, economic disruption, and risks of wider regional instability.

The ongoing war involving the United States, Israel, and Iran has intensified with multiple military strikes targeting Iranian energy and military infrastructure, including missile factories and gas fields. Israel claims significant degradation of Iran's nuclear and missile capabilities, while Iran retaliates with missile and drone attacks on Israel and Gulf states, escalating regional tensions. The United States is heavily involved, with substantial military operations and budget requests exceeding $200 billion, amid incidents such as the emergency landing of a US F-35 reportedly hit by Iranian fire. International actors express concern over the humanitarian toll, damage to cultural sites, and the risk of broader regional destabilization, with calls for diplomacy and restraint from countries like China, Turkey, and the UN. Public opinion in the US shows low support for ground invasion despite expectations of troop deployment, highlighting domestic unease about the conflict's expansion.
