Small States Strengthen Bilateral and Regional Diplomacy

Small State Diplomacy 2026-03-20 • 11 articles in total

In brief
  • Small states like Solomon Islands and Turkmenistan actively expand diplomatic ties and cultural exchanges to enhance their international presence and soft power.
  • Countries such as Laos, Liberia, and Liechtenstein focus on practical cooperation in trade, travel, security, and regional stability.
  • Some small states engage in global governance forums and adopt cautious diplomatic stances to navigate complex geopolitical challenges.

Recent developments in small state diplomacy highlight active efforts by countries like Solomon Islands, San Marino, Turkmenistan, and Liberia to deepen bilateral ties and regional cooperation. Solomon Islands notably expanded its international relations by establishing diplomatic ties with San Marino and signing a development agreement with Saudi Arabia. Turkmenistan engaged in academic and cultural exchanges with Japan and Kazakhstan, emphasizing its independent and neutral foreign policy. Other small states such as Laos, Liechtenstein, and Liberia focused on enhancing cross-border cooperation, security, and travel facilitation agreements. These activities reflect a broader trend of small states leveraging diplomacy to boost development, education, and political dialogue on the global stage.

Solomon Islands' Expanding Global Partnerships

Solomon Islands is actively broadening its diplomatic and development cooperation, signaling a strategic move to enhance its international presence. The establishment of diplomatic relations with San Marino and the landmark agreement with the Saudi Fund for Development demonstrate its intent to engage with both small states and major development partners. This approach underscores Solomon Islands' commitment to diversifying its diplomatic and economic ties beyond the Pacific region.

Turkmenistan's Academic and Cultural Diplomacy

Turkmenistan emphasizes its independent and neutral foreign policy through academic and cultural exchanges. By hosting round tables in Japan and Kazakhstan, it showcases its efforts to foster regional cooperation in education and promote its national identity internationally. These initiatives reflect Turkmenistan's strategy to build soft power and strengthen ties within Eurasian academic networks.

  • A round table was held at L.N. Gumilyov Eurasian National University in Astana on the initiative of Turkmen students
  • A round table at Chiba University in Japan dedicated to the 2026 slogan: 'Independent, Eternal Neutral Turkmenistan – Home of the Mighty Horse'

Bilateral and Regional Cooperation Among Small States

Several small states, including Laos, Liberia, and Liechtenstein, focus on practical cooperation to enhance regional ties and facilitate mobility. Laos prioritizes cross-border trade and infrastructure with Vietnam, Liberia and Barbados sign agreements to ease travel and political consultations, while Liechtenstein strengthens security and economic cooperation with Germany. These efforts illustrate a pragmatic approach to diplomacy aimed at mutual development and stability.

Engagement of Small States in Global Forums and Neutrality Stance

Some small states engage in global governance and maintain principled stances on international issues. The Central African Republic's participation in the World Government Summit reflects its outreach to global governance dialogues. Fiji's advocacy for neutrality in the Gaza conflict highlights a cautious diplomatic posture to avoid polarization. Belarus' hosting of a U.S. envoy indicates attempts to navigate complex geopolitical tensions. These perspectives show small states balancing global engagement with careful diplomatic positioning.
