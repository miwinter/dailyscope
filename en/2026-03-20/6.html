Divergent EU Alignments: Hungary's Moscow Ties and Serbia's Ambiguous EU Path

EU Alignment Diplomacy 2026-03-20 • 9 articles in total

In brief
  • Hungary under Orbán balances EU membership with pro-Russia actions, blocking aid to Ukraine and straining EU unity.
  • Germany criticizes Serbia's false EU integration efforts and close ties to Russia and China, threatening EU enlargement.
  • Other EU members pursue deeper integration and security partnerships, exemplified by the EU-Iceland defense deal and support for Ukraine's energy security.

The analyzed articles reveal contrasting approaches within Europe regarding EU alignment and foreign policy. Hungary, under Viktor Orbán, exhibits a complex stance, balancing EU membership with closer ties to Russia, notably blocking significant EU aid to Ukraine and drawing criticism for undermining EU unity. Serbia faces sharp criticism from Berlin for its ostensible EU aspirations while simultaneously aligning with Moscow and Beijing, casting doubt on its EU integration prospects. Meanwhile, other EU members pursue deeper integration and cooperation, exemplified by the EU-Iceland defense agreement and active EU involvement in Ukraine's energy security. These dynamics underscore tensions within the EU over cohesion, external alignments, and the future of enlargement.

Hungary's Ambivalent EU-Russia Position

Hungary, led by Viktor Orbán, maintains a contentious position within the EU by simultaneously engaging with Moscow and resisting EU consensus, particularly regarding Ukraine aid. Orbán's long tenure and electoral challenges reflect internal debates, while his actions, such as blocking a €90bn loan to Kyiv, strain EU unity and provoke criticism from other member states. This viewpoint highlights Hungary's strategic balancing act and its implications for European cohesion.

Berlin's Critique of Serbia's EU Commitment

German officials express strong skepticism about Serbia's commitment to EU integration, accusing President Vučić of feigning progress toward membership while fostering closer ties with Russia and China. This perspective underscores concerns about Serbia's foreign policy undermining EU enlargement efforts and calls for a clearer, more genuine pro-EU orientation from Belgrade.

Pro-EU Integration and Strategic Cooperation

Other EU members emphasize advancing integration and strategic partnerships, as seen in the EU's defense agreement with Iceland and active monitoring of Ukraine's energy infrastructure. These actions reflect a commitment to strengthening EU cohesion, security, and enlargement prospects, contrasting with the more ambivalent or obstructive stances of Hungary and Serbia.
