Caribbean braces as Hurricane Melissa intensifies causing widespread disruption

2025-10-27 • 50 articles in total

Hurricane Melissa has rapidly intensified to a Category 4 storm with projections of reaching Category 5 strength as it approaches Jamaica, Haiti, Cuba, and the Dominican Republic, prompting extensive emergency preparations and evacuations. The slow progression of the hurricane raises concerns over prolonged heavy rains and catastrophic flooding, compounding its destructive potential. The storm has already caused fatalities, damage to infrastructure, and significant humanitarian challenges, including water shortages affecting over a million people. Countries in the Caribbean are implementing advanced aid delivery measures, such as drone food distribution and international relief coordination. Meanwhile, abroad, protests and awareness efforts call for improved disaster management and accountability following previous catastrophic floods.

Caribbean nations' urgent hurricane preparedness and response

Caribbean countries such as Jamaica, Dominican Republic, Haiti, Cuba, and the Bahamas focus on immediate safety measures including mandatory evacuations, alerts for hundreds of thousands of residents, and emergency service mobilization. Authorities are emphasizing sheltering, resource allocation, and public warnings to mitigate the hurricane's impact. International bodies like the IOM and WFP are preparing for post-storm assistance specifically for Jamaica. This group underscores the acute human and infrastructural risks posed by Hurricane Melissa, describing direct experiences and official responses to protect lives and property.
