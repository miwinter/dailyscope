Venezuela Releases Prisoners Amid Political Tensions and Disputed Numbers

Venezuela Prisoner Release 2025-12-27 • 12 articles in total

In brief
  • Venezuela announced the release of 99 prisoners after the 2024 elections to ease tensions and promote reconciliation.
  • NGOs dispute the numbers and call for the full release of political prisoners, citing ongoing repression.
  • Opposition and international observers view the releases as partial concessions amid continued political conflict and human rights concerns.

Venezuela has announced the release of 99 prisoners detained after the 2024 presidential elections and related protests, a move framed by the government as a gesture to ease political tensions and promote reconciliation. However, several NGOs and human rights organizations dispute the official number of released detainees, suggesting fewer prisoners were freed and calling for the complete release of all political prisoners. The releases occur amid ongoing domestic unrest, contested election results, and heightened international pressure, particularly from the United States. Opposition figures remain cautious, noting that repression continues despite these concessions, and some political leaders have announced plans to return to Venezuela. Overall, the prisoner release is seen as a partial political concession within a broader context of unresolved political conflict and human rights concerns.
