Germany-China Trade Talks: Balancing Cooperation and Geopolitical Challenges

Germany–China Trade Talks 2026-02-26 • 17 articles in total

In brief
  • German Chancellor Merz visits China to strengthen trade and political ties amid global challenges.
  • Both nations commit to deepening cooperation while addressing trade tensions and geopolitical concerns.
  • The visit reflects a balance between economic growth and managing geopolitical risks with pragmatic dialogue.

German Chancellor Friedrich Merz's visit to China marks a strategic effort to deepen bilateral trade and political relations amid complex global dynamics. Merz advocates for stronger EU-China cooperation, emphasizing economic interdependence while addressing concerns such as fair competition and geopolitical risks, including China's stance on the Ukraine war. Both German and Chinese leaders express commitment to elevating ties despite rising trade tensions and international uncertainties. The visit reflects Germany's balancing act between pursuing economic growth and managing geopolitical responsibilities, highlighting pragmatic engagement with China. Discussions also focus on reducing trade imbalances and fostering dialogue to maintain stability in global commerce.
