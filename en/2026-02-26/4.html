Mexico's Drug Cartel Turmoil After El Mencho's Death

Mexico Drug Cartel Crisis 2026-02-26 • 26 articles in total

In brief
  • El Mencho's death caused a surge of violence and internal conflicts in the CJNG, destabilizing Mexico's security environment.
  • Mexican authorities launched large military operations and arrests to combat cartel violence and restore order.
  • The situation remains volatile with ongoing risks of violent power struggles and challenges protecting civilians and foreign nationals.

The killing of Nemesio 'El Mencho' Oseguera Cervantes, leader of the Jalisco New Generation Cartel (CJNG), has triggered a surge of violence and instability across Mexico. This leadership vacuum has led to internal cartel conflicts, retaliatory attacks, and heightened security concerns, affecting both locals and foreign nationals. Mexican authorities have responded with large-scale military deployments and arrests of cartel figures to restore order. While the operation marks a significant blow to organized crime, experts warn of potential prolonged violence and challenges in achieving lasting peace. The situation remains volatile, with ongoing efforts to dismantle cartel networks amid fears of further unrest.
