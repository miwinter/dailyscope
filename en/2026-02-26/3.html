Deadly Shootout Between Cuban Coast Guard and US-Registered Speedboat Escalates Bilateral Tensions

Cuba US Maritime Shootouts 2026-02-26 • 62 articles in total

In brief
  • A US-registered speedboat carrying Cuban nationals fired on Cuban coast guard forces in Cuban waters, prompting a deadly shootout.
  • Cuba described the event as thwarting an armed infiltration to protect sovereignty, while the US is investigating amid heightened diplomatic tensions.
  • The incident exposes ongoing maritime border enforcement challenges and impacts US-Cuba security and diplomatic relations.

A deadly maritime confrontation occurred when a US-registered speedboat, reportedly from Florida and carrying Cuban nationals, entered Cuban territorial waters and opened fire on Cuban coast guard forces. Cuban authorities returned fire, resulting in four deaths and multiple injuries among the boat's occupants. Cuba characterized the incident as a thwarted attempt at armed infiltration or terrorist activity, emphasizing their right to defend territorial sovereignty. The United States has launched investigations into the incident, with officials expressing concern and promising appropriate responses. This event has significantly heightened diplomatic and security tensions between Cuba and the United States, underscoring ongoing challenges in maritime border enforcement and bilateral relations.

Cuban Government Perspective: Defensive Action Against Terrorist Infiltration

Cuban authorities present the incident as a necessary defensive response to an armed attempt to infiltrate Cuban territory by a US-registered boat carrying individuals with alleged terrorist intentions. They emphasize the legitimacy of their actions to protect national sovereignty and security, framing the shootout as a prevention of a hostile attack. This viewpoint highlights Cuba's focus on border security and the threat posed by exile groups or armed actors operating from or through the United States.
