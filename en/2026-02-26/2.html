US-Iran Nuclear Talks Amid Rising Military Tensions and Divergent Perspectives

US–Iran Military and Nuclear Issues 2026-02-26 • 68 articles in total

In brief
  • The US demands an indefinite nuclear deal and enforces military pressure on Iran to prevent nuclear weapon development.
  • Iran seeks fair negotiations while preparing militarily and warning of retaliation against attacks.
  • International mediators warn of escalation risks amid fragile talks and regional power struggles.

The United States and Iran are engaged in critical nuclear negotiations in Geneva, representing a last diplomatic effort to prevent military conflict. While Iran expresses conditional willingness to reach a fair and balanced agreement, the US demands an indefinite nuclear deal and maintains a strong military posture, including deploying fighter jets and aircraft carriers in the region. US President Trump has issued ultimatums and threatened military action if Iran does not comply, escalating tensions. Iran, meanwhile, prepares for possible war, seeking support from allies and warning of severe retaliation to any attacks. The international community watches closely as mistrust, sanctions, and military buildups complicate the fragile diplomatic process.
