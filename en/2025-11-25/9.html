Mass Kidnapping of Nigerian Schoolchildren Highlights Security Crisis

Nigeria Schoolgirls Kidnapping 2025-11-25 • 21 articles in total

In brief
  • Over 300 students and staff were abducted from a Catholic school in Nigeria, with over 250 still captive.
  • The Nigerian government closed schools and intensified rescue efforts amid ongoing violence and security challenges.
  • Global and national actors condemn the kidnappings and call for urgent protection of educational institutions.

Over 300 students and staff were abducted from a Catholic school in Nigeria's Niger State, marking one of the largest mass kidnappings in the country's recent history. While about 50 students have managed to escape captivity and reunite with their families, over 250 remain held by armed groups. The incidents have drawn widespread national and international condemnation, highlighting the severe security challenges facing Nigeria's education sector. In response, the Nigerian government has closed numerous schools and intensified rescue efforts, but the ongoing violence continues to disrupt education and instill fear in communities. Religious and international leaders have also called for the immediate release of the abductees, underscoring the humanitarian urgency of the crisis.
