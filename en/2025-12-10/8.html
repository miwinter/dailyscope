Lithuania's State of Emergency Amid Belarus Balloon Incursions

Lithuania Emergency Over Belarus Balloons 2025-12-10 • 12 articles in total

In brief
  • Lithuania declared a state of emergency due to repeated balloon incursions from Belarus posing security threats.
  • These balloons caused airport closures and increased border vigilance, escalating tensions involving Belarus and Russia.
  • The situation highlights broader regional security concerns and prompts measures like asset freezes and defense readiness.

Lithuania has declared a state of emergency in response to repeated incursions of balloons originating from Belarus, which are perceived as security threats and hybrid attacks. These balloons have been linked to smuggling activities and surveillance, causing disruptions including airport closures and heightened border vigilance. The Lithuanian government views these actions as deliberate provocations escalating tensions with Belarus and potentially involving Russian interests. Measures under consideration include freezing Belarusian assets and increasing national defense readiness. The situation reflects broader regional security concerns in the Baltic area amid strained Belarus-Lithuania relations.
