Escalating US-Venezuela Tensions Marked by Military Incursions and Political Threats

US–Venezuela Diplomatic Escalation 2025-12-10 • 29 articles in total

In brief
  • The US increased military pressure on Venezuela, citing drug trafficking and criticizing Maduro's regime while not excluding intervention.
  • Venezuela and allies condemn US incursions as sovereignty violations and accuse the US of aggressive regime change attempts.
  • Third-party countries seek diplomatic mediation to prevent regional destabilization and military conflict.

Tensions between the United States and Venezuela have sharply escalated, highlighted by multiple incursions of US F-18 fighter jets into Venezuelan airspace and territorial waters, which Venezuela condemns as violations of sovereignty. US President Donald Trump has repeatedly warned that Venezuelan President Nicolás Maduro's days are numbered and has not ruled out military intervention, including a potential ground invasion. Venezuela, supported by allies such as Iran and Russia, has responded by reinforcing its military and denouncing US actions as aggressive and colonial threats. The US justifies its military presence as efforts to combat drug trafficking, while Venezuela and its supporters view it as provocations aimed at regime change. Diplomatic efforts by countries like Panama and Qatar to mediate reflect international concern over the risk of further destabilization in the region.
