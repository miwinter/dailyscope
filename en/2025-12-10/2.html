Escalating Thailand-Cambodia Border Conflict Causes Massive Displacement and Regional Tensions

Thailand–Cambodia Border Clash and Airstrikes 2025-12-10 • 55 articles in total

In brief
  • The Thailand-Cambodia border conflict has led to deadly clashes, airstrikes, and displaced over 500,000 civilians.
  • Both countries accuse each other and report military and civilian casualties while highlighting sovereignty and defense efforts.
  • International bodies call for ceasefire and negotiations due to humanitarian crises and regional instability risks.

The border conflict between Thailand and Cambodia has intensified, resulting in deadly clashes, airstrikes, and the displacement of over half a million civilians. Both countries accuse each other of initiating hostilities, with casualties reported among soldiers and civilians alike. Despite a ceasefire brokered by US President Donald Trump, renewed fighting has jeopardized peace efforts and heightened regional instability. International actors, including the United Nations and ASEAN, have called for restraint and diplomatic negotiations to prevent further escalation. The conflict has also severely impacted border trade and regional cooperation, with humanitarian concerns rising due to the large-scale evacuations and civilian suffering.
