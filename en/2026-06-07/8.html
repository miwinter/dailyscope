Bernadette Chirac, former French first lady, dies at 93

Bernadette Chirac Obituary 2026-06-07 • 10 articles in total

In brief
  • Bernadette Chirac, former French first lady, died at 93 and was known for her public role and charitable work.
  • French leaders praised her determination and compassion, prompting nationwide reflection on her legacy.
  • Global media highlighted her influence on French social life and official tributes from presidents.

Bernadette Chirac, former first lady of France and longtime public figure, has died at the age of 93. Reports emphasize her public role beside President Jacques Chirac, her charitable work and local political service, and her influence on French social life. French leaders including Emmanuel Macron and François Hollande issued warm tributes, praising her determination, independence and compassion, prompting nationwide reflection on her legacy. International outlets likewise framed her passing as the loss of an influential and respected figure in modern French history.
