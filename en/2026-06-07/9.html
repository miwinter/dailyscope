Clashes over roadblocks leave police wounded amid wider protests

Bolivia Protests Clashes 2026-06-07 • 8 articles in total

In brief
  • Clashes during roadblock clearances in Bolivia have left dozens injured, including multiple police officers hit by gunfire.
  • Nationwide blockades by peasants, miners, and workers have paralyzed cities and disrupted transport and supplies.
  • Economic grievances and political polarization risk prolonging instability despite authorities' vow to restore order.

Clashes during attempts to clear roadblocks across Bolivia have left dozens injured, with multiple police officers among the wounded and several hit by gunfire. Some reports describe about 20 people injured in a single clearance operation, while others record up to six officers hurt, four by gunfire. The demonstrations—involving peasants, miners and other workers—have paralyzed cities and disrupted transport and supply routes. Authorities have vowed to restore order, but accounts give limited detail on perpetrators, arrests or civilian casualties, and analysts warn that economic grievances and political polarization could prolong instability.
