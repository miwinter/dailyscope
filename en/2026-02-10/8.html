Iran Nuclear Talks: Conditional Offers Amid Persistent Tensions

Iran Nuclear Sanctions Talks 2026-02-10 • 10 articles in total

In brief
  • Iran is willing to dilute uranium enrichment to 60% only if all sanctions are fully lifted.
  • US-Iran talks show cautious optimism but face major disagreements and Israeli opposition.
  • Iran's internal protests complicate negotiations and influence both Iranian policies and US strategies.

Iran has shown a cautious willingness to dilute its enriched uranium stockpile to 60% purity, but only if all US and international sanctions are lifted, signaling a conditional approach to reviving the nuclear deal. Despite positive rhetoric from both Tehran and Washington, significant disagreements remain, with Iran assessing the seriousness of US intentions and Israel opposing any diplomatic agreements. Internal unrest and protests within Iran add complexity, influencing US negotiation strategies and Iran's domestic policies, including increased arrests. The talks reflect a delicate balance between diplomatic engagement and geopolitical tensions, with both sides signaling potential compromise but also maintaining firm conditions.

Iran's Conditional Negotiation Stance

Iran emphasizes its readiness to dilute uranium enrichment levels only if all sanctions are lifted, reflecting a strategic and conditional approach to negotiations. Tehran also highlights the importance of assessing US seriousness and maintains a firm stance against military threats, while continuing internal crackdowns amid protests. This viewpoint underscores Iran's balancing act between diplomatic engagement and domestic control.

US and Allied Perspectives on Negotiations and Internal Iranian Dynamics

From the US and allied perspectives, the negotiations are cautiously optimistic but fraught with unresolved issues. Former US officials link the success of Iranian protesters to negotiation dynamics, indicating that internal Iranian unrest influences diplomatic strategies. Experts acknowledge progress yet emphasize persistent disagreements, while Israel strongly opposes any diplomatic path that would ease sanctions on Iran.
