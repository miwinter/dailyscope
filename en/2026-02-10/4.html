Cuba's Deepening Energy Crisis Amid US Sanctions

Cuba Energy Crisis 2026-02-10 • 25 articles in total

In brief
  • Cuba faces a severe energy crisis due to US sanctions and halted Venezuelan oil supplies, causing fuel shortages and service disruptions.
  • The Cuban government enforces emergency measures and calls for international support while Cuba and Russia denounce US policies as economic warfare.
  • Airlines suspend flights and Cuba's population endures rationing and closures, reflecting deep logistical and social hardships.

Cuba is facing a severe energy crisis marked by critical shortages of aviation and general fuel supplies, leading to widespread disruptions including flight suspensions by major airlines such as Air Canada and rationing of essential services. The crisis is largely attributed to the tightening US sanctions and the cessation of Venezuelan oil supplies, which have significantly constrained Cuba's access to fuel. The Cuban government is implementing emergency measures like closing hotels and protecting essential services to mitigate the impact, while also appealing for international solidarity and dialogue. Russia and Cuba strongly condemn the US policies, accusing them of economic suffocation, whereas airlines and affected countries highlight the humanitarian and logistical challenges arising from the fuel scarcity. The situation continues to isolate Cuba internationally and threatens to deepen economic and social hardships for its population.

Cuban and Allied Perspectives on US Sanctions

Cuba and its allies, including Russia, frame the energy crisis as a direct consequence of aggressive US sanctions and an oil blockade designed to economically suffocate the island. They emphasize the humanitarian impact and call for international solidarity against what they describe as unjust and coercive policies. Cuba also signals willingness for dialogue but rejects pressure or preconditions, highlighting the political dimension of the crisis.

Airlines and International Travel Disruptions

Airlines from Canada, Europe, and other countries report severe operational challenges due to Cuba's jet fuel shortages, leading to flight suspensions, cancellations, and rerouting. These disruptions reflect the broader logistical and economic difficulties faced by Cuba amid the energy crisis. Airlines emphasize the uncertainty and monitor the situation closely, underscoring the impact on international connectivity and tourism.

Local Impact and Government Mitigation Efforts in Cuba

Reports from within Cuba and neighboring countries describe the tangible effects of the fuel shortage on daily life, including rationing of health services, hotel closures, and reliance on alternative fuels by citizens. The Cuban government is taking emergency measures to protect essential services and reduce energy consumption, reflecting the severity of the crisis on social and economic activities. These accounts highlight the growing hardship faced by the Cuban population.
