US Government Shutdown Ends After 43 Days Amid Political and Economic Strain

2025-11-14 • 41 articles in total

The United States' longest government shutdown in history lasted 43 days, significantly disrupting federal operations and affecting millions of workers. It ended after intense bipartisan negotiations, culminating in President Donald Trump signing a temporary funding bill to reopen government services and restore stability. The shutdown caused widespread economic consequences, estimated at $15 billion, and led to canceled flights and delayed public services. Despite the resolution, political divisions remain, with ongoing debates about budget priorities and the broader implications for governance. The reopening provides temporary relief but highlights persistent challenges in US political cooperation and fiscal management.
