Europe's Drive for Strategic Autonomy Amid Shifting US Relations

EU Strategic Autonomy and US Threat Perception 2026-02-15 • 16 articles in total

In brief
  • European leaders push for strategic autonomy to reduce US dependence and build independent defense, including nuclear deterrence.
  • Some advocate stronger European roles within NATO to enhance transatlantic security through cooperation.
  • Others stress balancing relations with the US and China while managing economic and diplomatic risks.

European leaders and officials increasingly emphasize the need for strategic autonomy to strengthen the EU's defense and geopolitical role. Figures like Macron, von der Leyen, and Merz advocate for Europe to develop independent security capabilities, including nuclear deterrence, to reduce reliance on the US. This push reflects concerns over changing global power dynamics and perceived limits of US leadership, especially under previous administrations. While some European voices call for a more assertive NATO with stronger European participation, others express confusion or caution about the US viewing the EU as a threat. Overall, the discourse highlights Europe's ambition to balance transatlantic ties with greater self-reliance amid external pressures from Russia, China, and shifting US policies.

Pro-European Strategic Autonomy Advocates

This group, including leaders from France, Germany, and the European Commission, strongly supports Europe developing independent defense capabilities and geopolitical influence. They argue that Europe must reduce dependence on the US and act as a global power with its own security architecture, including nuclear deterrence. Their perspective is driven by concerns over US unpredictability and the need for Europe to protect its interests amid global instability.

Calls for Stronger European Role within NATO

Some leaders emphasize enhancing Europe's role within NATO rather than decoupling from the alliance. They advocate for increased European defense commitments and leadership to complement US efforts, viewing NATO as the primary framework for transatlantic security. This viewpoint stresses cooperation and unity rather than confrontation with the US.

  • Starmer: Europe is a “sleeping giant,” we need a more European NATO
  • Pedro Sánchez says he does not understand why the US sees the European Union as a threat

Geopolitical Balancing and Economic Concerns

Analysts and leaders from countries like Romania highlight the complexity of balancing relations with major powers such as China and the US. They caution against economic risks from shifting alliances and emphasize the need for nuanced diplomacy. This perspective underscores the challenges Europe faces in pursuing strategic autonomy while managing global economic and political ties.
