Massive International Protests in Munich Against Iranian Regime

Munich Iran Protest 2026-02-15 • 13 articles in total

In brief
  • Mass protests in Munich drew up to 250,000 participants showing global opposition to the Iranian regime and calling for political change.
  • The rallies occurred during the Munich Security Conference and featured speeches by opposition leader Reza Pahlavi.
  • Protesters united diverse communities, emphasizing human rights and freedom with symbolic slogans like 'Woman, Life, Freedom'.

In Munich, a series of massive protests against the Iranian regime drew between 80,000 and 250,000 participants, reflecting widespread international opposition and solidarity with Iranian dissent. The demonstrations coincided with the Munich Security Conference, amplifying global attention on Iran's political and human rights crises. Prominent opposition figures, including Reza Pahlavi, addressed the crowds, symbolizing hopes for regime change and reform. The protests united diverse diaspora communities and international supporters, emphasizing calls for freedom, human rights, and political transformation in Iran. Authorities monitored the events closely due to their scale and political significance.
