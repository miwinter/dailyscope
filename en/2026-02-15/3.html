Divergent US-Iran Diplomatic Approaches Under Trump Era

US–Iran Diplomatic Talks Under Trump 2026-02-15 • 28 articles in total

In brief
  • US-Iran relations combined diplomatic nuclear talks in Geneva with Trump's calls for regime change and increased US military presence.
  • US officials showed readiness for military escalation alongside ongoing peace negotiations involving mediators like Switzerland and Oman.
  • International views stress the fragile negotiations and risks of escalation amid conflicting US policies and regional tensions.

During the Trump administration, US-Iran relations were marked by a complex interplay of diplomatic negotiations and hardline rhetoric. Multiple articles report scheduled nuclear talks in Geneva, often mediated by Switzerland and Oman, signaling ongoing efforts to resolve tensions through diplomacy. Concurrently, President Trump publicly advocated for regime change in Iran, suggesting it as a potentially positive outcome, which reflects a more confrontational stance. This dual approach is further complicated by increased US military presence in the Middle East, underscoring the persistent risk of escalation. Perspectives vary internationally, with some emphasizing diplomatic engagement and others highlighting the risks of military action and regime change ambitions.
